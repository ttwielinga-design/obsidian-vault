# **Terms of Service for DayVibe's Waiting List Program: A Comprehensive Legal Compliance Report**

## **1\. Introduction and Acceptance of Terms**

This report outlines the essential legal framework for DayVibe's Terms of Service ("ToS" or "Agreement"), specifically addressing the pre-launch waiting list program and anticipating future application functionalities. DayVibe, a Dutch Limited Liability Company (BV) based in Utrecht, Netherlands, is developing a voice-first journaling application with AI-powered insights. This document serves as a legally binding agreement between DayVibe and any individual ("User") accessing or utilizing the DayVibe website or waiting list program.

The current ToS primarily governs participation in the pre-launch waiting list, which involves the collection of email addresses and related communications. It is crucial to understand that future access to the full DayVibe application and its comprehensive features—including voice-first journaling, AI-driven emotional feedback, goal tracking, and the freemium monetization model—will be subject to a separate, updated, and more extensive set of Terms of Service and a Privacy Policy. Users will be required to review and explicitly accept these subsequent terms before gaining full access to the application.

For the acceptance of these ToS, DayVibe will mandate active and overt consent through a "clickwrap" mechanism. This requires users to click an "I Agree" checkbox or button, explicitly affirming that they have read, understood, and consented to be bound by these terms.1 This approach is not merely a technical preference but a fundamental legal safeguard. Dutch contract law, while accommodating electronic contract formation, places significant emphasis on clear and unambiguous consent.3 The European Court of Justice has affirmed that clickwrap is sufficient for forming a "durable record" of agreement, thereby strengthening the enforceability of the terms.2 This method provides robust evidence of consent, which is vital for demonstrating compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation's (GDPR) stringent consent requirements, especially as DayVibe will eventually handle sensitive personal data.4 Relying on implied consent, such as "browsewrap," carries substantial risks, as it may not adequately prove user awareness or agreement to the terms, potentially undermining the validity of data collection and contract enforceability if disputes arise.1 Therefore, to protect DayVibe and ensure adherence to legal standards, clickwrap is indispensable for the initial waiting list ToS, establishing the necessary "clear affirmative act" required by GDPR.4 Users who do not agree to these ToS will not be permitted to join the waiting list or utilize any associated services.

## **2\. User Eligibility & Account Creation**

### **2.1. Age Restrictions**

To ensure compliance with data protection regulations, users must be at least 16 years old to join the DayVibe waiting list. The GDPR Article 8 sets the default age of consent for information society services at 16 years, although individual Member States retain the flexibility to lower this threshold to as young as 13 years.6 In the Netherlands, the age of consent for Information Society Services is specifically set at 16 years.7

For any users who are below the age of 16, consent for data processing must be explicitly given or authorized by the holder of parental responsibility over the child.6 DayVibe is obligated to make reasonable efforts to verify such parental consent, taking into consideration available technology.6 This age restriction is a critical GDPR compliance point that directly impacts the validity of data collection and future user engagement strategies. If DayVibe were to collect email addresses from users under 16 without verifiable parental consent, such data collection would be unlawful under GDPR, potentially leading to significant fines imposed by data protection authorities.4 Moreover, as the DayVibe application will eventually involve the collection and processing of sensitive data, such as voice recordings and emotional insights, a failure to secure proper consent from minors at the initial waiting list stage could result in a tainted dataset, leading to ongoing legal challenges as users transition to the full application. Therefore, DayVibe must implement an age-gate or a clear age declaration mechanism on its website for the waiting list signup to ensure compliance with the 16-year age limit or to properly manage verifiable parental consent for younger users. This proactive measure also informs the design of future app development, ensuring age-appropriate features and consent flows are in place.

### **2.2. User Responsibilities**

Users who join the DayVibe waiting list agree to provide information that is accurate, current, and complete, such as their email address. While the initial waiting list primarily collects only email addresses, users will also be responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of any login credentials should they be introduced in future phases of the DayVibe service. This provision serves to future-proof the Terms of Service for when the full application launches and requires user accounts.

## **3\. Waiting List Program Specifics**

### **3.1. Nature of Waiting List**

Participation in the DayVibe waiting list is presented as an expression of interest only and does not constitute a binding contract for the provision of the full DayVibe service. DayVibe explicitly reserves the right to determine the eventual launch date, specific product features, and the selection criteria for alpha and beta testers at its sole discretion.9 This clarity is essential for managing consumer expectations and mitigating any implied contractual obligations that might arise under the Dutch Civil Code or the Dutch Consumer Protection Act, both of which aim to prevent significant imbalances in rights and obligations to the detriment of the consumer.10

The waiting list, though seemingly a simple pre-launch mechanism, carries significant legal risks related to consumer expectations and marketing practices if not managed with utmost transparency. The Dutch Authority for Consumers and Markets (ACM) and the Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD) strictly prohibit misleading or aggressive selling actions, including offering incorrect or incomplete information, creating false urgency (e.g., "limited spots left"), or using deceptive interfaces known as "dark patterns".11 DayVibe's launch strategy involves selecting a limited number of users for alpha and beta testing based on a ranking order from the waiting list.9 If communications imply a guaranteed spot or specific features that are later altered, this could be perceived as misleading. Failure to transparently manage these expectations could lead to consumer complaints, substantial regulatory fines (up to €900,000 by the ACM) 13, and damage to DayVibe's brand reputation. Establishing trust through transparent and fair practices from this initial touchpoint is crucial for long-term user retention and aligns with DayVibe's stated brand tone of being a "friendly, human voice".9

### **3.2. Transparency and Communications**

DayVibe is committed to providing accurate and non-misleading information regarding its product, features, and launch timeline in all pre-launch communications.13 The process for selecting users for alpha and beta testing will be transparently communicated, avoiding any implication of guaranteed access.9

Users must provide explicit consent to receive email communications related to the waiting list, product updates, and future marketing. Both the GDPR and the ePrivacy Directive mandate prior user consent for direct marketing communications, generally prohibiting unsolicited emails.4 DayVibe will provide a clear, free, and easy mechanism for users to unsubscribe from email communications at any time, ensuring that consent withdrawal is as straightforward as giving consent.4 Furthermore, DayVibe will maintain detailed records of consent, including when and how it was obtained, to comply with the accountability principles of the Dutch Data Protection Implementation Act (UAVG) and GDPR.8

## **4\. Data Privacy & Protection**

Data privacy and protection form a cornerstone of DayVibe's operations, particularly given the sensitive nature of the information processed. Adherence to GDPR and Dutch privacy laws is paramount.

### **4.1. Personal Data Collected**

For the waiting list, DayVibe primarily collects email addresses. For the future application, the scope of data collection will expand significantly to include voice input (raw audio), transcription data, AI-generated sentiment analysis, topic extraction, emotional feedback, goal tracking data, usage data, and device information.9 A clear definition of what data is collected at each stage is essential for transparency.

### **4.2. Legal Basis for Processing**

All personal data processing by DayVibe must have a valid legal basis under GDPR. For email marketing and the processing of sensitive data (such as voice recordings and emotional insights), explicit consent will be the primary legal basis. This consent must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous, obtained through a clear affirmative act.4 For data strictly necessary to provide a requested service (e.g., sending beta invites if explicitly agreed as part of the waiting list purpose), contractual necessity may serve as a legal basis. Limited processing for internal analytics or security, where user rights are balanced against DayVibe's interests, may rely on legitimate interests.4

### **4.3. Special Categories of Data (Health/Emotional Data)**

DayVibe's AI-powered emotional feedback, when linked to journaling about personal well-being, is likely to constitute "health data" or "data concerning a person's sex life or sexual orientation," which are classified as "special categories of personal data" under GDPR Article 9\.21 This classification significantly elevates the requirements for data processing. Processing such data requires explicit consent, unless a specific exemption applies.4 DayVibe will rely on explicit consent for this purpose. Furthermore, strict adherence to purpose limitation and data minimization principles is required, meaning only data necessary for the stated purpose will be collected and processed, and only for that specific purpose.5

The classification of emotional feedback as a "special category" under GDPR necessitates a fundamentally different approach to consent and data governance compared to a typical application. Emotion tracking, especially when tied to journaling about personal well-being, strongly indicates this data falls under "health data".23 GDPR Article 9 imposes strict restrictions on processing sensitive data, requiring explicit consent.4 The EU AI Act further complicates this by classifying "emotion recognition" as "high-risk," triggering significant compliance burdens such as risk assessment, data quality, and human oversight.25 Without explicit, granular consent for each specific purpose of processing emotional and voice data (e.g., sentiment analysis for personal insights, anonymized aggregation for product improvement), DayVibe faces severe GDPR non-compliance risks, including fines up to 4% of global annual turnover.28 The Dutch Data Protection Authority (DPA) has specifically warned about the risks of sharing sensitive data with AI chatbots.29 A robust privacy framework, transparently communicated, will be a competitive advantage, fostering user trust and aligning with DayVibe's brand identity.

### **4.4. Voice Data Handling (Future-Proofing)**

Voice data, including raw audio and transcriptions, must be carefully classified under Dutch privacy law and GDPR. Raw voice data may be considered biometric data if used for identification purposes 21, while transcriptions are personal data. Explicit consent will be obtained for the processing of voice data by AI for sentiment analysis, topic extraction, and emotional feedback. Users must clearly understand how their voice data will be analyzed and what insights will be derived. DayVibe will establish clear retention periods for voice data and derived insights, ensuring data is not kept "longer than necessary," with particular emphasis on minimizing retention for sensitive data.18

### **4.5. AI & Automated Decision-Making (Future-Proofing)**

DayVibe's AI-powered emotional feedback may fall under the definition of "emotion recognition" systems. While the EU AI Act prohibits such systems in workplaces and educational institutions 25, general emotion recognition systems are classified as "high-risk".25 If DayVibe's AI is classified as high-risk, it will be subject to strict obligations, including adequate risk assessment, ensuring high-quality datasets to minimize bias, logging of activity, detailed documentation, clear information provision to the deployer, appropriate human oversight, robustness, cybersecurity, and accuracy.25

Transparency and explainability (XAI) will be crucial. DayVibe will provide clear information about the logic involved in AI-powered insights, how they are generated, and their purpose. Users will be informed when they are interacting with an AI.25 Measures to mitigate bias in AI algorithms will be addressed proactively, especially given the sensitive nature of emotional data. The Dutch DPA emphasizes preventing discrimination from algorithms.34 If AI insights lead to decisions that significantly affect users (e.g., limiting access to features based on emotional state), users will have rights to human intervention, to express their point of view, and to contest the decision, as mandated by GDPR Article 22\.31

### **4.8. Cross-Border Data Transfers**

Personal data of EU residents will primarily be processed within the European Economic Area (EEA). For transfers outside the EEA, such as to service providers in the United States, specific legal bases and safeguards will be employed. ## **5\. Intellectual Property Rights**

### **5.1. DayVibe's Intellectual Property**

All intellectual property rights in the DayVibe application, including its underlying software, proprietary AI algorithms, design, trademarks, and original content (excluding User-Generated Content), are owned by DayVibe or its licensors. DayVibe will actively pursue trademark protection for its name and logo in key markets, including the Netherlands, the EU, and globally. The Dutch Trade Name Act provides protection for company names 58, and proactive trademark registration offers stronger legal safeguards.

The application's software, design elements, and original content are automatically protected by copyright under Dutch law from the moment of their creation, without the need for formal registration.58 The Dutch Copyright Act (Auteurswet) grants automatic protection for original literary, scientific, or artistic works.59

DayVibe's proprietary AI algorithms and models are considered valuable trade secrets. To ensure their protection, these algorithms must genuinely be secret, possess commercial value due to their secrecy, and DayVibe must implement reasonable precautions to maintain their confidentiality.37 Such precautions include non-disclosure agreements (NDAs), robust digital security measures, and strict access controls.39 The Dutch Trade Secret Protection Act allows for legal action against unauthorized use or disclosure of such secrets, which is crucial for preserving DayVibe's competitive advantage.9

### **5.2. User-Generated Content (UGC) Ownership & Licensing**

Users retain ownership of their original content, such as voice recordings and journal entries, that they create and submit to the DayVibe application. This is consistent with the Dutch Copyright Act, which protects original works created by individuals.59

However, for DayVibe to operate, improve, and provide its AI-powered services, users will grant DayVibe a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license. This license permits DayVibe to use, reproduce, modify, adapt, publish, translate, create derivative works from, distribute, and display their UGC for purposes such as AI analysis, personalization, and data aggregation for insights. This license will explicitly cover the use of UGC for AI training and model improvement, potentially in an anonymized or aggregated form, in line with the privacy policy.61 The interaction between user-generated voice data and DayVibe's AI creates complex intellectual property considerations. While users own their raw inputs, the insights generated by DayVibe's proprietary AI are considered DayVibe's intellectual property. Without a sufficiently broad license for UGC, DayVibe could be legally restricted from using user data to train and improve its AI models, which is fundamental to its "insight depth strategy" and "development priorities".9 This could significantly impede product development and competitive differentiation. The Terms of Service must clearly define the scope of this license, including explicit permission for AI processing, model training, and the creation of derivative (AI-generated) insights, aligning with industry practices seen in other platforms.62

DayVibe reserves the right to monitor, remove, or edit UGC that violates the Terms of Service (e.g., harmful, illegal, or infringing content), while adhering to the transparency obligations outlined in the Digital Services Act.11

### **5.3. Copyright Notices and Attributions**

Appropriate copyright notices (e.g., © DayVibe) will be included on the platform. If user-generated content is displayed publicly (e.g., in community features, if implemented), any requirements for user attributions will be specified.

## **8\. Liability & Indemnification**

### **8.1. Limitation of Liability**

DayVibe's Terms of Service will state that its liability is limited to direct damages and will exclude liability for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages (e.g., lost profits, data loss), to the maximum extent permitted by law. However, it is crucial to acknowledge that under the Dutch Civil Code (Article 6:237) and the Unfair Contract Terms Directive, clauses excluding or limiting liability for intentional conduct or gross negligence are strictly prohibited, void, and unenforceable.10 Furthermore, damage caps or exclusions for consequential damage may also be deemed unfair.80 Therefore, any liability limitations included must be reasonable and must not create a "significant imbalance" to the detriment of the consumer.3

Dutch law's strict stance on liability limitations, particularly for gross negligence and intentional conduct, means DayVibe cannot simply adopt standard broad disclaimers common in other jurisdictions. While some competitor terms, such as those of Replika 66 and MindShift 61, include broad disclaimers for various types of indirect damages, these may not be fully enforceable under Dutch consumer law. If DayVibe includes overly broad or prohibited liability clauses, they risk being "annulled by a Dutch lawyer" 3, which would render them legally invalid and potentially expose the company to unlimited liability in cases of gross negligence or intentional harm. DayVibe's liability section must therefore be meticulously drafted to comply with these specific Dutch legal nuances, ensuring enforceability while still protecting the business to the maximum permissible extent. This requires a tailored approach rather than simply replicating clauses from international competitors.

### **8.2. Indemnification**

### **9.1. Governing Law**

### **9.2. Jurisdiction**

### **9.3. Arbitration**

Implementing an arbitration clause for DayVibe's global consumer base presents legal complexities due to the significant differences between EU/Dutch and US approaches to consumer arbitration. In the EU, arbitration agreements in consumer contracts are presumed unfair if they were not individually negotiated or if they do not provide the consumer with the right to elect to go to regular state courts.85 Specifically, Dutch law states that such agreements are "not binding on consumers, if such consumers are not given the right to elect to go to the regular state courts instead (subject to a one month limitation period)".85 This contrasts sharply with the US approach, where the Federal Arbitration Act generally does not impose such restrictions on pre-dispute arbitration agreements.87

If an arbitration clause is included in DayVibe's ToS, it must offer consumers a clear option to opt out and choose the Dutch state courts instead, typically within a specified period (e.g., one month).85 The clause should also specify whether arbitration is binding or non-binding.89 Binding arbitration generally offers faster resolution but limits appeal options.89 If DayVibe includes a mandatory arbitration clause without a clear opt-out to state courts, it risks the clause being deemed unenforceable for EU and Dutch consumers, potentially leading to consumers bypassing arbitration and directly pursuing litigation in state courts, thereby defeating the purpose of the clause. DayVibe must weigh whether the benefits of arbitration (speed, specialized arbitrators) 89 outweigh these legal complexities and potential unenforceability for its primary EU market. A pragmatic approach would be to include an arbitration clause with a clear opt-out mechanism for consumers to choose state courts, or to rely solely on Dutch courts and alternative dispute resolution mechanisms. Given DayVibe's target of "global users" 9, a multi-jurisdictional approach to dispute resolution is essential.

### **9.4. Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) Mechanisms**

Users will be informed about available Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) mechanisms for consumer disputes in the EU. It is important to note that the European ODR platform is being discontinued as of July 20, 2025\.90 DayVibe will, therefore, refer users to alternative Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) bodies certified under the Digital Services Act.90 The Consumer Rights Directive and ODR Regulation (despite changes) aim to provide consumers with accessible out-of-court dispute resolution options.

## **Legal Compliance Summary**

* **Burgerlijk Wetboek (Dutch Civil Code):** Contracts will be formed with clear consent and transparent terms.3 "Forbidden terms," such as unilateral changes, unreasonable liabilities, or exclusions for intentional/grossly negligent conduct, will be avoided.3 DayVibe acknowledges liability for digital products not meeting specifications, providing consumer remedies.93 Information duties for distance selling (seller identity, product characteristics, price, delivery, cooling-off period) will be adhered to.75 Any agreed-upon shorter limitation periods for claims will not be less than one year for consumers.94

## **Risk Assessment and Mitigation Strategies**

### **1\. Identified Legal Vulnerabilities**

DayVibe faces several legal vulnerabilities that require proactive management:

* **Misclassification of AI/Service:** There is a risk that DayVibe's AI-powered emotional feedback could be reclassified as a "medical device" under the EU Medical Device Regulation (MDR) if marketing or functionality implies diagnosis or treatment. Such reclassification would lead to extensive and costly regulatory burdens.72  
* **Insufficient Consent for Sensitive Data:** A high risk of non-compliance with GDPR Article 9 exists if explicit, granular consent for processing voice and emotional data is not obtained, or if the purposes for processing are not clearly defined. This extends to consent for using data for AI training.4  
* **Unenforceable Liability Clauses:** Dutch law heavily restricts limitations of liability, particularly for gross negligence or intentional conduct. Broad disclaimers, common in other jurisdictions, could be deemed void, leaving DayVibe exposed to greater liability.10  
* **Unfair Commercial Practices in Waiting List:** DayVibe risks consumer complaints and fines from the ACM if its pre-launch communications are misleading, create false urgency, or imply guarantees of access or features that are not delivered.13  
* **Non-Compliant Subscription Terms:** Failure to adhere to specific Dutch and EU rules on automatic renewal (e.g., indefinite term, one-month notice) and cooling-off periods (14 days, with specific digital content exceptions) could lead to contract annulment or regulatory action.75  
* **Weak International Data Transfer Mechanisms:** Transfers of personal data to countries without an EU adequacy decision, if not supported by proper Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), Data Processing Agreements (DPAs), or Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs), could lead to GDPR violations and DPA enforcement actions.41  
* **AI Act Non-Compliance (Future):** As an AI system performing emotion recognition, DayVibe is likely to be classified as "high-risk" under the upcoming EU AI Act. This will trigger significant obligations related to risk management, data quality, human oversight, and transparency that require proactive planning.25  
* **Accessibility Non-Compliance:** Failure to meet European Accessibility Act (EAA) and WCAG 2.1 AA standards by June 2025 could result in fines and market bans across the EU.97

### **2\. Proposed Mitigation Actions**

To address these vulnerabilities, the following mitigation actions are proposed:

* **Clarity in Service Definition:** Consistently position DayVibe as a "wellness and personal development tool" across all platforms and communications. Prominently display clear "no medical advice" disclaimers and avoid any language that suggests diagnosis, treatment, or clinical intervention.55  
* **Enhanced Consent Flows:** Implement a robust Consent Management Platform (CMP) to manage GDPR-compliant explicit consent, particularly for sensitive data processing and marketing. Ensure granular options for users to control data use and provide easily accessible mechanisms for consent withdrawal.4  
* **Tailored Liability Clauses:** Draft liability limitations meticulously to comply with the Dutch Civil Code, explicitly carving out liability for gross negligence and intentional conduct, and setting reasonable, enforceable caps on other forms of liability.10  
* **Transparent Waiting List Management:** Clearly communicate that joining the waiting list is an "expression of interest" and does not guarantee access or specific features. Be transparent about the selection process for alpha/beta users and that product features and timelines are subject to change. Strictly avoid any deceptive marketing tactics or "dark patterns".13  
* **Compliant Subscription System:** Design the subscription flow to align with Dutch automatic renewal rules, ensuring subscriptions convert to an indefinite term with a one-month cancellation notice period. Implement a clear "cancel contract" button accessible online, and manage cooling-off periods for digital content with explicit user acknowledgment of the loss of withdrawal right upon commencement of service.75  
* **Robust Data Transfer Framework:** Prioritize engagement with service providers located in countries with EU adequacy decisions. For other international transfers, ensure up-to-date Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are in place, accompanied by thorough Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) and Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) to evaluate local laws and ensure effective protection.41  
* **Proactive AI Act Preparedness:** Initiate internal assessments of DayVibe's AI features against the requirements of the EU AI Act. Document data quality, implement bias mitigation strategies, and plan for human oversight and explainability features. Adopt a "privacy by design" approach for all AI development.18  
* **Accessibility Integration:** Integrate WCAG 2.1 AA standards into the design and development process from the outset. Conduct regular accessibility audits, publish a comprehensive accessibility statement, and provide a clear feedback mechanism for accessibility issues.99

## **Implementation Recommendations**

### **1\. Technical Implementation**

* **Clickwrap Integration:** Implement a robust clickwrap solution on the DayVibe website for Terms of Service acceptance during waiting list signup. The checkbox should be unticked by default, requiring active user input. This ensures explicit consent, which is critical for GDPR compliance and contract enforceability.1  
* **Consent Management Platform (CMP):** Deploy a comprehensive CMP to manage user consents for cookies, marketing communications, and sensitive data processing. This platform should allow for granular control over data processing activities and provide easily accessible mechanisms for users to withdraw their consent at any time.4  
* **Age Verification:** Implement an age-gate or a clear age declaration mechanism at the point of signup to comply with GDPR Article 8 and Dutch law (16 years old). For users under 16, integrate verifiable parental consent mechanisms to ensure lawful data collection.6  
* **Secure Data Storage:** Ensure all personal data, especially voice recordings and emotional insights, are stored securely using strong encryption 39, robust access controls, and regular security audits. This is vital for protecting sensitive data and mitigating data breach risks.8  
* **Automated Data Retention:** Implement systems to automatically delete or anonymize personal data once its defined retention period expires, in accordance with GDPR and Dutch DPA guidelines.30  
* **Accessibility Features:** Integrate WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliant features into the website and app development from the initial design phase. This includes proper semantic HTML, text alternatives for audio/video (transcriptions), keyboard navigation, clear language, and compatibility with assistive technologies.99
