# **Pain Discovery**

### **Level 1: Surface Pain (What they complain about)**

1. What do they actively complain about on social media?  
2. What makes them say "I hate it when..." or "Why is there no..."?  
3. What do they spend time venting about to friends/colleagues?  
4. What processes do they describe as "such a hassle" or "so annoying"?

### **Level 2: Hidden Frustrations (What they've accepted)**

1. What do they do manually that feels outdated?  
2. What workarounds have they created for broken systems?  
3. What do they say "it is what it is" about?  
4. What inefficiencies have they just learned to live with?

### **Level 3: Emotional Impact (How it makes them feel)**

1. What makes them feel stupid, frustrated, or powerless?  
2. What causes them stress or anxiety regularly?  
3. What makes them feel left behind or inadequate?  
4. What keeps them up at night worrying?

### **Level 4: Cost Analysis (What it's actually costing them)**

1. How much time do they waste on this problem weekly?  
2. What opportunities are they missing because of this issue?  
3. How much money is this problem costing them directly/indirectly?  
4. What relationships or reputation damage does this cause?

### **Level 5: Identity Conflict (Who they want to be vs. reality)**

1. How does this problem conflict with their self-image?  
2. What does solving this allow them to become?  
3. How does this prevent them from being the person they want to be?  
4. What would their ideal day look like without this problem?

## **The Pain Validation Questions**

### **For Direct Research:**

1. "Walk me through your worst day dealing with \[area\]. What happened?"  
2. "If you had a magic wand to fix one thing about \[process\], what would it be?"  
3. "What's the dumbest thing you have to do regularly that you wish you didn't?"  
4. "When you tell people about your job/hobby/life, what do you always end up complaining about?"

### **For Observation:**

1. What do they Google search for solutions to?  
2. What Facebook groups/Reddit threads are they active in complaining?  
3. What do they pay for that barely works?  
4. What do they apologize for when talking to others?

## **The 4 Types of Pain to Look For**

### **1\. Time Pain**

1. "This takes forever"  
2. "I waste so much time on..."  
3. "By the time I finish this, I could have..."

### **2\. Money Pain**

1. "This is so expensive for what it is"  
2. "I'm paying for something that barely works"  
3. "I could save so much if..."

### **3\. Effort Pain**

1. "This is so complicated"  
2. "Why do I need 5 different tools for this?"  
3. "I have to remember so many steps"

### **4\. Emotional Pain**

1. "This makes me look unprofessional"  
2. "I'm embarrassed by..."  
3. "I feel stupid when I can't..."

## **Red Flag Questions (Weak Pain Signals)**

Be wary if you only find:

1. "It would be nice if..." (want, not need)  
2. "Sometimes I wish..." (occasional frustration)  
3. "In a perfect world..." (unrealistic expectation)  
4. "I guess it could be better" (mild preference)

## **The Pain Intensity Test**

Ask yourself:

1. Would they pay to make this pain go away today?  
2. Do they actively seek solutions, even bad ones?  
3. Do they complain about this unprompted?  
4. Would solving this meaningfully change their day/life/business?

## **Mining Pain from Different Sources**

### **Social Media Archaeology**

1. Search Twitter/X for "\[your industry\] \+ sucks/hate/annoying"  
2. Look at 1-star reviews of existing solutions  
3. Check Reddit complaint threads  
4. Monitor Facebook group frustrations

### **Direct Conversation Questions**

1. "What's broken in your world that everyone just accepts?"  
2. "If you could eliminate one daily frustration, what would it be?"  
3. "What do you pay for that you feel ripped off by?"  
4. "What makes you irrationally angry that others might not understand?"

### **Professional Context**

1. "What part of your job do you dread?"  
2. "What makes you look bad to your boss/clients?"  
3. "What prevents you from being as good at your job as you want to be?"  
4. "What would make you a hero at work if you could solve it?"
