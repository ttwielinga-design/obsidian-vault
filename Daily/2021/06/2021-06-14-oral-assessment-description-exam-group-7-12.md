Oral assessment module Professional Hospitality group 7 - 12 
 
 
1. General set up and procedures 
Based on the financial reports created and used in the practical departments of RDM and Refresh, the 
student defends the own activities within these departments to the General Manager and provides 
detailed advice for the near future.  
 
The exam is an oral assessment of 25 minutes on an individual basis, and an equal amount of time will be 
spent on RDM and Refresh. The sub elements follow from the classes of Financial Management, Hotel 
Business and HR Management and are divided between the own experiences and assignments created in 
class (part 1 & 3) and advice for the near future (part 2 & 4). All sub elements can be found in section 3 
and 4 of this document.  
 
During the assessment, some topics are always discussed, and some vary: 
- 
Financial management RDM: always part of the assessment 
- 
Financial management REFRESH: always part of the assessment 
- 
Hotel Business: a choice is made between RDM and REFRESH 
- 
HRM: a choice is made between RDM and REFRESH 
 
The student is allowed to show a limited number of materials during the assessment that will assist in 
discussing the required elements to be found below in section 3 and 4 of this document. It is permitted 
to use the following documents during the assessment: 
 
- 
All materials used to determine room prices (RDM) 
- 
Elements selected from the Revinate program (RDM) 
- 
Menu engineering sheet (REFRESH) 
- 
Food cost calculations from HOREKO (REFRESH) 
- 
Own designed service blue print, including concept description/visualization (REFRESH) 
 
During the assessment, the student will be able to share the computer screen, allowing the assessors to 
follow along with the documents prepared. In addition, the preparation can be send to the assessor 
beforehand. Be aware that the assessment is not a presentation, assessors will actively ask questions. 
 
The student is allowed to cooperate with others regarding the preparation of this assessment and the 
completion of documents allowed to be used in the oral.  
 
The assessor will take the role of the General Manager. The assessor is a lecturer within the module 
‘professional hospitality’ and the lecturer will be accompanied by a third- or fourth-year student.  
 
The specific planning of the individual assessments will be sent by the module coordinator in a separate 
schedule. Due to the COVID-19 measures still in operation, the assessments will take place via the online 
video conferencing program Bluejeans. This program establishes a video connection between the student 
and the assessor, and allows to share documents in real-time. An access link can be found in the additional 
document ‘protocol for online assessment’.  
 

 
2 
 
The grade of the oral assessment will count for 50% regarding the overall examination ‘Professional 
Product Professional Hospitality’. The other 50% consists of the grades regarding the practicals of RDM 
and Refresh. Both elements require a minimum grade of 5.5.  
 
The grade of the oral assessment will be communicated after the completion of all assessments in your 
examination week. Information regarding the procedure around the inspection opportunities will also be 
provided then. If required, one resit opportunity will be offered in the next module.  
 
The language of the assessment is English for the international stream and Dutch for the national stream.  
 
The registration code for this examination on OSIRIS is ‘M-HH-1-PH-PP’ Professional Hospitality 
Professional Product.

3. Specific requirements for the RDM department 
1 
General discussion of the theory assignment from the RDM week  
a. Determination of room prices and the possible effect on the hotel’s ADR, occupancy and 
RevPAR (Financial Management) 
b. Service quality & GAP analysis (Hotel Business) 
c. Staff performance (HR Management) 
2 
Advise for the near future – Week for analysis: Monday September 20th up until and including 
Sunday September 26th 
a. Advised room prices and its effect on ADR, occupancy and RevPAR (Financial 
Management) 
b. Proposed improvements to service quality – closing gaps in the GAP analysis model (Hotel 
Business) 
c. Proposed improvements for staff performance (HR Management) 
 
Set up following the order of the structure of the defense: 
1a: The student discusses the determined room prices for the second block in the RDM department. The 
student needs to show which documents and sources were used to set the room prices, and the student 
will need to defend the choices that were made. In addition, the effect of the proposed room prices on 
the revenue elements need to be explained. (Financial Management) 
 
1b: The student discusses the service quality of the RDM department whilst using the GAP analysis model. 
In addition, a link needs to be made to the five dimensions of perceived service quality (Tangibles, 
Reliability, Responsiveness, Assurance, Empathy) and the elements of consistent quality (zone of 
tolerance, measurable goals, special/common causes, satisfiers/dissatisfiers, critical to quality). If 
possible, use outcomes from the Revinate system. (Hotel Business) 
 
1c: The student discusses the staff performance linked to the organizational structure and the service 
profit chain. (HR Management) 
 
After the discussion of the theory assignment of RDM, the student needs to provide advice for the near 
future. This advice will be focused on the week of September 20th (Monday up until and including Sunday).  
 
2a: The student will provide advised room prices for the assigned week (Monday September 20th up until 
and including Sunday September 26th), using similar documents and databases as before (data from Clock 
PMS system, competitor prices) and will explain the possible effects on the ADR, occupancy and RevPAR 
for the THCB. The criteria for effective revenue management and drivers of RevPAR need to be known. 
(Financial Management) 
 
2b: The student will provide proposed improvements to the service quality of the RDM department in 
order to close down the gaps in the GAPS analysis model. As before, a link to the five dimensions of 
perceived service quality and the elements of consistent quality will need to be made. (Hotel Business) 
 
2c: The student discusses proposed improvements for staff performance linked to the organizational 
structure and the service profit chain. (HR Management)

4. Specific requirements for Refresh 
3. General discussion of the theory assignment from the Refresh week 
a. Menu engineering analysis including the food cost calculation per dish (Financial 
Management) 
b. Service blue print analysis (Hotel Business) 
c. Staff satisfaction (HR Management) 
 
4. Advise for the near future  
a. Optimized menu for a day of choice, including food cost calculations and effects on 
profitability (Financial Management) 
b. Refresh concept design based on the service blue print (Hotel Business) 
c. Proposed improvements for staff satisfaction (HR Management) 
 
Set up following the order of the structure of the defense: 
3a: The student discusses the menu engineering analysis within the theory assignment of the theory week 
of Refresh. On both elements of the menu engineering technique (popularity and gross profit) an analysis 
needs to be made, involving a thorough understanding of the methodology and a translation into the 
official classifications (Winner, Runner, Sleeper & Loser). In addition, a link needs to be made to the overall 
cost of sales percentage for Refresh. (Financial Management) 
 
3b. The student can apply the service blue print model to Refresh. The Refresh concept needs to be linked 
and translated into the official parts of this theoretical model, being the physical evidence, customer, 
contact person onstage & backstage, and the support processes. (Hotel Business) 
 
3c. The student discusses the staff satisfaction linked to attitudes and the service profit chain. (HR 
Management) 
 
After the discussion of the theory assignment for Refresh, the student needs to provide advice for the near 
future.  
 
4a. The student selects a day of choice and creates an optimized menu based on the analysis in the theory 
week. The menu needs to follow the take-away concept of Refresh as discussed in the practicals: 
Pasta/Noodles, Focaccias, Sandwiches and Salad. Per optimized dish, a foodcost calculation needs to be 
completed including a cost of sales percentage. The effects per dish on the overall cost of sales for Refresh 
are to be discussed, including an understanding as to how this number can be influenced. (Financial 
Management) 
 
4b. The student will provide an adapted concept for Refresh based on your own experiences in the 
department. The adapted concept is to be linked and translated into the official parts of the service blue 
print and the chosen menu. Differences between the blue prints for the standard concept of Refresh and 
the own chosen concept are required to be discussed. (Hotel Business) 
 
4c. The student discusses proposed improvements for staff satisfaction linked to attitudes and the service 
profit chain. (HR Management)
