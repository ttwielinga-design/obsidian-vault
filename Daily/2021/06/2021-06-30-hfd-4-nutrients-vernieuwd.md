Chapter 4: Vitamins, minerals and energy
We tried to insert the factors of the ‘wheel of five’ as much as possible in our dishes. Comparing the dogfish dish to the (edible parts of the) wheel of five, we see that the dish contains fish (dogfish), lipid fats (olive oil, heavy cream) and starch (parsnip, which is a starch vegetable). At first, these elements formed the dogfish dish. However, we had to ‘cover’ the vegetable and fruit part of the wheel of five too. Therefore, we decided to add the pickled cucumber.
Looking at the turbot dish, all elements of the wheel of five are present. The dish contains Fish, which fits in the ´fish, meat, egg and nuts´ part. The shii-take fits in the ‘vegetables and fruit’ part. The butter and olive oil fit in the ‘lipids’ part. In first instance, we only wanted to use the shii-take as garnish. But we missed starch or grains, and we also missed a crisp in the dish which could meet the 6 culinary success factors. Therefore, we added the cassava crisps.

In the appendix, you can view a schematic overview of all the present vitamins and minerals. On the right, you can see the amount of energy every ingredient contains. By matching the dishes to the factors of the wheel of five, we also created a broad and varied combination of vitamins and minerals that are present in both dishes.
