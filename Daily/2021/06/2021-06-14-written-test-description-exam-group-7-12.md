Written exam module Professional Hospitality group 7 – 12 (blockperiod 4) 
 
 
1. General set up and procedures 
The written exam consists of 6 open questions based on a case hotel. The exam has a duration of 2 hours 
and is closed book, meaning that no books or notes can be brought along. The exam is divided into three 
parts: Hotel Business (2 questions), Financial Management (2 questions), and Human Resource 
Management (2 questions). The questions might be divided into several sub questions. The exam is based 
on the theory weeks ‘Hospitality Industry 1’ and ‘Hospitality Industry 2’ and focuses on knowledge and 
application of all discussed elements. The assignments that you worked on during these weeks are the 
best preparation for this test. 
 
Every part (Hotel Business, Financial Management, Human Resource Management) is worth 15 
scorepoints with a maximum of 45 scorepoints for the total examination. The grade for the exam is 
determined by taking the overall scorepoints divided by 4.5, i.e. 27 scorepoints will lead to a 6 as a final 
grade. For every question the maximum of possible scorepoints will be clearly stated on the examination 
paper. There is no minimum required score for one of the 3 parts, the grade is determined with the overall 
score. The coordinator is allowed to change the grade calculation if unforeseen circumstances occur. 
 
The minimum grade to pass the examination is a 5.5. The preliminary results will be communicated by e-
mail within 3 workweeks of the exam date. 
 
The specific time, date and location of the written examination can be found on your roster and in addition 
will be communicated by the Examinations Office.   
 
Information regarding the procedure around the inspection opportunities will be communicated along 
with the preliminary results. If required, one resit opportunity will be offered in the next module.  
 
You are advised to bring a non-graphical calculator to the examination, which needs to contain the HMSM 
sticker. You need to identify yourself with an official photo-ID (Zuyd card, driver’s license, ID card, 
Passport) at the start of the exam. Scrap paper will be provided by school. 
 
The language of the exam is English for the international stream and Dutch for the national stream.  
 
The registration code for this examination on OSIRIS is ‘M-HH-1-PH-WT’ Professional Hospitality Written 
Test. 
 
 
2. Required literature and materials 
The written exam is based on the theory weeks ‘Hospitality Industry 1’ and ‘Hospitality Industry 2’ and 
focuses on knowledge and application of all discussed elements. The mandatory materials are therefore 
the same as being used in these two weeks, and can be found in the descriptions per week but also in the 
overview on the next page.
