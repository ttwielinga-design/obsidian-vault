# Chapter one; Justification of ingredients

When creating our dishes for gastronomic lunch, we tried to come up with healthy and seasonal products to use. The choices were made with a lot of thought, thinking about sustainable ways to receive our products. Important to us was creating a tasty and interesting dish and yet trying to keep things simple. Therefore, the preparation of the ingredients is fairly simple but very refreshing as you will not see this very often.
For our first dish, we chose the dogfish. This is an atypical and unknown small shark species, that live in the waters around Western Europe. The fish will contribute to low transportation costs as it comes straight from the North Sea (Knack weekend, 2016). It is mostly caught as bycatch by fishermen, and therefore sustainable. We will be complimenting the dogfish with parsnip and cucumber, both vegetables locally grown in The Netherlands. Parsnip grows during winter and is, therefore, a product that is in season right now. When looking for seasonal and sustainable products, these ingredients thick the boxes.
For our second dish, we picked the turbot. August until February is the prime season of this fish, which contains healthy vitamins and minerals. Next to the turbot fish, on the plate, there will be shiitake mushrooms. These mushrooms will tick the box as of sustainability,  as we will grow these ourselves on a bed of sawdust. As you can grow these mushrooms inside, there is no season connected and therefore always in season. This way, we wanted to make our dish locally sourced and therefore sustainable.
Looking at all the ingredients, the theme of our gastronomic lunch is dishes made from locally grown, sustainable, and seasonal products. The reason why we chose this theme is that it is important to be more conscious about the things you eat considering the impact it has on the environment.











# Chapter two; The menu

## Grilled dogfish
The cooking techniques that we use for our grilled dogfish are:
Grilling – The dogfish filet
The grilling of the dogfish will make it more smokey
Pickling – The cucumber
The effect that this will have on the cucumber is that the taste will be intensified with more coating and contracting.
Purée – Parsnip
When the parsnip is pureed this will lead to a more coating substance
Deep-frying – Dogfish skin
The deepfried dogfish will be crispy and have a more coating aspect because of the oil that it is deepfried in.


### Explanation of the FIS profile
In the TSI profile you can see that for contracting we gave the dogfish a 3. This is because if you combine all the elements together, the dogfish the pickled cucumbers, the parsnip purée, and the deep fried dogfish skin, the pickles are there to give it the contracting but, the parsnip purée and the fattiness of the fish counter act this acidity.

Within the purée, and the fish the coating really comes out. The acidity of the pickles however does breakdown some of that coating therefore we came to a 5.

The dryness in this dish we felt was quite low. This is because like we said before the purée and the fish bring a lot of that coating and “weight” to the table. The fish and the pickles are nice and juicy but there is some dryness from the deep-fried dogfish skin and form the purée there for we decided that it was a 3 for dryness.

Altogether this dish brings a lot of different flavors to the table. However none of these flavors are really intense and they balance each other out quite well. For flavor intensity we went with a 5.

Then for the ripe- and freshness of this dish. We felt that this dish was predominantly ripe, this is because of the parsnip, the grilled fish, and the deep-fried fish. There for we gave the ripeness a 6. As for the freshness it was there because of the cucumbers and the acidity of it. Therefore we went with a 2 there.

### Chosen wine for with the Dogfish: Meerlust chardonnay 2019 – white wine from Stellenbosch, Zuid-Afrika (Vivino, n.d.)
The terroir in Stellenbosch South-Afrika is very good for chardonnay. Because of the compesition of the soil, sub-soil is predominantly clay, and the topsoil is mostly granite (Meerlust). Granit leads to a more heavy feeling in the mouth. The chardonnay is in the coastalclimate in South-Afrika a coating in taste, and less fresh / acidic in smell (olfactory) grape.

## Turbot
Glanzing
The turbot filet is glanzed with butter to give the turbot a soft coating, which will add up to the coatiness of the dish.
Simmering and reducing
The stock used for the Hollandaise sauce is simmered and reduced to evaporate a part of the liquid, and therefore keep a concentrated and flavourful stock
Deep-frying
The Cassave is deep-fried, which will make the cassava crispy and dry. This will add up to the dryness of the dish.

### Explenation FIS
We started with pan frying the turbot. Afterwards we glazed the turbot with butter. For assembling the plates, the turbot cooks in the oven at 140 degrees. The core temperature of the turbot must be 64 degrees. Next, a fish stock is drawn from trimmings of the turbot. This stock will be used in the beurre blanc. Next, a hollandaise sauce is made which is, among other things, made from butter and muscadet. In the process, the clarification technique and the au bain marie technique are used (Cracknell, 2011). Now, we have all the ingredients in order to make a rich beurre blanc: bay leaves are added, more muscadet, fish stock, cream fraiche and butter as well. A process of simmering and reducing is used, until finally the hollandaise sauce is added. In this process, it is important that the egg does not curdle and solidify. The shii takes are now being pan fried. For completing the dish, we deep-fried cassava carrots into cassava chips. We did this in arachide oil. After deep-frying, the chips will cool down on a paper towel, to collect as much as oil. In this way, the chips will have a more drying effect. The cassava chips is added in an ample amount, to add up to the dryness of the dish, and therefore it also matches with level of dryness of our wine (Klosse, 2014).

### Chosen wine for with the Turbot: Chablis first cru ‘beauroy’ 2017
The vineyard of the grape is in Chablis, in the region of Paris. The soil of the yards contains high amounts of clay, there also lots of nutrients for the grapes. This will resolute in to giving the wine a more intense flavor and a riper aroma. This ripeness correlates with the ripeness of the dish. The summer of 2017 was an extreme hot summer, which makes the grapes in this wine more sweet than usual. This sweetness creates a coating mouthfeel, which corresponds perfectly with the mouthfeel of the beurre blanc and turbot. The dryness of this wine is balanced by the cassava crisps, which give the dish a drying touch.

The wine is made the Chardonnay grape, or ‘beaunois’ as the locals in Chablis call it. Next, the cylinder technique is used, where a plastic bag will slowly blow and will push the grapes on to the surfaces to the cylinder. Grape juice is pushed out, which immediately is the first cru. The second press has a higher acidity level and is therefore more contracting. After this the wine will get an alcoholic and a malolactic fermentation. Yeast is added, but because of the high rate of sugar, some sugars will not convert into alcohol, which will later on give a more coating mouthfeel (Klosse, 2014). The wine finishes in a French oak barrel, where apple acids will turn into CO2, which causes a more balanced taste in the wine. The barrels will also give a nutty flavor and aroma, even a bit buttery flavor.

# Chapter three; Allergens

## Dogfish
In this dish, there are four allergens to be found, fish, mustard, celery, and lactose. Mild allergic reactions to eating fish or shellfish are seen on the skin as red bumps, hives, or itchy skin. Other mild reactions are swelling of the lips, a tingling or tightening feeling in the mouth and throat, a runny nose, or digestive problems such as cramps, stomach pain, nausea, or vomiting. (Department of Health, State Government of Victoria, Australia, 2020). Severe reactions lead to difficulties in breathing, swelling of the tongue and throat or tightness of the throat, coughing, and dizziness. An allergic reaction to mustard from the mustard cress is similar to those of fish (Gotter, 2018), the same goes for an allergic reaction to celery seeds (Allergieplatform, 2021). Eventually, someone with a severe reaction can collapse. Young children with fish allergies get pale or tend to flop or hang. The lactose allergy comes from heavy cream, butter, and milk. Symptoms for this allergy are a bloated feeling, stomach cramps, or diarrhea.

## Asian turbot
Two of the allergens mentioned above, fish and lactose, also apply to this dish. The third allergen in this dish is eggs, mild reactions can be hives. A severe reaction leads to anaphylaxis. The stadium before this can be recognized by the person having a rapid heartbeat, fainting, dizziness, and swelling (MedicalNewsToday, 2020) (FARE, Unknown). An allergic reaction to soy can range from hives, a tingling or itchy feeling in the mouth or of the skin. Swelling of mouth and throat area, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and nauseousness, and vomiting. A severe reaction will lead to an anaphylactic shock.

# Chapter four; Vitamins, minerals and energy
We tried to insert the factors of the ‘wheel of five’ as much as possible in our dishes. Comparing the dogfish dish to the wheel of five, we see that the dish contains fish (dogfish), lipid fats (olive oil, heavy cream), and starch (parsnip, which is a starchy vegetable). At first, these elements formed the dogfish dish. However, we had to ‘cover’ the vegetable and fruit part of the wheel of five too. Therefore, we decided to add the pickled cucumber.
Looking at the turbot dish, all elements of the wheel of five are present. The dish contains Fish, which fits in the ´fish, meat, egg and nuts´ part. The shiitake fits in the ‘vegetables and fruit’ part. The butter and olive oil fit in the ‘lipids’ part. In the first instance, we only wanted to use the shiitakes as garnish. But we missed starch or grains, and we also missed a crisp in the dish which could meet the 6 culinary success factors. Therefore, we added the cassava crisps.
