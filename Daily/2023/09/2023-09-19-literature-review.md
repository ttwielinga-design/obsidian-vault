Title Article 2: How do Corporate Social Responsibility and Innovation Co‑evolve with Organizational Forms? Evidence from a Transitional Economy

Hu, H.W., Zhang, J. How do Corporate Social Responsibility and Innovation Co-evolve with Organizational Forms? Evidence from a Transitional Economy. J Bus Ethics 186, 815–829 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1007/s10551-023-05435-8

Main Idea of the Paper:
The paper explores the co-evolution of corporate social responsibility (CSR) disclosure, innovation investment, and organizational forms in a transitional economy, with a focus on China. It investigates how these factors affect firm market value and how their interplay changes over time.

Supporting Ideas:
CSR reporting, as a form of information disclosure, lowers the cost of capital, enhances transparency, and generates moral capital in the context of low market transparency in transitional economies.
High R&D investment intensity can lead to contradictory reactions in the equity market, and CSR plays a crucial role in mitigating uncertainty and ethical concerns associated with innovation activities.
The paper emphasizes the importance of considering the dynamic nature of organizational forms and their transformations over time in assessing the effects of CSR and innovation investment.
CSR reporting is particularly valuable for start-up firms and privately owned enterprises (POEs) in gaining legitimacy and reducing market uncertainty.
The study suggests that the influence of CSR reporting diminishes when start-up firms transition to established firms but remains consistent during POE transformations.
The co-evolutionary perspective is used to capture the interplay between CSR, innovation, and changing institutional environments in transitional economies.

Methodology:
The paper utilizes panel data on 2204 Chinese listed firms for the period of 2008–2017 to analyze the effects of CSR reporting and innovation (R&D investment) on firm market value, considering different types of firms and their transformations over time.


Findings:
CSR reporting positively affects market responses to higher R&D investment intensity, as it communicates ethical standards, lowers information asymmetry, and indicates the potential quality of a firm's innovation activities.
The positive effect of CSR reporting on market value is more pronounced for start-up firms and privately owned enterprises due to their higher uncertainty and lower legitimacy.
The impact of CSR reporting on market value diminishes when start-up firms transition to established firms.

Implications:
The paper highlights the complementary role of CSR and innovation in improving firm market value, particularly in transitional economies.
It suggests that CSR reporting is crucial for firms with high uncertainty and low legitimacy.
The findings emphasize the need for a dynamic approach to account for the co-evolution of innovation practices, CSR engagement, and organizational form transformations over time.
The study has practical and policy implications for firms operating in transitional economies, where R&D investment returns may be discounted due to low market transparency.

Conclusion:
The paper contributes to the literature on innovation, CSR, and co-evolution in transitional economies, emphasizing the role of CSR in addressing uncertainty associated with R&D investment. It also underscores the significance of considering the dynamic nature of organizational forms in understanding the interplay between CSR, innovation, and firm performance. The findings provide insights for firms seeking to enhance their market value through CSR and innovation strategies in transitional economies.


Title Article 3: Developing a Measurement Scale of Corporate Socially RE (CSRE): A Conceptual Framework

Gallardo-Vázquez, D., Herrador-Alcaide, T.C. & de la Cruz Sánchez-Domínguez, J. Developing a measurement scale of corporate socially RE in sustainable management. Rev Manag Sci (2023). https://doi.org/10.1007/s11846-023-00658-5

Main Idea of the Paper:
This paper focuses on the concept of Corporate Socially RE (CSRE) and aims to develop a measurement scale to assess the traits of individuals engaged in CSRE. It combines entrepreneurship and corporate social responsibility (CSR) to create a framework for sustainable development through entrepreneurship.

Supporting Ideas:
The paper highlights the importance of entrepreneurship in promoting sustainable development, economic growth, and social well-being.
It emphasizes the need to integrate CSR principles into entrepreneurship, creating a new approach called CSRE.
A measurement scale (CSRE-s) was developed with 84 items, which were subjected to validity and reliability tests.
Exploratory factor analysis (EFA) and confirmatory factor analysis (CFA) were conducted to identify the key variables related to individuals engaged in CSRE.
The CSRE-s consists of 6 entrepreneurship factors and 3 CSR dimensions (environmental, social, and economic), each assessed by a total of 76 items.
The paper suggests that the CSRE-s can be used to incorporate CSR values into entrepreneurial training programs, public sector policies, and business plans to promote sustainable entrepreneurship.

Methodology:
The paper employs quantitative research methods, including the development of the CSRE-s measurement scale, validity and reliability tests, exploratory factor analysis (EFA), and confirmatory factor analysis (CFA).

Findings:
The findings indicate that CSRE involves a combination of entrepreneurship and CSR traits, leading to a unique and innovative framework for sustainable development through entrepreneurship.

Implications:
The paper suggests that the proposed CSRE framework and measurement scale can be used to promote CSR values in entrepreneurial training programs, government policies supporting entrepreneurship, and strategic business plans, ultimately benefiting all stakeholders through sustainable entrepreneurial projects.

Conclusion:
The paper concludes by presenting a theoretical model of the corporate socially responsible entrepreneur, emphasizing the integration of personal traits, organizational behaviors, and societal impacts in the context of CSRE.

Strengths of the Paper:
Development of a comprehensive measurement scale (CSRE-s) to assess traits related to CSRE.
Integration of entrepreneurship and CSR principles to promote sustainable development.
Clear articulation of the theoretical framework for corporate socially RE.
