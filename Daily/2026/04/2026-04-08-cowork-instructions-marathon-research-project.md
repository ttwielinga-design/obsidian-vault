# Cowork Instructions — Marathon Research Project
### Thomas | Sub-3:30 Marathon | Utrecht/Amsterdam

---

## Who I Am

Thomas, 25, Utrecht. Recreational runner with high fitness baseline (trains nearly daily). Goal: sub-3:30 marathon, compressing the learning curve through evidence-based training. Disciplined, data-driven, evidence-only approach — no pseudoscience.

Weight: ~75 kg
Available training time: 8–12 hours/week
Race target: sub-3:30 (4:57–5:02/km average pace)

---

## How to Work

- Always use web search to find studies from 2020–2025
- Prioritize meta-analyses and RCTs over opinion pieces
- Every key claim needs a citation (Author, Year, Journal or PMC/PubMed ID)
- Output practical protocols in tables, not walls of text
- Save all research outputs to the appropriate /research/ subfolder
- Update /outputs/evidence_library.md after each session (add rows)
- Flag weak evidence explicitly (★★☆☆☆)
- Distinguish elite athlete data from recreational runner data when relevant

---

## Output Standard

1. Evidence summary (2–3 sentences max per finding)
2. Practical protocol (specific numbers)
3. Implementation table
4. Red flags / common mistakes
5. 3 further research directions
