# Marathon Training Research Prompt
### Optimized for Claude Sonnet 4.6 | Web Search Enabled | Evidence-Based Framework

---

## HOW TO USE THIS PROMPT

Paste the system prompt block below into Claude's system prompt field (or prepend it to your message). Then use the **Query Templates** section to fire specific research questions. Each query is designed to pull the most current peer-reviewed evidence and translate it into actionable protocols.

For best results: enable **web search**, ask one domain at a time, and always end with: *"Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table."*

---

## SYSTEM PROMPT (paste this in full)

```
You are an elite marathon performance coach with a PhD in exercise physiology and sports nutrition. You have deep expertise in translating cutting-edge academic research into practical, periodized training protocols.

Your knowledge base spans:
- Exercise science: VO2max, lactate threshold, running economy, neuromuscular fatigue
- Sports nutrition: carbohydrate periodization, fueling strategies, body composition
- Recovery science: HRV, sleep, inflammation, adaptation timelines
- Biomechanics: cadence, ground contact time, stride mechanics, injury mechanics
- Periodization models: polarized, pyramidal, threshold, block periodization
- Mental performance: race execution, pain tolerance, pre-race protocols

RESEARCH STANDARDS:
- Prioritize meta-analyses and systematic reviews from the last 5 years (2020-2025)
- Secondary: RCTs and high-quality cohort studies
- Tertiary: expert consensus and practitioner case studies
- Always flag when evidence is weak or conflicting
- Distinguish between elite athlete data and recreational/masters data when relevant
- Cite specific studies by author, year, and journal when making key claims

OUTPUT FORMAT:
1. Evidence summary (2-3 sentences max per key finding)
2. Practical protocol (specific numbers, timing, thresholds)
3. Implementation table (when applicable)
4. Red flags / common mistakes
5. Further research rabbit holes (3 bullet points)

CONTEXT ABOUT THE ATHLETE:
- Name: Thomas
- Age: 25
- Background: Recreational runner, high fitness baseline (works out nearly daily)
- Goal: Compress learning curve, maximize performance gains efficiently
- Training style: Disciplined, data-driven, high commitment
- Base: Utrecht/Amsterdam
- Available time: Assume 8-12 hours/week training capacity
- Approach: Evidence-based, no pseudoscience, quantify everything possible
```

## MASTER QUERY LIBRARY

### SECTION 1: TRAINING STRUCTURE & PERIODIZATION

**1.1 — Annual Plan Architecture**
```
Using the most recent research on marathon periodization (2020-2025), design a full 
annual training structure for a recreational runner targeting a sub-3:30 marathon. 
Cover: macrocycle phases, mesocycle duration, when to peak, how many goal races per year, 
and how to structure a base-building block vs race-specific block. 
Compare polarized vs pyramidal vs threshold-dominant approaches and recommend 
which produces the best results for non-elite runners with limited time. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**1.2 — Weekly Volume & Progression**
```
What does current research say about optimal weekly kilometer targets for marathon 
improvement across different training levels (50km/week vs 80km vs 100km+)? 
What is the evidence-based maximum safe progression rate (the 10% rule is outdated — 
what does the literature actually say)? How should volume be distributed across 
7 days for someone training 10 hours/week? Include guidance on down weeks, 
volume periodization within a mesocycle, and how to count cross-training volume equivalents. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**1.3 — Intensity Distribution**
```
What is the current scientific consensus on training intensity distribution for marathon 
performance — specifically the 80/20 polarized model vs other approaches? 
What are the precise physiological zones (by HR, pace, and lactate), how should zone 2 
training be structured, and what does the research say about the role of threshold work 
(zone 3) in marathon preparation? Include how intensity distribution should shift 
during race-specific blocks (8-12 weeks out). 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**1.4 — Key Workouts**
```
What are the most evidence-supported workout types for marathon-specific adaptation? 
Cover: long runs (optimal duration, fueling, pace), tempo runs (duration, pace targets), 
marathon pace workouts (when, how much volume), interval training (VO2max sessions — 
optimal duration, rest ratios), and strides/neuromuscular work. 
For each: what physiological adaptation does it target, what is the optimal dose, 
and how frequently should it appear in a training week? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 2: PHYSIOLOGICAL PERFORMANCE METRICS

**2.1 — VO2max Development**
```
What does the current literature say about the most efficient methods to improve VO2max 
for trained recreational runners? Compare: 4x4 min intervals (Norwegian method), 
30/30s, 40/20s, long easy running, and hybrid approaches. 
What is a realistic VO2max improvement over 16 weeks for a trained 25-year-old male? 
How much of marathon performance variance is explained by VO2max vs lactate threshold 
vs running economy? Include how to estimate VO2max from field tests (Cooper, 1-mile, 
Garmin estimates). 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**2.2 — Lactate Threshold Optimization**
```
Explain the current research on lactate threshold (LT1 and LT2) as the primary determinant 
of marathon performance. What are the best field-test methods to identify LT1 and LT2 
without a lab (pace-based, HR-based, breathing-based proxies)? 
What training methods most efficiently shift LT rightward — and what is the evidence 
for "threshold runs" vs "cruise intervals" vs sub-threshold steady state? 
How does threshold change over a 16-week block and how should training adapt? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**2.3 — Running Economy**
```
What does current research say about running economy (RE) — how to measure it, 
what drives it, and how to improve it? Cover: cadence optimization (is 180spm universal?), 
vertical oscillation, ground contact time, shoe selection (carbon plate evidence), 
strength training contributions to RE, and plyometric protocols. 
What is the magnitude of RE improvement achievable through training interventions 
over 12-16 weeks? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 3: STRENGTH & INJURY PREVENTION

**3.1 — Strength Training for Runners**
```
Summarize the most current evidence on strength training for marathon performance improvement 
and injury risk reduction. What does research say about: concurrent training interference, 
optimal strength session frequency for runners, which exercises have the highest transfer 
(heavy compound vs explosive vs single-leg), periodization of strength relative to 
running volume, and timing within the training week. 
Should a marathon runner do hypertrophy work or purely strength/neural adaptations? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**3.2 — Injury Prevention Protocols**
```
What are the most common running injuries in marathon training and what does the 
current evidence say about preventing each? Cover: IT band syndrome, plantar fasciitis, 
shin splints, Achilles tendinopathy, runner's knee, stress fractures. 
What are the evidence-based prehab protocols (exercises, load management rules, 
surface variation, footwear)? What does the research say about barefoot/minimalist 
training, orthotics, and foam rolling for injury prevention? 
What are the early warning signs that predict injury 2-4 weeks before it becomes serious? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**3.3 — Plyometrics & Neuromuscular Training**
```
What is the current evidence for plyometric training improving marathon performance 
through enhanced running economy and neuromuscular efficiency? 
What protocols (volume, intensity, frequency, exercises) are supported? 
How should plyos be periodized — more in base phase or race-specific phase? 
What is the injury risk and how is it managed? Are there diminishing returns for 
already-trained runners? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 4: NUTRITION & FUELING

**4.1 — Daily Nutrition Framework**
```
What does current sports nutrition research (2020-2025) recommend for daily macronutrient 
targets for a 25-year-old male marathon runner weighing approximately 75kg training 
8-12 hours/week? Cover: protein targets (g/kg), carbohydrate periodization by training 
load, fat intake, and total energy availability. 
What does the evidence say about "train low, compete high" carbohydrate periodization? 
What are the risks of low energy availability (RED-S) in male runners — is it a real risk? 
Include specific meal timing around training. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**4.2 — Race Day Fueling & Carbohydrate Loading**
```
What is the evidence-based protocol for carbohydrate loading before a marathon? 
How many grams, over how many days, what sources? During the race: what does research 
say about optimal carbohydrate intake per hour (the 60g vs 90g vs 120g debate, 
multiple transportable carbohydrates), gel timing, and sodium/electrolyte requirements? 
What is the evidence on caffeine for marathon performance — dose, timing, responder 
vs non-responder variation? Cover GI distress prevention strategies. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**4.3 — Hydration Science**
```
What is the current evidence on hydration for marathon performance? 
The old "drink before thirsty" vs "drink to thirst" debate — what does the most 
current research say? What are the evidence-based sweat rate estimation methods? 
Hyponatremia risk — who is vulnerable and how to prevent it? 
Pre-race hyperhydration — does it work? Sodium loading protocols? 
Heat acclimatization impact on hydration needs? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**4.4 — Supplements with Evidence**
```
Rank the following supplements by strength of evidence for marathon/endurance performance: 
creatine, beta-alanine, sodium bicarbonate, nitrates (beetroot), iron, vitamin D, 
omega-3s, magnesium, collagen/glycine for tendons, and ashwagandha. 
For each with meaningful evidence: dosing protocol, timing, expected effect size, 
who benefits most, and contraindications. Flag the ones that are overhyped. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 5: RECOVERY SCIENCE

**5.1 — HRV-Based Training Adjustment**
```
What does the current literature say about using Heart Rate Variability (HRV) to guide 
training load decisions for endurance athletes? Which HRV metrics matter most 
(rMSSD, SDNN, HF power)? How should a runner interpret day-to-day HRV fluctuations 
vs trends over weeks? What is the evidence that HRV-guided training produces better 
outcomes than fixed periodization? Which apps/devices are validated (Garmin, Whoop, 
Oura, Elite HRV comparison)? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**5.2 — Sleep & Recovery Optimization**
```
What does exercise science research say specifically about sleep for endurance athletes? 
Optimal duration, sleep extension protocols before key training blocks, 
impact of sleep deprivation on performance, and practical interventions (timing, 
temperature, light, supplements). What does research say about napping for athletes 
(timing, duration, effectiveness)? 
Cover: how to identify when accumulated fatigue requires extra recovery days 
using objective markers beyond HRV. 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**5.3 — Active Recovery & Modalities**
```
Rate the following recovery modalities by current evidence quality for marathon runners: 
cold water immersion (ice baths), contrast therapy, compression garments, massage, 
foam rolling, easy runs vs complete rest, sauna, normatec-style compression, 
and infrared light therapy. 
What does the blunting-adaptation controversy mean in practice — should marathoners 
avoid ice baths during heavy training blocks? When are recovery modalities appropriate vs 
counterproductive? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**5.4 — Overtraining & Overreaching**
```
What are the evidence-based markers of functional overreaching vs non-functional 
overreaching vs overtraining syndrome in endurance athletes? 
How do you distinguish between planned fatigue accumulation (normal) and 
dangerous overtraining? What are the blood biomarkers worth tracking (ferritin, 
CK, cortisol, testosterone, CRP)? What is the recovery protocol once overtraining 
is identified? How long does full recovery take? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 6: RACE STRATEGY & MENTAL PERFORMANCE

**6.1 — Pacing Strategy**
```
What does the research say about optimal marathon pacing strategy? 
Even split vs negative split vs positive split — which produces the fastest times 
and what is the physiological explanation? What is the evidence on the "wall" 
(glycogen depletion at km 30-35) and how does pacing affect it? 
How should pace targets account for temperature, elevation, and wind? 
What are the validated field formulas for predicting marathon time from half marathon, 
10km, or VO2max estimates? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**6.2 — Tapering Science**
```
What is the evidence-based optimal taper for a marathon? Duration (1 week vs 2 vs 3 weeks), 
volume reduction percentage, maintaining intensity, and managing the psychological effects 
of tapering (taper madness, phantom symptoms). 
Should strength training be maintained during taper? 
How should nutrition shift during the taper phase? 
What are the signs of over-tapering and under-tapering? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**6.3 — Mental Performance & Pain Tolerance**
```
What does sports psychology research say about the mental skills most predictive of 
marathon performance in trained recreational runners? Cover: dissociation vs association 
strategies during racing, self-talk protocols, pre-race routine design, 
visualization effectiveness, dealing with the inevitable suffering in km 30-42, 
and building race-specific mental toughness in training. 
What does the research say about perceived effort (RPE) as a performance limiter 
vs physiological limiters? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

### SECTION 7: ADVANCED & EMERGING TOPICS

**7.1 — Heat & Cold Adaptation**
```
What does the current research say about heat acclimatization for marathon performance — 
even in temperate race conditions? What are the protocols (passive heat exposure, 
sauna post-training, heat sessions) and what performance gains are achievable? 
What about altitude training and live-high/train-low approaches for recreational runners — 
is it worth it? What are the cold weather adjustments for training and racing? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**7.2 — Carbon Plate Shoe Science**
```
What does the peer-reviewed evidence (not manufacturer claims) say about carbon-plated 
marathon shoes? What is the measured performance improvement, who benefits most 
(is it pace-dependent?), how long do they last, and should they be used in training 
or only racing? What is the evidence on injury risk with carbon plates — particularly 
for the lower leg and foot? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

**7.3 — Gut Training**
```
What does current research say about training the gut to accept higher carbohydrate 
intake during marathon racing? What are the protocols for gut training in training runs, 
how long does adaptation take, and what are the evidence-based strategies for preventing 
GI distress on race day? Is there a genetic component to GI distress vulnerability? 
Cite the primary studies and give me the practical protocol in a table.
```

## RAPID-FIRE DIAGNOSTIC QUERIES

Use these shorter queries for quick evidence checks on specific questions:

```
1. "What is the current evidence on the optimal long run duration for marathon 
   preparation — 2h30 vs 3h vs 3h30? Does time-on-feet or distance matter more?"

2. "What does research say about running cadence — is the 180spm target evidence-based 
   or myth? How much does increasing cadence by 5-10% reduce injury risk?"

3. "What is the evidence for double-day training (two runs per day) for recreational 
   marathon runners training under 100km/week?"

4. "What does current research say about the minimum effective dose of strength training 
   to reduce injury risk in marathon runners — 1 session/week vs 2 vs 3?"

5. "Is there evidence that cold water immersion blunts endurance adaptations? 
   Should marathon runners avoid ice baths during base training?"

6. "What is the optimal protein intake timing around running sessions for muscle 
   repair and adaptation — does the anabolic window matter for endurance athletes?"

7. "What does research say about the performance impact of running in heat 
   (>20°C) and the recommended pace adjustment per degree above threshold?"
```
