## Master Evidence Table

| Domain | Topic | Key Finding | Primary Source | Practical Rule |
|--------|-------|-------------|----------------|----------------|
| Periodization | Annual structure | Pyramidal in base → polarized in race-specific phase = optimal | IJSPP Systematic Review 2022; Nature Sci Reports 2025 | Build aerobically first; sharpen race-specifically |
| Periodization | Polarized vs. pyramidal | Polarized = 30% greater marathon improvement; but ~32% of runners are pyramidal responders | Nature Sci Reports 2025 | Start pyramidal, test polarized response in build |
| Periodization | Volume | Monthly km = #1 predictor of marathon time | PubMed 36207466 (2022) | Protect every easy run |
| Periodization | Progression | Down week every 3–4 weeks = supercompensation | Springer Sports Med 2025 | 20–30% volume cut every 3–4 weeks |
| Periodization | Intensity | ≥75% Z1; Z3 correlates with SLOWER times | PubMed 39616560 (2024) | Enforce genuinely easy easy runs |
| Periodization | Long run | 90–180 min optimal; time-on-feet > distance | PMC12082016 (2025) | 2:00–2:30 in base; up to 3:00 in peak |
| Periodization | Intervals | 4×4 min @ Z4/5 → 7.2% VO2max gain | PMC12218014 (2025) | 1× VO2max session per week |
| Physiology | VO2max determinants | LT and RE explain more variance than VO2max in trained runners | Joyner & Coyle 2008 | Train threshold and RE, not just HIIT |
| Physiology | VO2max intervals | Optimal: 140s work / 165s recovery; 3 sessions/week for 3–6 weeks | PMC11022784 (2024) | 4×4 min format; max 1× per week for marathon runners |
| Physiology | Lactate threshold | LT2 = strongest predictor of marathon performance | Faude et al. 2009; EJAP 2025 | Weekly threshold work non-negotiable |
| Physiology | LT field test | Speed-derived estimates (10K pace + 15–20s) have smallest error | EJAP 2025 | Use race-time formulas; re-test every 8 weeks |
| Physiology | Running economy | Heavy strength training ≥85% 1RM → 2–4% RE gain | Multiple meta-analyses | 2× heavy compound sessions per week |
| Physiology | Cadence | 180 spm is a myth; individualize with +5–10% from baseline | Multiple RE studies | Film and adjust gradually |
| Physiology | Carbon shoes | 1.4–4.2% RE improvement; pace-dependent | PMC12464892 (2025) | Races + key quality sessions only |
| Strength | Heavy strength > plyos | Heavy resistance outperforms plyos alone for RE | Track & Field News review 2024 | Prioritize compound heavy lifts |
| Strength | Concurrent training | Minimal interference in trained runners (2×/week) | Huiberts et al. 2024 | Place strength on same day as quality run |
| Strength | Injury prediction | Prior injury = strongest predictor of future injury | PMC11986186 (2025) | Address past injuries before cycle starts |
| Strength | ITBS | Hip abductor weakness is primary driver | Frontiers Sports 2024 | Daily clamshells + Copenhagen adduction |
| Strength | Achilles | Eccentric loading (HSR) = gold standard | JOSPT CPG 2024 | Daily eccentric calf raises throughout cycle |
| Strength | Plyometrics | 1–3% RE improvement; base phase, low volume | Beattie 2017; Saunders 2006 | 2×/week in base; 1×/week in build |
| Nutrition | Carb loading | 8–12 g/kg/day × 3 days = full glycogen saturation | Burke 2011; PMC11911277 | Start T–3, familiar foods only |
| Nutrition | Race fueling | 60–90 g CHO/hr requires dual-transporter (glucose+fructose) blend | Jeukendrup 2014 | Multi-transporter gels from km 7–8 |
| Nutrition | Caffeine | 3–6 mg/kg, 45–60 min pre-race = 1–3% time improvement | IOC consensus 2022 | Non-negotiable race-day tool |
| Nutrition | Hydration | "Drink to thirst" — overhydration causes hyponatremia | Sawka 2007 | Never drink beyond thirst during marathon |
| Nutrition | Protein | 1.6–2.0 g/kg/day for endurance + strength | Morton et al. 2018 | ~120–150 g/day for 75 kg Thomas |
| Nutrition | Nitrates | 400–500 mg, 2–3 hrs pre-race; less effective at elite level | PMC12106159 (2025) | Good sub-elite addition; test GI response |
| Nutrition | Iron | Ferritin <30 µg/L = significant performance impairment | IOC consensus | Test at start of every training cycle |
| Nutrition | Gut training | SGLT1 upregulation allows 90 g/hr after 4–6 weeks | Jeukendrup 2017; PMC12258207 | Practice race nutrition every run >60 min |
| Recovery | HRV-guided training | Better vs. fixed plan for avoiding overreaching | PMC8507742 (2021) | Oura/WHOOP daily; downgrade on amber/red |
| Recovery | Device accuracy | Oura Gen 4 > WHOOP > Garmin for HRV | Physiol Reports 2025 | Oura or WHOOP recommended |
| Recovery | Sleep | Most effective athletic performance intervention | PMC10354314 (2023) | 8–9 hrs/night; prioritize over everything |
| Recovery | Napping | 20–30 min at 13:00 = partial recovery restoration | PMC9744144 (2022) | Use when sleep-restricted |
| Recovery | Cold water immersion | Does NOT blunt endurance adaptations (does blunt strength) | PubMed 33146851 | Avoid during training; use post-race |
| Recovery | Sauna | 3-week protocol → plasma volume + performance improvement | PMC7862510 (2021) | 15–20 min after easy sessions, 3×/week |
| Race Strategy | Pacing | Negative split → better times for recreational runners | PMC12307312 (2025) | Start 5–10 sec/km slower than goal pace |
| Race Strategy | Taper | 3-week taper, 40–60% volume cut = ~5.5 min median improvement | PMC8506252 (2021) | Trust the taper; don't add sessions |
| Race Strategy | Self-talk | Positive self-talk improves TT by ~18% | Blanchfield 2014 | Prepare 3–5 mantras; practice in training |
| Race Strategy | RPE model | Perceived effort is the primary performance governor | Marcora 2008–2022 | Learn RPE zones — a trainable skill |
| Advanced | Sauna acclimatization | Heat adaptations benefit performance even in temperate conditions | Springer BMC Sports Sci 2024 | 6+ weeks of 3–5×/week post-run sauna |
| Advanced | Heat pacing | +10 sec/km per 5°C above 15°C | Racinais 2015 | Adjust race pace based on forecast |
| Advanced | Carbon shoes | 2–4% RE; save for races (~500 km lifespan) | PMC12464892 (2025) | Races + key sessions only |
| Advanced | Carbon shoe fatigue | Altered lower limb biomechanics under fatigue | PMC11850346 (2025) | Build eccentric calf strength before using |
| Advanced | Low-FODMAP | Reduces GI distress significantly pre-race | PMC12258207 (2025) | Eliminate garlic, onion, beans 48 hrs out |
| Advanced | NSAIDs risk | Ibuprofen pre-race = higher gut permeability + GI crisis risk | Multiple studies | Never take ibuprofen before/during marathon |
