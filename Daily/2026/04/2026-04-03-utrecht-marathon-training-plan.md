5. Recovery & mobility protocol
Dynamic warm-up — before every run (5-7 min)
• Glute bridges — 10 reps (activates glutes, directly protects patellofemoral joint)
• Leg swings forward + lateral — 15 each side
• Hip circles — 10 each direction
• Walking lunges with torso rotation — 8 each side
• High knees — 2x20m
• Butt kicks — 2x20m
• A-skips — 2x20m
• 3x30 sec easy jog to finish
Post-run static stretching — after every run (10 min)
• Hip flexor lunge stretch — 60s each side (critical for runners knee management)
• Standing quad stretch — 45s each side
• Seated hamstring stretch — 60s each side
• Calf/Achilles wall stretch — 60s each side (two variants: straight and bent knee)
• Figure-4 glute stretch — 60s each side
• Pigeon pose — 60s each side (IT band + hip capsule)
• Thoracic rotation — 10 reps each side
Runners knee PT — every single day without exception (5 min)
Patellofemoral syndrome is almost always caused by weakness in the hip abductors and VMO (inner quad).
These exercises address the root cause, not just the symptom.
• Terminal knee extensions (TKE with band) — 3x20 each side. Isolates VMO. Do before every run.
• Side-lying clam shells (with resistance band) — 3x15. Hip abductor strength is the primary fix.
• VMO squats (terminal 30 degrees of extension only) — 3x15. Short-arc quad work.
• Single-leg glute bridge — 3x12 each side. Gluteal strength reduces valgus knee collapse.
• This routine takes 5 minutes. Do it while watching TV. Non-negotiable every day.
Foam rolling — nightly (5 min)
• IT band — 60s each side (slow, 2 seconds per roll, pause on tender spots)
• Quadriceps — 60s each side
• Hip flexors — 45s each side
• Calves — 45s each side
• Thoracic spine — 30s
Strength sessions — 2x per week (weeks 1-6)
Focus on posterior chain and hip stability. These muscles protect the knee, drive running economy, and prevent
late-race form breakdown.
• Bulgarian split squats — 3x10 each side (weeks 1-4 loaded, weeks 5-6 bodyweight)

Pre-race nutrition
• 3 hours before gun: 800-900 kcal carb-rich breakfast — white rice or oats + banana + white bread with honey.
Minimal fibre, minimal fat. 500 ml water.
• 45-60 min before gun: 250 mg caffeine (2x125mg capsules or equivalent). No new foods.
• 10-15 min before gun: final 150 ml water, half a gel optional.
Race nutrition — 60-90g carbs per hour
• First gel: km 7-8 (35-40 min in). Do not wait until you feel depleted.
• Then every 35-40 min: km 14, 20, 26, 32, 37. Total: 5-6 gels.
• Always chase gel with water, not sports drink (to avoid gut overload).
• Salt tablet or electrolyte drink at km 21 to prevent late-race cramping.
• One caffeinated gel at km 28-30 for the final push.

• Only use gels you have practised with in training. Zero new products on race day.
Pacing strategy
• 0-10 km: feel easy, hold yourself back. HR should be 145-152. Don't chase the front.
• 10-21 km: settle into goal pace 4:37/km. HR 148-157. Comfortable rhythm.
• 21-30 km: the assessment zone. If HR is rising above 162, back off 3-4 sec/km. If steady, hold.
• 30-35 km: this is where races are won or lost. Stay relaxed, shorten stride, maintain turnover.
• 35-42 km: give everything that's left. Push HR to 165-172.
• Race-day decision rule: go through 21 km no faster than 1:37:00 regardless of how good you feel.
Carb loading (2 days before)
• Thursday and Friday: 8-10g carbs per kg bodyweight = 576-720g carbs/day.
• Focus on white rice, white pasta, white bread, bananas, sports drinks.
• Avoid high-fibre foods, cruciferous vegetables, and anything new or unusual.
• Expect weight gain of 1-2 kg (glycogen + water retention) — this is correct and intentional.
• No alcohol from Monday through race day.
Final note on shoes. If you don't currently have a carbon-plated race shoe, this is worth addressing in the next
2-3 weeks. The research consistently shows 3-4% performance improvement with modern supershoes — that
delta is almost exactly the gap between your Riegel-predicted 3:22 and the 3:15 target. Adidas Adizero Adios
Pro 3, Nike Vaporfly 3, or Saucony Endorphin Pro 4 are all strong options at this pace range. Break them in with
3-4 runs before race day.
