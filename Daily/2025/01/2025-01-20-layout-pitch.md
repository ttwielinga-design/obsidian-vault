Slide 1: Introduction
Title:
"Who is Thomas Wielinga?"
Content:
Name: Thomas Wielinga
Role: Junior Project Manager at Aedes
Tagline: "Driven by Passion, Grounded in Expertise"
Visual Elements:
Portrait Image: A professional photo of you (ideally, a headshot with a warm, approachable expression).
Background: Use a clean, modern backdrop (e.g., soft gradient of blue or gray) to keep the focus on you.
Map or Timeline (Optional):
If you want to highlight your global mindset, a minimal map or line showing cities you've lived in (San Francisco, Sevilla, London, Utrecht) can be placed subtly in the background or along the bottom of the slide.
Visual Accent:
A subtle icon or image that reflects your expertise in hospitality or real estate (e.g., a small building or a globe).
This could be in the corner or integrated into the slide's design for a professional touch.
Slide Layout:
Top: Title ("Who is Thomas Wielinga?") centered in bold, professional font (use something like Arial or Helvetica for readability).
Middle (Left): Your name, role, and tagline, formatted clearly.
Middle (Right): Your photo (portrait) with the optional global map/timeline behind it.
Bottom (Subtle): Small icons representing hospitality and real estate (if you choose to use them).
Additional Design Tips:
Font Colors: Dark text for clarity (e.g., black or dark blue for your name and tagline).
Background: Simple and sleek, using a neutral gradient or a minimal background to not distract from the content.
Slide Timing: Aim for this slide to appear for about 30-45 seconds, with you briefly introducing yourself and setting the stage for the rest of the presentation.
Slide 2: Personal Story & Motivation
Title:
"From Dreams to Realities"
Content:
Brief Story:
Intro: "What drives me? It all started with a curiosity about how the world works and a passion for hospitality, finance, and real estate."
Journey: "Living in diverse cities like San Francisco, Sevilla, London, and now Utrecht has shaped how I approach challenges. I learned that every experience brings a unique perspective that fuels my drive to create meaningful projects in hospitality and real estate."
Connection to Career: "This blend of curiosity, adaptability, and global perspective fuels my work today, allowing me to approach every project with a fresh mindset and a passion for making an impact."
Visual Elements:
Map of Cities:
A sleek, minimalist map with markers for San Francisco, Sevilla, London, and Utrecht. You can use an animation or simple line connecting the cities to represent your journey.
Images (Optional):
Photos of Each City: If you feel it adds value, include a small image of each city or landmark related to the places you’ve lived. This adds a personal touch while reinforcing the global perspective.
Quote or Motivational Phrase:
Include an inspiring quote related to your journey or motivation. Example: "Every city taught me something new, but my drive to create remains constant."
Slide Layout:
Top: Title ("From Dreams to Realities") centered, with a subtle font.
Middle (Left): Your brief story and connection to your career.
Middle (Right): Map with city markers, and optionally, small images of each city.
Bottom: Motivational quote or key takeaway: "A journey of growth that shapes every project I lead."
Design Tips:
Font Style: Use a clean font for the text, but consider a slightly larger, italicized font for the quote or motivational phrase to make it stand out.
Color Scheme: Continue with a professional but warm tone. Light backgrounds with darker text (perhaps navy blue) will keep it cohesive.
Timing: Aim to spend about 1-2 minutes on this slide, as it will help you introduce the personal side of your story while connecting it to your professional aspirations.
Slide 3: Professional Qualities and Values
Title:
"Core Strengths that Drive My Success"
Content:
Strategic Thinking:
"I excel at identifying actionable solutions to complex problems, always with a long-term view in mind. Whether it’s optimizing hotel assets or managing multi-faceted projects, I ensure that every decision supports broader business goals."
Passion-Driven:
"I approach every challenge with enthusiasm and perseverance. I believe that a genuine passion for what I do is key to achieving meaningful results and inspiring those I work with."
Global Perspective:
"Having lived and worked across diverse global markets, I understand the nuances of different business landscapes. This global mindset helps me navigate complex international projects and fosters inclusivity and innovation."
Adaptability:
"In fast-paced environments, I thrive by embracing change and continuously adjusting strategies. My adaptability enables me to manage projects effectively, even in highly dynamic and evolving circumstances."
Visual Elements:
Icons Representing Each Quality:
Strategic Thinking: A chess piece (symbolizing strategy).
Passion-Driven: A heart or flame icon.
Global Perspective: A globe or world map.
Adaptability: An arrow curving or a gear symbol.
These icons can be placed beside each quality to give a visual representation and reinforce the message.
Background Image (Optional):
A subtle, abstract image representing diversity or international exposure, perhaps a world map or a series of icons that symbolize different sectors like hospitality, real estate, and finance.
Colors and Design:
A clean design with space between each quality for easy reading.
Use soft gradients or subtle patterns in the background for a polished look, but keep the focus on the text and icons.
Slide Layout:
Top: Title ("Core Strengths that Drive My Success") in bold.
Middle (Left): Brief description of each professional quality (Strategic Thinking, Passion-Driven, Global Perspective, Adaptability) with the corresponding icon next to each one.
Middle (Right or Bottom): Optional global image or additional design accents.
Design Tips:
Font Style: Use clear, professional fonts with slight bolding for headers to ensure readability.
Font Size: Make sure each quality and description has enough space to breathe, ensuring the text doesn't feel crowded.
Timing: Spend about 2-3 minutes discussing how each of these qualities has helped you in your career and can contribute to future projects.
Slide 4: Key Achievements
Title:
"Milestones that Define My Impact"
Content:
Amstel 111 Hotel Development:
"As part of the Aedes team, I played a pivotal role in shaping the Program of Requirements for Amstel 111, a prestigious high-end hotel project in the heart of Amsterdam. My contributions were crucial in aligning the development vision with operational and financial strategies, setting the groundwork for a successful launch."
Graduation Presentation Excellence (9.5/10):
"I earned a 9.5/10 for my graduation defense, which not only reflected my strong academic grounding but also my ability to communicate complex ideas clearly and persuasively. This achievement highlights my ability to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application."
Penta Hotels Pitch Competition:
"In a highly competitive pitch against 40 peers, I developed a winning strategy for Penta Hotels. My approach demonstrated a deep understanding of market dynamics and provided actionable insights for the brand’s expansion strategy, which impressed the judges and secured the win."
Visual Elements:
Icons or Imagery:
Amstel 111: Image of a modern hotel or architectural plans, symbolizing development.
Graduation Presentation: An academic cap or a podium to represent the graduation defense and presentation excellence.
Penta Hotels Pitch: A trophy or podium symbol, indicating competition and victory.
Visual Timeline or Achievement Highlights:
Create a simple, clean timeline or set of icons with brief descriptions next to each key achievement, providing a visual progression of your milestones.
Background Design:
Use a subtle gradient or geometric design to keep the focus on the content while providing a polished look.
Slide Layout:
Top: Title ("Milestones that Define My Impact") centered in bold.
Middle (Left): A list of your key achievements with icons/images for each.
Middle (Right or Bottom): A visual timeline or achievement bar that connects the three milestones.
Design Tips:
Font Style: Use clear, professional fonts, ensuring the descriptions are easy to read. Slight bolding for achievement names (e.g., Amstel 111, Graduation, Penta Hotels).
Colors: Use professional and subtle colors (e.g., dark blues, grays) to make the icons and images stand out without overwhelming the text.
Timing: Spend about 2-3 minutes discussing each achievement, highlighting the challenge, your approach, and the impact of each success.
Slide 5: Ambitions and Future Goals
Title:
"Building the Future: My Path Ahead"
Content:
Short-Term Goals (1-3 years):
"In the coming years, I aim to further develop my expertise in project management, particularly in the pre-opening phase of hotel developments. I’m focused on refining my skills in feasibility analysis, stakeholder management, and operational integration to ensure projects are delivered smoothly from concept to completion."
Mid-Term Goals (3-5 years):
"I aspire to deepen my financial and data analysis capabilities, focusing on real estate investment projects. I want to build and manage financial models that optimize asset performance, contributing to a strong ROI for both stakeholders and clients."
Long-Term Goals (5+ years):
"My ultimate ambition is to become a leader in hospitality and real estate investments, combining my operational expertise with financial acumen to lead large-scale, impactful development projects. I envision myself managing cross-functional teams, overseeing complex developments, and guiding strategic decisions that drive long-term success."
Visual Elements:
Progression Arrow or Pathway:
Use a visual arrow or pathway to represent your career progression over the next few years, breaking it into short, medium, and long-term milestones. Each stage can have an icon or image representing that goal (e.g., project management for short-term, financial analysis for mid-term, leadership for long-term).
Icons for Each Goal:
Short-Term Goals: A calendar or checklist icon.
Mid-Term Goals: A financial graph or calculator icon.
Long-Term Goals: A leadership or building icon, symbolizing a senior role in large-scale projects.
Background Design:
Use a clean, forward-looking design with an upward trend to visually symbolize growth and forward movement.
Slide Layout:
Top: Title ("Building the Future: My Path Ahead") in bold, clean font.
Middle (Left): A list of short, mid, and long-term goals with their respective icons.
Middle (Right or Bottom): A visual representation of your career progression, with milestones connected by an upward arrow or timeline.
Design Tips:
Font Style: Use clear, professional fonts with bold headings to separate each goal category.
Font Size: Make each section easily readable with a slight hierarchy in font size (larger font for the headings, smaller for the descriptions).
Color Scheme: Consider using green (growth) or blue (stability) as a background or accent color, which symbolizes progress and trust.
Timing: Spend about 1-2 minutes explaining how each goal will help you build a strong foundation for your future career and how they align with your passion for hospitality, finance, and real estate.
