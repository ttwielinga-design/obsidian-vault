## 🧩 Frontpage
The **frontpage** sets the tone of your presentation — it must look crisp, clean, and professional.

### **Key Elements**
- **Title:** ≤ 8 words, communicates topic clearly  
- **Sub-headline (optional):** Adds brief context or nuance  
- **Name of Company / Client:** Choose branding based on ownership of work  
- **Date and Time**  
- **Logos and visual identity:** Consistent with client or firm template  

> **Tip:**  
> - For *external* positioning → Use consulting firm logo and color scheme.  
> - For *internal* positioning → Use client’s branding.

## 🧠 Executive Summary
Often called *“At a Glance”*, this is the **single most important slide** in your deck.

### **Purpose**
To summarize the **storyline**, **key arguments**, and **supporting evidence** from the full presentation — enabling any executive to grasp the main message in one minute.

### **Structure: The SCR Framework**
The **Situation–Complication–Resolution (SCR)** framework is used across McKinsey, Bain, and BCG.

| Element | Description | Example |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| **Situation** | Context / current state | “Revenue growth has stagnated at 2% YoY.” |
| **Complication** | Problem or opportunity | “Operational costs increased by 8%, eroding margins.” |
| **Resolution** | Recommended action | “Restructure cost base and optimize sourcing to regain 5pp margin.” |

> **Rule:**  
> If your audience only reads one slide — this is it.

## 📊 Body of Slides
The **core** of the deck — typically 20–60 slides — containing both quantitative and qualitative analysis.

### **Structure of a Consulting Slide**
Every slide must include **three key parts**:

1. **Action Title**  
   - A full sentence stating the *key insight or takeaway*  
   - Avoid neutral titles (“Revenue over time”)  
   - Use *active insight* titles (“Revenue has plateaued despite rising customer acquisition”)

2. **Subheadings**  
   - Describe the data being shown or provide nuance  
   - Examples:  
     - “Forecasted battery costs by 2030 ($/kWh)”  
     - “Sales of personal luxury goods, US Market ($bn)”

3. **Slide Body**  
   - Visual data supporting the insight: charts, text, tables  
   - Include only information directly proving your action title  
   - Remove irrelevant or redundant data  

### **Golden Rules**
- Nothing in title that isn’t proven in body  
- Nothing in body that doesn’t support title  
- Slides should “read like a story” through their **titles alone**

## 🧱 Building the Storyline (Horizontal Flow)
The **storyline** is how your slides build the logical argument — it’s the *narrative spine* of the deck.

### **SCR Framework for Storylines**
1. **Situation:** What is happening now?  
2. **Complication:** Why is this a problem or opportunity?  
3. **Resolution:** What should be done about it?  

### **Process**
- Draft **action titles** for each slide before data is finalized  
- Test logical flow by reading only slide titles in order — it should tell a clear story  
- Use “Slide Sorter” view or print slides to visualize the narrative arc  
- Build your own **slide library** to reuse common structures and visuals

## ✅ Conclusion / Recommendation
This section converts analysis into **clear actions**.

### **Core Objective**
Summarize *how to act* on your findings:
- Actions required to address the issue  
- Implementation plan and timeline  
- Immediate next steps  

### **Guidelines for Effective Recommendations**
| Principle | Description | Example |
|------------|-------------|----------|
| **Group** | Cluster recommendations logically | “Marketing initiatives”, “Cost optimization” |
| **Label** | Use numbering or consistent tags | “1. Grow digital channel revenue” |
| **Action Verbs** | Use active, forward-looking language | “Increase”, “Target”, “Optimize”, “Reduce” |

> Recommendations should be **concise, actionable, and prioritized**.

## 📎 Appendix
The **appendix** contains all supporting evidence not included in the main storyline.

### **Purpose**
- Keep the main deck clear and concise  
- Store backup data and deep dives for reference  

### **Best Practices**
- Clearly label all appendix sections  
- Maintain consistent formatting with the main deck  
- Cross-reference appendix slides where relevant (e.g. “See Appendix B1 for sensitivity analysis”)

## 🎨 Formatting & Design Best Practices

### **Color**
- Use a limited, consistent color palette  
- Highlight key insights using accent colors sparingly  
- Follow a defined **color hierarchy** (e.g., blue = primary, gray = secondary)

### **Fonts**
- Use only 1–2 fonts across the deck  
- Common consulting standards:  
  - McKinsey → Arial (body), Georgia (titles)  
  - BCG → Trebuchet MS  
- Maintain consistent font sizes across slide types

### **Alignment & Margins**
- Never cross slide margins  
- Align all recurring elements (titles, logos, page numbers, sources)  
- Check “Slide Master” to enforce consistency  

### **Text**
- Be concise: remove filler words  
- Titles ≤ 2 lines  
- Bullet lists only when necessary  
- Avoid overuse of numbered lists unless ranking items  

### **Visuals & Icons**
- Use visuals to simplify complexity  
- Replace bullets with icons only if meaningfully representative  
- Use consistent icon style (e.g., Streamline Light)  
- Ensure all visuals are high-resolution and well-labeled  

### **Other Design Principles**
- **Slide numbers** and **data sources** at the bottom  
- **No animations or transitions**  
- **Alignment is sacred:** flip through your slides — headers shouldn’t move

## 🧾 Slide Construction Checklist
Before finalizing your presentation, review each slide using this checklist:

| # | Question | Pass/Fail |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Does the action title convey a clear takeaway? | ☐ |
| 2 | Is every visual relevant to the insight? | ☐ |
| 3 | Are fonts, colors, and alignments consistent? | ☐ |
| 4 | Can the slide be understood in <10 seconds? | ☐ |
| 5 | Is the storyline clear when reading only titles? | ☐ |
| 6 | Are all sources and slide numbers present? | ☐ |
| 7 | Are recommendations grouped and action-oriented? | ☐ |
| 8 | Is unnecessary data moved to the appendix? | ☐ |
