# AI Consulting Strategy to €25k/mo (2026)
A no‑fluff, copy‑and‑paste playbook built from the transcript. Use this as your operating manual. Everything is explained plainly, with scripts, checklists, numbers, and templates you can lift right now.

## 0) North‑Star Plan (what we’re building)
Goal: Consistently hit €25,000+/month by Month 3–4 with a mix of project sprints + small retainers.
Offer mix (example): - Flagship sprint (21 days): “AI Revenue Ops Sprint” — setup of 1–2 high‑ROI automations (lead capture → enrichment → routing; or renewal/churn saves; or sales inbox triage). Price: €8k–€15k. - Support Agent Setup (Zendesk/Intercom/HubSpot Service + LLM): Price: €5k setup + €1.5k–€3k/month optimization/monitoring. - Paid Diagnostic (5–7 days): “Automation & Data Audit” — map bottlenecks, quick wins, and a 90‑day roadmap. Price: €2.5k (credited to sprint if accepted).
Pipeline math (how we reach €25k): - Assume avg project = €8,000. Then 3 projects ≈ €24k (+ any retainer puts you over €25k). - Cold email volume to 3 deals/month (example assumptions): - Send 2,000 emails/month → 2.5% reply (50) → 40% book (20) → 80% show (16) → 20% close (~3.2) → ≈ €25.6k (3.2 × €8k). - Supplement with Upwork/Platforms + LinkedIn DMs + Communities for risk‑diversified deal flow.
Positioning: “We install revenue and cost‑saving automations that pay for themselves in weeks, not quarters.” Lead with outcomes (time saved, error reduction, revenue added), not tools or models.

## 1) Niche, ICP, and Problems (choose one lane to start)
Pick 1 of these to start (expand later): - B2B SaaS (seed–Series B): messy handoffs, slow SDR response, pipeline leakage, renewal risk. - DTC/eCommerce (€1–10M ARR): abandoned checkout, slow CX SLAs, poor post‑purchase comms, RFM segmentation. - Professional Services/Agencies: lead qualifying, proposals follow‑up, invoicing, client onboarding. - Local Services/Clinics: missed calls → no‑shows, intake form chaos, referral leakage.
ICP card (fill before any outreach): - Industry, size, tool stack, buying trigger(s), top 3 pains, 2 quantifiable outcomes you can promise (with ranges, not guarantees).
Translate pains → productized systems: - Lead Capture → Enrichment → Routing (adds speed-to-lead; improves win rate). - Sales Inbox Triage Agent (auto‑summarize threads, draft replies, schedule next steps, update CRM). - Renewal/Churn Save Playbooks (usage dip alerts, automated outreach, save offers). - Support Deflection & QA Agent (FAQ automation + escalation guardrails + daily QA sample review). - Collections/Invoice Follow‑up (gentle, branded nudges across email/SMS/WhatsApp).

## 2) Offers, Guarantees, and Pricing
Core principles from the transcript: frame price as an investment, show clear ROI logic, and drop price after value is built.
Three productized offers (copy): 1) Automation & Data Audit (5–7 days) - Deliverables: current‑state map, bottlenecks, quantified leak sizes, prioritized backlog, 90‑day build plan. - Price: €2,500 (creditable toward sprint). 2) AI Revenue Ops Sprint (21 days) - Deliverables: 1–2 automations live + docs + Loom walkthrough + KPI dashboard + 30‑day hardening. - Price: €8,000–€15,000 depending on scope. 3) Agent Care & Optimization Retainer (monthly) - Deliverables: monitoring, prompt/cue updates, guardrails, analytics, A/B tests, backlog burn‑down. - Price: €1,500–€3,000/mo.
Light guarantee options (pick one): - Milestone guarantee: if Milestone 1 isn’t delivered by Day X, we discount Y% of setup (de‑risks speed). - Outcome guardrails: if baseline KPI doesn’t move by agreed range in 30 days, we extend work 2 weeks free.

## 3) Lead Generation Engine (4 channels)
From the transcript: use Platform Sales, Cold Email, Communities, Cold DMs. Each has trade‑offs. Execute two deeply; keep the other two warm.

Pros: buyers already aware + ready; Cons: time‑heavy; lowest barrier (crowded).
Daily routine (45–60 min): - Save searches for your ICP/tool stack (e.g., “HubSpot automation”, “Zendesk AI”, “Salesforce flow”, “Make.com scenario”). - Submit 10 tailored proposals/day. - Attach a 30–60 sec custom Loom + mini spec (how you’d solve step 1).

> Hey  — read your post and noticed . I’ve shipped similar systems for  that <outcome, e.g., cut first‑response from 14h→2m>.
> First win (week 1): .
> I made a 48‑sec Loom showing exactly how I’d approach it + a mini‑spec (attached).
> If helpful, I can ship a Day‑3 prototype in your sandbox so you can feel it before you commit.
> –
Metrics target: 200 proposals/month → ~10% interviews (20) → ~15% closes (3) → if €3k avg → €9k/mo (these are ballpark; your mileage varies).

- ICP list: 800–2,000 leads/month (tools like Apollo/Clay/Manual research).
- Personalization data: website lines, tech tags, recent news, pain indicators.
Email structure from transcript (copy): personalization → value prop → niche → goal → case → cliffhanger → CTA.

Follow‑up ladder (timing): +3d, +7d, +14d, +24d. Keep messages short, value‑led, and add a new proof/insight each time.
### 3C) Communities (authority building)
Where: relevant Slack/Discord groups, Reddit subs, niche forums, RevOps/CS/CX communities.
Cadence: 3 posts/week + daily 10–15 min replies.

> Body: context → exact workflow → before/after metrics → pitfalls → a tiny Google Sheet/Notion checklist.

### 3D) Cold DMs (LinkedIn/X/IG)

Cadence: 30 personalized DMs/week.

## 4) Sales Call — Exact Flow & Scripts (30–45 min)
Structure (memorize): rapport → set the schedule → discovery → solutions → investment → proposal.
Rapport opener (10–30s): > “Where are you based? Love . Been there for . Ready to jump in?”
Set the schedule (take control): > “Here’s how these calls go: first I’ll ask why you booked, then dig into your numbers, then if I think we can help I’ll show exactly what we’d do and next steps. Sound good?”
Discovery (gold‑mine questions): - “Why did you decide to book a call with me vs others?” - “What’s the goal for the next 90 days?” - “If we fix this, what changes in the business? (time saved, € saved/added)” - “Why hasn’t this been solved yet?” - Follow‑ups: ACV? cycle length? current tools? how decisions get made? budget range?
Transition to solution: > “We’ve solved similar problems for . To make it short: we’ll solve X by doing Y; our expectation is it saves Z/month. Want to see a quick demo?”
Demo: show a template/system from your library (5–7 mins). Make the outcome feel tangible.
Investment framing (drop price last): > “Based on what you told me — that this leak costs ~€50k/quarter — our sprint would be €19k. Assuming no extra scope, that pays for itself in the first month.”
> Say the price, then pause. First to speak loses.
Objection patterns: - “Too expensive” → remove scope items, not price; ask which outcomes matter most. - “Need to think” → “Totally. Just to confirm — does this solve the needs you put in front of me?” (Find the real objection.)
Proposal next step: > “I’ll send a written proposal with scope, milestones, and dates right after this. It’ll auto‑follow up over the next 10 days so nothing gets lost.”

## 5) Proposal, Scope & Auto‑Followups
Sections to include: Overview • Outcomes & KPIs • Detailed scope (what’s in/out) • Milestones & dates • Dependencies/access • Investment & terms • Light guarantee • Acceptance + payment link.
Expiry & nudges: 7–10 day validity; automatic reminders on Day 2/5/9. Optional sweetener (bonus SOP) before expiry.

## 6) Onboarding — Run a Tight Kickoff (live, 30 min)
Agenda: intro → goals & timeline → communication rules → access/2FA (live) → wrap.
Open & frame: > “Goal today: give us everything we need so we don’t have to bug you daily. We’ll confirm milestones, set daily update rhythm, and do all access/2FA live on this call.”
Timelines: - Day 0: kickoff - Week 1: Milestone A live (first visible win) - Week 2: Milestone B - Week 3: Hardening, docs, handover, training
Communication rule (saves chaos): > “You’ll get a single daily update (Mon–Fri) via Slack/email: status, what’s next, and any blockers. We’ll batch questions into that update.”
Access (do it live): list the exact tools (e.g., Slack, CRM, Helpdesk, Make/Zapier, data sources). Handle 2FA by reading codes on the call.
Wrap: > “All set. First update lands tomorrow by 5pm. Ping us only if something is urgent; otherwise we’ll cover it in the daily.”

## 8) Retention & Expansion (strike while it’s hot)
Over‑deliver by ~5%, then ask for the next domino.
Reciprocity pitch (paste): > “While building the Slack workflow we noticed your funnel isn’t sending SMS on key events. Teams at your scale that added it saw +€10–15k/mo. Want me to show the 15‑minute version of how we’d add this — whether or not you hire us?”
Common expansions: - Add a second automation (renewals, collections, NPS→ticketing). - Move from project to Agent Care retainer. - Quarterly RevOps roadmap (paid).
Ask for proof: quick testimonial, 2‑slide case study, or G2/Capterra/LinkedIn post.

## 11) 12‑Week Execution Timeline
Weeks 1–2: Foundation
- Pick niche + write ICP card.
- Build 3 productized offers + light guarantee.
- Create 5 demo systems + 3 one‑pager case studies (even if from pilot/side projects).
- Set up domains, CRM, proposal template, kickoff & access checklists.
Weeks 3–4: Lead Gen Launch
- Platforms: 10 proposals/day with a custom Loom.
- Cold email: ramp to 100/day sending.
- Communities: publish 3 teardown posts; invite DMs for SOP.
- DMs: 30/week targeted.
Weeks 5–8: Close & Deliver
- Run the sales script; send proposals same‑day; auto‑follow.
- Kickoff same‑week; daily updates; ship first wins in Week 1 of each project.
- Film Looms; package delivery like a product.
Weeks 9–12: Retain & Scale
- After each delivery, pitch the next domino + offer retainer.
- Collect 2–3 testimonials; publish a teardown post.
- Systematize: templatize what you just built; add to library.

## 12) KPIs & Dashboard (inspect what you expect)
Top‑of‑funnel (weekly): emails sent, reply %, booked %, show %, meetings, DMs sent, platform proposals, interviews.
Mid‑funnel: proposals sent, win rate, time‑to‑close, avg deal size.
Delivery: milestones on‑time %, client NPS (after Week 1 & at handoff), incident count.
Retention: upsell rate %, months on retainer, churn reasons.
Targets to start: - Reply ≥ 2%, Booked ≥ 35%, Show ≥ 75%, Win ≥ 15–25%, Avg deal ≥ €6–10k, 1st milestone on‑time ≥ 90%.

## 13) Legal & Risk Guardrails (keep it simple)
SOW/MSA: scope in/out, milestones, acceptance criteria, change orders.
Data & security: least‑privilege access; secrets vault; no PII in prompts unless approved; rollback plan.
AI guardrails: human‑in‑the‑loop for escalations; logging; monthly QA samples; prompt‑injection hygiene.

## 15) Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them
Selling tools, not outcomes → always tie to money/time/risk.
Price too early → build value, then price.
Messy scope → write what’s in/out; use change orders.
Silent delivery → daily updates; show progress.
No first win → ship something visible in Week 1.
No expansion ask → over‑deliver 5%, then pitch the next domino.
