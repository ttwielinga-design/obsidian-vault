Black Label Hospitality (BLH), a Third-Party White Label Operator (TPWLO), currently specializes in luxury and
lifestyle-oriented hotel services and has secured several multi-unit development agreements with leading hotel
chains. They are looking to expand from the Netherlands into the German market, which is currently poorly
understood due to a lack of available information on the German TPWLO market. To create an expansion that is
guaranteed to succeed they need to be able to answer the following question:
Management Question
To answer this management question, we are going to address it in three components: Growth Pathways,
Partnership Selection, and Risk Assessment. By addressing these three components we aim to bridge the
TPWLO knowledge gap.

hotel chains. Being selectively exclusive will increase investor confidence, 
operational efficiency and market credibility. It will also improve your project 
allocation and negotiation position with chains.
For the other stakeholders, such as, developers BLH should maintain a diverse set 
of relationships for more potential project locations.

Step 3: Adjusting Focus
A and B cities are preferred forbrand awareness, because chains usually prefer projects 
with higher visibility and brand awareness in central locations. This is relevant for BLH 
since chains allocate around 80% of the total amount of TPWLO projects.
Given the congestion and high prices in A and B cities, BLH should prioritize conversions 
there, while still being open to new builds in B and C cities for a more economical 
approach. Due to the large amount of demand but lack of opportunities for new builds in 
A cities. The costs in A cities are much higher than for B, but especially C cities, where 
there is more room and opportunity for new builds.

Focus Areas
BLH must target 3, 4, or 5 key federal states for resource management and strong 
regional reputation and decide for A and B cities for brand recognition.
Location Strategy
Due to the size of Germany and the differences between federal states, it is difficult to 
maintain an overview to grow properly and steadily. BLH should narrow down their focus 
to 3, 4, or 5 provinces of Germany. BLH should focus on for example federal states with the 
largest economic growth, like North Rhine-Westphalia, Bavaria or Baden-
Wuerttemberg. Focusing on specific federal states will help BLH maintain a good 
reputation and create financial stability, thereby gaining trust. It is important to bear in 
mind that different federal states have different laws and cultures.

BLH must evaluate their financing options to optimize their 
franchise agreements and long-term profitability.
Aim for a balance between upfront cash (key money) and ongoing franchise fees. Always have a conversation regarding the 
different ways of financing. Negotiating for a lower franchise fee will in the short term be less profitable but in the long term 
allow higher profitability. There is not a one size fits all solution here. These aspects need to be considered on a project-to-
project basis.
Conduct extensive financial analyses to identify if key money allocation matches predicted returns. Compare different ways of 
funding such as bank loans, shareholder assistance, or utilizing BLH’s (secured) cash flows. There is a cost implication in 
accepting key money, since it will be paid back through increased franchise fees.

Step 6: Centralized Expansion Strategy
Operating Cashflow Ratio
Utilizing this KPI will do two things for BLH, it will work as a liquidity indicator, helping to assess how
BLH is able to cover its short-term liabilities. This can be used as a risk management tool to help
identify potential cash flow problemsearly, allowing BLH to take corrective action.
Revenue Growth Rate
This KPI measures the percentage increase in revenue over a specific period, which helps BLH
understand their ability to increase revenue. To Somerset Capital Partners this can demonstrate their
rate of expansion and growth. This KPI must be tracked monthly, quarterly and annually for a holistic
overview.
Time to Market
This KPI measures the duration of a hotel project from start to opening. It will help BLH gain insight
into the ratio of completed projects (time management) and the costs of project completion (cash
drain). Reviewing this metric quarterly willhelp identify bottlenecks.
