M6: Performance Management - Marketing Workshop 3.2  
(week 3, workshop 2) – Product Differentiation and Gastronomy 
Building Customer Value 
90 minutes (content and instruction 45 minutes, application 45 minutes) 
Learning goals: 
At the end of this workshop, you should be able to: 
1. Define the different levels of product and understand that service is an integrated part of the 
product. 
2. Understand the concept of differentiation and its application to any of the marketing mix 
elements. 
3. Understand how service quality is perceived by customers and the role of both staff and 
customers in maintaining service quality. 
4. Understand the role of gastronomy in product and service design, specifically for F&B. 
 
Preparation: Please listen again to the video lecture 
on Managing Products and Brands which is found 
on Moodle and read Chapter 9 in your marketing 
textbook

and Brands (required textbook) 
 
What are you expected to know at the start of the session? 
1. You can identify how product and service differentiation influence guest and staff satisfaction 
in the HOTS simulation. 
2. You understand the importance of integrating gastronomy concepts from Module 2 into your 
F&B concept and menu. 
 
Prepare and bring to the session 
1. Draft of Strategic Marketing Plan which should be completed up to Chapter 3 
2. Results of HOTS Year 1 
3. Examples of F&B successes that support specific market trends in the hotel industry and 
define behavior of certain market segments. 
4. Menu concepts that you started to reflect on after workshop 3.1 
 
What are we going to do in this workshop? 
• 
During the first 45 minutes of this workshop, an example of a successful F&B concept will be 
presented by each group and discussed and compared for inspiration.  
• 
In the next 45 minutes of the workshop, each HOTS group will be working on completing the 
menu for their hotel along the lines of the learning goals for gastronomy for this workshop 
(see below). Important guidelines to follow are the menu type and supplier type that you 
have identified for your restaurant, market segment trends in F&B (behavioral or 
psychographic), and the positioning and target groups you have identified. The overall 
objective is to generate more F&B revenues, while controlling the cost of sale for food and 
beverage.  
• 
The groups will upload a PDF menu on the F&B page and the HOTS coach will score this in the 
HOTS simulation game for Year 2.  

How is the content of this session reflected in the test, assessment or professional product?  
You are able to explain what product/service differentiation strategies were followed in the HOTS 
simulation, and be able to link it to departmental costs and a relevant trend in the market. 
Gastronomy learning goals (formative assessment): 
a. The student, in a group, creates a complete menu (three, four or five courses depending on 
the menu type selected within the game) that is consistent with the target groups and ties in 
with at least one of the market trends in the HOTS market reports. 
b.  The student can justify how the menu created in a group is consistent with menu type and 
supplier as defined within the framework of the HOTS game.
