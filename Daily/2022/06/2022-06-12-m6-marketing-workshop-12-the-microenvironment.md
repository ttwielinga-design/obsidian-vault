M6: Performance Management - Marketing Workshop 1.2  
(week 1, workshop 2) – The Marketing Environment (Micro) 
90 minutes (content and instruction 30 minutes, application 60 minutes) 
Learning goals: 
At the end of this workshop, you should be able to: 
1. Discuss the importance of the company’s microenvironment, which includes the company, 
suppliers, marketing intermediaries, customers and the public 
2. Understand the role of marketing intermediaries and suppliers in the hotel industry 
3. Identify trends and characteristics of specific customer segments in the hotel industry 
Preparation: 30 minutes 
Finalize the process of reading market reports from 
HOTS that was initiated in workshop 1.1 and 
identifying relevant market trends  
On Moodle and Literature:  
Kotler, Marketing for Hospitality and Tourism 
Chapter 4: The Marketing Environment 
 
Literature SWOT/TOWS analysis on Moodle  
What are you expected to know at the start of the session? 
In Module 4, you analyzed the microenvironment for a specific international hotel, so you are 
expected to know how to analyze the microenvironment of a hotel and the role of the different 
stakeholders. In this workshop, this analysis will be applied to a hotel in a controlled simulation where 
you will be working within the microenvironment in the role of the hotel, making decisions that can 
influence your interaction with the other stakeholders. 
 
Prepare and bring to the session 
In the previous marketing workshop 1.1, you received the passwords for HOTS and were able 
to log on to the HOTS simulation, so bring along your laptops so that you are able to access 
market reports and navigate through HOTS decisions again. Your DESTEP should have been 
worked on in the last workshop and finalized by the end of the week for the MT meeting.  
 
What are we going to do in this workshop? 
• 
The first 30 minutes of this workshop, we will go over any further questions on the HOTS 
simulation and continue to get familiar with the decisions required in the game, as you will 
need to be prepared to run a trial of 6 months in HOTS on Thursday. Who are the suppliers in 
your HOTS simulation, customer segments, marketing intermediaries, competitors? 
• 
During the next 30 minutes of this workshop, you will identify the microenvironment of your 
HOTS setting and identify trends and characteristics of specific customer segments in the 
HOTS market reports and background information.  The market segment trends can be added 
to the opportunities and threats of your SWOT analysis. On the other hand, you can already 
identify some of the strengths and weaknesses of the hotel that are evident at the start and 
which you may be able to influence through decisions during the trial run.  
• 
In the last 30 minutes of the workshop, the role of the balanced scorecard will be discussed in 
determining goals and KPIs (targets) for an organization or hotel. Each HOTS group will be 
working on developing an initial strategy for their hotel for Thursday so it is important to 
understand how a balanced scorecard relates to both marketing and financial goals, and how 
to develop SMART goals with specific targets. 
How is the content of this session reflected in the test, assessment or professional product?  
You are able to understand the microenvironment in which you are working and begin to understand 
the impact of decisions on performance and interaction with the microenvironment.
