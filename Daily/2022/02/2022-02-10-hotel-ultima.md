Hotel Concept:
Hotel Ultima is a modern 5 star luxury hotel, located in the fashionable Pijp area in Amsterdam.  The hotel is an independently owned and operated hotel. The hotel is owned by Georges Papadopoulos, a flamboyant 33 years old Greek national, who earned his fortune in trading in crypto currencies.
Georges Papadopoulos has a  passion for fashion, design and music. He has, however, no experience in hotel management and he wishes to hire a management team and staff that complement the bold and modern look and feel of the hotel.

The hotel is bold in its design, bright colors, well-engineered lighting and smart rooms with many electronic gadgets. The interior design of the property reflects the owner’s passions and the core values Sassy – Fueled –Extravagant.  The lobby and public areas are cleverly designed to make a transition from day into night. The hotel is energized with the sound of deep house music.
The hotel’s uniforms will be extravagant, colorful and fashionable.

The hotel has the ambition to become the place to see and to be seen for famous athletes, celebrities and influencers.  The property will have a large appeal to millennials and modern jet-set.

Although the owner is wealthy, he has not forgotten his simple descend. Georges Papadopoulos has expressed his desire that the organizational culture reflects a community feeling, focus on social responsibility, care for the associates and high in business ethics and integrity.

Based on the owner’s desires and the core values, Hotel Ultima has formulated the following mission statement:

We are loud and proud. We serve the guests and the community in the Ultimate way. Nothing is too extravagant, everything is ultimately possible.

The vision of Hotel Ultima is:
Ultimate challenges and disrupts, but is deeply rooted in the community and attached to their guests.

Unique Selling Points
Hotel Ultimate prides itself that no guest request has become unfulfilled. Ultimately, everything is possible. The service style is informal, modern and cheeky, yet polite, punctual and accurate.  The staff is energized and have a ‘can do’ attitude. The associates are unique individuals, who can operate in a team.  The staff is empowered to take decisions and enjoy a fair amount of freedom within the framework of the Standard Operating Procedures. The operating philosophy is to meet and exceed every guests’ expectation.

Facilities:
Hotel Ultima is still under construction. It is expected to open its doors  in Q4 -2022. The shell of the property is a historic building. The interior of the building has been stripped entirely to make way for an eclectic lobby and the other facilities
The hotel rooms are modern and smart. Many electronic gadgets help the guests to “operate” the room: closing curtains, mood lights, entertainment system and air conditioning are operated via a tablet in the room.
The property has 2 restaurants, a bar, wellness facilities (spa of 3 treatment rooms and a state-of-the art gymnasium).

Facilities:
100 Superior rooms – 34 m2 kingsize bed
90 Spectacular rooms – 34 m2 kingsize bed – city view
1 Ultimate suite – 102 m2 – living room, dining area and 2 bedrooms with en suite bathrooms, plus butler service
9 Executive suites – 68 m2 – living area plus one bedroom with en-suite bathroom
Lobby Lounge – Serving coffee, tea, pastries. Open 9 am -22.00 every day, 30 seats
Restaurant “The Tube” the name of the restaurant is a playful twist on the location of the hotel in “De Pijp”.  The Tube is open for dinner only and seats 80 covers. The Tube is an elegant and modern venue serving high end ‘from farm to table’ dishes. The Tube has the ambition to eventually become a Michelin star venue.
The Ultimate Brasserie serves breakfast, lunch and dinner. The capacity is 80 covers. The ambiance is an interesting mix of the traditional French brasserie, but with a modern twist. Supported by the open kitchen concept, guests can enjoy the fresh flavors of the food and the spectacle in the kitchen.
Bar “Raw”. Not the typical hotel bar, but a resemblance of the rough underground scene of the seventies and eighties in Berlin, London and New York. The bar “Raw” hosts themed nights around raves, punk, rock, hip hop and house music and transforms into a nightclub. The venue is 300 m2 over 2 stories and has the potential to become the hotspot of Amsterdam. Open Wed-Sat 21.00 – 04.00
Wellness facilities: state of the art gymnasium and a spa with 3 treatment rooms
Strategy:
The hotel wants to be bold, modern and playful. Not  a hotel for the average guest, but a hotel for the millennials, modern jet set and the young at heart.
The guest segmentation is 60% corporate and 40% leisure.
The hotel is a 5 star deluxe hotel, the operating philosophy is that true luxury derives from guest contact.  Due to the ambiance, service style and bold character of the hotel, there are only few competitors in Amsterdam. This allows the hotel to choose a Differentiation Focus Strategy (Porter 1980) and to set the room rates accordingly.
The associates of the hotel are unique individuals who are able to function in a team environment. The hotel does not mind piercings, tattoos and funky hairdo’s. The associates are extraverts, with a passion for service excellence and hospitality.  Service has to be pro-active, rather than reactive. Anticipating guest needs and going the extra mile are in the DNA of the property.
The hotel does not believe in outsourcing. All departments are on the payroll of the property. This ensures more control over the quality of the services.

As mentioned:
The property will open its doors on 1st January 2023. It is assumed that corona is no longer a factor by then.
The hotel owner expects an upswing in occupancy in 2023, back to 2019 levels. The ADR will be high as a result of the premium (skimming) strategy.
It is now time to plan ahead and hire a management team and the associates in time to support the pre-opening stages of the hotel.
Due to the specific character of the hotel, it requires special talents to make this hotel operation a success.

Assignment:
The assignment for you, as consultants, is to design a HR plan for the hotel management in which you propose elaborate on the following topics:
How to define the organizational culture of the hotel
How to shape a new organizational structure for the hotel?
Quantitative staffing and financial aspects: please create a manning guide plus salaries & benefits for the re-opening of the property. You will create a payroll budget for 2023.
Reflect also on the qualitative aspect of the staff: what should be the general competence profile of all associates of the hotel in the new setting?
Draw up a plan for the internal communication structure for the hotel
Elaborate on 2 elective themes regarding human resource policies for the hotel that you think are most relevant in this situation. What should these policies entail and how should they be implemented in the hotel?
