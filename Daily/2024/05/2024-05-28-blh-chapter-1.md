# Chapter 1. Introduction
Black Label Hospitality (BLH), a Third-Party White Label Operator (TPWLO), specializing in the fast-moving hospitality sector, offering luxury and lifestyle-oriented hotel services. BLH has already secured several multi-unit development agreements with the leading hotel chains - Marriott, Accor, Hilton, Wyndham, Hyatt, and IHG. The company is now looking to expand (Black Label Hospitality, 2023). However, before we discuss TPWLOs, we need to know what they are. In short, they are a hotel management company without a trademark (Wagner, 2024). As a TPWLO, BLH is the vital bridge between the property (developers) owners and hotel chains, thus, it is the key player that influences the improvement of its partners' brand value and operational efficiency. The role of the chain here is to provide support to the franchisee (e.g. helping with negotiating OTA fees, finding staff, and supply chain) in exchange for a franchise fee. On the contrary, a branded TPWLO does not have a franchisor so do not need to pay these royalty fees however they do not have the added benefit that a chain can offer. BLH is looking to broaden its focus, from only the Netherlands to now spreading its wings into the German market. BLH emphasizes financially viable projects, striving for a GOP of 35% in the first stable year, and fits with their standards and strategic objectives, such as long-term leases of 20 years or more (Black Label Hospitality, 2023).
As of now, the TPWLO market in Germany is poorly understood due to a lack of available information, creating a significant research gap. This is because the German TPWLO market is mostly unclear. This observation was confirmed by two long-standing industry professionals namely, Hurkmans (2024) and Schöningh (2024). They both stressed the value of the interviews to get a better idea of the market's inner workings. The research gap becomes evident through these first interviews in combination with the lack of secondary research. Hence, this paper aims to fill this gap by analyzing How Black Label Hospitality can optimize its strategy for entering the German market, to enhance its White-Label positioning. This management question is the key issue not only for BLH's successful market penetration but also for the broader practical understanding of the TPWLO market due to its closed nature. Through the collection of primary data in the form of interviews, this study aims to provide valuable insights for BLH as they investigate this opportunity.
This paper has seven chapters, and it is the result of research on the German TPWLO market. Chapter Two starts with an all-around literature review, which includes secondary sources and expert opinions, and rounds off with the research questions. The methodology is described in Chapter Three, with an emphasis on getting fresh material through interviews. Chapter Four uses the findings of the qualitative data to answer the research questions. Chapter Five concludes this research report by answering the management question in combination with giving our recommendation to BLH. Finally, Chapter Six will go into the study's limitations and recommendations for further research.
