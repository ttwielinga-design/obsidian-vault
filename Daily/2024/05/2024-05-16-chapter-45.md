## 4.5 What are the first steps a TPWLO should take when expanding to Germany, according to competitors, industry experts (consultants & brands)?
Expanding into the German market presents challenges and opportunities for TPWLOs, requiring a smart approach to ensure success. Industry experts, competitors, and consultants highlight the importance of understanding local market dynamics, securing trustworthy partnerships, and managing operational and financial logistics. Missteps in these areas can lead to financial loss and reputational damage. Given these challenges, the first steps a TPWLO should take when expanding to Germany are acquiring extensive local knowledge, hiring responsive and active leadership, outsourcing HR and finance to local experts, establishing trust with local landlords, forming strategic plans with hotel chains, evaluating real estate opportunities for conversion, conducting feasibility studies, focusing on prominent cities, and building strong networks through industry events. Furthermore, providing financial stability through well-structured financial strategies and upholding a good reputation are critical for obtaining the trust of local stakeholders. Effective market presence and exposure at industry events strengthen the company's position and lead to new prospects.
### 4.5.1 Human Resources and Market Expansion
To successfully enter the German market, TPWLOs must emphasize gaining a thorough understanding of local market dynamics and hiring professionals with German market expertise. Competitors and industry experts underline the necessity of having a responsive and active CEO who is well-known and quick to respond to ensure smooth operations and growth (HSC_04). Furthermore, outsourcing HR and finance to local expertise is essential for negotiating the complexity of the German market (INV_01). Building confidence with local landlords and establishing a strong presence in the German market are equally important first stages (HSC_06).
### 4.5.2 Operational and Financial Management
Operational and financial management are critical for effective market entry. To ensure strong collaborations, industry experts propose developing strategic plans with two to three hotel chains (HSC_06). Financial talks require a focus on rent, indexation, and guarantees, as well as an understanding of the company's setup. Evaluating real estate options for conversions rather than new builds can result in financial savings and faster market entry (HSC-04). A good reputation and a sustainable financial strategy are essential for ensuring that the operator is viewed as trustworthy and financially secure (HSC_04, HSC_03).
### 4.5.3 Strategic Market Positioning
Strategic market positioning necessitates extensive market research and strategic entrance preparation. Conducting feasibility studies, emphasizing local knowledge, and employing local teams are critical for understanding market dynamics and achieving early success (CNS_05, COM_01. Experts advise beginning in significant cities (A or B locations) and considering MICE and leisure categories to develop a strong market presence (DEV_02, INV_03). Networking and visibility at industry events such as Expo Real and IHF are also essential for making relationships and expanding market presence (INV_02, INV_03). Forming strategic alliances and focusing on important opportunities can help to improve market positioning (DEV_01, COM_02).
