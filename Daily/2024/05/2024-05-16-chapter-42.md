## 4.2 Answer to the Research Question: “Is a TPWLO a commodity or a differentiated product?”
The presence of numerous established hotel operators that frankly offer the same service to the guest, call into doubt the extent to which TPWLOs differ. Stakeholders frequently face the issue of identifying unique value propositions in a homogeneous market. Is a TPWLO fundamentally a commodity, with operators providing nearly identical services, or is there considerable differentiation that affects strategic and operational effectiveness? The research provides a nuanced viewpoint. While TPWLOs have strong commodity features in their basic business models and operational frameworks, strategic differentiation based on market focus, brand alignment, and local flexibility provides points of uniqueness by combining commodity and differentiated product traits.
### 4.2.1 Core Business Model
The analysis provides a nuanced view of whether TPWLOs are commodities or differentiated products. According to the majority of respondents, TPWLOs have significant commodity features and little distinction amongst operators. A consultant stated "Essentially, they are the same,"(CNS_05, Page 2). A different consultant emphasized the importance of economies of scale, citing the operators' quick scaling and established market presence as truly important (CNS_03). This viewpoint was shared by an industry expert, who explained that there is not much differentiation between TPWLOs, only a difference in the average room rate (ARR) (EXP_02) Other consultants noted that TPWLOs are largely commodities, distinguished by their size and capital structures (CNS_03).
### 4.2.2 Operational Effectiveness
There are minor differences in operational performance amongst TPWLOs that point to differences. An expert from one of the biggest hotel chains emphasized the significance of brand and location synergy (EXP_03). Several responders agree with this sentiment, citing TPWLOs' particular adaptation to local surroundings (HCS_05, EXP_01). Furthermore, a hotel chain stresses the necessity of matching objectives and building trust within franchise agreements, citing various operational methods depending on customer needs and local market realities (HCS_05).
### 4.2.3 Strategic Differentiation
Strategic differentiation is more prominent among TPWLOs. A consultant from a prominent firm and other respondents observed variations in market positioning and expansion prospects, underlining the importance of strategic goals and market emphasis (CNS_05). For example, a different consultant noted that Black Label Hospitality concentrates on the luxury and lifestyle areas, whereas German operators choose to target the mid-scale category (CNS_04). This says something about how BLH is perceived in the market and where they are different from their competition. This strategic focus is reinforced by a competitor, who points out that different TPWLOs concentrate on distinct niches, such as limited-service hotels vs larger, more monumental sites (COM_01). Furthermore, a different hotel chain noted, "There are white label operators that focus on A locations and those that specifically target B and C locations," emphasizing the need for strategic location differences (HCS_04).
