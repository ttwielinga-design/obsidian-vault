### 4.12 Risks of Cultural Integration When Expanding into Germany
Research Question: What are the risks of cultural integration when expanding into Germany, according to competitors, brands, industry experts, and consultants?
Industry experts (EXP), competitors (COM), hotel chains (HCS), and consultants (CNS) have all called attention to the dangers associated with cultural integration while expanding into Germany. The main reason for these concerns is the requirement to adjust to the formal, efficiency-focused communication style of the German hospitality sector. Building trust through dependable and regular acts, honing diplomatic communication skills, attaining operational precision, and strictly adhering to timetables are all necessary for this. Furthermore, it is extremely difficult to find and develop local talent that can successfully navigate the cultural differences between the local market and the company's culture. A successful market entry requires a strategic plan that considers local laws and market conditions. This plan should place a strong emphasis on forming strategic relationships with surrounding businesses to assist them in effectively navigating the local business climate.
4.12.1 Selective Codes:
Five topics have come forth from the qualitative interviews, each of which addresses a different feature of risks associated with cultural integration and methods for a successful market entry into Germany. These themes capture BLH's main areas of interest and concern.
The first theme, Business Communication and Relationship Building, emphasizes how crucial it is to understand and adjust to Germany's formal communication style. The requirement for precise and unambiguous communication was highlighted by one consultant who stated that "they would at least be able to give directions (...) I think language is key there" (CNS_03, Page 12). Naturally, you shouldn't be that straightforward, according to the advice of another interviewee; You ought to adopt a more diplomatic stance in your dealings (CNS_03, Page 8). This highlights how important tact and diplomacy are in social situations. Moreover, another consultant said that "building trust takes time, and you need to show consistency in your actions" (CNS_04, Page 15) and that "building trust is a gradual process that requires consistent and reliable actions."
The second subject, Cultural Competence and Adaptation, emphasizes the need for cultural sensitivity as well as the ability to change business practices to adhere to the formal, efficiency-driven ideology of German hospitality culture. A professional in the field said, "I would say that the German hospitality culture is more formal and focused on efficiency" (EXP_02, 1). This methodical and effective strategy necessitates adaptability and flexibility from firms. "I think if you have the sensitivity to be able to adapt to the German style of doing business, you'll do fine," said a hotel chain representative (HCS_03, Pages 8 & 9). Another interviewee emphasized the significance of adaptability, saying, "Adaptability is essential. You have to be prepared to modify your plans in response to local demands" (CNS_06, Page 3).
Next, the requirement for high levels of Operational Excellence and Compliance in Germany is the subject of the third theme, Operational Excellence and Compliance. "German operations expect a high level of precision and adherence to schedules," said an industry expert (EXP_05, Page 2). This is a high requirement that requires careful preparation and implementation. "If we sign a deal, great," said another consultant. As stated on page 8 of CNS_03, "If we don't sign it yet, we keep working towards it," demonstrating the dedication needed to live up to these high standards.
The fourth theme, Organizational Human Resources Management, addresses the challenge of finding and training the right talent who can bridge the company's culture with local market expectations. As one HR manager pointed out, "Finding the right talent who understands both the local market and your company's culture is a challenge" (HRM_01, Page 5). This challenge is compounded by the need to train staff to meet the high standards expected in Germany, as another HR manager noted, "training staff to meet the high standards expected in Germany is crucial" (HRM_02, Page 6).
The fifth and final theme, Strategic Market Entry and Expansion, emphasizes the need for a well-defined strategic plan that considers local regulations and market conditions. A strategic advisor explained, "Entering the German market requires a clear, strategic plan that takes into account local regulations and market conditions" (STR_01, Page 7). Additionally, forming strategic partnerships with local firms can significantly ease the entry process and help navigate the local business landscape, as another strategic advisor stated, "Partnerships with local firms can ease the entry process and help navigate the local business landscape" (STR_02, Page 8).
These insights provide a thorough understanding of the risks and strategies for cultural integration when expanding into Germany. They highlight the importance of adapting to local business practices, building trust through consistent actions, achieving operational precision, recruiting and training the right talent, and forming strategic partnerships.
4.12.2 Important Observations:
The interviews also revealed important observations that underscore the challenges and necessities of integrating into the German market. These observations are crucial for understanding the nuances of cultural integration and successful market entry.
Formality and Efficiency: The experts consistently stress the importance of understanding the formal and efficiency-driven nature of German hospitality culture. Success in this market is heavily dependent on adapting to these cultural norms to develop strong partnerships. The expectation for formal interaction is not just a preference but a critical component of everyday business operations, reflecting the country's broader cultural emphasis on order and precision.
Diplomatic Communication: A diplomatic approach to communication is highly valued over direct methods, which helps in building trust and avoiding misunderstandings. This style is not only about being polite but also about being strategic in interactions to ensure that relationships are built on a solid foundation of mutual respect and understanding.
Trust and Consistency: Building trust in Germany involves demonstrating consistent and reliable business practices over time. German business culture values reliability and consistency highly, making it essential for any business seeking to establish long-term relationships.
Operational Precision: There is a high expectation for operational precision and adherence to schedules. Careful planning and execution are required to meet the German standards of business operations. This extends beyond simple efficiency and into every aspect of business operations, from project planning to daily management and customer service.
Adaptability and Flexibility: Success in the German market also requires flexibility and the willingness to adjust strategies to meet local demands. The market is dynamic, and being too stiff can be a disadvantage. Companies that can pivot and adapt to changing circumstances or customer needs are more likely to succeed.
Local Talent Acquisition: Finding and training the right talent who understands both the local market and the company's culture is a significant challenge. Local talent not only brings knowledge of the local business environment but also carries cultural insights that can bridge any gaps that might exist between a foreign company and local business practices.
Strategic Partnerships: Forming partnerships with local firms can help market entry and help navigate local business regulations and practices. These partnerships can provide valuable local insights, ease logistical challenges (when relevant), and enhance credibility in a new market.

For this particular research question there were no real contradictory statements and outliers in the answers from respondents.
