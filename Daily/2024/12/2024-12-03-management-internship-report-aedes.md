# Chapter 1: Company Analysis

Customer Segments
Aedes targets very wide customer sectors, including but not limited to hotels, individuals (tenants), and businesses (also renters of real estate). These represent both B2B and B2C tactics.
Value Proposition
Aedes' value proposition—"Creating places with heart and soul that have a positive impact"—shows that they want to be sustainable and to develop responsibly. There is, however, a slight mismatch in how this value proposition is realized in various client segments. For example, hotel operations and real estate asset management have different measures of success; guest satisfaction would be the measure of success for the former, while appreciation of assets would be for the latter. In those cases, Aedes' mission does not explain how such "positive impact" would come into play. One possibility I see is to reword the value offer so that accrued benefits of each segment are featured. Hotels would, therefore, likely to focus on guest experience, and real estate might focus more on long-term financial and social sustainability.
Channels
Aedes does a multi-channeling using social media, networking, and conventions. Through this, it is easy to target B2B as well as B2C markets.
Customer Relationships
Aedes maintains relationships through landlord services, automated services, and communities.
Revenue Streams
Aedes creates value from a variety of revenue streams-speculation in the purchase and sale of real estate, real estate development, renting, asset management, and hotel operations. This diversification is at the same time its strength and its weakness. The complexity of the hospitality portfolio and that of real estate may set up conflicting demands for resources. Aedes could tone down this inconsistency by making it a specialized company within itself-one dealing with real estate development and another dealing with hospitality management since this is the case at present. It would allow the firm to align its resources and capabilities more worthily with each revenue stream's demands.
Key Resources
Aedes' key resources are skilled people, financial resources, physical assets and intellectual property, among others.
Key Activities
Its main activities include feasibility studies, research, design, financial control, hotel management amongst others.
Important Allies
Aedes depends on real estate agents, contractors, architects and consultants as key partners.
Cost Structures
The cost structure for Aedes includes operational costs, cost of labor, and project-based expenses together. One area of incongruity in this regard is that the operations cost may turn out to be in the higher side since the company covers a wide range of activities. This can be reduced by streamlining the processes and use of technology-driven efficiencies, such as even more than currently the case, automated financial management system. A lean management style, especially regarding asset management and operation would enable Aedes to be profitable at the same time achieving operation excellence.

# Chapter 3: Leadership Analysis
In this paragraph I am going to talk about two managers at the W Fort Lauderdale. In order to talk about them objectively I am going to use the situational leadership model, Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership, and Dr. Mehrabian’s 7-38-55% Rule.
## 3.1 Joost (Head of Hospitality)
In analyzing Joost's leadership style through the Situational Leadership Model (SLM), it is clear that he operates primarily within the delegating style. This style involves low directive and low supportive behavior, where he provides his team with substantial autonomy, focusing on results rather than dictating methods. Joost’s approach centers on entrusting his team members with responsibility and ensuring tasks align with ethical standards and company values. In my experience, this was quite a shift, as he gave me an extensive task list upon joining and checked in only during weekly meetings. These check-ins allow team members to update him on project progress and raise any concerns they face. His ability to delegate effectively, while maintaining oversight through consistent weekly check-ins, showcases his confidence in his team and his belief in fostering an independent work environment.
Using Maxwell’s 5 Levels of Leadership to evaluate Joost, he appears to operate between Level 4 (People Development) and Level 5 (Pinnacle). Joost is highly invested in the growth and achievements of his team members, regularly setting ambitious but achievable targets and providing the necessary resources to help them succeed. For instance, a few weeks ago, he assigned me the task of calculating linen needs for two hotel properties with a strict deadline of three days. He initially provided the core information and directed me to additional resources, giving me the freedom to build an Excel model independently. After completing the task within two days, he reviewed my work, offered feedback on minor details, and promptly approved it for the supplier—showing a strong commitment to both development and results.
Joost’s leadership style also hints at him evolving into a Level 5 leader. His approach of treating team members as peers and providing freedom fosters a culture of respect, and his relatively young age suggests he is still progressing in his leadership journey. As he continues to refine his approach, I believe Joost is well on his way to becoming a Pinnacle leader who will inspire others through both respect and his dedication to team development.
## 3.2 Madelon (Head of Assets)
Using the SLM to assess Madelon’s style, she primarily operates within the coaching and supporting approaches, particularly at the beginning of a working relationship. Her coaching style (high directive, high supportive) is evident in how she explains tasks step-by-step, ensuring a clear understanding of project requirements. As she observes growing confidence and competence in her team members, she transitions to a delegating approach. A significant example of this was during a project related to the refinancing of Amstel 111, where I worked alongside her on creating slides with key calculations for a valuation. This slide presentation was critical as it would be shared with the bank. Madelon allowed me full autonomy to design my slide, only reviewing it afterward. This experience was instrumental to my growth, as it marked the first time she shifted to a more delegating style, trusting me to handle a key component of the project independently.
In terms of Maxwell’s 5 Levels of Leadership, Madelon’s style aligns with Level 2 (Permission) and Level 4 (People Development). She is deeply committed to building strong, trusting relationships with her team members, encouraging open communication about both work-related matters and personal well-being. This personal investment fosters an environment where people feel comfortable sharing their challenges and progress, which helps her maintain a close pulse on the team's overall morale and performance. Her commitment to development shines through in how she mentors her team members, balancing professional guidance with genuine concern for their growth and welfare. This blend of relational and developmental focus exemplifies Madelon’s approach to leadership and strengthens the loyalty and trust within her team.

3.3 Alignment with Aedes' Company Culture
	Aedes’ values are humble sustainability, relaxed hospitality, local commitment, diverse perspectives, self-empowerment, and passion. Both Joost and Madelon reflect these principles in their own way. Joost, with his focus on delegating and empowering team members, aligns strongly with self-empowerment. By entrusting his team with autonomy and providing minimal yet structured guidance, he allows them to take ownership of their work, cultivating confidence and independence. This approach also resonates with Aedes’ passion and commitment to diverse perspectives by allowing team members to approach tasks creatively and with their own methods.
Madelon’s leadership style mirrors Aedes’ values of local commitment and relaxed hospitality. Her emphasis on building personal connections and creating a supportive environment aligns with the company's vision of fostering a warm, inclusive workplace. Madelon’s coaching approach encourages openness, making team members feel valued both personally and professionally. Her focus on development ensures that each person feels invested in, aligning with Aedes' commitment to sustainability by nurturing long-term talent and growth.
3.4 Personal Reflection
From Joost, I want to adopt his ability to empower team members by providing both freedom and accountability. His trust in others inspires a sense of responsibility, which I believe is critical for a leader aiming to cultivate a proactive, motivated team. # Chapter 4: Sustainable Development
Aedes is fully dedicated to connecting its projects with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), I have observed that they focus particularly on Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities and Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. They actively contribute to these goals and are devoted to moving them forward through new initiatives and sustainable practices.
## 4.1 Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities
“Creating Places with Lasting Relevance”— Aedes (2024)
Aedes' view on sustainability is to "enhance urban environments by integrating eco-friendly design and construction practices" (Aedes, 2024a). This is in line with SDG 11, which aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable (United Nations, 2024).
### 4.1.1 Key Achievements and Innovations over the Years at Aedes with Their Projects:
2011: The Aquifer Thermal Energy Storage system introduced in Burgerziekenhuis represented their concerns about energy consumption and sustainable building. (Aedes, 2024a)
2012: The Sedum roofs and the ATES in Andaz Amsterdam brought amenity to energy efficiency and biodiversity-enabling sustainable urbanism. Aedes 2024a.
2017: Solar panels and landscape restoration work at the Landgoed Vels estate symbolize the high standards of sustainability, in that resilience in urban areas is promoted.
2018: An example of water resource management with the green-blue roof and Permavoid system that has already been installed in Soho House Amsterdam makes it more sustainable in respect to its urban infrastructure.
2020: The zero-gas apartment building in Sarphatistraat 24 symbolizes their interest in carbon footprint reduction and energy-efficient residential construction, toward mitigation of urban pollution levels.
2021: The Mannoury smart roof project brings forth insight into sustainable roofing. This furthered their knowledge on implementing science to build resilient urban areas.
### 4.1.2 Innovation Laboratory
Aedes also has its innovation lab, Aedes Cares, which researches new concepts relating to sustainability. In this regard, it has support for glass recycling and advanced solar panels (Aedes, 2024a). These, therefore, add to SDG 11, as they reflect innovative responses towards sustainable urban living.
### 4.1.3 Sustainability PoR
Aedes' sustainability approach integrates cutting-edge principles of energy performance and operation practices in all projects. This holistic approach provides for and surpasses the minimum requirements of sustainability, therefore directly contributing to SDG 11 by ensuring that projects are sustainable and resilient.
This can be noticed, for example, in Amstel 111-a project currently under construction right beside Carre. Here, I contributed by upgrading the sustainability PoR of Aedes which will be used for all future hotel developments.
### 4.1.4 Amstel 111 Project
Core: Focus on reusing existing materials contributes to SDG11 by following sustainable construction methods respectful to the historical value of the building.
Extra: Amenities like native plants planting and insect hotel will add to more urban biodiversity, making the ecosystem of the urban resilient.
Innovation: Upcycling of materials and electric boat transport proves our concern for innovative solutions to sustainably support urban development.
In the final analysis, Aedes is committed to Goal 11 of the Sustainable Development Goals—that is, Sustainable Cities and Communities—through its innovative environmental-friendly initiatives. Aedes promotes sustainable urbanism and resilience in cities by the integration of systems such as ATES and Sedum roofs, pushing the envelope for such techniques as green-blue roofs and smart roofing. Further, their innovation lab and sustainability PoR showcase how they work on building long-lasting and environmentally sustainable urban settings. Really bringing home their commitment to this SDG and sustainability in general.
## 4.2 Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production
Their commitment to sustainability is not confined to a single SDG. For example, they can act on SDG 12 by facilitating the efficient use of resources and waste reduction across all functions of the business. United Nations 2024.
### 4.2.1 The way Aedes Measures
Aedes has developed a set of criteria for Core (the minimum), Extra (over and above), and Innovative (out of the ordinary) sustainability measures for its residences. Some examples of these are as follows:
Core Measures
By using low-emission, FSC-certified material and designing demountable building elements, Aedes intends to minimize waste and ensure responsible material usage.
Air quality and material specification for a better living space and efficient use of resources.
Additional Measures
Assuring a room is optimized for quality sleep is their pledge to sustainable consumption with better overall health and comfort. This is further evidenced by the fact that one of the USPs of Aedes is the promise of "best sleep ever.".
Including blue roofs and high-quality materials use fosters environmental sustainability; in that respect, it enables responsible production and consumption.
Innovative Measures
Upcycling of glass for windows, application of biobased materials reduces general waste, promotes the use of sustainable materials for SDG 12.
Zero-waste application and making sure of radical transparency serve as our reflections to efficient resource use and responsible means of production.
To sum up, Aedes' innovative measures set the company apart as an industry leader in setting new norms on sustainability. These are aligned perfectly with SDG 12 and contribute to better operational efficiency and environmental stewardship, indicative of Aedes's commitment toward a more sustainable future.
