Chapter 1: Company Analysis 
This chapter analyzes Aedes by using the BMC (Business Model Canvas): how the 
building blocks match with the strategic objectives and operations. It looks at possible 
misalignments and their implications for marketing, communication, sales, and revenue 
management by focusing on some key areas: Customer Segments, Value Proposition, and 
Revenue Streams. Strategic insights are used to reveal the opportunities for improvement that 
keep Aedes competitive and in tune with its mission. This analysis points where the company 
has room to work more harmoniously and utilize resources better by indicating 
inconsistencies and proposing viable solutions

1.1 BMC analysis 
Customer Segments 
Aedes targets very wide customer sectors, including but not limited to hotels, individuals 
(tenants), and businesses (also renters of real estate). These represent both B2B and B2C 
clientele. In the past few months, I have noticed there sometimes is misalignment between 
teams due to a lack of specific knowledge when it comes to certain departmental niche areas 
(e.g., technical knowledge for writing PoR descriptions in the hospitality team was missing). 
Having a clear split in teams that focus on hotels, commercial real estate, and office buildings 
could help this optimization. If this were to be implemented there would however be a need for 
more FTE since the workload would need to be spread-out more to stick to this structure. 
Value Proposition 
Aedes' value proposition—"Creating places with heart and soul that have a lasting 
positive impact"—shows that they want to be sustainable and to develop responsibly. There is, 
however, a slight mismatch in how this value proposition is realized in various client segments. 
For example, hotel operations and real estate asset management have different measures of 
success; guest satisfaction would be the measure of success for the former, while appreciation 
of assets would be for the latter. In those cases, Aedes' mission does not explain how such 
"positive impact" would come into play. One possibility I see is to reword the value offer so 
that combined benefits of each segment are featured. Hotels would, therefore, likely focus on 
guest experience (with a motto like: Creating places with heart and soul that have a lasting 
impact on the guest), and real estate might focus more on long-term financial and social 
sustainability. 
Channels 
Aedes multi-channels using social media, networking, and conventions to market 
themselves. Using these different marketing tools, it is relatively easier to target B2B as well as 
B2C markets. 
Customer Relationships 
Aedes maintains customer relationships through landlord services, automated services, 
and communities. This ranges from assisting in maintenance and upkeep of key systems, like 
the HVAC in hotels, to assisting customers directly with housekeeping through the hotels. Using 

 
5 
more advanced tools such as Proprli should help in the optimization on the technical side of 
their Real Estate portfolio ensuring better upkeep will help enhance customer relationships1. 
Revenue Streams 
Aedes creates value from a variety of revenue stream—speculation in the purchase and 
sale of real estate, real estate development, renting, asset management, and hotel operations. 
This diversification is at the same time its strength and its weakness. The complexity of the 
hospitality portfolio and that of real estate may set up conflicting demands for resources (time 
and money). Aedes could tone down this inconsistency by making it a specialized company 
within itself-one dealing with real estate development and another dealing with hospitality 
management since this is the case at present. It would allow the firm to align its resources and 
capabilities more worthily with each revenue stream's demands.  
Key Resources 
Aedes' key resources are among others skilled people, financial resources, physical 
assets and intellectual property. 
Key Activities 
Its main activities include feasibility studies, research, design, financial control, hotel 
management amongst others. 
Key Partners 
Aedes depends on real estate agents, contractors, architects and consultants as key 
partners. These assist in the purchasing, designing and building of the real estate. 
Cost Structures 
The cost structure for Aedes includes operational costs, cost of labor, and project-based 
expenses together. One area of incongruity in this regard is that the operations cost may turn 
out to be in the higher side since the company covers a wide range of activities. This can be 
reduced by streamlining the processes and use of technology-driven efficiencies, such as even 
more than currently the case, automated financial management system. A lean management

2.1.1 Financial Performance Review  
When it comes to finances, Aedes wants to stay profitable and keep head office costs 
low. For the Amstel project, one key KPI is the approval for refinancing, which will allow the 
Amstel project to stay on track. Another goal is to keep Operating Supplies & Equipment 
(OS&E) costs within 5% of the budget to control running costs. For De Durgerdam, a target of 
24% yearly revenue growth and a target of 170% YoY GOP growth, supports a strong and 
ambitious financial base for current and future growth trajectories at this property.  
Monthly P&L reviews at De Durgerdam are an important step to monitor costs and 
income regularly. These reviews will help De Durgerdam stay on budget and make quick 
adjustments if needed. Together, these financial KPIs give Aedes a full view of its financial 
health and cost control efforts. 
2.1.2 Customer Perspective 
 
When looking at the customer perspective, Aedes is mostly looking at metrics with 
regards to De Durgerdam. For this property the goals are for the most part what you would 
expect of a 5-star hotel. They want to obtain an 85% guest satisfaction in combination with 
retaining a minimum of 4,5 stars on rating platforms. This will lead to more repeat customers 
with them aiming to obtain a minimum of 15% repeat guests.  
2.1.3 Internal Process Perspective 
 
Here Aedes is looking to achieve an 80% compliance with the critical path for Amstel 
111. Ensuring no major delays here will have an incremental upside when looking at financial 
perspectives. This goal will coincide with the PoB, OS&E, and staffing guide being almost or 
fully complete by Q2, Q2, and Q3 respectively.

2.2 Evaluation of the HR Policy or Strategy 
 
Aedes' HR policy, even though formalized to a minimum (meaning there are no specific 
people hired for this role), contributes to achieving BSC objectives. By outsourcing HR 
functions (like end-of-year reviews) to limited interactions, Aedes supports financial and

internal process goals while maintaining efficiency. This evaluation examines these

2.2.1 Financial Perspective: Cost Management and GOP Growth 
 
Outsourcing essential HR tasks reduces overhead, directly enhancing financial 
performance. This approach supports GOP Growth by focusing resources on revenue-
generating activities. 
 
The Hospitality department's role in staffing further aligns with financial goals by 
consolidating HR responsibilities, maximizing efficiency, and ensuring recruitment 
accountability. Regular P&L reviews at properties like De Durgerdam reinforce the cost-

2.2.2 Internal Process Perspective: Manning Guide and P&D Roadmap

2.2.3 Reinforcement through Stakeholder Actions 
The Hospitality department's proactive development of the manning guide and P&D

Chapter 3: Leadership Analysis 
In this paragraph I am going to discuss two managers at Aedes. In order to talk about 
them objectively I am going to use the Situational Leadership Model (SLM) and Maxwell’s 5 
levels of leadership. 
3.1 Joost (Head of Hospitality) 
 
When looking at Joost's leadership style through the SLM, there is little doubt that he 
works most out of the delegating style. This means low directive and low supportive behavior 
where he gives his team considerable freedom to get on with things themselves, focusing on 
the result rather than the way something is done. The key to Joost's leadership style is the 
responsibility he places on each of his team members (aligned with the ethical standards and 
corporate values). This was a big change for me, as upon coming aboard he provided a laundry 
list of things for me to do and only checked in with me during our weekly meetings. These 
check-ins provide the possibility for his team members to update him about projects and any 
issues they encounter. His ability to delegate and stay on top through these regular weekly 
check-ins indicates confidence in his people and his belief in empowering his people to work 
independently.

Based on Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership, Joost seems to operate on a level between 
Level 4, People Development, and Level 5, Pinnacle. Joost is very interested in growing people

equips the team with what they need to do their jobs well. For instance, a couple of weeks ago,

completed within three days. He provided me with key information and directed me to other 
sources I may need. He expected me to create an Excel model myself. I did this within two 
days, he then reviewed my work, gave feedback on a few minor details, and finally approved it

team) and results (that were obtained).

3.2 Madelon (Head of Assets) 
 
From the SLM perspective on Madelon's style, at the very start of any working 
relationship (new subordinate), she first adopts a coaching and supporting approach. As 
Madelon explained herself, she works in a genuine coaching style (directive). This is supportive 
since she explains things step by step to ensure members have clarity in their heads on what 
needs to be delivered on a particular project. According to her, with each task that is completed 
confidence grows. When team members show competence, she automatically moves into a style 
where more delegation is done. A great example of this was on a project surrounding the 
refinancing of Amstel 111, whereby I worked with her on slides containing key calculations for 
a valuation. The slide presentation was crucial since it would be presented to the bank. Madelon 
gave me complete freedom in designing my slide and only afterward reviewed my work. This 

 
14 
was important for my growth, since for the first time, she shifted to a more delegating style, 
letting me work independently on an important component of the project. 
 
Max's 5 Levels of Leadership, Madelon's style fits Level 2, Permission, and Level 4, 
People Development. She is one of those highly committed to building strategic, trusted 
relationships with her team members, promoting openness in communication about work-
related, but also personal issues. This personal investment also provides the avenue whereby 
people share struggles and advances to maintain proximity to her about morale and 
performance. Her commitment to people's development shines brightly in how she has 
mentored her team members by providing professional guidance coupled with a genuine interest 
in growth and welfare for all of them. The relational and developmental blend that Madelon 
brings to her leadership helps to solidify the loyalty and trust within her team. 
3.3 Alignment with Aedes' Company Culture 
 
Aedes’ values are humble sustainability, relaxed hospitality, local commitment, diverse 
perspectives, self-empowerment, and passion (Aedes, 2024). Both Joost and Madelon reflect 
these values in their own way. Joost, with his focus on delegating and empowering team 
members, resembles strongly with self-empowerment. By entrusting his team with autonomy 
and providing minimal yet structured guidance, he allows them to take ownership of their work, 
cultivating confidence and independence. This approach also resonates with Aedes’ passion and 
commitment to diverse perspectives by allowing team members to approach tasks creatively 
using their own methods. 
 
Madelon’s leadership style mirrors Aedes’ values of local commitment and relaxed 
hospitality. Her emphasis on building personal connections and creating a supportive 
environment aligns with the company's vision of embracing a warm, inclusive workplace. 
Madelon’s coaching approach encourages openness, making team members feel valued both 
personally and professionally. Her focus on development ensures that each person feels invested 
in, aligning with Aedes' commitment to sustainability by nurturing long-term talent and growth.

Chapter 4: Sustainable Development 
Aedes is fully committed to connecting its projects with the UN Sustainable 
Development Goals (SDGs), I have observed that they focus particularly on Goal 11: 
Sustainable Cities and Communities and Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. 
They actively contribute to these goals and are devoted to moving them forward through new 
initiatives and sustainable practices. 
4.1 Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities 
“Creating Places with Lasting Relevance”- Aedes (2024) 
Aedes' view on sustainability is to "enhance urban environments by integrating eco-
friendly design and construction practices" (Aedes, 2024). This is in line with SDG 11, which 
aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable (United 
Nations, 2024). 
4.1.1 Key Achievements 
 
Aedes has (since the beginning) achieved significant milestones in integrating 
sustainability into its projects (Aedes, 2024a): 
- 
2011: The Aquifer Thermal Energy Storage system introduced in Burgerziekenhuis 
represented their concerns about energy consumption and sustainable building.  
- 
2012: The Sedum roofs and the ATES in Andaz Amsterdam brought amenity to energy 
efficiency and biodiversity-enabling sustainable urbanism. Aedes 2024a. 
- 
2017: Solar panels and landscape restoration work at the Landgoed Vels estate 
symbolize the high standards of sustainability, in that resilience in urban areas is 
promoted. 
- 
2018: An example of water resource management with the green-blue roof and 
Permavoid system that has already been installed in Soho House Amsterdam makes it 
more sustainable in respect to its urban infrastructure. 

 
16 
- 
2020: The zero-gas apartment building in Sarphatistraat 24 symbolizes their interest in 
carbon footprint reduction and energy-efficient residential construction, toward 
mitigation of urban pollution levels. 
- 
2021: The Mannoury smart roof project brings forth insight into sustainable roofing. 
This furthered their knowledge on implementing science to build resilient urban areas. 
4.1.2 Innovation Laboratory 
Aedes also has its innovation lab, Aedes Cares, which researches new concepts relating 
to sustainability. In this regard, it has support for glass recycling and advanced solar panels 
(Aedes, 2024a). These, therefore, add to SDG 11, as they reflect innovative responses towards 
sustainable urban living. 
4.1.3 Sustainability PoR 
Aedes' sustainability approach combines cutting-edge principles of energy performance 
and operation practices in all projects. This approach provides for and surpasses the minimum 
requirements of sustainability, therefore directly contributing to SDG 11 by ensuring that 
projects are sustainable and resilient. 
This can be noticed, for example, in Amstel 111—a project currently under construction 
right beside Carré. Here, I contributed by updating the sustainability PoR of Aedes which will 
be used for all future hotel developments. The ways Aedes categorizes sustainable 
achievements is as follows: 
- 
Core: Focus on reusing existing materials contributes to SDG11 by following 
sustainable construction methods respectful to the historical value of the building. 
- 
Extra: Amenities like native plants planting and insect hotel will add to more urban 
biodiversity, making the ecosystem of the urban resilient. 
- 
Innovation: Upcycling of materials and electric boat transport proves our concern for 
innovative solutions to sustainably support urban development. 
In summary, Aedes is committed to Goal 11—i.e., Sustainable Cities and 
Communities—through its innovative environmental-friendly initiatives. Aedes promotes 
sustainable urbanism and resilience in cities by the integration of systems such as ATES and 
Sedum roofs, pushing the envelope for such techniques as green-blue roofs and smart roofing. 
Further, their innovation lab and sustainability PoR showcase how they work on building long-
lasting and environmentally sustainable urban settings. Really bringing home their commitment 
to this SDG and sustainability in general.

4.2 Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production 
Their commitment to sustainability is not limited to a single SDG. For instance, they act 
on SDG 12 by facilitating the efficient use of resources and waste reduction across all functions 
of the business. United Nations 2024. 
4.2.1 The way Aedes Measures 
Aedes has developed a set of criteria (as mentioned in 4.1.3) for Core (the minimum), 
Extra (over and above), and Innovative (out of the ordinary) sustainability measures for its 
residences. Some examples of these are as follows: 
Core Measures 
- 
By using low-emission, FSC-certified material and designing demountable building 
elements, Aedes intends to minimize waste and ensure responsible material usage. 
- 
Air quality and material specification for a better living space and efficient use of 
resources.  
Additional Measures  
- 
Assuring a room is optimized for quality sleep is their pledge to sustainable 
consumption with better overall health and comfort. This is further evidenced by the 
fact that one of the USPs of Aedes is the promise of "best sleep ever". 
- 
Including blue roofs and high-quality materials use fosters environmental sustainability; 
in that respect, it enables responsible production and consumption. 
Innovative Measures 
- 
Upcycling of glass for windows, application of biobased materials reduces general 
waste, promotes the use of sustainable materials for SDG 12. 
- 
Zero-waste application and making sure of radical transparency serve as our reflections 
to efficient resource use and responsible means of production. 
To sum up, Aedes' innovative measures set the company apart as an industry leader in 
setting new norms on sustainability. These are aligned perfectly with SDG 12 and contribute to 
better operational efficiency and environmental stewardship, indicative of Aedes's commitment 
toward a more sustainable future.

4.3 Future Contributions 
If I were to give advice on Aedes’ sustainability practices, I would look forward to 
enhancing Aedes' contribution to SDG goals by: 
• More quantifiable measures could be developed and applied to capture sustainability 
aspects, such as reduction of carbon footprint, water savings and resources efficiency.
