Market Report 2022 | 2021 Q1-Q4
Germany | Hotel
Market overview
2021 proved another challenging year for the hotel business in the 
wake of record lows in 2020. Hotels continued to perform below the 
anticipated average, particularly in Germany’s top 7 cities. The second 
year of the pandemic proved especially challenging in cities that 
traditionally rely heavily on international travelers as well conferences 
and conventions. The average room rate continued to fall in a number 
of cities and across the country. While Munich, Cologne, Stuttgart and 
Düsseldorf brought in the highest room prices in 2019 (> €100), none 
of these cities managed to rank under the top 3 in 2021. 
Hotel market availability
Roughly 3.1 million beds were available at 50,715 establishments on 
the German commercial accommodation market in 2021. The number 
of open establishments was down 1.4% yoy and 2.7% compared to 
2019. Considerably more beds were on offer in 2021 compared to the 
previous year (+50%). This number, however, was down 2.9% 
compared to 2019. The change can be attributed to the second 
lockdown, which lasted into June 2021 in some sectors. High-capacity 
concepts in particular tended to stay closed longer. Entire floors at 
some hotels targeting business travelers in the country’s top 7 cities 
remained empty due to the shortage in demand.

Hotel market demand
A look at KPIs reflecting demand in Germany shows that 
business travel did not pick up in the second year of the 
pandemic. This is reflected in the fact that most guests tended 
to stay longer at their accommodation during the pandemic, 
which points to people traveling for personal reasons. The 
average annual duration of stay typically fell between 2.6 and 
2.8 days in the years leading up to the pandemic. This figure 
rose for the first time to 3.1 days in the first year of the 
pandemic. With the number of check-ins continuing to fall by 
1.3% in 2021 and the number of overnight stays increasing 
(+2.6%), the duration of stay continued to rise as well (0.1 days). 
Hotel garni establishments particularly saw an increase in 
demand in the second year of the pandemic (+4.4%) compared 
to 2020. The bed occupancy rate at these establishments was 
almost identical to that at full-service hotels.
We have been confronted time and again with surprises in the 
Germany-wide ranking of the country’s 10 most popular cities 
since the pandemic began. More and more German travelers 
are starting to turn to local options when planning their 
vacations. Cities near the coast like Lübeck, which made it into 
Germany’s top 10 most popular travel destinations in 2021, are 
some of the destinations benefiting from this trend.

Hotel performance
A look at key performance indicators clearly illustrates the 
extent to which the German hotel market has been impacted 
by recent events. Although the occupancy rate has improved 
somewhat compared to the first year of the pandemic 
(+1.9%), there is still a large gap between the current result of 
31.5% and that posted in the year prior to the pandemic 
(2019 occupancy rate: 72.1%).

The average room rate (ARR) dropped significantly across Germany 
compared to the first year of the pandemic by an additional €4, 
coming to €83 in 2021. 
The disparity on the markets during the pandemic is reflected in the 
fact that two cities located in eastern Germany, Erfurt and Rostock, 
rented out rooms at higher prices in 2021 than those being paid in 
luxury hotel locations like Munich and Düsseldorf.  
Not a single one of the country’s top 7 hotel locations managed to 
make it onto the roster when it came to average revenue per 
available room (RevPAR). Rostock posted the highest average 
revenue per available room at €47 followed by Erfurt and Kiel, each 
with €40. Lübeck took third place (€39). RevPAR in Berlin, 
Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, Hamburg, Cologne, Munich and Stuttgart 
proved considerably more modest, ranging from €18 to €28.

Outlook
The German hotel market was unable to keep pace with its pre-pandemic performance in 2021. The impact of the 
pandemic continued to be felt across all key indicators. 
Cities with extensive trade fair and conference offers as well as those that traditionally attract a large number of 
international visitors will experience a particularly long recovery phase. City destinations, however, remain high on 
traveler agendas. Accommodation establishments will need to make adjustments to address changes in guest 
requirements as well as in the nature of their message and what they offer.

Definitions
Accommodation establishment = The term accommodation establishment refers to the full spectrum of lodging 
offers. 
Accommodation establishments offer tourists and business travelers a place to spend the night.
Hotel
Hotels are accommodation establishments with a reception desk, services, daily room service, additional facilities 
and at least one restaurant for guests and visitors. 
A hotel typically has more than 20 rooms.
Hotel garni 
A hotel garni is a hotel that offers lodging, breakfast, beverages and, at most, snacks.
Bed occupancy rate
Bed occupancy rate refers to the ratio between the actual number of overnight stays and the number of 
overnight stays that is theoretically possible. The number of theoretically possible overnight stays is based on the 
number of available beds and the hotel’s days of operation.
Occupancy
Occupancy refers to the ratio between the number of booked rooms and total rooms available.
