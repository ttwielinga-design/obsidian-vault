Topic 1: Overlapping Roles
Can you map out your current responsibilities in each phase of the process?
If not mentioned: What do you see as your key task per phase?
If multiple roles:
How do you currently manage your roles if they overlap?
How do you prioritize tasks to ensure productivity (meeting deadlines is maximized without risking delays?
Have you experienced any instances where role overlaps caused delays or inefficiencies?
Can you describe any processes or tools that have been helpful in managing overlapping responsibilities without sacrificing productivity?
Would you recommend any changes in role distribution or processes to prevent delays caused by overlaps?
Topic 2: Accountability
How is accountability organized internally (e.g., deadlines)?
Are you given enough responsibility, support, and resources to fullfill your tasks succuesfully?
Are there any KPIs that you are currently being held to?
Are there specific KPIs aimed at tracking efficiency or avoiding bottlenecks that you’re accountable for?
Do you think these KPIs effectively measure (your) contribution to minimizing delays?
Can you describe the current sign off process? (for example, regarding DNR)
Do you see any chance for optimization here?
Who is responsible for each phase?
Are there clear criteria for when something is considered ready / signed-off or is it more ad-hoc?
What influence do you think this has on the process?
How many iterations of feedback should be allowed (before a final where everyone signs off)
Do you think the current accountability structure helps or hinders efforts to stay on schedule and maintain productivity
How would you change it?
Are there any gaps (defined as: tasks that fall in between current (job) functions) in accountability that might increase the risk of delays?
Accountability Across Departments:
How do you handle accountability when multiple departments or stakeholders are involved in the same phase? Do you think this impacts timelines or efficiency?
Are there any checks and balances in place to ensure accountability during critical phases where delays could have the most impact?
If not or not enough, what could these checks and balances be?
Topic 3 Long- vs Short-term
If you were to put all of your tasks on a timeline, do you think they are in general more long- or short-term oriented?
Where do you feel this orientation should be?
What’s stopping you from reaching that point?
What do you need to get to your desired spot?
Do you think more emphasis on long-term planning could reduce the risk of delays while increasing productivity? If so, how?
Do short-term pressures (such as meeting immediate deadlines) often take precedence over long-term planning?
How does this affect overall productivity?
What strategies do you think could help balance short-term deliverables with long-term goals to ensure both are met without compromising project timelines?

Topic 4 Ideal world scenario
In an ideal world scenario…
How would the sign off structure be implemented?
How would you design a sign-off process that minimizes delays while ensuring thoroughness?
Do you see any opportunities within your role to further minimize delays or enhance productivity?
What are the KPI’s each department should be held to?
How would you ensure accountability and adherence to agreements?
What specific changes would you make in terms of communication between departments to reduce the risk of delays?
When looking at the 5 phases: What risk mitigation strategies would you implement across all phases to maximize productivity?
If applicable: Looking at past projects, what were the most significant causes of delays? What solutions were implemented, and were they successful?
From your experience, what would you identify as the biggest 'productivity killers,' and how can they be avoided in future projects?

Interview Guide Structure

1| Introduction:
Purpose of the Interview:
Briefly explain that you are conducting a Business Process Improvement (BPI) project focusing on minimizing delays and maximizing productivity during the hotel exploitation process.
Highlight that you’re investigating how stakeholders coordinate, balance priorities (price, timeline, and quality), and ensure accountability.
Confidentiality & Consent:
Ensure the interviewee knows their responses will remain confidential and are to improve processes.

2| Stakeholder Roles and Responsibilities:
Key: "Can you describe your role in the project and your key responsibilities?"
"What do you see as the most critical tasks for you at each project phase?"
"How do you manage your different roles (if you wear multiple hats)? Do you face conflicts or difficulties due to these overlaps?"
Follow-up for Department Heads (e.g., Amber, Joost): "What is the extent of your authority in decision-making? When do you need to escalate decisions to the Managing Director?"

3| Balancing Price, Timeline, and Quality:
Key: "How do you balance the demands of price, timeline, and quality in your role?"
Follow-up for Development (Amber): "How do you ensure that your decisions are balanced between these three elements?"
Follow-up for Joost (Hospitality): "How do you push for higher quality without disrupting the timeline or increasing costs?"
Follow-up for Project Management: "How do you protect the timeline while responding to requests for higher quality or cost reductions?"
Follow-up for Asset Management: "How do you manage costs without negatively impacting quality or delaying the project?"

4| Accountability and the Sign-off Process:
Key: "Can you describe how the current sign-off process works?"
"Who is responsible for signing off at each stage of the process?"
"Are there clear criteria for when something is considered ready for sign-off, or is it more ad hoc?"
"How many iterations of feedback do you think should be allowed before a decision is final?"
Hypothetical Question: "In an ideal world, how would the sign-off process be structured to ensure efficiency while maintaining quality?"

5| Collaboration Between Departments:
Key: "How do you collaborate with other departments (Development, Project Management, Operations, Asset Management)?"
"Are there common points of miscommunication or misalignment between departments?"
"What do you think could improve the flow of information between departments?"
6| Risks and Delays:
Key: "Where do you see the most potential for delays in your role, and how do you mitigate these risks?"
"Do you think the current structure allows for proactive risk management, or do you feel more reactive?"
COSO Framework Follow-up (if relevant): "How do you currently identify and address risks, such as project delays or quality issues?"

7| Continuous Improvement and Accountability:
Key: "How do you think we can improve accountability in the current structure?"
"Is there a system in place for continuous improvement, or do improvements happen more reactively?"
"What type of feedback loop would help prevent issues from recurring?"
8| Closing Questions:
Key: "Is there anything else you would like to add about how we can improve stakeholder engagement and minimize delays?"
"What would be your ideal vision for the future structure of accountability and decision-making within Aedes?"
