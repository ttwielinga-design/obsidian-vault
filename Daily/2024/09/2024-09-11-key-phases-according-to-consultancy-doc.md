Key Phases to Understand:
Feasibility Study, Business Case, Concept (Phase One):
This involves initial site analysis, including reviewing land boundaries, topography, rights of way, and compliance with local codes.
During this phase, consultants (e.g., architects, interior designers, MEP, landscape, security, and lighting consultants) prepare conceptual designs, sketches, and project schedules. They also assess operational functionality and budget alignment.
Key Stakeholder Role: Amber (Development) oversees site analysis, space planning, and cost estimation. Coordination with various consultants is key to ensuring the concept meets Hyatt’s standards and aligns with the developer’s budget and timelines.
Design & Licensing (Phase Two):
The consultants attend operational briefings to familiarize themselves with the project’s requirements, including space planning, material selection, and code compliance.
The schematics phase confirms the preliminary project design, focusing on space functionality, building systems, and materials selection. Consultants also estimate construction costs and establish timelines.
Key Stakeholder Role: Amber and the team focus on finalizing designs and ensuring coordination among consultants, while obtaining necessary permits and approvals. This phase requires close alignment between Development (Amber) and Operations (Joost) for ensuring designs support operational efficiency.
Contractor Selection (Phase Three):
The design development phase builds on schematic designs to finalize the project’s scope, materials, and systems. Consultants prepare detailed specifications and coordinate the work of all other consultants.
Key Stakeholder Role: Amber’s role involves ensuring alignment between design and budget while maintaining project feasibility. This phase might involve advising on any changes needed to maintain project costs or timelines.
Construction and Quality Control (Phase Four & Five):
Consultants prepare construction documents and participate in contractor selection by issuing tender packages and analyzing received bids.
During construction, they monitor progress, review shop drawings, and ensure quality control through site inspections.
Key Stakeholder Role: Amber will focus on ensuring designs are followed, while Joost and the Operations team will need to ensure that construction meets operational needs, particularly in areas like layout, equipment, and overall functionality.
Important Questions to Keep in Mind:
How does Amber’s Development team align its work with Joost’s Operations team during the early phases?
Where have there been challenges in communication or coordination across these phases?
What processes can be improved to reduce delays in approvals, budgeting, or design finalization?
