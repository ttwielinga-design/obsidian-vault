Part 1: Process Identification
Objective: When insourcing the entire hotel building process from start to open process: How do you minimize risk (delays) and maximize gains (productivity) from a hospitality perspective?
Identified Process for Improvement: Stakeholder engagement and coordination during each phase of the hotel exploitation process, which includes:
Phase 1: Feasibility Study, Business Case, Concept
DNR: IH, PD
Phase 2: Design & Licensing
DNR: SO, VO, DO, TO
Phase 3: Contractor(s) Selection
DNR: PC
Phase 4: Construction and Quality Control
DNR: UO, Uitvoering, Exploitatie
Phase 5: Project Delivery, Soft/Pre-opening and Closure
Stakeholders:
Development (Amber, Rowin)
Project Management (Rowin, Jeroen, Wesley)
Operations (Wesley, Ajar, Joost)
Asset Management (Madelon)
Goal: To improve the timing and effectiveness of stakeholder involvement, ensuring that their contributions align with the phases to avoid time delays.

Part 2: Process Discovery
Purpose: Identify when stakeholders should be involved, what roles they play, and where miscommunication or bottlenecks occur.
Methodology:
Conduct interviews with stakeholders: Amber, Rowin, Jeroen, Wesley, Madelon, Ajar, and Joost.
Map out the current process flow and stakeholder activities in each phase.
Key focus questions:
When should each stakeholder be most active?
What are the major communication breakdowns and inefficiencies?
How are responsibilities divided, and where is accountability unclear?
What are the current KPI's
What do you perceive as the biggest bottleneck throughout the process in general?
Use a process map to document:
Start and end points of each phase.
Key tasks and decision points.
Timeline of stakeholder involvement.
Points where tasks run in parallel or overlap.

Part 3: Process Analysis
Analysis Focus:
Evaluate the current process map to identify bottlenecks, communication gaps, and delays.
Analyze interview data to understand which tasks or phases suffer from inefficiencies.
Risk analysis (use COSO and RI&E frameworks) to pinpoint:
Project delays.
Quality control issues.
Communication breakdowns
Key Questions for Analysis:
Where are the most common delays?
What steps are redundant or unnecessary?
Which phases contribute most to bottlenecks or miscommunication?
How are decisions affecting project timelines?
Part 4: Process Redesign
Goal: Redesign the process to align with stakeholder needs, improve communication, eliminate bottlenecks, and save time.
Redesign Strategies:
Propose clearer communication protocols.
Establish phased feedback loops (PDCA) to ensure regular reviews and adjustments.
Introduce accountability measures to ensure all tasks have owners.
Develop a streamlined process for each stakeholder to engage at the right time.
KPI Examples:
Time saved in decision-making phases.
Reduction in project delays.
Improved communication flow and responsiveness.
Redesign Outcome: The process should achieve resource savings (time and effort), improve productivity, and prevent project delays.

Part 5: Process Implementation
Pilot Testing:
Test the redesigned process with a focus on communication improvements and stakeholder engagement.
Monitor the impact on project timelines and stakeholder satisfaction.
Quality Assurance: Conduct tests to ensure the new process functions as intended, and address unforeseen issues before full implementation.
Implementation Strategy:
Ensure phased introduction of redesigned tasks.
Implement checklists and transition plans to guide stakeholders.
Ensure clarity in the roles and timelines to avoid rework.
Part 6: Process Monitoring and Controlling:
Monitoring Tools:
Set up regular reviews with stakeholders using KPIs.
Create a feedback loop to continuously monitor the process, adjusting when necessary.
Evaluate the effectiveness of the implemented process over time, ensuring it adapts to new challenges.
KPI Review Process:
Regular assessment of project delays, communication breakdowns, and productivity metrics.
Adjust the process based on ongoing performance data and feedback from stakeholders.
