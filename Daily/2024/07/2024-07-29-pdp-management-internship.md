# Your internship journey is about to begin!
Look how far you have come! Your time at HMSM is soon about to come to an end. In these last two modules, you have the chance to explore a part of the hospitality industry, gain new business insights, acquire expertise know-how, build on your core qualities, face a few personal pitfalls, expand your professional network, discover (in)effective leadership, collaborate with a variety of positions and personalities ….. and you can only wonder what other opportunities might occur.
We invite you to think big, aim high and be humble at the same time. This is the moment to seize opportunities for learning in a safe environment. Dare to ask! Nonetheless, remember that you are there to gain competence and acquire knowledge in the organization/department chosen. Be curious, openminded and willing to learn from others. Commit yourself to add value to the team, lead by example and show genuine and correct work ethics.
A couple of weeks before you start your internship, we ask you to put the ambitions you have for this internship on paper by means of defining clear learning and development goals. This Personal Development Plan template is designed to help you do that and functions as a conversation starter for the meeting with your HMSM internship supervisor before the start of your internship and with your company mentor once you’ve started.
As this internship is the final chapter of your education at HMSM, we expect you to show your readiness for graduation and take ownership of your personal, professional and educational development. By the end of your internship, you will need to demonstrate that you master the learning outcomes as described in the National Dutch qualifications profile (PEP) and that you are ready to receive your Funda! For more information, click here.
You are in the driver’s seat and you get to determine the course of your internship journey. Your dedicated HMSM internship supervisor and your company mentor will be at the sideline for support.
Fasten your seatbelt and enjoy the ride!


Instructions
1. Contact your HMSM internship supervisor and make an appointment to discuss your Personal Development Plan (PDP). The appointment should take place before the start of your internship.
2. Follow the instructions of this document and upload your completed Personal Development Plan and a completed Internship Timeline (Moodle) in OnStage before your appointment with your HMSM internship supervisor
4. Process the feedback received from your HMSM internship supervisor and upload a new version in OnStage
5. Discuss your PDP with your company mentor within the first two weeks of your internship. Following this meeting, you may still need to make adjustments to your PDP. Please note that all adjustments will need to be vetted by your HMSM internship supervisor. A final version of your PDP should be uploaded in OnStage.

In the preparation phase of your internship you have investigated a number of topics that were important to take into account in your decision making process of determining your internship preferences. A copy of this form (Internship Preparation form) is already uploaded in your OnStage profile.





Your personal development plan continues building on the insights you gained back then. Therefore, keep your Internship Preparation form at hand while completing this document and consistently refer back to what you wrote down in the various paragraphs. 

To help you arrive at relevant learning goals that will help you fulfill your dreams and aspirations, we highly recommend you to complete the entire form, starting with establishing the foundation first (chapter one). Alternatively, should you have enough clarity to determine what your learning goals need to be, you can move on to creating your development goals (chapter two) of this Personal Development Plan directly and continue to complete chapter three Managing Expectations too. Should your internship supervisor determine that your learning goals are of insufficient quality, you might be requested to complete chapter one after all.

## Part three: Managing expectations
## (H) Contribution to the company (what’s in it for the company?)
Which HMSM value and what behavior in particular that belongs to this value will you live while performing your internship that will add value to your internship company? Justify your answer.


(I) Business process improvement report
Next to producing a final report (written or video) towards the end of your internship, you need to carry out a small scale project. This project is related to your daily activities at work and should improve a departmental process following the guidelines as written in the student instruction (posted on Moodle). As the schedule is very tight, we ask you to write down what you need to focus on in order to bring this project to a successful end. Think about questions such as What actions/tasks/steps do you need to undertake during this project? What will most likely be easy for you? What do you consider to be difficult for you? What challenges can you foresee? Which of your qualities can you utilize to tackle some of the tasks? What competencies do you need to address and develop to stand up to the challenges that you foresee and/or complete some of the actions/tasks/steps?

(J) Expectations from the internship supervisor 
During the internship you will be supervised by a teacher who will coach you and assess you during the internship. (S)He will be there to help you should you encounter any problems during your internship. Clarify what you expect from your HMSM internship supervisor and what (s)he can expect from you.

Should there be any specific circumstances that (will) apply to you that might have an impact on you/ your internship, please consider sharing this with your internship supervisor as these circumstances could impact the success of your internship and the support/coaching you might need.
(K) Expectations from the internship 
You will start your internship in a company, where you will become part of the daily operation quickly and you will get to know the colleagues and managers you will be working with. What are your expectations of your internship, in terms of content, coaching and support? Ensure to discuss these during your first week of the internship with your company mentor.
