In this paragraph I am going to talk about two managers at the Aedes. In order to talk about them objectively I am going to use the situational leadership model and Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership.

Joost

It is clear from a Situational Leadership Model (SLM) perspective that Joost practices mainly a delegating style of leadership—that is, low directive and low supportive behavior. The degree of autonomy Joost gives to his subordinates shows more concern for results than for methods. His approach is based on giving responsibilities to his subordinates. From my experience, this was quite a shift, as he gave me a detailed task list at the time of joining and checked in on the progress only during the weekly meetings that we have. I feel that this approach allows each member to update him regarding project progress and raises any issues they may be facing. His ability to delegate effectively yet maintain control through regular weekly check-ins speaks much about his trust in his team and his belief in growing independence at work.

Using Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership to evaluate Joost, one would find him operating between Level 4, People Development, and Level 5, Pinnacle. Joost is heavily invested in his people's growth and success, setting high yet attainable goals with resources to help them achieve those goals. For example, a couple of weeks ago, he assigned me the task of calculating linen needs for two hotel properties, with a strict deadline of three days. He initially provided the core information and directed me to additional resources, giving me the freedom to build an Excel model independently. He then went through my work, gave me some minor feedback, and approved it immediately with the supplier after I completed the task in two days—exactly showing a strong commitment to development and results.

Joost's leadership style also gives indications of him becoming a Level 5 leader. His style of treating team members as equals and giving them freedom breeds a culture of respect, and he is after all relatively young, which implies that he is still in the development trajactory of his career. As he continues to hone his style, I have no doubt that Joost is on his way to becoming a Pinnacle leader—someone who will inspire through respect and by his dedication to team development.

Madelon

Using the Situational Leadership Model to evaluate Madelon's style, she typically falls within the coaching and supporting styles, especially in the initial stages of a working relationship. In her coaching style (high directive high supportive), the way she explains in detail what has to be done step-by-step ensures that there is no confusion over the project's requirements. As she notices increased confidence and competence in the team members, she begins to adapt a delegating approach. A major one was during the refinancing of the Amstel 111 project, wherein I worked with her on preparing slides containing some major calculations for a valuation. This slide show was very important because it was to be presented to the bank. Madelon gave me complete freedom to design my slide and just reviewed it later. It was quite instrumental in my growing process, for it was the first time she had moved to a more delegating style and trusted me to manage an important part of the project by myself.

Regarding Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership, Madelon's practice falls under Level 2 (Permission) and Level 4 (People Development). She is very committed to building strong, trusting relationships with her staff members, encouraging open communication on both work-related matters and personal well-being. That personal investment fosters an environment where people will be comfortable sharing challenges and progress with her, hence allowing her to really keep her finger on the pulse of the morale and performance of the team.
Her commitment to development shows up in how she mentors her team members, balancing professional guidance with real concern for their growth and welfare. In so doing, she epitomizes the relational and developmental focus of leadership that Madelon brings and further solidifies the loyalty and trust within her team.

Alignment with Aedes' Company Culture
Aedes’ values are humble sustainability, relaxed hospitality, local commitment, diverse perspectives, self-empowerment, and passion. Both Joost and Madelon reflect these principles in their own way. Joost, with his focus on delegating and empowering team members, aligns strongly with self-empowerment. By entrusting his team with autonomy and providing minimal yet structured guidance, he allows them to take ownership of their work, cultivating confidence and independence. This approach also resonates with Aedes’ passion and commitment to diverse perspectives by allowing team members to approach tasks creatively and with their own methods.

Madelon’s leadership style mirrors Aedes’ values of local commitment and relaxed hospitality. Her emphasis on building personal connections and creating a supportive environment aligns with the company's vision of fostering a warm, inclusive workplace. Madelon’s coaching approach encourages openness, making team members feel valued both personally and professionally. Her focus on development ensures that each person feels invested in, aligning with Aedes' commitment to sustainability by nurturing long-term talent and growth.

Personal Reflection
From Joost, I want to adopt his ability to empower team members by providing both freedom and accountability. His trust in others inspires a sense of responsibility, which I believe is crucial to have as a leader aiming to cultivate a proactive, motivated team. Adopting his approach would help me create an environment where colleagues feel encouraged to take initiative and where their unique contributions are valued.

Madelon’s focus on personal relationships is also something I want to incorporate. Her ability to connect with her team on both personal and professional levels fosters a sense of belonging and openness. In my own leadership journey, I aim to create an environment where team members feel genuinely supported and seen, building a foundation of trust that motivates them to bring their best selves to work. Adopting Madelon’s relational focus would align with my goal of building not only a productive team but also one where every individual feels valued and empowered.

Balanced Scorecard Evaluation and Explanation
The main goals, measures (KPIs), and actions of the Balanced Scorecard (BSC) have been planned to meet Aedes' 2025 targets. The BSC covers five areas: Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth, and Sustainability. Each area has at least two goals, clear KPIs, and actions to help Aedes reach its main goals. Below is an overview of the financial performance and how two main goals support the company’s objectives.

Financial Performance Review
In the Financial area, Aedes aims to stay profitable and keep head office costs low. For the Amstel project, one key KPI is the approval for refinancing, which will allow the Amstel project to stay on track. Another goal is to keep Operating Supplies & Equipment (OS&E) costs within 5% of the budget to control running costs. For De Durgerdam, a target of 24% yearly revenue growth and a target of 170% YoY GOP growth, supports a strong and ambitious financial base for current and future projects.
Monthly P&L reviews at De Durgerdam are an important step to monitor costs and income regularly. These reviews will help De Durgerdam stay on budget and make quick adjustments if needed. Together, these financial KPIs give Aedes a full view of its financial health and cost control efforts.

Contributions to Key Goals

Goal 1: Improve Guest Experience and Brand Reputation
In the Customer area, the focus is on raising guest satisfaction and loyalty at De Durgerdam. My role in this goal included helping look over post-stay surveys and follow-ups, which help gather useful feedback from guests. We also encouraged reviews on TripAdvisor and Google, aiming for a 4.5+ star rating, to boost the hotel’s reputation. These actions support our goal of 85% guest satisfaction and a 15% increase in repeat guests. By focusing on feedback and reviews, we’re building a brand that looks to listen to its guests and makes them feel valued, helping De Durgerdam retain loyal customers.

Goal 2: Promote Innovation and Sustainability
In the Learning and Growth area, Aedes is committed to sustainability and team engagement. I supported this goal by writing and updating the PoR for sustainability. In this way I tried to contribute to the Sustainability team that working towards the Green Globe certification for Amstel 111. This team’s work on biodiversity, CO2 reduction, and circularity ensures Aedes meets its long-term sustainability goals. By promoting environmental responsibility and personal development, Aedes builds a motivated workforce aligned with its sustainability goals.
