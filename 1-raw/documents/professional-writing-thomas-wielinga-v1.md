**"Sustainability and Seating: The Key to Penta Hotel Wiesbaden's Brand Reputation and Revenue Growth"**

Currently, Penta hotel Wiesbaden is facing challenges. To try to keep up with the competition we have identified some areas that Penta can work on. Penta has outdated conference rooms that have not been properly renovated since 2008. Furthermore, they do not have enough seating area in their restaurant to host dinners for the larger banquets that they have. Knowing these struggles we looked in the confrontation matrix to see if we can find a way to get a deeper understanding of this issue and what opportunities and threats could help us attack these areas. We formulated the following question: What are the potential strategic implications for Penta's brand reputation due to the lack of proper refurbishment of its meeting rooms, and the absence of sustainable practices?[^1] What are potential issues for Penta when looking at the restaurant space and their competitive set?[^2] The reason why these are the issues for Penta are as followed: <span class="mark">The lack of proper refurbishment of Penta's meeting rooms since 2008, coupled with the absence of sustainable practices, can have significant strategic implications for the hotel's brand reputation and customer satisfaction.</span> <span class="mark">Penta's lack of seating area in the restaurant for larger banquets is a strategic issue that can hinder their ability to attract larger conventions and corporate guests, and ultimately affect their revenue as competitors within their set are able to get higher ADR while maintaining the same occupancy as Penta.</span>

Like aforementioned Penta has not done a proper refurbishment of the meeting rooms since 2008. We came about this data by talking to management during our visit to Wiesbaden. What is also noteworthy is that from our review analysis, we found that there are six reviews about the fact that the air-conditioning is old and noisy in the living room area and the conference areas.

The reason why sustainability is an opportunity for Penta comes from our STEEP analysis. We found that Wiesbaden is trying to reduce its carbon footprint and that they have great initiatives for it like the “Ökoprofit”. This is an initiative of 127 companies and institutions working together (The Environment & Economy in Wiesbaden, n.d.).

Not paying attention to sustainability when looking at conference rooms is an issue because it can have a negative impact on the environment and society. Conference rooms in hotels are often used for large gatherings and events, which can result in a significant amount of waste and energy consumption. For example, at Penta, there are a lot of disposable products like plates, cups, and utensils being used, they can end up in landfills and contribute to pollution. Similarly, if excessive energy is used to power lighting and air conditioning, it can lead to unnecessary carbon emissions and contribute to climate change. “Generally, lighting will consume from 20% until 50% of the electricity consumption” (Wan Norsyafizan, 2010).

Furthermore, in a 2022 article, Mazumder discusses sustainability as essential for building a strong brand. He writes that providing excellent products and services is a fundamental aspect of running a business, but in the 21st century, a company's reputation is also assessed based on its efforts to contribute to social betterment. As sustainability is becoming more critical, people are no longer relying solely on public organizations, and are instead expecting their preferred brands to take the initiative in bringing about positive social change (Mazumder et al., 2022).

By looking into sustainable practices within their conference rooms, Penta can not only reduce their negative impact on the environment but also demonstrate their commitment to responsible business practices. This can help attract environmentally conscious customers and differentiate the hotel from its competitors.

Another issue facing Penta is that they do not have enough seating area in their restaurant for their larger banquets. In the largest meeting room that they have they can fit three hundred people, whilst in their restaurant they can currently only fit two hundred and forty. This leaves the hotel with, when that meeting rooms is filled, with sixty guests that cannot have lunch or dinner at the restaurant. The way this is sometimes solved is by having lunch in shits instead of all the guests at once. However, this is highly impractical. This information was also given to us during the tour we got of Penta during our visit one month ago.

The threat attached to this weakness is that the competitors within their competitive set are able to get a higher ADR whilst maintaining the same occupancy as Penta. This was found in our market analysis that we did using STR data[^3].

The has a slightly better occupancy rate when compared to its competitors. This indicates that Penta has been successful in attracting customers and filling its rooms. However, this was at the cost of the ADR, which is lagging behind its competitors by 9.44%. This means that Penta's room rates are relatively lower than its competitors, which can be an obstacle in generating higher revenue per available room (RevPAR). In fact, Penta's RevPAR is only 1.78% higher than its competitive set, which is not at all a significant margin.

The reason why this is a strategic issue is that right now Penta’s bottle neck is not the rooms or the conferences but the fact that they do not have enough space for their corporate guests to sit when they have large conferences. This is one of the reasons the larger conventions do not want to go to Penta. After all, they feel Penta cannot accommodate them because of their lackluster restaurant area. They would much rather go to a different hotel that does have this capacity, like the H+ hotel. They have room for six hundred guests on sight (Tagungshotel Wiesbaden \| Seminare Und Feiern \| H-Hotels.com, n.d.).
