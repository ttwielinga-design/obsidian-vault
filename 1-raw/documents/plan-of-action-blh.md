# Chapter 1: Management Question

After having secured multiple multi-unit development plans with the big six hotel chains (Marriott, Accor, Hilton, Wyndham, Hyatt, and IHG) Black Label Hospitality (BLH) is looking to expand towards the east, namely Germany. BLH aims to enhance its white label positioning to secure more suitable and feasible projects in Germany, mainly focusing on the ‘Big 7’ cities (Berlin, Düsseldorf, Frankfurt, Hamburg, Cologne, Munich, and Stuttgart). While also keeping an eye on the smaller ‘B’ and ‘C’ cities in Germany, being open to exploring these potential hotel development spots.

The challenge for BLH lays in understanding their positioning in the market and the way they are perceived by stakeholders, developers, private equity parties, and their competitors alike. Identifying ways in which they can improve their competitive advantage and how to grow fast in a sustainable fashion. Making sure to keep in mind the differences between the Dutch and German markets. To effectively analyze the situation, it is crucial to investigate:

- The presence and positioning of partner brands in the target cities,

- Potential niches and/or dual-branding opportunities,

- Differences in market dynamics between the Netherlands and Germany, and

- The structure and focus of development departments within the partner hotel chains.

The management question thus becomes: **How can BLH optimize its strategy for entering and expanding in the German market to enhance its white label positioning?**

The goal of this project is to develop a nuanced understanding of the German hospitality market, leading to a strategic entry and expansion plan. This includes a ranking of optimized product-market combinations based on detailed research and analysis, brand presence, niche opportunities, and dual-branding potentials.

## 2.1 Things we currently know 

### 2.1.1 White Label Views 

According to Mr. Schöningh, Accor isn’t keen to sign a **step-in clause** (Schöningh, 2024). He has only signed two step-in clauses while working as the director of hotel development at Accor hotels Netherlands (Schöningh, 2024). We currently think that some hotel brands are keener to give out this clause than others (Schöningh, 2024). Some hotel brands offer these clauses and others do not (Hendricks, 2024; Schöningh, 2024). For White Label Operators (WLO’s) and the property owners a step-in clause is ideal (Peeters, 2024). This is because the property owner has the security that the property is always being operated on (i.e. step-in) (Peeters, 2024). This guaranty allows the rent or security deposit for the WLO’s to likely be lower.

Another important WLO-aspect is **key money.** This is ideal for a WLO operator, since the first few months of the hotel opening may be tough (Peeters, 2024). Hotels in that stage often generate a negative cashflow (Van Erp, 2024). Thus, choosing a franchisor that offers key money might be a strategic move for a WLO. (Van Erp, 2024) According to Mr. Schöningh, he only offers it to exclusive brands in key locations (Schöningh, 2024). In paragraphs two and three we will go into other interviews that we have scheduled in the near future to guarantee a well-rounded perspective on this topic.

Next, **turnkey** **leasing** is ideal for WLO since you lease including FFE (Van Erp, 2024). Meaning that the property owner is responsible for acquiring the FFE and maintaining the quality of the FFE (Van Erp, 2024). The one issue can be that the property owner does not maintain the FFE up to the WLO Franchisor brand standards (Van Erp, 2024). This needs to be described well into the leasing agreement since the asset can be sold to a different party (Van Erp, 2024).

WLO demonstrates its commitment to property owners and franchisors through long-term **lease agreements**. These agreements, referred to as "turnkey leases," typically range from 20 to 50 years in duration. However, it is important to note that the maximum legal lease term in Germany is 30 years (Baker McKenzie Resource Hub, n.d.).

### 2.1.2 Legal and Labor contracts

According to Krug (2020) hospitality is an essential economic factor in Germany and is one of the largest employers. Hospitality jobs are one of Germanys most popular skilled occupation (Krug, 2020).

Germany has a lengthy and hard process of starting up businesses, and the process takes a substantial amount of time and money (Globalization Partners, 2021). Furthermore, the bureaucracy in Germany is known to be long and a lot of work on both national and local levels (Globalization Partners, 2021). Germany also has one of the strongest employee protection laws in the world, making it important for the employer to be aware if and apply (Globalization Partners, 2021). Although the laws are very strict, it does provide a clear guide for employers on how to handle aspects such as dismissal, benefits, and entitlements (Troadec & Horizons, 2024). The laws include strict rules on working hour limits, holidays and maternity leave (Troadec & Horizons, 2024).

### 2.3.2 Niche Markets

Because of the dynamic and growing hotel market in Germany, several niche markets are emerging (Chappel, 2022). **A conversion brand** is a scenario where owners of existing hotel properties switch from hotel brand (Burnett, 2024). These changes are frequently facilitated by a parent company, to expand its footprint without constructing new hotels from scratch (Burnett, 2024). Rebranding under a different brand could allow the company to quickly establish a presence in new markets (Burnett, 2024).

Collaborating brands can develop, market and distribute together, this is called **dual branding** (Ma et al., 2023). This strategic approach balances market positioning and brand identity. There are various ways dual branding can be executed (Ma et al., 2023).

- **Brand Alliances**: Hotel brands with different starting positions work together to appeal to other target audiences (Ma et al., 2023).

- **Shared Facilities** can result in cost savings and synergies, this could mean sharing a restaurant, conference rooms or wellness (Ma et al., 2023).

- **Cross-promotion**: By conducting joint marketing initiatives Brand hotel A could offer discounts on the restaurant of Brand hotel B (Ma et al., 2023).

- **Expanding customer base:** Dual branding could lead hotels to reach new customer segments (Ma et al., 2023).

Other brands that are seeing more-and-more attention are:

- **Service Apartments**: With the rise of demand for extended stays like service apartments and accommodations, travelers seek a blend of hotel services and residential comfort (Chappel, 2022). Fully furnished apartments offer flexibility and amenities for both business and leisure guests (Chappel, 2022).

- **Eco-Friendly and Sustainable Hotels**: With global growth on environmental consciousness, hotels prioritizing sustainability attract environmentally conscious travelers (Ploppa, 2022).

- **Digital Nomad-Friendly Spaces**: Due to the pandemic remote working is becoming more common (Ploppa, 2022). Hotels that offer co-working spaces, high-speed internet and comfortable workstations attract digital nomads (Ploppa, 2022).

### 2.3.3 Optimize Agreements and how BLH Should Choose Brands

Contract optimization is a crucial aspect for maximizing value and minimizing risk in agreements, this benefits all parties involved (Juro, 2022). There are various strategies to optimize these agreements:

- **Engage with stakeholders**: Understand their interests and needs. This (possibly) time-consuming method offers opportunities to collaborate with relevant parties during the drafting of address budget constraints or legal to alleviate contractual risk (Project Central, 2023).

- **Automate Contract Optimization**: Contract automation tools like Juro (Juro, 2022). These templates allow you to standardize contract clauses which could streamline the process (Juro, 2022).

- **Conditional Logic within contracts**: Ensuring consistency and efficiency, this refers to the ability to automate processes based on specific data or conditions (Team & Team, 2023). Benefits are reducing administrative work, efficient contract drafting, and it improves the user experience (Team & Team, 2023). Users get more relevant content based on selections and automated rules and clauses save legal teams (valuable) time (Team & Team, 2023).

- **Optimal terms**: Negotiate in favor of business considering profit distribution and remove bottlenecks[^1] hindering efficiency (Furoore, 2024).

# Chapter 3: Professional Product

The final product is based on quantitative analyses from STR reports of “Big 7” cities, as well as qualitative research conducted through interviews with property owners, developers, hotel brands and hotel consultants. The focus lies in the new development and the turnkey.

**The focus is on aligning BLH’s strategy with the right brands in the optimal respective locations, ensuring an optimal match**. The strategic vision of BLH will be harmonized with unique attributes and positioning of the brands. By evaluating the compatibility between brands, stakeholders, and properties, we aim to create successful advice for long-lasting partnerships.

# Chapter 4: Methodology and Analysis for BLH's Expansion

In the following chapter the data collection methods will be conducted, and how they will be executed and why they were chosen.

1.  **Interviews:** The stakeholders listed in the project scope will be the subject of structured interviews. The purpose of these interviews is to retrieve in-depth information on the development procedures, hotel chains' selection standards for operators, and different points of view on third-party operating markets (Verhoeven, 2019).

2.  **Case Studies and Reviews of Secondary Literature:** The basic data will be augmented with case studies of comparable expansions and a thorough analysis of outside literature, which will offer BLH's plan context and benchmarking possibilities (Blumberg et al., 2014).

3.  **STR data analysis: **This data refers to the hotel market in Germany and its developments. Looking at supply, demand, RevPAR, and other KPI's.

## **4.1 Data Collection Methods:**

A mixed-method analysis will be performed on the gathered data, integrating the qualitative insights allowing for a thorough grasp of the market dynamics, stakeholder viewpoints, and positioning opportunities (Blumberg et al., 2014).

1.  **Quantitative examination:** By providing a quantitative basis, the examination of STR-data will aid in comprehending market trends, occupancy rates, and competitive analysis among the "Big 7" cities. Also allows further understanding of where potential opportunities lie in the market (Blumberg et al., 2014).

<!-- -->

2.  **Qualitative Analysis:** A broader analysis of the interview responses will reveal recurring themes, problems raised by stakeholders, and possible approaches that BLH could use to enter and grow its market. Investigating market niches, stakeholder expectations, and dual-branding potential are some examples of this (Blumberg et al., 2014).

## 4.2 Execution for Methodical Thoroughness

To guarantee the comprehensiveness of the interviewing process:

- **Getting interview guides ready:** customized for every stakeholder group to guarantee thorough covering of subjects pertinent to BLH's strategic goals (Blumberg et al., 2014).

- **Team training:** Making sure that all team members have the knowledge and ability to formulate precise, concise questions to enable everyone to conduct interviews.

- **Strict data administration:** interviewing, transcribing, coding, and closely examining data to fully grasp the breadth of insights offered by stakeholders (Blumberg et al., 2014).

To ensure a detailed security data analysis:

- **Getting required data:** ensuring all the relevant STR data is already procured before the start of this chapter is essential (Verhoeven, 2019).

- **Ensuring timelines:** having the data be relevant and topical is essential for ensuring a good data analysis.

- **Close data monitoring:** making sure all data that is produced is readable and understandable.

To guarantee the literature review's thoroughness and relevance:

1.  **Searching and Synthesis: **The process of reviewing the literature will be done iteratively. First, important terms about BLH's management question will be defined. Next, searches in pertinent databases will be conducted. Finally, the results will be assessed and synthesized (Blumberg et al., 2014).

2.  **Critical Evaluation:** To make sure that the strategy is informed exclusively by the most relevant and excellent literature, each source will be evaluated critically for its authority, relevance, and contribution to the subject (Blumberg et al., 2014).

3.  **Integration with Primary Research:** To provide a comprehensive picture of the market and strategic insights, the results of the qualitative interviews and the analysis of the STR report will be combined with the insights from the literature research (Blumberg et al., 2014).

## 4.3 Justification for Chosen Methods

The selection of these methods is driven by the exploratory nature of BLH's expansion objectives, the complexity of the hospitality market in Germany, and the need for a nuanced understanding of (key) stakeholder perceptions and market dynamics.

- **In-depth Stakeholder Insights**: Direct interviews with a broad range of stakeholders offer unparalleled depth of understanding, critical for navigating the complexities of market entry and expansion (Verhoeven, 2019).

- **Market Specificity**: The combined use of quantitative and qualitative data provides a balanced view, allowing BLH to tailor its strategies to the nuances of the German market, particularly when looking at its hotel brand positioning (Blumberg et al., 2014).

- **Strategic Alignment**: This approach aligns with BLH's need for strategic advice based on a thorough analysis of market conditions, stakeholder expectations, and competitive landscapes (Blumberg et al., 2014).

Further, adding a literature review enriches the methodology by providing a foundation grounded in existing academic and practical insights (Verhoeven, 2019).

- **Theoretical and Empirical Insights: **Provides a solid foundation for strategic decision-making by enhancing understanding of market dynamics, competitive strategies, and brand positioning using theoretical frameworks and empirical research (Blumberg et al., 2014).

- **Gap Identification: **Points out areas for further research as well as fresh chances for distinctiveness and a competitive edge, directing BLH toward creative strategies for entering and growing new markets (Blumberg et al., 2014).

- **Strategic Depth:** Enhances the research's depth and rigor and guarantees that BLH's strategy is based on a comprehensive grasp of market entry tactics and hospitality management techniques (Blumberg et al., 2014).

By employing these methodologies, we aim to craft a robust strategy for its entry and expansion into the German market, grounded in a detailed understanding of the operational landscape and stakeholder perspectives.

# Chapter 6: Project risks

Protecting the quality, price, and schedule of this consultancy project to help further introduce BLH into the German market requires early detection and effective risk management from our end. Regulatory adherence, stakeholder engagement, market entrance dynamics, and other factors are, in our opinion, all included in a well-rounded risk management approach.

There are inherent difficulties when entering a new market, particularly one as competitive as Germany. A thorough **SWOT analysis** and **market research** are crucial for comprehending the competitive environment and spotting unusual opportunities to reduce these. By leveraging partnerships and investigating specialized markets, we can help BLH differentiate and create a maintainable and durable competitive edge thanks to this strategic insight.

Encouraging stakeholder participation effectively is essential to preserving project quality. We, as consultants, can ensure alignment and satisfaction among all parties involved by adjusting project scopes and expectations based on stakeholder feedback through transparent communication and regular updates.

Timeline delays can pose a threat to the project's timeline. If we are unable to lock down essential interviews early on our project might run the risk of not having enough data points to give beneficial advice. By using agile project management practices (i.e. being flexible) and creating a thorough project schedule with buffers, we feel we can ensure that the project stays on track.

We should not undervalue the risk associated with brand positioning and perception in a new market. Since BLH is a new player in the German market it is essential for them to start in the right way to ensure a long-lasting brand

Finally, success requires an awareness of Germany's market dynamics and cultural quirks. A thorough study of this can enable us to steer clear of cultural pitfalls and customize its strategy to the particularities of the German market.

We can effectively handle the challenges of entering the German market by implementing proactive planning, ongoing monitoring, and a flexible approach to risk management. This will ensure the project's success while staying in line with strategic objectives.
