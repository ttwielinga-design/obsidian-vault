**Paragraph 1:**

I started working on the 29th of August at the W in Ft. Lauderdale. The first day was orientation and getting to know my supervisor there. Up until this point, I have done two different jobs:

1.  Being a pool attendant: this is putting down towels for the guest that are going to the pool and helping them with any other things they may want. The big downside to this is that because it is the low season currently, and the main pool is getting restored, I am standing around and waiting a lot. Nevertheless, this job has helped me with understanding what type of guests come to the hotel and what level of service quality is required.

2.  The second job that I have done so far is being a food runner. This job is also pretty linear it is just basically bringing food from the East wing of the hotel to the West pool. This job has given me an insight as to how the kitchen is being run as well as how the communication between the waiters and the kitchen is.

So far, I have had two contributions to the F&B departments.

1.  The first one has to do with how the towels are folded and presented to the guests. Before I was at the W there was no clearly defined way the towels were presented. I suggested that we present the towels with the W logo always showing on top as well as making towel rolls instead of just putting them down folded. The result of this suggestion is that now an SOP has been put in place, by the manager, on how to fold and present the towels to all guests that come to the pool.

2.  The second change that I suggested has to do with the ambiance at the pool. Before I arrived at the W there was only music at the pool on Saturdays and Sundays because on those days, they hired a DJ. What I suggested was that we put a music box behind the bar and turn on a playlist when there is no DJ. The reason why I thought that this was a good idea is two-fold:

    1.  First of all, this solves the issue that guests bring their own music, which happens a lot on the days that there is no DJ. We as pool attendants are currently only allowed to tell the guests that they can't play the music if it is not "appropriate" which is of course very hard to judge.

    2.  The other problem that it solves is making sure that there is always a nice ambiance. In my opinion, it is nicer to walk into a pool that has some LOFI beats playing because it creates a good atmosphere.

The major challenge for me currently is that I feel like I am not in a very challenging environment. However, I did discuss this during my call with Ruud Bogaert, and he told me that I should discuss this with my company supervisor so I can be put into a position where I can learn more. 

The teamwork currently is going quite well among me and my coworkers. However, I feel there is a general sense of discontent within the team at the pool because the construction of the main pool is taking more time than expected and this is leading to a lot of employees not wanting to work because they don't make enough money in tips to "survive", and they would much rather work at their other job(s).

Horizontal communication is very well organized and accessible (there is a general sense of we want to help each other out) while communicating vertically (to managers) is much more difficult. You can see the power distance here. An example was on my second day when I couldn't figure out where the towel bins were supposed to go. I tried calling my supervisor like 3 times and he didn't answer. Then I called a co-worker of mine who actually had their day off and they directly answered and helped me with where I should put the bins and how I should take off the covers.

As far as norms and values go, I believe the W as a whole has a value that overlap with my values. Curiosity is one that I can find myself in. I too try to be curios all the time when I am working. Trying to learn something new every day is something that I strive for.

**Learning goal 1**: I want to become proficient at upselling within F&B in order to help further my F&B career, I want to achieve this by the end of January 2023.

Currently I am working as a towel boy at the pool. So how I have been able to up sell is through getting people to get a cabana instead of a normal daybed. There was this one time when this really worked well a few days ago. There were four guys that wanted four daybeds. It was very busy at the pool and there were about six daybeds left at the pool and one cabana.

The task that I had gotten from my supervisor is to get people to rent the cabana for the day because that increases revenue. Everyone on the team is expected to do this.

Before I explain how I sold them the cabana I do have to say that it felt pretty intimidating to try to get somebody to buy something (extra) from you. For this reason, before I actually upsold to guests, I talked to colleagues on how they did it. One of my colleagues explained to me that you have to find out what the guest wants and then you need to find a way to give that to them and go the extra mile. So that is what I tried to do with these specific guests. The action that I took in this situation to get them to rent the day bed was to explain to them how the cabanas are very good positioned between the bar and the DJ so they will not have to go very far and they will get extra attention from our waiters. I could tell that these guys wanted to get a VIP experience so that is what I tried to sell them.

The result of this way of upselling was that they decided to rent the cabana for the day. I got a nice tip from them for all the help I gave them throughout the day. I also got a compliment from my manager because all of the cabanas for that day were sold out which resulted in 250\$ \* 4 = 1000\$ in extra revenue.

Reflecting back, I believe this upselling experience has helped me better understand how it works and will also make sure that I am less reluctant to upsell next time. Upselling is not only making more revenue for the hotel it is also giving the guest a better opportunity in general.

**Learning goal 3**: Within the 6 months that I am interning at the W I want to better understand how the management structure looks like as well as what management styles they are using. I will use the situational leadership model as well as the Quinn model in order to do this.

What I have noticed while working as a WET attendant is that very quickly, after they have explained anything to you, you get the power to make a lot of decisions by yourself. Your first week you get “coached” so that is high supportive behavior and high directive behavior. But from the second week onward you very quickly go from D2 to D3. The leaders here find it very important that you are impowered to make decisions with their support. An important thing to note here is that you still need to discuss with your leader what your decision is so that they know what is going on you can’t make a decision without letting them know (D4).

Regarding the culture of the B&F department there are a few different ones with in departments. For the WET attendants there is a clan culture as a WET attendant you work together. I have noticed that all the WET attendants help each other out when possible, regarding shifts, workload, and helping guests.

The culture between the bartenders and waiters is very much a result-oriented culture. The focus for the staff here is on tips. They are mostly oriented on themselves and how much they make in a day. Furthermore, tipping out employees is always, from what I have noticed, a problem since every employee uses a different system and different percentages. The issue with this is that the leaders can’t say anything about it because they are not allowed to say anything about tips to employees.

I currently can’t say anything about the leadership since I have not had a lot of experience with them but I am hoping to find out more in the next couple of weeks.

**Learning goal 4**: I want to actively ask for feedback and incorporate it while I'm working at the W so I can grow as a F&B intern.

The first week that I was working as a pool attendant I got a lot of feedback from my supervisor due to the fact that everything was new to me. I remember one specific piece of feedback was during my first closing shift as a pool attendant. The situation was that I was cleaning up the pool around 7 pm and I was struggling to get some guests out of the pool and getting them to leave because the pool closes at 7 pm.

When you are closing you have to do a few things: first of all, you have to get all the bins from the beach and drag them inside this takes about 30 minutes. The next thing you need to do is put down all the beds and collect all the towels at the pool. The third thing you need to do is clean up the water station and collect all the trash around the pool if there is any. And the last thing you need to do is make sure all of the guest are gone by 7 pm.

I did all these actions the way I was told to do them however on this particular night some of the guests were quite intoxicated and refused to leave the pool and wanted to argue with me and ask me why they need to leave the pool. How I dealt with this situation was telling them in a polite manner that they are not allowed to be at the pool without supervision and the pool will open again at 8 am. The guests didn’t agree with me but never the less after explaining to them that I cannot change anything for them they left.

When I explained this situation to my supervisor, he gave me some incredibly logical and smart feedback. He said that I could have handled this situation a bit different. He said the way that he would have approached this situation was by one hour before the pool closes to make a round and tell all the guests that the pool will close in one hour so everyone has a timely heads-up. The next thing he would do is get all the bins from the beach, and when he comes back do another round so the guests have another heads-up. And if the guests don’t leave at 7 pm just call security and let them handle it that is what they are trained.

The result of implementing this feedback into this situation would have most likely led to a smoother guest experience.
