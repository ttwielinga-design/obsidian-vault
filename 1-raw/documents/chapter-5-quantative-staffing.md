For the Quantitative staffing, payroll and related expenses (manning guide) we have tried to keep in mind the following ratio’s: Average payroll per employee, Payroll per centage and payroll per centage per department. We did not look at FTE / Room since we cannot compare our hotel with the Statista data as we have 4 F&B outlets and that is not the average for a 5-star hotel. Below we will present the ratios with some explanation, for the full payroll please see the appendix.

For our gross operating revenue we ended up at € 20.987.511,25. Our ADR is €226,-. With this information we were able to calculate that our REVPAR =€185,55. TREVPAR is €287,50 and our GOPPAR = € 139,83.

Average payroll per employee:

For us this is at € 38.416 ,11, this is almost € 200 lower than the low benchmark for our ratio’s. This is likely due to the fact that we have a lot of trainees in our hotel. We believe in bringing in a lot of new energy and inspiration as we believe that young people possess such traits. We also show that we are invested in the trainees since we pay them 50% more than normal[^1].

Payroll %:

Our “Total Payroll incl. employers’ benefits / Total Revenue” is 29% which fits perfectly between the Statista averages which are between 27% and 32%.

Payroll% per department:

- Rooms: 14%, for Statista is between 14% and 17%.

- Food and Beverage: 37%, for Statista is between 40% and 45%.

- M.O.D.: 76%, for Statista this is between 33% and 50%.

- A.&G.: 3%, for Statista this is between 3% and 4%.

- S.&M.: 2%, for Statista this is between 3% and 4%.

- Property operations: 2%, for Statista this is between 2% and 3%.

For F&B it is clear that we are on the high side, this is due to the fact that we have more F&B outlets than average for a 5-star hotel. The Tube aspires to be a Michelin star restaurant hence, we want to make sure that there is enough staff to go around.

The M.O.D. average is rather high for us since we don’t have that much revenue other than the spa and that is not that lucrative because it needs a lot of manhours to be fully operational. We also have one employee working in the gym who are only needed form Wednesday until Sunday from between 8 and 4 because we expect those are the hours the gym is busiest on the other days/hours the spa employees can help out. For the rest we don’t have any other M.O.D. departments other than the spa.

For our S.&M. we also have a lower than usual ratio since we make a lot of revenue per year because of out F&B outlets. That is why our S.&M. payroll is lower.

To conclude we feel that the 157 is a realistic amount of FTE’s necessary to be fully operational with this hotel. Most of our ratios are similar to the Statista average. We feel we can confidently give this payroll / manning guide to hotel Ultima, and with using this they will be successful.
