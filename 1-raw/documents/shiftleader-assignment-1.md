**Assignment A: coach talk with student**

I had my coach talk with Pim Vencken; he was in my group at refresh along with Caspar. I chose to do the coach talk with Pim because he and I already had a talk before during the first practical week and we discussed that these things were not going so great and he didn’t finish on time with a lot of the assignments during the online refresh. We had the coach talk in my hallway on campus (15c). The coach talk was on the 20<sup>th</sup> of May, and it took around 20 minutes. The observer was Juliette de Meer.

The competencies that Pim choose that he already possessed were: be cheerful, passion/love for the profession, empathy, and being there for others.

The last time Pim showed passion/love for the profession was a few weeks ago in the service department for his practical’s. He really enjoyed working there and when talking to the supervisor he told him that he showed passion for this work. He also told me that this passion could also stem from the fact that his parents own a restaurant so he was brought up with passion for the hospitality industry.

Pim also told me he really tries to show that he is there for others. He does this by expressing to his friends that they are important to him and that he is there for them if they ever need him.

The competencies that Pim chose to develop are: giving personal attention, being able to listen well to guests, being able to cope with stress better, and taking responsibility (good and bad situations).

Being able to cope with stress is an aspect that he needs to work on this is because he feels that when he is working, he is always in a rush, trying to do things as fast as possible, for example things like the mise-en-place.

Giving personal attention and listening well to guests are also aspects that Pim wants to work on. This, we concluded during the interview, is lead back to the aspect of stress again, because if Pim were to stay calmer during work he would be able to give more attention to guests and he would be able to listen better. So, working on reducing the stress level will also contribute to these points of development.

We also discussed the fact that Pim feels that taking responsibility is a part that we need to look into. This for him is al about owning up to the mistakes that he makes and to quote him: “for me it is about growing up”.

As for the conversation its self the verbal and non-verbal skill that I used were:

- Facial expression – During the coach talk I tried to give encouraging facial expressions like smiling and nodding to encourage him to talk more

- Eye contact – During the talk I created eye contact with Pim as much as possible. This was to try to be kind and to encourage him to feel safe and tell me more. I noticed that in the beginning Pim was really hesitant with his answers like you can read above with the facial expression but the more the conversation went on the more comfortable he got.

- Body posture – My body posture during the coach talk was open pretty much the entire time. My shoulders were laid back, my hands were open and, on the table, (when I was not making encouraging gestures or using them).

- Encouraging gestures – During the coach talk I tried to give encouraging gestures to try to make Pim elaborate more where needed. An example of this is when I asked: “Do you see yourself as a person who is always there for someone?” He replied to this question: “Yeahhhh… yep I think so yes”. This was not the response I wanted so by moving my hand in a spinning motion I tried to encouraged him to tell me something more. It worked, he continued with: “I think I am a person who is there for others because of the way I was raised (…) the way I think I should treat others is the way I want to be treated”.

- Minimal encouragers – I used minimal encouragers in order to provide a short reaction and let Pim finish his train of thought. When talking about his SMART goals Pim said “I think my goal is specific”. To encourage him to say more I said: “Uh-uhh, why is that?”. Pim then went on to say: “This goal is specific because I am aware where I have to work on and also what course of action, I need to take in order to accomplish this.

- Asking open-ended questions (‘Why’ questions) – I used open ended question in order to get more substantial answers since Pim wasn’t really talkative during the beginning of the coach talk using open-ended questions I tried to get him to tell me more. A good example I think was when setting the SMART goal, I asked him: “Why is your goal measurable?”, knowing that the way it was formulated at this moment it wasn’t. Pim then answered back by saying: “well uhm… is it measurable? (…) aha well I guess it isn’t maybe I should reformulate it to a more concrete goal.” I think this citation shows that Pim really started to think by himself and actually got engaged during the coach talk.

- Closed questions – Sometimes I wanted a concrete answer in order to round off a topic or to make sure I had it correct in my head. An example of when I did this was when we were talking about how he liked the refresh practical week. He was being kind of vague and not really giving me a clear answer, this is when I asked him: “So overall did you like the refresh week?”. He answered with: “yes, yes, I did generally speak”. This was what I wanted to hear so then I rounded off the topic and moved on to the next topic

- Paraphrasing of content – I did this to make sure I have understood everything and in order to clearing up any misunderstandings. A moment where I paraphrased Pim was when we were talking about his inability to sometimes give proper personal attention when working. To get a good grasp of whether I understood him correctly I said: “So if I understand you correctly you sometimes struggle with giving personal attention (…) how do you think we can improve this?”

- Reflection of feelings – In order to make sure Pim understood my frustration on some points I sometimes outed my understanding of his emotions. Pim told me: “I have been struggling with refresh this week because it is online and that really hurst my willingness to put in the work”. I replied by saying: “I understand your frustration there. However, everyone in our team is going thru the same thing so it is no excuse to slack right now”.

- Concreteness – During the beginning of the conversation it was sometimes pretty difficult to get a concrete answer out of Pim. He didn’t show a lot of concreteness. By using facial expressions, encouraging gestures, and saying this like “go on” or “can you elaborate on that” or “can you be a bit more precise”. I tried to get more concrete answers out of him.

- Summarizing – At the end of every topic I tried to summarize what we just discussed. An example of this is after the end of the entire coach talk, I said: “Just to briefly summarize, we talked about the competences how we are going to do things in the future, when we will sit down again to talk about your goals. (…) Do you for now have any more questions for me?”.

I felt that in the beginning the conversation with Pim went a Pit rough but the more we got into it he started to loosen up and tell me more and more things. I think the conversation was not as in-depth as possible but I think we scratched all the surfaces and did a fine job for looking where development is needed. This is going to be measured by Pim by him giving a stress score every day.

Pim really wanted to work on getting less stressed during the practical’s, this is why we formulated one smart goal aimed at doing exactly that: reducing stress. This will not only benefit him but his entire team, now and in the future.

The two SMART goals Pim formulated were:

By next Friday I want to make sure I get less stressed inside of the kitchen environment, I am going to do this by when I feel stressed take a few seconds to breath and then go on.

Specific – It is specific because Pim knows what he wants to attain, getting less stressed.

Measurable – It is measurable because every day he is going to give a score of how stressed he feels and we will see if during the week by applying these actions it will get better.

Attainable – The goal is attainable because we set clear guidelines and Pim knows what to do and how to get started.

Relevant – It is relevant for Pim because he believes, and so do I, that if he gets less stressed, he will perform better in the kitchen.

Timebound – It is timebound because Pim and I are going to have a sit down (PS it already happened the 28<sup>th</sup> of may) and we are going to discuss his progress.

Pim for his second SMART goal is going to improve his listening skills. He is going to do this by making a commitment to himself that he will try his best to listen to others very attentively and try to understand the complete message being sent.

Specific – He will listen to others carefully every time

Measurable – He will reflect and ask others on how they think his listening skills were

Attainable – He believes he can book a lot of progress if he keeps asking friends/colleagues how he is listening and how he can improve. So, he can attain progress.

Relevant- It is relevant for Pim because this will make sure he can understand people better and he wont zone out during a conversation.

Timebound – Pim and I are again going to evaluate this together. (PS it already happened the 28<sup>th</sup> of May).

With Pim I main an appointment on the 28<sup>th</sup> of May. There we evaluated the situation and we talked about how the second week of practicals went. We also talked about his scores and his stress during Refresh. It turns out that it actually helped! I was really happy to hear that and Pim told me that he is really content with his lower stress now.

Talking about the content of the coach talk I think I helped Pim with three things:

So overall I think I helped Pim a great deal with this coach talk. I am also pretty satisfied with the way the coach talk went. However, for myself I do have a few points of improvements: Letting a person finish his or her train of thought, asking more open-ended questions, and making sure the surrounding of where the coach talk is held is completely private so people open up more.

**Assignment B: the dilemma game**

*I played the dilemma game with Marije and Fleur.*

**Part 1: Feedback received**

My dilemma during my first week of practical’s was that one team member (Pim) didn’t want to work and contribute to the team. This was partly due to the fact that the first week of practical’s I had were online Refresh. The other part was in my opinion also a bit of laziness.

The way saw it in this scenario I had two options: number 1 one is going to a coach / teacher to tell them that a team member is not participating and not doing his work on time. The other option I saw was having a talk with Pim and looking how we together can make sure that the assignments that we had to make for refresh will get finished on time.

I chose to go for the second option. I had a sit down with him at the beginning of third and final day of online refresh. I talked to him about how he wasn’t adequately preparing for the classes and he did all of his assignments for like 60%. I also talked how that made me, as the Shiftleader make me feel that I had to take action. He agreed that it wasn’t going to well and that we had to find a new way forward. The way forward that we agreed upon eventually was that we, as a group, would do all the assignments together in the living room to make sure that we all finish on time and no one doesn’t finish his assignments.

I think the approach I had to my dilemma was assertive. Meaning that I stood up for what I stand for, telling him that he needed to change his behavior. I also stood up for my emotions, and I expressed how I felt about the situation. I also shared my observation without judging him for it. I told him: “I am seeing that your work hasn’t been finished on time this week”. This also contributes to the assertive behavior. In my behavior I was not aggressive I didn’t (try to) make him feel bad about himself. Also, I didn’t come over authoritative because I made sure not to talk down to him, and I didn’t raise my voice. I also didn’t have a sub assertive style, I didn’t hesitate, I knew what I was going to say and didn’t shy away form telling him how his actions made me feel. I most definitely didn’t let him walk over me.

The feedback that I received on my approach form Fleur was mostly positive, she agreed on me not going to a supervisor and figuring it out myself. The only thing she didn’t agree on is the way I told him to do his work. She told me that I could have been a bit more dominant and direct, straighter to the point. For the rest she liked the way I handled it and how I put everyone together to make sure all the assignments would get finished on time.

From Marije I hear more of the same, however she told me that she thought that maybe by putting the team together and doing it all together it may lead to Pim sitting back even more and, thinking that the team will do it anyway so he doesn’t have to contribute. She said that maybe I should put something in place to motivate Pim so that he would ‘have to’ contribute, like giving him extra work if he doesn’t deliver.

I think the feedback that Fleur and Marije gave me was truly helpful in the way that this conversation made me think about the situation differently and helped me in case I ever come across this scenario again. I think Marije for sure had a good point in that I should have maybe motivated Pim more to make sure he doesn’t do nothing. I also agreed with Fleur in the sense that I should have maybe cut straight to the point first and tell him that he wasn’t finishing is assignments and that he should fix it, then I could have started to talk about the preparation, laziness, and the way forward.

I am going to try to implement this feedback into the future if I ever come across a scenario like this again. Meaning that I will cut straight to the point at the beginning of the talk be a bit more direct and make sure there is some sort of motivator to make sure the work is done.

**Part 2: Feedback given**

The dilemma that I found most interesting was the dilemma from Fleur. Her dilemma was about that her students didn’t clean fast and efficient anymore after a couple of hours of work. This was due to the fact that they were lacking motivation and she could tell the rooms weren’t clean enough to give it to a paying guest. Here she had 2 options:

1.  She could have gone to the supervisor/teacher and tell her which students were lacking motivation and not participating well enough. So, she could solve the problem for her.

2.  Be understanding because it is a long shift and try to motivate them by making some small changes.

Here Fleur chose the second option. She called all the students that weren’t working and told them to come to the office and she there explained that there needed to be changes. She gave them all 5 minutes to freshen up and then sent them back to work. This made them more motivated again and they got back to work immediately.

The feedback that I gave Fleur was the following: I told her that I would have mostly done the same in that situation. However, I felt that putting the students together will only lead to the students being less productive. So, I would give them a break / let then relax a bit, and then I would not put them together in one room because I feel like that would be less efficient cause they would only talk more.

When looking at the theory I noticed a few things. I found that my feedback was specific, because I refer to what point I don’t fully agree with. I gave process specific feedback as well focusing on the job itself. I maybe didn’t really observe well enough and could have given Fleur more room to explain further this would have helped me with giving my feedback.

When looking at improving my feedback I think I should maybe rephrase it to this:

*Fleur, I would have done mostly the same in your situation. However, I would make one change. I would not let them work together because I think this will only set them back even more because they will start to talk with each other. What do you think about this?*

This how I would rephrase my feedback towards Fleur.
