Dear Thomas,

I hope this letter finds you well I, Thomas 25 years old, am writing you now the Thomas that is on that campus in Maastricht. Yes you, or me it depends on how you look at it. This letter will be about us. How you will look in 5 years. And let me tell you, you had a hell of a journey.

But before we begin, Thomas, use this letter as a guideline as to where you want to go in the future. If you ever feel lost just read this letter and let it remind you what you are working for and how you will accomplish this. And most of all who you are doing it for: **yourself!**

So who are we now. Before I get into all the tests and that kind of stuff. Let’s just look at what you said to yourself on a bike ride in the summer of 2020 from Utrecht to De Banjaard, Zeeland. On this bike ride of more than 170km you near the end wanted to almost give up. But than a little voice in your head said this: “Why don’t I give up? Because I am not a quitter! Why am I not a quitter? Because I Don’t give up!”. You Climbing to the top of the Zeelandbrug with the wind and gravity pulling you down screamed this from the top of your lungs on that bridge and that is hwo you made it to De Banjaard! This is who you are, **you** **are not a quitter!** Don’t let anyone tell you something different.

So do you remember what your personality tests said in march, 2021? No? Well let me tell you. In the TSI test you scored really high in yellow you got a 87% there which means you are an inspirer. It also told you that you interact really good with other people. And you a tendency to help other people with their needs.

In the big 5 test it showed you that you scored high in openness, this is logical since you are an original and creative person. You scored 42 in conscientiousness, this is because you used to really struggle with being reliable and being well-organized. Furthermore you scored an 85 in extraversion, this is similar to the TSI profile and proves both test also have a consistency with analyzing your personality. In aggregability you scored 54, this shows that you are sympathetic but also if need be you can be critical and harsh. In neuroticism you scored a 50, which to be honest is true. In some cases you are really calm and collective. But when someone with higher authority talks down on you, you can get tense and lose all focus. But don’t worry this is a point that you worked on.

The Values of the HMSM are: Passion, Professionalism, Initiative, Openness and Inspiration. The reasons why you also believe in these values is because:

- You are passioned, everything you do you do with care. Your passion is how you keep on going forward. It is your motor.

- You are professional, you treat each problem separately and you don’t let your preconceptions lead you in any way shape or form.

- You are initiative takers, you don’t wait for the last moment to do something.

- You are open, you share your losses and victories with other people.

- You are inspirational, you try to include everyone and let everyone reach their fullest potential.

This is you have of course not all accomplished yet but you will don’t worry. It is important to set high goals. Now that we have looked at all this what are actually your values? What is what drives you? When you cut thru all the bullshit what values are you left with? My values are as followed: Enthusiasm, Optimism, Determination, Creativity, and Enjoyment.

Let’s start with my most important value: **enthusiasm**. For me enthusiasm is so important because this is my drive. When I am enthusiastic is thrive it helps me to get things done. It helps me to inspire other people.

Secondly for me it’s **optimism**. This is what helps me to reach the most seemingly impossible goals. For me optimism is also about hope, if you are optimistic you can will start with the hardest of challenges. It gives you hope in your dark times. Without a little optimism you don’t get anywhere because you will think every task is to big.

My third value is **determination**. This is for me the ability to pull thru and keep going. This is what will help you to put first things first. Break loose form your old bad habits. This is also that value that when you think you know it all will tell you no you need to sharpen the saw and refine all you skills. This is one that you have developed over the last 5 years later on in this letter I will explain to you how you will accomplish this.

The next value is **creativity**. For me this is all bout thinking out of the box. Like they say in the book the 7 values of highly effective teens: “Think different”. This means that there is not always just one route to get things done, there are multiple. 1 + 1 is not always 2 it can be a plethora of outcomes depending on how you look at the situation. This value really helps me to look at problems form a different angle.

The fifth and final value for me is **enjoyment**. For me without enjoyment I **can’t** be a highly effective teen. In my opinion you, me, everyone needs enjoyment in their life otherwise when you are old you are going to look back at it and think what have I actually done? Yes, I have all these accomplishments but have I actually enjoyed any of it? I really hope that when I am older and I look back at this be it in 10, 20 or 30 years I can say yes, yes I have enjoyed every minute of it!
