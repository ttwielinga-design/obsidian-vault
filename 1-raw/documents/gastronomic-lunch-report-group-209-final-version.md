# Chapter one; Justification of ingredients 

We tried to come up with healthy, sustainable and seasonal products to use. Important to us was creating a tasty and interesting dish and yet trying to keep things simple.

For our first dish, we chose the dogfish. This is an a small shark species, that lives in the waters around Western Europe. The fish will contribute to low transportation costs as it comes straight from the North Sea (Knack weekend, 2016). It is mostly caught as bycatch by fishermen, and therefore sustainable. We will be complimenting the dogfish with parsnip and cucumber, both vegetables locally grown in The Netherlands. Parsnip grows during winter and is, therefore, a product that is in season right now. When looking for seasonal and sustainable products, these ingredients thick the boxes.

For our second dish, we picked the turbot. August until February is the prime season of this fish, which contains healthy vitamins and minerals. Next to the turbot fish, on the plate, there will be shiitake mushrooms. These mushrooms are sustainable, as we will grow these ourselves on a bed of sawdust. As you can grow these mushrooms inside, there is no season connected and therefore always in season. This way, we wanted to make our dish locally sourced and therefore sustainable.

Looking at all the ingredients, the theme of our gastronomic lunch is dishes made from locally grown, sustainable, and seasonal products.

## Grilled dogfish 

Used cooking techniques:

- Grilling – The dogfish filet

  - The grilling of the dogfish will make it more smokey

- Pickling – The cucumber

  - The effect that this will have on the cucumber is that the taste will be intensified with more coating and contracting.

- Purée – Parsnip

  - When the parsnip is pureed this will lead to a more coating substance

- Deep-frying – Dogfish skin

  - The deepfried dogfish will be crispy and have a more coating aspect because of the oil that it is deepfried in.

### Explanation of the FIS profile

We gave the dogfish a 3 for contracting. This is because if you combine all the elements together, the pickles are there to give it the contracting but, the parsnip purée and the fattiness of the fish counter act this acidity.

Within the purée, and the fish the coating really comes out. The acidity of the pickles however does breakdown some of that coating therefore we came to a 5.

The dryness in this dish we felt was quite low because the purée and the fish bring a lot of that coating and “weight” to the table. There is some dryness from the deep-fried dogfish skin and form the purée there for we decided that it was a 3 for dryness.

Altogether this dish brings a lot of different flavors to the table. However none of these flavors are really intense and they balance each other out quite well. For flavor intensity we went with a 5.

Then for the ripe- and freshness of this dish. We felt that this dish was predominantly ripe, this is because of the umami taste in the the grilled fish, and the deep-fried fish, but also the ripe parsnip. There for we gave the ripeness a 6. As for the freshness it was there because of the cucumbers and the acidity of it. Therefore we went with a 2 there.

### Explenation FIS

The turbot is glazed with butter. Together with the creaminess of the butter in the hollandaise sauce, ‘coating’ is a six. Because of the lemon juice and crème fraiche in the sauce, ‘contracting’ is a four. The dryness of the dish is a four, due to the ample amount of deep-fried cassava chips which are, after cooking, dried on a paper towel. The dish has some intense flavors like the freshness of the hollandaise sauce, but they don’t overrule the dish. Therefore we gave the flavor intensity a six. The fish has a umami flavor, which is ripe. The cassava chips are, because of the Maillard reaction, ripe too. Ripeness is therefore a five. Because of the lemon juice in the sauce, freshness is a three.

### Chosen wine for with the Turbot: Chablis first cru ‘beauroy’ 2017

The vineyard of the grape is in Chablis, in the region of Paris. The wine is made the Chardonnay grape. The summer of 2017 was a hot summer, which makes the grapes in this wine more sweet than usual. Coating is a six, because this creates a coating mouthfeel. This corresponds perfectly with the mouthfeel of the beurre blanc and turbot. The dryness of the wine, which is a four because of the grape species, wine is balanced by the cassava crisps, which give the dish a drying touch. The soil of the yards contains high amounts of clay, there also lots of nutrients for the grapes which gives the wine a higher flavor intensity. Therefore, flavor intensity is, together with the sweetness, a six.

The wine got an alcoholic and a malolactic fermentation. The wine finishes a ripening process in a French oak barrel, where apple acids will turn into CO2, which causes a more balanced taste in the wine and therefore ripeness is a five. Freshness is a three, because not all apple acids will turn into CO2.

# Chapter three; Allergens

## <img src="media/image6.png" style="width:2.5in;height:1.88681in" />Dogfish 

In this dish, there are four allergens to be found, fish, mustard, celery, and lactose. Mild allergic reactions to eating fish or shellfish are seen on the skin as red bumps, hives, or itchy skin. Other mild reactions are swelling of the lips, a tingling or tightening feeling in the mouth and throat, a runny nose, or digestive problems such as cramps and dirrea. (Department of Health, State Government of Victoria, Australia, 2020). Severe reactions lead to difficulties in breathing, swelling of the tongue and throat or tightness of the throat, coughing, and dizziness. An allergic reaction to mustard from the mustard cress and celery seeds is similar to those of fish (Gotter, 2018). Eventually, someone with a severe reaction can collapse. Young children with fish allergies get pale or tend to flop or hang. The lactose allergy comes from heavy cream, butter, and milk. Symptoms for this allergy are a bloated feeling, stomach cramps, or diarrhea.

## Asian turbot

Two of the allergens mentioned above, fish and lactose, also apply to this dish. The third allergen in this dish is eggs, mild reactions can be hives. A severe reaction leads to anaphylaxis. The stadium before this can be recognized by the person having a rapid heartbeat, fainting, dizziness, and swelling (MedicalNewsToday, 2020) (FARE, Unknown). An allergic reaction to soy can range from hives, a tingling or itchy feeling in the mouth or of the skin. Swelling of mouth and throat area, abdominal pain, diarrhea, and nauseousness, and vomiting. A severe reaction will lead to an anaphylactic shock.

# Chapter four; Vitamins, minerals and energy 

Comparing the dogfish dish to the wheel of five, the dish contains fish (dogfish), lipid fats (olive oil, heavy cream), and starch (parsnip, which is a starchy vegetable). At first, these elements formed the dogfish dish. However, we had to ‘cover’ the vegetable and fruit part of the wheel of five too. Therefore, we decided to add the pickled cucumber.

Looking at the turbot dish, all elements of the wheel of five are present. The dish contains Fish, which fits in the ´fish, meat, egg and nuts´ part. The shiitake fits in the ‘vegetables and fruit’ part. The butter and olive oil fit in the ‘lipids’ part. In the first instance, we only wanted to use the shiitakes as garnish. But we missed starch or grains, and we also missed a crisp in the dish which could meet the 6 culinary success factors. Therefore, we added the cassava crisps.
