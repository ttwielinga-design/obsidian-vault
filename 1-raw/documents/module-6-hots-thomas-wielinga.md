Financial analysis

For our financial analysis we are going to look at the following topics:

- Occupancy

- ADR

- RevPAR

- Ratio’s

  - Liquidity

  - Solvency

  - Profitability

- Flow-through

In this part we will refer to year 0 as the year we didn’t play but the hotel did preform and we will refer to year 1 as the first year that we played. When comparing year 0 to year 1 we can see that the we went from an average occupancy of 43% in year 0 to a 72% occupancy in year 1 this is a 29% point increase. Furthermore our ADR went from \$89,22 to \$93,33. Not surprisingly our RevPAR on average went up from \$37,44 in year 0 to \$67,52 in year 1.

What you can see from our ratio analysis is that all of our ratio’s have gotten better when comparing year 1 to year 0. The ratio’s that we wish to improve on the most and that we will be monitoring closely are our number of times interest earned and our profit margin. These two ratio’s are more important to us because we we don’t have that many assets and we will not really aquire many more assets. There for looking at our profit margin will be a good indication of how our hotel is doing and our number of times interest earned will give a good overview of how we are able to pay off our dept.

Chapter 3

Marketing and financial goals

For our goals for year 2 and 3 we have 3:

1.  We want to increase our RevPAR with at least 10% before year 3 May.

2.  We want to stabilize our occupancy with a upper limit of 95% and a lower limit of 60% we want to achieve this stabilization before Year 3 May.

3.  We want to also increase our guest satisfaction from 56% to 70% before Year 3 July.

The reasons we have chosen these 3 goals are as follows: first of all, all these goals will ultimately contribute to a higher revenue. Secondly stabilizing the guest satisfaction will also mean that more guests will be prow to return because our reviews will be consistent. Increasing our RevPAR will not only contribute to a higher revenue but also to a better positioning within the market. Positioning ourselves as a more green and better option comes with a price, and our target customer is willing to pay this.

To conclude increasing our RevPAR, and guest satisfaction will contribute to our marketing goals. Stabilizing our occupancy will contribute to our financial goals.

Chapter 5

Extra facilities

To differentiate our hotel from the rest we have chosen a things to differentiate ourselves from the competition. First of all we started by building a new parking lot since we read in the case file that this was highly necessary especially since we wanted to market ourselves as a business hotel. Good and fast parking therefore was a must. Because we decided to focus on the business guest we chose to also buy a business center as well as conference rooms. To make sure our guests are able to get in and out of the hotel as fast as possible we also went and got quick check in/out. In order for our guests to be 100% comfortable in their rooms we decided to get internet access data point as well as room mini-bar. Finally we decided to opt for executive rooms because we saw that some guests just wanted that extra bit of comfort and luxury.
