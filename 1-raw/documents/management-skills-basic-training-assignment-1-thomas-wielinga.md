One goal for over three months: campus

My goal for on-campus is as followed: **In 3 months I will take out the trash every time it is full and I will help will cleaning the kitchen even if I didn’t use it.**

This goal is SMART because it is:

**S** – specific, I know that I have accomplished this goal when I clean up the kitchen when it is dirty and when the trash is full, I will take out the trash put a new trash bag in, and take out the full one.

**M** – measurable, I know when I have succeeded. I succeeded when every time I see a dirty kitchen, I clean it and every time I see a full garbage bag, I empty it. 

**A** – achievable, I know I can achieve this goal if I put in the extra effort that this takes. This could mean that instead of scrolling through Instagram for 30 minutes I could take out the trash and clean the kitchen. I believe that this is a sacrifice that I am willing to make.

**R** – relevant, this goal is relevant because, when I achieve this, I will feel happier and less irritated. For me living in a mess is irritating and I lack focus if it is not. I also think my fellow roommates will find this relevant because they will also benefit from this goal, it is a real win-win goal. This goal is also relevant because this will prove to myself that I have follow-through and I am willing to go the extra mile even though I don’t like the thing that I am doing.

**T** – timebound, this goal is timebound because I have set a time limit, I want to achieve this goal within 3 months. So, I will have a specific time (June, 28th) when this will be accomplished.
