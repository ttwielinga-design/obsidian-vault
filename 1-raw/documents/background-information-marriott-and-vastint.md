VASTINT – international real estate organization

Vastint uses BREEAM and LEED ratings to asses all of their buildings to make sure they are environmentally certified

BREEAM and LEED are two of the most widely used environmental certification systems for buildings. BREEAM (Building Research Establishment Environmental Assessment Methodology) is a UK-based system that has been in use since 1990. It uses licensed assessors who examine the evidence against the credit criteria. If BRE decides the building meets its requirements, BREEAM accreditation is issued. On the other hand, LEED (Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design) is a US-based system that has been in use since 1998. Unlike BREEAM, LEED does not use assessors to collect evidence for certification. Instead, the building’s design team collects data and sends it to the USGBC (U.S. Green Building Council) ²⁴.

Both systems are based on a points system, with buildings earning points for meeting specific performance criteria. BREEAM ratings range from Pass, Good, Very Good, Excellent, to Outstanding, while LEED ratings range from Certified, Silver, Gold, and Platinum ³.

The main difference between the two methods is the process of certification. BREEAM has trained assessors who assess the evidence against the credit criteria and report it to the BRE (Building Research Establishment), who validate the assessment and issue the certificate. While LEED does not require training, there is a credit available if an accredited professional (AP) is used ¹.
