# Chapter 2. Description of activities

The ComIn Project that I chose was ‘My Future Day’. This project is all about two important aspects. First, helping the Module 9 students create a meaningful personal and especially professional connection with their ‘hotel’ group. They also learn how to connect with their values and what career paths might work out well for them. Leading up to the coaching days, where you help these students, you have a number of training days. You are actively participating in preparatory coach training, which includes train-the-trainer sessions, coaching sessions where you get general insights into how to coach, and a few more. This practical approach makes it so that you not only learn the theory of coaching but can actually apply it effectively.

This ComIn project, in my opinion, is a game-changer. It empowers you as a student by encouraging essential competencies, such as help building connections, active listening, providing structure, and motivating teams. These skills may in the future help pave the way for leadership and change management roles.

Like mentioned, during this project the aim is that you help your fellow students from Module 9 to create a connection within their group ergo helping your (hotel school) community. Since the hotel school is paying ‘Stiching Wigwam’ for using their facilities. This project also indirectly supports them. This non-profit organization focuses on providing support and opportunities for families with children who have special needs or disabilities. By offers a range of services and programs designed to enhance the well-being and quality of life for these families. They play a vital role in directly supporting families of children with special needs and indirectly contributing to the community by fostering a more inclusive and understanding society.

## Actual MFD

The feedback that I received from the students that I coached was extremely positive. They told me they connected with their group, understood more clearly what they wanted to pursue in the future, and internalized their values in a more meaningful way.

The connection was brought forth, mostly, by the ‘hand exercise’ where students draw a hand on one of the pages of the booklet and explain their hand, meaning:

- Thumb: where lies my strength?

- Index: what guides me in life?

- Middle finger: What do I truly hate?

- Ring finger: What am I loyal to?

- Little finger: Where and when do I feel small?

This exercise really helped my coachees connect and it started a dialogue on where they differed and where they were similar.

Finding what they wanted to pursue was found mostly by using the combination game. Here they need to make scenarios for possible future ‘routes’. During the game the coachee makes ideal dream-work-wish combinations, that will give them a peak into the future. In my groups this was, most of the time, the most difficult part of the day, because of the endless possibilities. They way I tried to guide them was to talk to the individual about what they have done so far. What also helped was writing down what they especially didn’t want to do, ruling this out helped narrow down the search most of the time.

Lastly, the value game helped, hence the name, to select/internalize the values that resonated most with the coachee. This is because knowing which values they want to have in they work and life, enables them to choose their work more carefully. Also, they this can allow them to answer questions like: “Who am I?”. Since most of the participants had already worked on their values throughout their hotel school career this was not all too difficult for most of the groups that I coached.

# Chapter 3. Service performance

What was this a successful and relevant project to undertake? In order to break that down I am going to first breakdown: what is the HMSM community to me? Then I am going to go into why was this project relevant for the local community and the national community. Lastly, I am going to discuss why this project was successful. ## What is community?

In my opinion, the HMSM community resembles a close-knit family whose members are dedicated to encouraging and supporting one another. In addition to learning about hotel administration, this area brings together students, instructors, and alumni to exchange stories, provide advice, and create enduring relationships.

In our community, networking is more than just passing business cards back and forth; it's about developing sincere connections that continue after graduation. Students support one another academically, seniors provide industry insights, and alumni provide connections for internships and employment. It's about developing mutually on a personal and professional level.

HMSM is a breeding ground for future leaders in the hospitality industry, not just a school. The relationships made here last a lifetime, not just the length of the course. You come here to study, and when you go, you have a supportive community behind you.

## Why was it relevant to the national and local community?

The ComIn Project 'My Future Day' that I selected had a great deal of significance for both the national community and our area at the hotel school. This is the reason why:

'My Future Day' served as a lighthouse of self-discovery and community connection for our neighborhood. With the help of our 'hotel' groups, we students forged deep connections by diving into the core of our values, abilities, and goals. This was about building a support system that was aware of our ideals and professional aspirations, not just about making friends. With my training as a coach, I was able to foster these crucial connections in my role as a trainer. I witnessed directly how my peers resolved conflicts and reaffirmed their essential beliefs, which is crucial in a setting as changing as ours.

This project had an impact on the national community that extended beyond the boundaries of our institution. This was demonstrated by our partnership with "Stichting Wigwam," a nonprofit that supports families with children who have special needs. Through our use of their facilities, we helped a company that promotes diversity and offers necessary services. This collaboration brought to light the significance of civic involvement and social responsibility, two principles that are essential to the hospitality industry.

Furthermore, my coachees' encouraging comments served as a crystal-clear indication of the project's success. Activities such as the 'combination game' and the 'hand exercise' not only facilitated personal development but also stimulated conversations on empathy, diversity, and inclusivity. These are attributes that are necessary for creating a compassionate society as well as for providing hospitality.

## Aspects that contributed to the success

There are some significant aspects that contributed to the success of the 'My Future Day' project:

1.  **Real-World Coaching Experience:** The project's practical coaching methodology was very helpful. Through my involvement in preparation coach training and the acquisition of practical coaching skills, I provided my peers with appropriate guidance and assistance. By bridging the gap between academic knowledge and practical application, this hands-on experience, in my opinion, increased the effect of the learning process.

2.  **Personal and Professional Development:** The project put emphasis on networking professionally and personal development. It promoted a greater awareness of students and their objectives by assisting them in identifying their values, strengths, and professional aspirations. For both professional and personal growth, self-awareness is essential.

3.  **Strengthening of the School's Community:** The project improved student's feeling of connection. Through the facilitation of meaningful connections inside their 'hotel' groups, a collaborative and supportive learning environment was established. An essential component of any educational endeavor's success is this sense of community and support from one another.

## MFD value

The 'My Future Day' project was a worthwhile project since it took a comprehensive approach to personal and professional growth, combining real coaching experience (for me) with creative, self-exploratory exercises (for the coachees). The project, I think, improved the educational experience by building deep, meaningful ties among the students, for future collaboration, and promoting a culture of mutual support. Furthermore, its collaboration with 'Stichting Wigwam' increased its influence by including social responsibility and community participation in the initiative's core. Positive feedback and tangible progress among participants attested to its usefulness. This project benefited more than just the hotel school community; it also benefited the community as a whole, reflecting the essential ideals of hospitality.**\**

# Chapter 4. feedback on the project

Since the project has been done multiple times already, quite successfully in my opinion. First, Feedback and Continuous Improvement: Establishing a comprehensive feedback system is crucial for the ongoing success and relevance of the project. This could involve surveys, focus groups, and open forums where participants, coaches, and partners can share their experiences and suggestions. By actively listening to and incorporating this feedback, the project can adapt and evolve to meet the changing needs of students. This continuous improvement cycle ensures that the project stays aligned with its objectives and remains a valuable and effective tool for student development. Additionally, this approach can identify new opportunities and areas for expansion, driving the MFD to the next level.

Second, Research and Publication: Undertaking research to evaluate the project's impact and publishing the findings can significantly enhance its credibility and reach. Even though this may seem like a far-fetched idea, this research could focus on various aspects, such as the development of coaching skills among students, the effectiveness of networking opportunities created, or the long-term benefits for participants (think of drop-out rates before and after the project has taken place). Publishing these findings in academic journals or industry publications not only highlights the project's success but also contributes to the field of hospitality education. Sharing best practices and success stories can inspire other institutions to adopt similar initiatives, broadening the project's impact. Furthermore, such publications can attract interest and support from the broader educational and professional community, think of expanding it to the entirety of ZUYD. For example, after any type of internship, such a MFD could take place, to not only reflect but also look forward.

All together I believe while these ideas might seem farfetched now, they could lead to a substantial and significant impact on the broader community.

# Chapter 5. Reflection

In this chapter I will discuss several relevant topics. Starting with how I have become a better coach though this project going into two distinct aspects. Next I will reflect on three out of the five values of the hotel school, namely professionalism, connection, and courage, and how they applied to this project. Furthermore I will go into three distinct examples of feedback that I received from the groups that I coached but also from the senior coaches. Ending up with a small summary with my final thoughts and overall reflection on the journey that this project was.

## Improved coach

Because of this ComIn project I have found out what it is to be a coach, not only understanding the significance of mentorship and the art of guiding others, but also the deep joy of supporting others in their leadership journey of personal and professional growth. There are specifically two aspects of coaching that I would like to point out.

First, I've learned the importance of active listening and empathetic communication in coaching. Being fully present and attentive to the needs and perspectives of those I'm coaching has been paramount. I learned this during the preparation of the first training day and the first training day itself. Here we talked about what we think coaching is, learning that it is all about active listening and being there with the coachee in the moment.

Second, I've realized the power of fostering self-discovery and empowering individuals to unlock their own solutions and insights, rather than imposing my own ideas or directives (do not be an “OMA”). This approach has led to more profound and sustainable growth for those I've coached within the ComIn project.

## Reflecting values

Professionalism is inextricably linked to coaching as a key principle, creating a foundation of integrity, respect, and knowledge within the coaching interaction. I believe the core of coaching is the creation of a professional atmosphere that values ethical behavior, confidentiality, and a dedication to excellence. A professional coach, which I aspired to be during this process, works within well-defined parameters, using extreme caution and secrecy concerning the client's (in this case the students') personal and professional information. This ethical behavior generates the trust and security required for the coaching process to thrive.

Connection as a value is inherently woven into the fabric of coaching, serving as the foundation for meaningful and transformational coaching partnerships. Coaching, at its core, relies on the development of true and significant relationships between the coach and the client. Empathy, understanding, and the capacity to establish true connections based on trust and rapport are all part of this trait. A coach may establish a secure and supportive environment in which clients feel understood, appreciated, and empowered to explore their thoughts, feelings, and ambitions freely by cultivating a deep relationship. During this project, I tried to create this connection, not only between the students but also, me, as a coach to the students. In my opinion, this enabled an open atmosphere during the MFD.

Courage, as a value, is an essential component of coaching, acting as a guiding force that drives both the coach and the client toward development, self-discovery, and transformation. Courage presents itself in numerous aspects of coaching, both for the coach and the individual being coached.

I think, it is also the desire to challenge traditional thinking, accept vulnerability, and venture beyond one's comfort zone. It requires the courage to ask probing questions, venture into unfamiliar territory, and address issues with honesty and integrity. A brave coach has the fortitude to leave aside preconceived assumptions, biases, or personal ambitions for the coaching process to evolve spontaneously, led by the client's needs and objectives.

For me, this showed during the MFD when I had to ask serious questions, like. "I hear you are talking only about your father's side of the family, what about your mother's side, how are things there?" These types of questions I felt were uncomfortable to ask but in the end helped push the conversation to a more untraversed, and more meaningful direction.

## Feedback

The first group that I coached was an incredible experience. Finally getting to put all the theory into practice was really fulfilling for me. From the first group I did however get the feedback that I was a little bit to anxious to get through the program. They told me that I should maybe take a bit more time in the beginning, I think this was entirely true. Taking more time there would allow me to focus on one of the more quintessential parts of the process, namely, the connection forming (introduction). For the rest they were quite loaving and were enthusiastic to work together for the rest of the modules (9 and 10) to come.

The second group that I had the pleasure of coaching was a lot different. They were an international group, which was not a problem for me, but it did give a fun and interesting new dynamic to the project. As a point of improvement they told me to help them a bit more during the career matching part of the day. While we were doing this exercise one girl in particular told me it. I tried to adjust and help her a bit more. Reflecting, I also found it quite difficult to think of careers and lines of work for my coachees. Next time I would like to employ the knowledge of some of the senior coaches to help with this.

Lastly, the general senior coach feedback. From my coaches I got a lot of positive comments on my style and way of approaching the day. However, there is of course always room for improvement. One of these areas is for sure the communication, as one male senior coach pointed out to me. I should focus on more probing questions. Allowing my coachees to talk more and thus discover more about themselves.

## Final remarks

Through the 'My Future Day' project, I discovered the essence of coaching: attentive listening and guiding self-discovery. Values like professionalism, connection, and courage were integral to this journey. Feedback from students highlighted the need for more initial connection-building time and support during career matching. This project not only impacted my personal growth but also reinforced values and social responsibility within HMSM. In my opinion, it was about more than coaching; it fostered connections, growth, and community impact, embodying the ideals of hospitality and education.
