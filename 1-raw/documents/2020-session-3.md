<tr>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;"><p><strong>Which activities will you engage in the course of the session?</strong></p>
<p><em><strong>“If you don’t know what you want to achieve in your presentation, your audience never will.” (Harvey Diamond – who wrote ‘Fit for Life’, not a book we endorse but it did make a fortune)</strong></em></p>
<p>In today’s session, all students will be invited to deliver their personal inspirational pitch. Note: the four-minute deadline is a four-minute deadline. Like in Dragon’s Den, you get a specified amount of time to deliver your pitch, and four minutes is your allotted time span: you will be stopped after four minutes have expired. Not that we WANT to stop you, but you need to learn to deliver your message effectively and efficiently and you can only do so if you have given it serious thought and planning and if you practiced it well.</p>
<p>The other students should complete a checklist on each student’s performance (you will be given about two minutes to do so at the end of each pitch) which, at the end of the session, they need to send to the students involved. Your lecturer will do the same. That means that each student will receive twelve different feedback forms with feedback (how you performed) and feedforward (how you could improve your performance).</p>
<p>This feedback should enable each of you to hone their performance for the mock assessment in weeks 6 or 7 and for the final assessment.</p></td>
