**Paragraph 1 – Analysis of the overall experience**

During this second part of my internship, I have been working as a manager mainly at the pool. This started on the first of October and I finished this part of my internship on the eleventh of November. This part of my internship can be distinguished into two phases:

1.  Before the pool renovations were finished.

2.  When the pool opened back up.

During these two phases, I had different roles. Below, I will first talk about my role during the time that the pool was not yet open, and after I will go into more depth on how my role changed when the pool did open up.

When the pool was still under renovation Carlos, the Food and Beverage director gave me a few projects. They were as followed:

- Ensure that all the sunbed chairs were painted.

- Create a handbook for all employees at the pool.

- Make sure that WET east is set up on the 20th of October.

- Produce a before and after the presentation for W corporate.

The first project that I started to work on was the chair project. I first had to find people that were willing to paint the chairs because we needed to paint the chairs with the current staff that we had at the pool. Most of the staff that we had didn’t want to paint because their hourly was too low in their opinion. So, what we did is we went to the hotel manager and asked if we could get the engineering hourly, this was \$15 per hour. She agreed and I started scheduling the WET attendants, and food runners to paint. There were around 400 chairs that needed to be painted and with the employees taking 30 minutes on average, per chair and having 5 people work on the chairs we were able to finish this project within the first week of October.

The second project that I was given was about creating training plans and new standards for the pool reopening that all employees would have to adhere to. I created handbooks for all WET employees (bartenders, servers, barbacks, pool attendants, and food runners). During the presentations that Scott and I gave to all these groups, we talked to the team about what the new expectations were going to be regarding the timeliness, uniforms, cell phones, smoking, and specific opening/closing checklists. I am happy to say that so far all of these new expectations are being lived up to by almost all employees.

The next task that I had was all about setting up the pool. With this, we started three days before the deadline (17th of October). I had scheduled 6 people to help me to realize this goal. Beforehand we had created a floor plan which we gave to all the employees that were going to help us so they knew where everything was going to go. Everything was going according to plan however we had thunderstorms on the 19th of October so we were not able to finish on the 20th. This was very upsetting to me and looking back we should have for sure taken two more days so that we could have been done by the desired deadline.

The last task was to produce a before and after the presentation to showcase what we had done during the renovations. This I must say took a lot of time and effort on my part. I am, however, very much pleased with the result, and more importantly, the hotel manager congratulated me on making an amazing video presentation.

The second phase for me was all about streamlining the departmental processes. During this phase, I got even more freedom and was able to work on projects that I thought of myself to help the WET team. For this period, I had one massive project that I took upon myself. This was creating a bar knowledge book. This book that I created had everything that a ‘new’ bartender would need to succeed at this property. 

The reason for this training manual was that I as a mystery guest went to WET and had someone order the same cocktail for me on 4 different occasions and from 4 different bartenders. What I noticed when tasting these cocktails was that while they were good, they all tasted a bit differently. So, this got me to talk to all the bartenders about the recipes that they all use, and I found out that there were no real recipes in place that the bartenders had to adhere to. I was shocked by this and suggested to them that I would create a training manual so that we could streamline the process. So, I created a 52-page training manual.

Of course, next to these projects I was on the floor talking to guests and employees resolving service opportunities, and making sure that all the standards were met. I also audited a lot of employees to see how they treat guests and what they can improve on.

At the W in Fort Lauderdale, we have five different types of revenue streams. We can break them down into three distinct groups. First of all, you have the outlets: Livingroom, WET west & east, and in-room dining. Then we have the events which of course are banquets. Lastly, we have a mix bar. This subdepartment takes care of all the mini fridges in the hotel rooms within the hotel.

Next, we have the expenses, the largest expense for the F&B department is employee salaries followed by the cost of sales. One thing to note is that this W has only third-party people working in the kitchens. This means that the hotel does not directly have to pay for its kitchen staff and this cost is not on the food and beverage P&L. This to me is very interesting because it seems that this boosts up the B&F numbers dramatically. It of course does not affect the hotel numbers in the end.

**Recommendations for the B&F department to increase revenue and decrease costs**

The first thing I would change, and that I have suggested to management here is to cut people faster when it becomes less busy. (Cutting of course is refers to sending employees home because there too many employees there.) This would decrease employee salaries significantly. Let me show you how fast this could go in a year with just a simple decrease. On average in my experience, while I have been working here there is at least one WET attendant (\$13 per hour) for 2.5 hours per day that is not doing anything. As well as 1 server and 1 bartender (\$9 per hour) for 2 hours per day. And 1 food runner (\$11 per hour) for 3 hours per day. This would in a year with a low approximation save the hotel at least \$37.000, -.

The second thing I would do is to offer ice cream or some sort of dessert at the pool. Currently, there are no dessert options available for the guests and I know that therefore we are missing out on a potentially huge revenue stream. I know this because I have personally worked as a server and WET attendant. While I was in these positions guests have asked me numerous times about our dessert options or just for an ice-cream for their children. Another thing I would implement is a platform, let’s say once a week, where the employees can discuss with the managers what they feel they need to succeed. I know this is not a direct boost to the revenue or a cost decrease however just like Mr. Marriott said: “Take good care of your employees, and they’ll take good care of your customers, and they customers will come back.” I believe it is vital for the employees at this W to be heard more so that the correct things will change.

Lastly, I would implement more SOPs in order to streamline guest interactions. An example of a SOP that I have started implementing here is to standardize the drinks behind the bar. This means to pour the same amount of alcohol for every cocktail so that the taste of the cocktail doesn’t change when a different bartender is making the drink. I did this by creating a “bar bible”, a book where all the cocktails as well as all the knowledge a bartender needs to succeed is located. I believe that if there were more of these SOP’s and standardize books in place a lot of the complaints that the hotel is currently experiencing, like guests complaining that their pizza was different than the pizza two days ago, would go away.

What I have written in the first periodic report is still true and I would like give a circumstance that really demonstrates for me the result-oriented culture that the bartenders and servers have.

While I was working as a manager one day a bartender called out, this means he was sick or something urgent had come up. So, we didn’t have enough bartenders. Knowing that I could bartend I said that I would jump in and help them out. Jordan the ‘head’ bartender was very happy with this because then he didn’t have to do it all alone and the work load wouldn’t be unbearable. So, I took action and worked a bartender shift. While working Jordan and I started talking about all sorts of things and eventually we stumbled on to tips, and how that works here. He told me that the servers are supposed to tip out the bartenders because we make the drinks for them. This made sense to me. But what I also found out was that the bartenders make their tips individually, so they don’t share their tips so the distribution can be quite unfair in my opinion. What I suggested to Jordan was that all the bartenders pool all the tips for that day and that way they didn’t need to fight over every tab and all the guests would be served in a better way. This resulted in Jordan talking to all the bartenders about this and this change has now been implemented.

This example to me showcases that in general that the bar here is super result oriented but sometimes you can slightly change the culture by asking the right questions and implementing them.

After this having been implemented for almost two weeks now, I can say that there, in my opinion, has been a change behind the bar and the bartenders work together better. All of this with one simple change.
