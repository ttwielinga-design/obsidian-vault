Methodology

The main focus of our research is to find an answer to the question: How can we provide the bleisure guest with the best sustainable experience? In order to adequately answer this we formulated three sub-questions namely:

1.  How do bleisure guests perceive sustainability?

2.  What are current hospitality sustainability trends?

3.  How does sustainability affect bleisure guest behavior?

Before conducting our first round of research we came up with six key assumptions:

1.  Bleisure guests are interested in attending events.

2.  Bleisure guests are willing to pay up to 20% more for a sustainable stay.

3.  Bleisure guests are willing to spend some of their free time in the hotel.

4.  A well-designed lobby should incorporate natural features.

5.  All bleisure guests want to have human and social interactions within the hotel.

6.  Bleisure guests spend most of their time at the hotel

Looking into the values we have tested it is important to note that our research focused on the business guest and making them want to stay longer, therefore turning them into a ‘bleisure’ guest. Not the other way around.

Regarding our research itself we used a combination of qualitative and quantitative research methods. We employed multiple research methods to investigate the perceptions of sustainability in hotels among bleisure guests. These methods included secondary research, direct observations, interviews, and surveys. Our goal was to gather sufficient data to gain insights into how we can approach our main research question.

During the interviews, we specifically focused on understanding the perspectives of bleisure guests regarding sustainability in hotels. This allowed us to gain valuable insights into their thoughts and attitudes towards sustainable practices.

To complement our primary data, we conducted secondary research to examine the sustainability initiatives implemented by leading hotels. This broader exploration helped us understand the strategies and approaches employed in the industry to promote sustainability.

Furthermore, we conducted direct observations to observe consumer behavior within sustainable environments. By observing how individuals reacted and whether they engaged in sustainable actions when given the opportunity, we aimed to gain a deeper understanding of their actual behaviors.

Lastly, we employed a survey to verify the last of our key assumptions mainly focusing on the data that needed to be tested quantifiably, ie. Assumptions two, three ,and six.
