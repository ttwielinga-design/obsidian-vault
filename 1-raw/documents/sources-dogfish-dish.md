Grilled dogfish dish

**Dogfish:**

Grilling is a process that involves cooking food on a rack over a heat source, usually a charcoal fire or ceramic briquettes heated by gas flames (*Is a grill the same as a barbecue?,* n.d.). Grilling makes the dogfish taste very pleasantly, the direct heat quickly sears the outside of the fish, producing a distinctive robust, roasted and sometimes charred flavor with a nice crust (*Grilled dogfish on Late-Summer caponata recipe*, 2015).

First, we have to separate the skin from the fish, after that we remove the spine and the unwanted parts. Then we are left with dogfish fillets we put them in a marinade of honey (Zest of) lemons, Thyme, garlic.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Sprinkle the fillets with 1/8 teaspoon of salt. Add fillets to the grill rack coated with cooking spray, grill 3 minutes then turn it for another 3 minutes until the desired degree of doneness.

**Pickled cucumber rolls:**

Pickling is the process of preserving or extending the shelf life of food by either anaerobic fermentation in brine or immersion in vinegar. The pickling procedure typically affects the food's texture, taste and flavor (*Exploratorium*, n.d.).

The result of the pickling process is that it makes the foot more sour and it gives a more intense flavor.

Slice the cucumbers in thin pieces, make sure that you use cucumbers with a few seats. Sprinkle them with a tablespoon of coarse salt, after that toss the cucumbers with the salt and let them sit for up to 3 hours in the refrigerator. Rinse them of to get rid of the excess salt. Chowdhary, N. (n.d.)

Put them in layers in a jar, add some springs of dill. To make the brine, we will be needing a medium saucepan, combine 3 cups of white vinegar or apple cider vinegar after that add 1 cup of sugar, 2 tablespoons of coarse salt, half a teaspoon of mustard seed and celery seed. Bring to a boil, stirring it until the sugar dissolves. Transfer the brine in a measurable cup, add the hot brine in the jars to completely cover the cucumbers. Make sure to cool them of before putting them in the fridge.

**Parsnip purée:**

Puréeing is taking a firmer substance like a potato for example and mashing it using a potato masher. This is usually done until the substance is nice and smooth with no lumps in it anymore. The mouthfeel of a puree is very coating and less firm.

For the parsnip purée we are going to boil it together with some garlic, cream, milk and butter. When that is boiling we are going to reduce the heat, cover it and let it simmer for 10 – 15 minutes. Then we are going to uncover let the liquid reduce by 50% and add some salt. After this we are going to blend it to a nice and smooth purée (Pomeroy, N., 2021).

**Dogfish skin:**

Deep-frying is a technique where a substance gets put into oil boiling at around 180C to give it a nice an crispy texture with a more coating flavor.

<img src="media/image1.png" style="width:6.3in;height:3.21319in" />As for the dogfish skin we are going to remove the skin from the flesh of the dogfish. Keeping the knife tightly against the skin and removing the extra fat and flesh from the skin side (you want to remove this because it hinders the ability for the skin to puff up. After this we are going to place the skin, scale side down, on a baking tray with baking paper on it. Then we are going to place the skin in the oven at 50 degrees Celsius for about 4 hours/until it dries out. Don’t forget to put the fan on in the oven. It is done when it is sure crispy and you can easily snap pieces off. To fry it you can take a little piece and place it into a deep fryer at about 180 Celsius until it puffs up to about 8 times the size (How To Make Sushi, 2019).

**Explanation of the TSI profile**

In the TSI profile you can see that for contracting we gave the dogfish a 2. This is because if you combine all the elements together, the dogfish the pickled cucumbers, the parsnip purée, and the deep fried dogfish skin, the pickles are there to give it the contracting but, the parsnip purée and the fatyness of the fish counter act this acidity. We feel that the contracting is there but not prominent therefore we gave it a 2.

As for the coating we felt that it was most definitely there. With in the purée, and the fish it really comes out. The acidity of the pickles however does breakdown some of that coating therefore we came to a 5.

The dryness in this dish we felt was quite low. This is because like we said before the purée and the fish bring a lot of that coating and “weight” to the table. The fish and the pickles are nice and juicy but there is some dryness from the deep-fried dogfish skin and form the purée there for we decided that it was a 2 for dryness.

Altogether this dish brings a lot of different flavors to the table. However none of these flavors are really intense and they balance each other out quite well. For flavor intensity we went with a 4.

Then for the ripe- and freshness of this dish. We felt that this dish was predominantly ripe, this is because of the parsnip, the grilled fish, and the deep-fried fish. There for we gave the ripeness a 6. As for the freshness it was there because of the cucumbers and the acidity of it. Therefore we went with a 2 there.
