Hospitality experience chapter 10

An individual’s mental programming is determined by:

- Human nature

- Culture

- Personality

Culture consists of:

- The unstated assumptions

- Standard operating procedures

- Ways of doing things that are shared within a group and that have been internalized by the group members to such an extent that they do not argue about them

There are four levels of culture:

- Macro cultures (nations)

- Organizational cultures

- Sub cultures

- Micro cultures (e.g., lifestyle)

Culture is a layered concept: it manifests itself in symbols, heroes, rituals (together referred to as practices) and values

Culture is a complicated subject to talk about because:

- Levels of culture can interfere (e.g., national and organizational culture)

- Values are relatively stable, whereas practices can change rapidly

- The distribution of values within cultures is normally shaped

- Some phenomena are very culture-specific

- Models of culture (e.g., Hofstede) can explain, but no predict behavior

Hospitality managers can and should use a number of strategies to reduce potentially negative effects of culture in the design and delivery of their hospitality experiences

Leaders and employees in hospitality need intercultural competencies (IC) to optimize customer out comes.
