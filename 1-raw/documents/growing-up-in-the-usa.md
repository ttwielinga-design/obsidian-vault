**Going from small to large**

*What you can really expect when you move to the USA.*

When I was 8, I moved from the Netherlands to the USA for 2 and a half years. These years have taught me a lot about the USA and how misconceptions can easily be accepted.

When I moved to the USA what I expected was mostly based on stereotypes. The most common stereotypes are that all Americans are fat, everything is big, and that people are fake.

I moved to Berkely, California, when I arrived there, I could not speak a word of English. So, when I was put in school the only phrase that I was taught by my parents was: “where is the bathroom”. Unlike what you might expect, from the stereotypes I just told you about, the people in my class as well as the teacher were very kind to me. I, with in three months, could speak English fluently and was able to interact with all my classmates normally.

In the next 2 years or so I found out that the USA really isn’t just one country it is more like 50 different countries that happen to be ruled by one central government. One example of this are the distances in the USA, they are enormous. I remember that we were on our summer holiday and we went from Arizona to Los Angeles in order to do that we had to drive thru a desert for 12 hours! That to me really put in to perspective the 2-hour drive to Zeeland that we sometimes made from Utrecht to go to the beach.

The truth is in the USA you are just like any other country going to find people who are fake, exaggerate everything, and fat. But in reality, you are also going to find people that are nice, kind and that just want to genuinely know how you are doing.
