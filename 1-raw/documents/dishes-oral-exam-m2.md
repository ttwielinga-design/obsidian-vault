The **CSF’s** (culinary success factors) are:

1.  Name and presentation fit the expectation

2.  Appetizing smell that fits the food

3.  Good balance in the flavor components in relation to the food

4.  Presence of umami

5.  Combination of hard and soft textures

6.  High flavor richness

**Dish 1 – Confit of suffolk lamb loin with Chinese, Jerusalem and globe artichokes**

The CSF’s are (mostly) present in this dish. It has a compelling name and a nice presentation that displays the ingredient beautifully. Even though I can’t smell it if I am imagining the smell from past experiences I think the lamb together with the artichokes and the cheese create a nice and ripe aroma. I also think that the balance in flavor is for sure there in relation to the food. An example of this is the combination of the roasted pine nuts with the buttery sweetness of the purée going into the rich flavor of the lamb with the mild nutty flavor of the globe artichoke. I think the flavors combine nicely. There is also a presence of umami in this this one of the ingredients that consists of umami is the goat cheese (toma della Rocca) for example. This dish in my opinion is lacking one element, a crunchy one. There is just no real crush present in this dish. I guess you could argue that the pine nuts and the sunflower seeds give a little crush but to be fair that for me just doesn’t cut it. The last factor is for sure present in the dish. It has a lot of flavor, it is really nice and rich the lamb in combination with the maillard reaction on the nuts and seeds together with the purée for sure give it a high flavor richness.

**Dish 13 – blackmore’s pure-bred wagyu beef fillet sous vide with morels and bone marrow**

The name for this dish I feel for sure fits the expectation. It to me sounds like a really appetizing and high end dish which on the presentation side, it looks good as well. I believe that the aromas form this dish will be very ripe and intense this is because of the wasabi. I think that the dish may be a little bit too over the top because there could be to many different things going on at the same time, first you have the wagyu fillet sous vide then you have the wasbi and the morels that each also bring a lot of richness and complexity, and to end you have your onions and the dry spinach. In my opinion it could be a little too much of the good. The wagyu beef in combination with wasabi root, the bone marrow and the onions give it a very high flavor intensity. For the reasons I explained above this dish will for sure have a really high flavor intensity.
