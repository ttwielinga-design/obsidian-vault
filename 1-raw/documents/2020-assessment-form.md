<th><p><strong>Test instructions Module 4 TedTalk</strong></p>
<p>In a pair, you will have to deliver a fifteen-minute (or 7.5 minute if you tackle this assessment individually) TedTalk. Note that each student must speak for roughly 7.5 minutes. If it is found you fall much below this, you will fail. In case a live delivery is prohibited, you will have to upload an edited version of your and your partner’s TedTalk to Youtube (check <a href="https://strangershow.com/how-to-upload-private-video-on-youtube/">https://strangershow.com/how-to-upload-private-video-on-youtube/</a> for instructions – you do need a YouTube account). The target audience for the TedTalk is the management of the case hotel you researched within the framework of module 4. Your goal is to convince the management how it can contribute to realizing the targets associated with certain SDGs, of which you have shown they are relevant to the country and the hotel.</p>
<p>You will be assessed on the level of your English (B2.2/B2+ is the minimum) and on the parameters stated in the professional product rubrics below. The minimum length is fifteen minutes (there is a 30-second downward margin), the upper margin is eighteen minutes (there is a 30-second upward margin), below and above which you will fail the assessment. Should a recording be in order, there is NO downward or upward margin. Reading off notes or off the screen (this, obviously, can be checked in the taped recording – if you look away from the camera at regular intervals, this will be construed as reading) means you will fail the assessment.In a short separate session, your lecturer MAY also ask questions for roughly five minutes in order to ascertain your understanding of the material should there be any doubt as to the originality of your work.</p>
<p>You will be scored on the basis of the rubric assessment grid, which can be found below. In order to pass, you need a 5.5 score on BOTH English and Professional Product. There is no compensation between the two.</p>
<p>Dress code for this assessment is business attire or smart casual.</p>
<p>NOTE: Should you have <u>failed to meet the participation requirement</u> that is in place (you can miss one session, either through absence for whatever reason or by not preparing adequately), you will need to complete an additional assignment. This must be completed FIVE working days prior to the assessment date and involves uploading a fifteen-minute TedTalk on how to deliver an attractive TedTalk. Only if this additional assignment is approved can you sit the final assessment or the resit.</p>
<p>Finally, feedback is the completed assessment form plus any remarks the lecturer provides orally. You can request a separate follow-up interview in order to learn why you failed the assessment. Should you disagree, you can appeal to the College van Beroep voor de Examens (via Loket Rechtsbescherming) .</p></th>
