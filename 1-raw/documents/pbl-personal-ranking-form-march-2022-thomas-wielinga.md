In the first year you have learned how to formulate feedback for your fellow students: peer-feedback. In the workshops management skills in module 1, 2 and 3 you have seen why peer feedback is important and how to use constructive comments of others in your professional development as a hospitality manager.

In Module 5 you will take a step further. Becoming a manager means that you will have to ‘rank’ your employees and assess how they are doing. “Managing the journey of your employees” also means that you have the responsibility to reflect on their performance and to give them feedback on this performance. This feedback should be given on the basis of solid criteria, known beforehand by the employees, and be expressed in such a way that your employees will be motivated and stimulated to keep on performing well or – perhaps - to do better.

In the PBL sessions of this module, you will evaluate your group members and give them feedback on a regular basis (at the end of each session). In week 8, you will *<u>rank</u>* your group members - and yourself – using the peer ranking form included (see below) .

Just like in the first year, *the HMSM values* are the starting point for the evaluation. The evaluation criteria relate to the <u>team tasks</u> to be performed as well as <u>the process</u> the team goes through in order to realize the tasks and to finalize them at the desired level.

In order to prepare for this session, we ask you to read *again the basic rules of giving and taking feedback* (see *checklis*t and *basic program Management skills*, in Moodle, Resources, Resources for PBL). This way you will be aware of the techniques you can use as well as the do’s and don’ts during the actual session.

**Before the final evaluation** you fill in the peer ranking form. You will rate with a grade between 1-10 and you will give your arguments for your rating (see ”Motivation”).

You do so for each of your team members AND for yourself ( = self-evaluation).

Please upload the forms in Moodle, *2 working days before the feedback session at the latest*.

In case this is technically impossible, your coach will make other agreements with the team.

Obviously every all team members are supposed to be present during this session in order to give each other constructive and well-argued feedback.
