**<u>Student’s name:</u> Thomas Wielinga**

1.  **Group or team**?

*Now you have reached the final phase of the event, you can look back on the cooperation within the team. This first question is concerned with the difference between working in a group and working in a team.*

*Do you believe you have worked as a team or as a group (please support your views with a number of arguments)?*

I believe we have worked more as a group than as a team. I feel like most of the time we were working more as individuals and not interdependently towards the same goal

2.  **Team dynamics**

*When working in your event team, to which degree did you experience the phases of group development as defined by Bruce Tuckman? Do you feel Tuckman’s model adequately describes your team or is the second model (= the punctuated - equilibrium model) more applicable?*

I feel like the stages do infact describe our team well. We did go through the stages but I don’t know whether we actually finished the last stage (adjourning).

3.  **Team effectiveness**

*How effective was your teamwork? Did you mutually trust each other? To which degree have you been able to realize team goals? How did you manage conflicts in the team, social loafing and other negative behaviour displayed by members of the team (“deviant workplace behaviour”)?*

I think our teamwork was effective most of the time, however sometimes I felt that the difference of performance differed a lot depending on who did the task. This has to do with understanding of the topic as well as the thoroughness of working. The trust for the most part was there and the team goals that we sett were for the most part realized. There were inmy opinion no real big conficts in the team only some smaller ones. Most conflicts however were about either planning or deadlines so I feel like that is something that we all need to work on.

4.  **Team compostion and size**

*How do you look back on the composition and size of the team? Was the team in balance, to some degree (cf Belbin roles) or was it disbalanced? Either way, how was this reflected in team cooperation, team decisiveness and the team’s ability to act?*

I feel like 5 people for this project is exactly enough. This is because most of the exersizes you can do with the team rather effeciantly, and if for some reason people don’t show up you can still do the assignments that you get for that day.

5.  **Interaction within the team**

*How did you experience interaction? To which degree can you observe differences in status between team members? To which degree were all team members able to have their say? Are you satisfied with this?*

I think the interaction within the team was fine, sometimes there were some miscommunications. I think we tried to give all team members their say. I also think that splitting up into subgroups was also a very good idea since then everybody gets to talk and all the ideas come above bourd. In the end I am satisfied with this team and the interaction within the team.

6.  **Team cohesion**

*How did you experience cohesion in the event team (i.e. to which degree were you a closely knit team)? How do you feel about the achievement norms that you had as a team? Was the bar set high enough? Is there, to your mind, a relationship between team cohesion, the efforts made by the team members and overall productiveness?*

I think in the beginning we were not theat closely knit but at the event itself I felt that we were actualy a team and we respected everybody within this team. I think we set the bar on a good enough hight however if I were to do this over again I would set the bar higher because I think that we had a good event but that extra step to make it amazing was missing in my opinion. The productiveness in some classes was mediocre but I think we all played a big part in our event.

7.  **Decision making**

*How did you take decision in the team? To which degree did group members feel free to express their opinions and could differences in opinion be openly discussed? Did you observe decision making phenomena such as groupthink and/or groupshift within the team?*

The decision making in the team was most of the time democratically. There are of course always individuals that have a more loud voice but in my opinion I think we all contributed to our decision-making process. I think we made decisions mostly as a groupthink because we made decisions based on the pressure that they get from the group.

8.  **In retrospect**

*What have you learned from working in a project team? Did you like it or hate it? What will you do differently next time round?*

In the process of creating this event I have really learned how fun but also sometimes how difficult it is to work in a team. I liked working in this team, however I sometimes did struggle with the fact that you need to rely on people to do their things and you cannot really influence the fact that they don’t do it. Next time round I would change two things: make it more clear to everyone that deadlines are deadlines. And that you should not underestimate how difficult it is to advertise and actually get people to come the event.
