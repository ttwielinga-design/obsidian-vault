**What to know for the oral**

**Week 1:**

BMC – when you can fill in all the 9 slots all the “business problems” are solved

1.  Value proposition; the products and services that will create the value for your customer segments

2.  Customer segments; your target group, for whom you are creating value

3.  Channels: What are the touchpoints between you and the customer for delivering the value

4.  Customer relationships: The type of relationships you have with your customers and how you retain them

5.  Key partners: Who can help you elaborate your business model

6.  Key recourses: What you need to create, deliver and catch the value

7.  Key activities: on which parts you have to perform well

8.  Revenue streams: How will we generate revenue, where does the money come from

9.  Cost structures: What will cost money, how will you generate money

The right side of the BMC is what everyone can see and the left side is what is going on backstage

Our group WHY is:

We wanted to give back the connection that people have been missing because of COVID. We wanted to make people smile and feel acknowledged.

The focus points are:

**Innovation**: authenticity, we wanted to be innovative with our idea and give an al around amazing experience. We did this with in the pub quiz by not only asking fun and interesting questions but also giving a speech about the red cross who they are and where the money that they donate(d) will go to.

**Hospitality**: meaning full experience, we wanted to give a meaningful experience we did this by personalizing by greeting them at the door by name before the event, as well as after the event.

**Sustainability**, as for being sustainable we did all our marketing via the internet so we would not need to print out fliers or letters. The SDG’s that we wanted to focus on were climate action, zero hunger, and peace justice & strong institutions. We tried to help these goals by donating the money to the red cross.

**Gastronomy**: meaningful experience, we had two gastronomic elements in our event the first one was that we gave everybody as a snack during the pub quiz a little snack of bitterballen (option vegan) and of course people could also order drinks at any time during the breaks. The second thing we did was have some quistions about food and drinks in the pub quiz.

Divergent thinking: no judgement, no limits, extremely creative

Imagineering: having the 4 focus points and from there (engineer) and imagine

**7 steps to measure the impact **

- What was the social issue you were addressing with your event? 

  - The social issue we were addressing with our event was: loneliness.

- What is the scale of this problem? 

  - The scale of this problem is global, but for this event we approached it on a local scale (Houten).

- What is the contribution of your event to this problem?  

  - Our event tried to (re)connect people that because of COVID have not seen a lot people. We did this by creating a fun and interactive activity, a pub quiz.

- What impact did we expect?  

  - We expected to make an impact by (re)connecting people

- What is the impact you achieved? 

  - The impact we achieved was that we had people come together have an enjoyable evening and we aswell they have been made aware of the challenges the world is facing at the moment and where their contribution is going to.**\**
