# Chapter 1: Company Analysis

This chapter analyzes Aedes by using the BMC (Business Model Canvas): how the building blocks match with the strategic objectives and operations. It looks at possible misalignments and their implications for marketing, communication, sales, and revenue management by focusing on some key matters: Customer Segments, Value Proposition, and Revenue Streams. Strategic insights are used to reveal the opportunities for improvement that keep Aedes competitive and in tune with its mission. This analysis points where the company has room to work more harmoniously and utilize resources better by indicating inconsistencies and proposing viable solutions

## 1.1 BMC analysis

***Customer Segments***

Aedes targets very wide customer sectors, including but not limited to hotels, individuals (tenants), and businesses (also renters of real estate). These represent both B2B and B2C clientele.

***Value Proposition***

Aedes' value proposition—"Creating places with heart and soul that have a positive impact"—shows that they want to be sustainable and to develop responsibly. There is, however, a slight mismatch in how this value proposition is realized in various client segments. For example, hotel operations and real estate asset management have different measures of success; guest satisfaction would be the measure of success for the former, while appreciation of assets would be for the latter. In those cases, Aedes' mission does not explain how such "positive impact" would come into play. One possibility I see is to reword the value offer so that accrued benefits of each segment are featured. Hotels would, therefore, likely to focus on guest experience, and real estate might focus more on long-term financial and social sustainability.

***Channels***

Aedes multi-channels using social media, networking, and conventions to market themselves. Using these different marketing tools, it is relatively easier to target B2B as well as B2C markets.

***Customer Relationships***

Aedes maintains customer relationships through landlord services, automated services, and communities. This ranges from assisting in maintenance and up keep of key systems, like the HVAC in hotels, to assisting customers directly with housekeeping through the hotels.

***Revenue Streams***

Aedes creates value from a variety of revenue stream—speculation in the purchase and sale of real estate, real estate development, renting, asset management, and hotel operations. This diversification is at the same time its strength and its weakness. The complexity of the hospitality portfolio and that of real estate may set up conflicting demands for resources (time and money). Aedes could tone down this inconsistency by making it a specialized company within itself-one dealing with real estate development and another dealing with hospitality management since this is the case at present. It would allow the firm to align its resources and capabilities more worthily with each revenue stream's demands.

***Key Resources***

Aedes' key resources are among others skilled people, financial resources, physical assets and intellectual property.

***Key Activities***

Its main activities include feasibility studies, research, design, financial control, hotel management amongst others.

***Important Allies***

Aedes depends on real estate agents, contractors, architects and consultants as key partners. These assist in the purchasing, designing and building of the real estate.

***Cost Structures***

The cost structure for Aedes includes operational costs, cost of labor, and project-based expenses together. One area of incongruity in this regard is that the operations cost may turn out to be in the higher side since the company covers a wide range of activities. This can be reduced by streamlining the processes and use of technology-driven efficiencies, such as even more than currently the case, automated financial management system. A lean management style, especially regarding asset management and operation would enable Aedes to be profitable at the same time achieving operation excellence.

## 1.2 Conclusion

The cost structure of Aedes includes the cost of operation, cost of labor, and project-based expenses put together. One area of inconsistency in this regard is that the operations cost may turn out to be on the higher side—since the company covers a wide range of activities. This can be minimized through the rationalization of processes and the introduction of technology-driven efficiencies, like automated financial management system, more than is the case currently. With a lean management philosophy, especially in asset management and operation, it can make Aedes reach profitable operations with the achievement of excellence within the operation.

### 2.1.1 Financial Performance Review 

When it comes to finances, Aedes wants to stay profitable and keep head office costs low. For the Amstel project, one key KPI is the approval for refinancing, which will allow the Amstel project to stay on track. Another goal is to keep Operating Supplies & Equipment (OS&E) costs within 5% of the budget to control running costs. For De Durgerdam, a target of 24% yearly revenue growth and a target of 170% YoY GOP growth, supports a strong and ambitious financial base for current and future growth trajectories at this property.

Monthly P&L reviews at De Durgerdam are an important step to monitor costs and income regularly. These reviews will help De Durgerdam stay on budget and make quick adjustments if needed. Together, these financial KPIs give Aedes a full view of its financial health and cost control efforts.

### 2.1.2 Contributions to Key Goals

***Goal 1: Improve Guest Experience and Brand Reputation***

In the Customer area, the focus is on raising guest satisfaction and loyalty at De Durgerdam. My role in this goal included helping look over post-stay surveys and follow-ups, which help gather useful feedback from guests. We also encouraged reviews on TripAdvisor and Google, aiming for a 4.5+ star rating, to boost the hotel’s reputation. These actions support our goal of 85% guest satisfaction and a 15% increase in repeat guests. By focusing on feedback and reviews, we’re building a brand that looks to listen to its guests and makes them feel valued, helping De Durgerdam retain loyal customers.

***Goal 2: Promote Innovation and Sustainability***

In the Learning and Growth area, Aedes is committed to sustainability and team engagement. I supported this goal by writing and updating the PoR (Program of Requirements) for sustainability (Aedes, 2024b). In this way I tried to contribute to the Sustainability team that working towards the Green Globe certification for Amstel 111. This team’s work on biodiversity, CO<sub>2</sub> reduction, and circularity ensures Aedes meets its long-term sustainability goals. Partially based on the sustainability PoR there was a sustainability training program created, aiming to have all staff trained in sustainability by Q2. By promoting environmental responsibility and personal development, Aedes builds a motivated workforce aligned with its sustainability goals.

## 2.2 Evaluation of the HR Policy or Strategy

Aedes' HR policy, even though formalized to a minimum (meaning there is no specific people hired for this role), contributes to achieving BSC objectives. By outsourcing HR functions (like end-of-year reviews) to limited interactions, Aedes supports financial and internal process goals while maintaining efficiency. This evaluation examines these contributions through actions and KPIs linked to internal stakeholders.

### 2.2.1 Financial Perspective: Cost Management and GOP Growth

Outsourcing essential HR tasks reduces overhead, directly enhancing financial performance. This approach supports GOP Growth by focusing resources on revenue-generating activities.

The Hospitality department's role in staffing further aligns with financial goals by consolidating HR responsibilities, maximizing efficiency, and ensuring recruitment accountability. Regular P&L reviews at properties like De Durgerdam reinforce the cost-saving impact of this streamlined HR approach.

### 2.2.2 Internal Process Perspective: Manning Guide and P&D Roadmap

Instead of a formal HR department, Aedes uses Hospitality-led staffing initiatives to meet Internal Process objectives. The P&D Roadmap KPI, targeting 100% staffing by opening and a complete employee journey by Q2, benefits from this hands-on strategy. Hospitality staff manage recruitment and workforce planning, ensuring alignment with operational goals.

This involvement minimizes recruitment misalignment, supporting the Critical Path KPI of 80% adherence for Amstel. Monthly HR check-ins track recruitment progress and milestones, ensuring readiness for opening.

### 2.2.3 Reinforcement through Stakeholder Actions

The Hospitality department's proactive development of the manning guide and P&D roadmap ensures staffing aligns with operational needs. These actions drive cost efficiency and operational success while integrating HR responsibilities into Aedes' framework.

### 2.2.4 Conclusion

The ‘modern’ Aedes HR strategy assist the goals of the BSC: through the outsourcing of HR and embedding staffing within the Hospitality department, Aedes is in a position to cut costs and optimize operations, with a direct contribution toward the financial and internal process perspectives. This focused and KPI-driven approach brings into sharp focus how internal stakeholders are able to realize strategic success sans a traditional HR setup.

# Chapter 3: Leadership Analysis

In this paragraph I am going to talk about two managers at Aedes. In order to talk about them objectively I am going to use the situational leadership model and Maxwell’s 5 levels of leadership.

## 3.1 Joost (Head of Hospitality)

When looking at Joost's leadership style through the Situational Leadership Model, there is little doubt that he works most out of the delegating style. This means low directive and low supportive behavior where he gives his team considerable freedom to get on with things themselves, focusing on the result rather than the way something is done. The key to Joost's leadership style is the responsibility he places on each of his team members (aligned with the ethical standards and corporate values). This was a big change for me, as upon coming aboard he provided a laundry list of things for me to do and only checked in with me during our weekly meetings. These check-ins provide the possibility for his team members to update him about projects and any issues they encounter. His ability to delegate and stay on top through these regular weekly check-ins indicates confidence in his people and his belief in empowering his people to work independently.

Based on Maxwell's 5 Levels of Leadership, Joost seems to operate on a level between Level 4, People Development, and Level 5, Pinnacle. Joost is very interested in growing people within his team, as he typically sets challenging but (mostly) reachable goals regularly and equips the team with what they need to do their jobs well. For instance, a couple of weeks ago, he asked me to calculate the linen needs for two hotel properties. The task needed to be completed within three days. He provided me with key information and directed me to other sources I may need. He expected me to create an Excel model myself. I did this within two days, he then reviewed my work, gave feedback on a few minor details, and finally approved it so it could be sent to the supplier. This showed dedication, both in terms of development (of his team) and results (that were obtained).

The leadership style adopted by Joost also hints that he is evolving into a Level 5 leader. The way he treats his team members as equals and gives them the freedom to work promotes respect in the culture, and at a relatively young age, he is still growing as a leader. As he continues to refine his approach, I believe Joost is on his way to becoming a Pinnacle leader who will inspire others through respect and dedication to team development.

## 3.2 Madelon (Head of Assets)

From the SLM perspective on Madelon's style, at the very start of any working relationship (new subordinate), she first adopts a coaching and supporting approach. As Madelon explained herself, she works in a genuine coaching style (directive). This is supportive since she explains things step by step to ensure members have clarity in their heads on what needs to be delivered on a particular project. According to her, with each task that is completed confidence grows. When team members show competence, she automatically moves into a style where more delegation is done. A great example of this was on a project surrounding the refinancing of Amstel 111, whereby I worked with her on slides containing key calculations for a valuation. The slide presentation was crucial since it would be presented to the bank. Madelon gave me complete freedom in designing my slide and only afterward reviewed my work. This was important for my growth, since for the first time, she shifted to a more delegating style, letting me work independently on an important component of the project.

Max's 5 Levels of Leadership, Madelon's style fits Level 2, Permission, and Level 4, People Development. She is one of those highly committed to building strategic, trusted relationships with her team members, promoting openness in communication about work-related, but also personal issues. This personal investment also provides the avenue whereby people share struggles and advances to maintain proximity to her about morale and performance. Her commitment to people's development shines brightly in how she has mentored her team members by providing professional guidance coupled with a genuine interest in growth and welfare for all of them. The relational and developmental blend that Madelon brings to her leadership helps to solidify the loyalty and trust within her team.

By entrusting his team with autonomy and providing minimal yet structured guidance, he allows them to take ownership of their work, cultivating confidence and independence. This approach also resonates with Aedes’ *passion* and commitment to *diverse perspectives* by allowing team members to approach tasks creatively using their own methods.

Madelon’s leadership style mirrors Aedes’ values of *local commitment* and *relaxed hospitality*. Her focus on development ensures that each person feels invested in, aligning with Aedes' commitment to *sustainability* by nurturing long-term talent and growth.

# Chapter 4: Sustainable Development

Aedes is fully dedicated to connecting its projects with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), I have observed that they focus particularly on Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities and Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production. They actively contribute to these goals and are devoted to moving them forward through new initiatives and sustainable practices.

## 4.1 Goal 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

*“Creating Places with Lasting Relevance”-*Aedes (2024)

Aedes' view on sustainability is to "enhance urban environments by integrating eco-friendly design and construction practices" (Aedes, 2024). This is in line with SDG 11, which aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable (United Nations, 2024).

### 4.1.1 Key Achievements and Innovations over the Years at Aedes with Their Projects:

- 2011: The Aquifer Thermal Energy Storage system introduced in Burgerziekenhuis represented their concerns about energy consumption and sustainable building. (Aedes, 2024a)

- 2012: The Sedum roofs and the ATES in Andaz Amsterdam brought amenity to energy efficiency and biodiversity-enabling sustainable urbanism. Aedes 2024a.

- 2017: Solar panels and landscape restoration work at the Landgoed Vels estate symbolize the high standards of sustainability, in that resilience in urban areas is promoted.

- 2018: An example of water resource management with the green-blue roof and Permavoid system that has already been installed in Soho House Amsterdam makes it more sustainable in respect to its urban infrastructure.

- 2020: The zero-gas apartment building in Sarphatistraat 24 symbolizes their interest in carbon footprint reduction and energy-efficient residential construction, toward mitigation of urban pollution levels.

- 2021: The Mannoury smart roof project brings forth insight into sustainable roofing. This furthered their knowledge on implementing science to build resilient urban areas.

### 4.1.2 Innovation Laboratory

Aedes also has its innovation lab, Aedes Cares, which researches new concepts relating to sustainability. In this regard, it has support for glass recycling and advanced solar panels (Aedes, 2024a). These, therefore, add to SDG 11, as they reflect innovative responses towards sustainable urban living.

### 4.1.3 Sustainability PoR

Aedes' sustainability approach combines cutting-edge principles of energy performance and operation practices in all projects. This approach provides for and surpasses the minimum requirements of sustainability, therefore directly contributing to SDG 11 by ensuring that projects are sustainable and resilient.

This can be noticed, for example, in Amstel 111-a project currently under construction right beside Carré. Here, I contributed by updating the sustainability PoR of Aedes which will be used for all future hotel developments. The ways Aedes categorizes sustainable achievements is as follows:

- Core: Focus on reusing existing materials contributes to SDG11 by following sustainable construction methods respectful to the historical value of the building.

- Extra: Amenities like native plants planting and insect hotel will add to more urban biodiversity, making the ecosystem of the urban resilient.

- Innovation: Upcycling of materials and electric boat transport proves our concern for innovative solutions to sustainably support urban development.

In summary, Aedes is committed to Goal 11—i.e., Sustainable Cities and Communities—through its innovative environmental-friendly initiatives. Aedes promotes sustainable urbanism and resilience in cities by the integration of systems such as ATES and Sedum roofs, pushing the envelope for such techniques as green-blue roofs and smart roofing. Further, their innovation lab and sustainability PoR showcase how they work on building long-lasting and environmentally sustainable urban settings. Really bringing home their commitment to this SDG and sustainability in general.

## 4.2 Goal 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

Their commitment to sustainability is not limited to a single SDG. For instance, they act on SDG 12 by facilitating the efficient use of resources and waste reduction across all functions of the business. United Nations 2024.

### 4.2.1 The way Aedes Measures

Aedes has developed a set of criteria (as mentioned in 4.1.3) for Core (the minimum), Extra (over and above), and Innovative (out of the ordinary) sustainability measures for its residences. Some examples of these are as follows:

**Core Measures**

- By using low-emission, FSC-certified material and designing demountable building elements, Aedes intends to minimize waste and ensure responsible material usage.

- Air quality and material specification for a better living space and efficient use of resources.

**Additional Measures**

- Assuring a room is optimized for quality sleep is their pledge to sustainable consumption with better overall health and comfort. This is further evidenced by the fact that one of the USPs of Aedes is the promise of "best sleep ever.".

- Including blue roofs and high-quality materials use fosters environmental sustainability; in that respect, it enables responsible production and consumption.

**Innovative Measures**

- Upcycling of glass for windows, application of biobased materials reduces general waste, promotes the use of sustainable materials for SDG 12.

- Zero-waste application and making sure of radical transparency serve as our reflections to efficient resource use and responsible means of production.

To sum up, Aedes' innovative measures set the company apart as an industry leader in setting new norms on sustainability. These are aligned perfectly with SDG 12 and contribute to better operational efficiency and environmental stewardship, indicative of Aedes's commitment toward a more sustainable future.

## 4.3 Future Contributions

If I were to continue with Aedes in a working/professional capacity, I would look forward to enhancing Aedes' contribution to SDG goals by:

- More sophisticated measures could be developed and applied to capture sustainability aspects, such as reduction of carbon footprint and resources efficiency. Such new KPIs might be of relevance and increase the transparency of their environmental footprint.
