Learning goals were:

**Improve communication clarity:** Implement the "plus" technique in at least three different communication scenarios each month over the next three months, aiming for an increase in overall (feedback) communication effectiveness as seen in peer feedback.

I used the plus technique through out module 10 because I felt like I was not communicating affectively enough with my group. Sometimes I wanted to convey a certain point and I couldn’t properly substantiate myself. This really bothered me.

So the task that I assigned myself was to use the “plus” technique, that I learned during one of the workshops, in order to provide better feedback in a more constructive manner.

An example of the action that I took this module was when Sebas, Daphne and I were discussing ideas for the proposal. We went around and instead of just saying that I did not like the idea, which I would usually just do, I tried to add constructive criticism. I remember one of them had an idea to completely change the check-in concept that Penta had. This in my head couldn’t work cause their main concept is based around a checkin bar and desk combination. However instead of only saying this I asked why she wanted to do this and this got the conversation flowing. It turned out she wanted to do it cause from the data we saw that the cross training was an issue. So our solution was we give better and more frequent training. Resulting in an extra point we added to our refurbishment plan for Penta.

Reflecting on this goal I saw that for a long time I wasn’t allowing my classmates the space to substantiate their ideas cause I tend to be quite dominate in some group settings. However this “plus” technique forces me to add why when I don’t agree and makes it so that I can better my own feedback techniques.

**Enhance self-awareness:** Maintain a journal practice, capturing insights on decision-making, teamwork, and honesty for the next two months, with bi-weekly reviews to track progress and identify consistent themes for ongoing development.

I was not content with the way I was feeling during module 9 and sometimes felt quite flustered. I read that journaling can help some people achieve more peace of mind so I wanted to try it out.

The task that I put on to myself was to journal every night to reflect on the day that I had.

This in practice was harder than I expected and out of the 30 days that I tried it I succeeded 25 days. I remember near the end of the project like 3 days before the hand in date I was feeling completely overwhelmed. Not because we weren’t on track but I just expected myself to “win” the pitch with my group. The journaling that night really but a lot of stuff that went through me that day into perspective, cause when I put it down on paper into words I realized that the world wouldn’t end if I didn’t make the presentation.

This resulted in me being way less stressed when I went to bed than that I would have been normally. Probably if I wasn’t journaling I wouldn’t have slept well at all that night.

Reflecting back now on that week I can say that the stress did pay off cause we did win that pitch. However there was no reason for me to get all that worked up for only a grade. I learned that you can achieve things and at the same time have peace of mind. This for me is such a valuable lesson.
