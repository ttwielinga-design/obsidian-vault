**Recommendations for the B&F department to increase revenue and decrease costs**

The first thing I would change, and that I have suggested to management here is to cut people faster when it becomes less busy. (Cutting of course is refers to sending employees home because there are too many employees on-duty.) This would decrease employee salaries significantly. Let me show you how fast this could go in a year with just a simple decrease. On average in my experience, while I have been working here there is at least one WET attendant (\$13 per hour) for 2.5 hours per day that is not doing anything. As well as 1 server and 1 bartender (\$9 per hour) for 2 hours per day. And 1 food runner (\$11 per hour) for 3 hours per day. This would in a year with a low approximation save the hotel at least \$37.000,-.

The second thing I would do is to offer ice cream or some sort of dessert at the pool. Currently, there are no dessert options available for the guests. While I was working as a server at the WET deck guests have asked me plenty of times if we have desserts/ice cream. I always offered them a smoothie or something along those lines however I theorize that if the hotel had a form of dessert or ice cream guests would be willing to pay for that. Therefore the hotel is potentially missing out on some revenue by not offering deserts.

Another thing I would implement is a platform, let’s say once a week, where the employees can discuss with the managers what they feel they need to succeed. I know this is not a direct boost to the revenue or a cost decrease however just like Mr. Marriott said: “Take good care of your employees, and they’ll take good care of your customers, and the customers will come back.” I believe it is vital for the employees at this W to be heard more so that the correct things will change.

Lastly, I would implement more SOPs in order to streamline guest interactions. An example of a SOP that I have started implementing here is to standardize the drinks behind the bar. This means to pour the same amount of alcohol for every cocktail so that the taste of the cocktail doesn’t change when a different bartender is making the drink. I did this by creating a “bar bible”, a book where all the cocktails as well as all the knowledge a bartender needs to succeed is located. I believe that if there were more of these SOP’s and standardize books in place a lot of the complaints that the hotel is currently experiencing, like guests complaining that their pizza was different than the pizza two days ago, would go away.

**Paragraph 3 – UN sustainable development goals**

The sustainability goals that I have chosen are:

- Climate action

- Responsible consumption and production

First let’s talk about climate action. One of the topics of this goal is atmosphere. Making sure that the global temperature doesn’t increase above two Celsius. How the W can contribute to this goal is by reducing the plastic that they are using. This can be done by using metal utensils instead of plastic ones. As well as using bamboo straws instead of plastic wrapped straws. Using reusable cups instead of only plastic cups at the pool.

Regarding the reusable utensils after some research I found that they can still be considered more environmentally friendly when taking into account the energy required to clean them. While doing research I found two studies that can attest to this claim.

Firstly, a study by the city of Portland, Oregon (Durable Dishware Instead of Disposables, n.d.) looked at the different types of utensils, including plastic, reusable plastic, and stainless steel. The study found that reusable utensils had a lower environmental impact than single-use plastic utensils, even when accounting for the energy used for cleaning.

<img src="media/image11.png" style="width:5.625in;height:1.6875in" />Source: Durable Dishware Instead of Disposables, n.d.

An example carbon footprint of disposable packaging (adapted from Eco-Products website)

“Reusable dishware uses far less energy and resources over its lifetime – often thousands of uses in a restaurant or home setting – than its disposable counterparts. Even with the energy and water needed to wash ‘real’ dishware, the overall environmental impact is substantially less than single-use, throw-away items.”

The second study was conducted by the Danish Environmental Protection Agency (Attention Required! \| Cloudflare, n.d.), compared the environmental impact of plastic cutlery and reusable cutlery made of various materials, including plastic, wood, bamboo, and stainless steel. The study found that reusable utensils had lower environmental impact than single-use plastic utensils in most cases, with bamboo and wood being the most environmentally friendly options.

I would also like to point out that using reusable utensils can also save money in the long run, as they do not need to be constantly replaced like plastic utensils do.

Regarding the Triple Bottom Line (TBL) I would like to touch on the fact that the choice of using reusable utensils vs plastic utensils ties into the environmental component of the TBL. Reusable utensils can be considered as a more sustainable and environmentally friendly option compared to plastic utensils.

From a social perspective, the use of reusable utensils can contribute to a more sustainable future, by reducing pollution and harm to wildlife caused by plastic utensils.

From an economic perspective, using reusable utensils can save money in the long run, as they do not need to be constantly replaced like plastic utensils do. Additionally, the W can benefit from this PR-wise because companies that take action on reducing their environmental impact, such as by switching to reusable utensils, can benefit from improved reputation, reduced costs and increased efficiency and innovation.

Then regarding responsible consumption and production, the same as for climate action can be said but can go one step further and look at all the food that is being wasted. In my opinion there are five things the W can do to reduce their food waste.

1.  Food recovery: Instead of throwing food away, the W can look into donating it to local food banks or shelters. Food recovery programs can help to feed those in need and reduce food waste at the same time.

2.  Composting: right now the W is doing no recycling whatsoever. Through composing food scraps and other organic waste they can decrease their ecological foot print.

3.  Education and awareness: the W can educate their staff and guests more about food waste and encourage them to reduce their own waste.

4.  Innovative Solutions: I have read in some articles that hotels are also experimenting with new technologies such as anaerobic digestion (Anaerobic Digestion, n.d.) or food waste digesters to convert food scraps into energy. This might be a bit farfetched currently however I believe that the W as a brand might benefit form looking into this.

5.  Reusing and repurposing: the W can reuse leftovers for the next day’s meals or repurpose them into new dishes.

Every single one of these points apart from number four is possible to implement within one year and I think that the impact can be immense.
