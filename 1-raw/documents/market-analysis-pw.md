Market analysis

When looking at Penta’s competitors we can state a couple of opportunities and threats. First, if we look at the relative change when comparing Penta’s ADR, Occupancy, and RevPAR to its competitors we see that Penta has a better occupancy (12.39%) but is losing out by 9.44% when looking at ADR, which leads to a RevPAR that is only 1.78% higher than the competitive set.

Next, when looking at the larger market environment we can see that COVID has had a large impact on the hotel market in Wiesbaden. Germany’s top 9 cities struggled hard in 2021 recording occupancy declines -8% RevPAR dropped as much as 10.2. What we can see now is that YTD 2022 has had a strong start with KPI’s heading towards normal 2019 levels (reference Cristy here). When zooming in on Wiesbaden we can see that in May of 2020 and 2021 Wiesbaden had an occupancy of under the 20% and we can see the recovery starting to happen in May of 2022 where the occupancy went up to 50% - 64%.

<img src="media/image1.png" style="width:3.96667in;height:2.39375in" />Something that we can see can influence Penta is that Domestic leisure demand is going up as well as the demand for resorts. This could be a problem since they are focusing on corporate guests. A great quote to illustrate this risk is the following one: “However, operators with an all-eggs-in-one-basket strategy and high exposure to single segments or shared accommodations such as hostels and hotels with a large share of group tours and older demographics will likely take longer to recover” (Christie&Co Research).

Another threat is that MICE, corporate, group & international demand is going down. People are realizing that it is also possible to do meetings online. (Ref SDSU)

However an opportunity for Wiesbaden is that city destinations are becoming less popular and rural areas are seeing more traction.
