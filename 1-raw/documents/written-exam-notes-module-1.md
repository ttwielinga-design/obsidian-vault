**USALI – format**

- Revenue generating departments (Rooms, F&B, Minor operated)

- Overhead departments (A&G, S&M, POM, Utilities, ICT)

- Fixed expenses (Rent, Interest, Insurance, Property tax, depreciation/amortization)

**Costs**

- Direct expenses: directly related to a specific department (housekeeping supplies, staff..)

- Indirect expenses: undistributed operating expenses (POM, SM)

- Non-operational expenses: Management fees and PRIO

- Rooms department:

1.  Assess the revenue (ADR, ARR, Occupancy percentage, RevPAR)

> ARR/ADR = $`\frac{Room\ revenue}{Rooms\ sold}`$
>
> Occupancy = $`\frac{Rooms\ sold}{Rooms\ available}\ x\ 100\%`$
>
> RevPAR = $`\frac{Rooms\ revenue}{Rooms\ available}\`$
>
> TRevPAR = $`\frac{Rooms\ revenue + F\& B\ revenue + MO\ revenue}{available\ rooms}`$
>
> NRevPAR = $`\frac{Room\ revenue - distribution\ costs}{Rooms\ available}`$ (directly the hotel)

2.  Assess the expenses (labour costs, other expenses)

3.  Assess the profit = GOI rooms

- Food & Beverage department:

1.  Average spend F&B = revenue per guest

2.  COS percentage

3.  Labour cost percentage

4.  GOI F&B = profit

- Whole hotel:

1.  GOI

2.  GOP

3.  Net profit margin – net profit after taxes

*De bank heeft een <u>eigen KPI</u> aangezien de bank alleen geïnteresseerd is of het hotel de rente kan betalen of niet. Ze berekenen daarom de zogenaamde ‘times-interest earned ratio’ die laat zien hoe vaak het hotel (in theorie) de rente kan betalen. Hoe hoger deze ratio, hoe veiliger en hoe zekerder de bank is dat de rente altijd netjes kan betaald worden. De ratio bereken je door de EBIT te delen door de rente (interest) zelf.*

- A product or experience can be devided into 4 (value adding) product levels:

1.  Core product (basic level) = primary need (bed, room, food)

2.  Facilitating product = enables / necessary to provide the core product (FO, employees)

3.  Supporting products = to add value to the product, can create a competitive advantage (spa, roomservice, 24h survice)

4.  Augmented product = how and where (the customer interacts)

- Physical environment – the atmosphere and the environment (ex a stylish library)

- Customer interaction service delivery system – how the product is delivered (ex. Reviews, word of mouth)

- Customer interaction with other guests – The same customers

- Customer cocreation (let the guest be a part)

- There are 3 approaches for a pricing strategy:

  - **Cost based** (fixed + variable costs + profit margin)

  - **Value based pricing** – what the customer is willing to pay for said product (using a measure)

  - **Competitive pricing**

    - Skimming: higher than competitive set (expensive)

    - Penetration: lower than competitive set (cheap)

    - Hidden: follows competitors, in the middle

    - Matching: follows one competitor

**Owner vs. Operator Dilemma**

1.  **Independent hotel**: entrepreneur runs hotel completely by himself

    1.  (freedom, all profit for himself) (no awareness)

2.  **Franchise contract**: runs by himself but rents a concept

(awareness) (no freedom + franchise fees)

<u>Franchise fee is for the Hotel Chain; if you buy a building and you want to run it by yourself but you want use someone else’s concept</u>

3.  **Lease/rent**: operator rents building from a landlord (Hilton hotels)

(for owner: steady income, no control) (for op

erator: no big investment + monthly rent)

4.  **Management contract**: owner hires a GM to run the hotel

(for owner: no trouble, limited control, manag. fee) (operator: no investment, fee, no profit)

<u>Management fee is for the Hotel Chain (Accor);</u> If you buy a building and want someone else to run it for you (from a Hotel Chain for example)
