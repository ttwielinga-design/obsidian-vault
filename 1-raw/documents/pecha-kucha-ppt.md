13. SMART goals

As for the SMART goals that I set for myself this module I really focused on the personal development side of things. Focusing on self-discipline and self-motivation linking back proclaimed self-direction. My first goal is oriented at improving my communication clarity, and my second goal is about enhancing self-awareness through journaling.

14. Goal 1

**Goal 1 Improve communication clarity:** Implement the "plus" technique in at least three different communication scenarios each month over the next three months, aiming for an increase in overall (feedback) communication effectiveness as seen in peer feedback.

15. S + T

I used the plus technique throughout module 10 because I felt like I was not communicating affectively enough with my group. So the task that I assigned myself was to use the “plus” technique, that I learned during one of the workshops, in order to provide better feedback in a more constructive manner.

16. A

During module discussions, instead of outright rejecting an idea, I applied the "plus" technique, asking for rationale. For example, when a team member suggested changing Penta's check-in concept, I questioned her motivation. This led to a constructive conversation, addressing cross-training issues and enhancing our refurbishment plan with an additional point.

17. Reflection

Reflecting on this goal I saw that for a long time I wasn’t allowing my classmates the space to substantiate their ideas cause I tend to be quite dominate in some group settings. However this “plus” technique forces me to add why when I don’t agree and makes it so that I can better my own feedback techniques.

18. Journaling – 19 - 20

**Enhance self-awareness:** Maintain a journal practice, capturing insights on decision-making, teamwork, and honesty for the next two months, with bi-weekly reviews to track progress and identify consistent themes for ongoing development.

In Module 9, feeling discontent and overwhelmed, I embraced journaling for peace of mind. Tasking myself to journal nightly, I succeeded for 25 out of 30 days. Three days before the M10 pitch deadline, I felt intense pressure to 'win' the pitch. Journaling that night provided perspective, relieving unnecessary stress. Reflecting on the week, I realized success doesn't demand sacrificing peace of mind. The lesson learned is invaluable — achieving goals need not come at the expense of peace of mind, a realization that significantly impacted my approach to stress and success.
