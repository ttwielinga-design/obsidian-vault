For Bonds:

Favorable outcomes (high satisfaction) = 30

Unfavorable outcomes (low satisfaction) = 150

Odds of high satisfaction for bonds = Favorable outcomes / Unfavorable outcomes = 30 / 150 = 1/5

For Stocks:

Favorable outcomes (high satisfaction) = 20

Unfavorable outcomes (low satisfaction) = 240

Odds of high satisfaction for stocks = Favorable outcomes / Unfavorable outcomes = 20 / 240 = 1/12

Now, to calculate the odds ratio, which measures the ratio of the odds of high satisfaction for bonds to the odds of high satisfaction for stocks:

Odds ratio = (Odds of high satisfaction for bonds) / (Odds of high satisfaction for stocks)

Odds ratio = (1/5) / (1/12)

To simplify the ratio, you can multiply by 12 to get rid of the fractions:

Odds ratio = (1/5) \* 12 = 12/5

So, the odds ratio is 12/5.

Interpretation:

An odds ratio of 12/5 means that the odds of high consumer satisfaction in bonds are 12/5 times higher than the odds of high consumer satisfaction in stocks. In other words, there is a higher likelihood of high consumer satisfaction in bonds compared to stocks, with a 12/5 (or 2.4) times higher odds.
