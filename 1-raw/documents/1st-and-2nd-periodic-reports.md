<p><strong><u>Learning goal:</u></strong></p>
<p><em>Understand the restaurant upselling program by week eight of my internship.</em></p>
<p><strong><u>Reflection on the progress made so far using the STARR method:</u></strong></p>
<p><em>A target that is set for the restaurant team is the upselling target. It is one of the KPI’s of the department. Every day at work the team has to seize opportunities to sell certain pre-determined items to the guest. These items are clarified in the upselling program that was explained to me on the second day of my internship. When the team sells these items, the restaurant can increase the revenue of the department. (<strong>S</strong>). All team members are expected to take part in the upselling program. One of my tasks when working a lunch shift is to promote the daily lunch special, sell an additional bottle of water or ask for coffee once the meal is done. By doing this I help increase the average spent per guest (<strong>T</strong>). Every time I am on shift I try to pick one item that is included in the upselling list and try to upsell it to the guest. The first two weeks I have mainly observed my colleagues when they tried to upsell. I noticed that they usually have a similar way of upselling: the timing when to suggest an upsell, the words to use when trying to upsell and the items that can be more easily sold than others. After two weeks my supervisor encouraged me to try and upsell an item myself. Even though I picked the easiest one (a bottle of water) I was a bit nervous to try. I practised upselling a few times with my colleagues just before lunch and that helped a lot. When the first table came in, I just went for it and actually said the same things as I practised before. Every day after the shift the supervisor keeps track of the upsells that have been made. You actually have to fill out a form to note down what you have sold. In the back office of the restaurant there is an overview of all team members and the results of their upsells. The next day you can see that how the ranking has changed. We usually challenge each other and try to sell more than the other (<strong>A</strong>). This week I have been quite successful in upselling bottles of water actually ☺. Just by selling these bottles I have been able to increase the average check of the guests and helped increase the restaurant revenue with € 100! With the whole team we managed to get more than € 1050 additional revenue this week (<strong>R</strong>). Looking back over these last three weeks I learned that upselling is not only trying to make a sale, but that it can also be a win-win for both the guest and the hotel. I thought that upselling was more difficult than it actually turned out to be. I noticed however that not all colleagues are fond of upselling. There are a few of them that remain at the bottom of the list and seem to rarely upsell. I have not yet observed how the supervisor or the F&amp;B Manager has tried to motivate those colleagues to upsell better. Maybe that I could try to involve them in our competition. Most colleagues enjoy the competition and the fact that you can also earn some money yourself. You get ten percent of all your upsells paid out at the end of the month. I am still curious to understand how the items on the upsell list were picked and will try to schedule a brief moment with the supervisor or F&amp;B Manager to discuss (<strong>R</strong>).</em></p>
