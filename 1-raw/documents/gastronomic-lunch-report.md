**Grilled dogfish**

As for our first dish we wanted to work with something which is atypical and unknown to most people; dogfish. This fish will be the hero of our dish. Dogfish is a small shark type which lives in the waters mostly around western Europe, from Iceland up until even the coasts of Senegal. Therefore, the fish lives in the North Sea, where it is being caught. This will contribute to low transportation costs, as it comes straight from our own Dutch waters. This helps making the dish a bit more environmentally friendly, as we don’t use a fish that is being caught in for example China. Secondly, and very important, the population is not endangered. This has to do with the fact that the fish is mostly caught as a side catch by fishermen. The fish reproduces itself throughout the whole year, which is better than a mating season because this way there will be dogfish all year round and helps with sustainability.

We will prepare the fish by grilling it, this might give it a different taste as well as texture. In this way we try to be innovative. Besides the preparation of the fish, we wanted to make sure the presentation is creative or maybe innovative as well. That is why we chose to present the dogfish on a skewer. We have never seen it before, therefore it we thought it was quite innovative.

This brings us to our next subject, which is seasonal. Earlier explained, dogfish is in season all year round. With the dogfish, we are serving a puree made from parsnip. This is vegetable grown during winter and is available throughout the winter and springtime. We thought it would be the perfect vegetable to go with the taste of our fish, as it is a bit sweet and does not have a very distinct taste. Apart from the parsnip being a seasonal product, it is a vegetable which is often being forgotten. That is why we wanted to bring back this beautiful and tasty vegetable.

At last, using these products we created, in our eyes, a healthy dish. The fish we are using contains 14.4 grams of protein and only 0.7 grams of fat per 100 grams of fish. For the preparation of the fish, we only grill the dogfish, so there will be very little fat added to the process. The parsnip itself is a vegetable that has an endless list of health benefits. It is in fact carrot, and a great alternative for potatoes. The carrot contains a lot of fiber which is beneficial for the body. In addition to the dogfish and parsnip, the dish will be served with cucumber and a marinade made from honey, lemon and thyme. In conclusion, a healthy, innovative, sustainable and seasonal dish.

**Asian turbot dish**

 

As for our second dish we have gone for a turbot. The dish is complemented with some drops of wasabi. This dish shows great variety and diversity, and it also applies to the standards of sustainability, innovation and health. 

 

The turbot fish is at its prime season during the months August till February. This means that the product is right now a seasonal product. Turbot fish are loaded with vitamin B3 and B12, and many minerals such as magnesium, selenium and phosphorus, which support your immune system, bones, etc. Because of those healthy aspects, we choose the turbot fish as our main ingredient. As far as sustainability concerns, the turbot fish is a fairly sustainable product. The latest stock assessments will tell you that it is in a healthy state with biomass above the MSY Btrigger. Only about 8% of the turbot fish is taken by trap and have a low impact on other species and habitats. 

 

As for the shiitake mushrooms, we had the nice idea of growing them ourselves. By growing the shiitake mushrooms on sawdust, the product will become even more sustainable than it already is. Shiitake mushrooms are proven to contain compounds which support your heart health and improve your immune system. There is no season connected to the shiitake mushroom, merely because of the fact that you can even grow it yourself. We also think it is in this way fairly innovative to get a product. The process of growing shiitake by yourself might take some time, but once the shiitake has grown it can be used for harvest for 4 to 6 years. 

 

Aside from the wonderful flavour cassava has, the root also holds lots of nutrients such as carbohydrates and vitamins B and C. Next to the cassava, we also have implemented wasabi in our dish, which also contains great health benefits. Such as boosting the circulatory system, cutting cancer risk, checks cholesterol, etc. Because of the strong flavor of the wasabi, we choose to add the product in a minimalistic way, so the balance of flavors will not be out balanced.

Grilled dogfish dish

**Dogfish:**

Grilling is a process that involves cooking food on a rack over a heat source, usually a charcoal fire or ceramic briquettes heated by gas flames. Grilling makes the dogfish taste very pleasantly, the direct heat quickly sears the outside of the fish, producing a distinctive robust, roasted and sometimes charred flavour with a nice crust.

First, we have to separate the skin from the fish, after that we remove the spine and the unwanted parts. Then we are left with dogfish fillets we put them in a marinade of honey (Zest of) lemons, Thyme, garlic.

Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Sprinkle the fillets with 1/8 teaspoon of salt. Add fillets to the grill rack coated with cooking spray, grill 3 minutes then turn it for another 3 minutes until the desired degree of doneness.

**Pickled cucumber rolls:**

Pickling is the process of preserving or extending the shelf life of food by either anaerobic fermentation in brine or immersion in vinegar. The pickling procedure typically affects the food's texture, taste and flavour.

The result of the pickling process is that it makes the foot more sour and it gives a more intense flavour.

Slice the cucumbers in thin pieces, make sure that you use cucumbers with a few seats. Sprinkle them with a tablespoon of coarse salt, after that toss the cucumbers with the salt and let them sit for up to 3 hours in the refrigerator. Rinse them of to get rid of the excess salt.

Put them in layers in a jar, add some springs of dill. To make the brine, we will be needing a medium saucepan, combine 3 cups of white vinegar or apple cider vinegar after that add 1 cup of sugar, 2 tablespoons of coarse salt, half a teaspoon of mustard seed and celery seed. Bring to a boil, stirring it until the sugar dissolves. Transfer the brine in a measurable cup, add the hot brine in the jars to completely cover the cucumbers. Make sure to cool them of before putting them in the fridge.

**Parsnip purée:**

Puréeing is taking a firmer substance like a potato for example and mashing it using a potato masher. This is usually done until the substance is nice and smooth with no lumps in is anymore. The mouthfeel of a puree is very coating and less firm.

For the parsnip purée we are going to boil it together with some garlic, cream, milk and butter. When that is boiling we are going to reduce the heat, cover it and let it simmer for 10 – 15 minutes. Then we are going to uncover let the liquid reduce by 50% and add some salt. After this we are going to blend it to a nice and smooth purée

**Dogfish skin:**

<img src="media/image2.png" style="width:6.3in;height:3.21319in" />As for the dogfish skin we are going to remove the skin from the flesh of the dogfish. After this we are going to place the skin, scale side down, on a baking tray with baking paper on it. Then we are going to place the skin in the oven at 50 degrees Celsius for about 4 hours/until it dries out. Don’t forget to put the fan on in the oven. It is done when it is sure crispy and you can easily snap pieces off. To fry it you can take a little piece and place it into a deep fryer at about 180 Celsius until it puffs up to about 8 times the size.

**Pan frying the turbot & shii-take** – ‘**Pan frying** or **pan-frying** is a form of [frying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frying) food characterized by the use of minimal [cooking oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cooking_oil) or [fat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fat) (compared to [shallow frying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shallow_frying) or [deep frying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_frying)), typically using just enough to lubricate the [pan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frying_pan).[<sup>\[1\]</sup>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_frying#cite_note-McGinnis_2006-1) In the case of a greasy food such as [bacon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacon), no oil or fats may need to be added. As a form of frying, the technique relies on oil or fat as the heat transfer medium,[<sup>\[1\]</sup>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_frying#cite_note-McGinnis_2006-1) and on correct temperature and time to not overcook or burn the food.[<sup>\[2\]</sup>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_frying#cite_note-Boskou_Elmadfa_2016-2) Pan frying can serve to retain the moisture in foods such as meat and seafood.[<sup>\[3\]</sup>](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_frying#cite_note-Publishing_2005_p._198-3) The food is typically flipped at least once to ensure that both sides are cooked properly’

The turbot will be pan fried, as well as the shii-take.

**Beurre blanc** – ‘***Beurre blanc*** ("white butter" in French) is a warm emulsified [butter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Butter) [sauce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sauce) made with a [reduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reduction_(cooking)) of [vinegar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vinegar)and/or [white wine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_wine) (normally [Muscadet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muscadet)) and grey [shallots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shallot) into which, softened, whole butter is whisked in off the heat to prevent separation. The small amount of emulsifiers naturally found in butter are used to form an oil-in-water [emulsion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emulsion).’

In our Beurre blanc, we processed fish stock from shii-take and fish skeleton in it. We also decided to add some Hollandaise sauce.

**Fish stock** – “**Fish stock** is a kind of [stock](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stock_(food)) used for fish dishes. It is used in [French](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_cuisine) and international [cuisine](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuisine). This stock is the basis of [fish soups](https://simple.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fish_soup&action=edit&redlink=1) and [sauces](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sauce). It is usually made with fish bones and fish heads, together with finely chopped [mirepoix](https://simple.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mirepoix_(cuisine)). The fish stock is cooked for 20–25 minutes—cooking any longer spoils the flavour. Concentrated fish stock is called "fish fumet".”

**Chosen wine for with the Turbot: Chablis first cru ‘beauroy’ 2017**

1.  The taste profile of the turbot dish shows a quite balanced flavor combination. The flavor intensity is grade for a 6. Because of the creamy Hollandaise sauce and the medium fatty fish, the coatyness is a dominant factor for the mouthfeel. Due to the vinegar in the sauce and the wasabi oil, this dish also has some contracting elements, but they don’t predominate the coating mouthfeel. The aroma of the dish has some slight fresh elements, but the ripe aroma is just a little more present. 

> All together, the dish has a coating mouthfeel with some contracting elements, a medium intense flavor intensity and a light dominant ripe aroma. As we learned from the book ‘The essence of gastronomy’ from Peter Klosse, you want the wine for that dish to correlate with the taste profile of the dish. In the most ideal situation, the matching wine will have the same ‘grades’ for every part of the taste profile.

2.  The vineyard of the grape is located in Chablis, a region that is around 180 kilometers away from Paris. The Serein river splits the Chablis region in two parts. Because of the river that is close to the vineyards, the soil of the yards contains a high amount of clay and therefore also a lot of nutrients for the grapes. These nutrients will give the wine (in the cellar process) a more intense flavor and a more ripe aroma. This correlates with the ripe aroma of the turbot dish and the instense flavor. Also, the river will have a cooling effect on the soil at night, so the grapes won’t ripe more during nighttime. This ensures a slight acid mouthfeel, which corresponds with the light acid mouthfeel of the dish. You may question yourself now with which part of the wine we will match the coating part of the dish. This comes to a very special part of this wine; the grapes were harvested after the summer in 2017, which was a extreme hot summer. Therefore, the grapes in this wine are more sweet than usual. Because of this sweetness, the wine will create a coating mouthfeel which will correspond with the coating mouthfeel of the hollandaise sauce and turbot.

>  The wine is made from the Chardonnay grape, or ‘beaunois’ as the locals in the region Chablis call it. An overal sweet grape. After the grapes are being harvested by hand, the grapes will be weighted and selected. After that, the grapes will go in to a big cillinder, with a plastic bag in it that slowly blows up and therefore pushes the grapes on to the surfaces of the cylinder. The juice of the grape is pushed out. After this process, the grapes will get pressed. The juice that runs out of the grapes immediately is the first cru, which is typical for this wine. The second press, where the grapes are pressed again, has a higher acidity level and is therefore more contracting. After this the wine will get an alcoholic fermentation and a malotanic fermentation. The yeast that is added will let the sugar in the grape juice convert to alcohol, but because of the high rate of sugar in the juice, there will be some sugars left. This will give the wine later on a more coating mouthfeel. Then, the wine will be put in a French oak barrel, which will allow the wine to get a melatonanic fermentation. In this specific fermentation process, the apple acids will switch to CO2, which causes a more balanced (less acid) taste in the wine. The barrels will also give the wine a nutty flavor and aroma, or even a buttery flavor.

# Chapter three: allergens/allergenen

<span class="mark">In this chapter, describe which allergens are present in the two recipes. Also mention the symptoms / illness images that belong to the allergy.</span>

Beschrijf in dit hoofdstuk welke allergenen aanwezig zijn in de twee recepturen. Benoem ook de verschijnselen /ziekte beelden welke horen bij de allergie.

**Grilled dogfish**

One of the allergens in this dish is fish allergy. This can develop at any age, even if one has eaten fish in the past. The allergy can vary from having mild reactions after eating fish or shellfish but some can also have a severe reaction to even touching or breathing in vapors of cooked fish.

Mild allergic reactions to eating fish or shellfish can be seen on the skin as red bumps. These are called hives. Also, an itchy skin is common. Treatment for this kind of rash is first of all to avoid the known triggers. If the cause of the hives is unknown or the trigger can’t be avoided, you can take histamines as medication to treat the hives. Some have to take 4 to 6 tablets a day to control the red bumps. These are available at the pharmacy or drug stores without prescription from a doctor. A side effect of the antihistamines can be drowsiness.

Other mild reactions to fish allergy can be swelling of the lips, a tingling feeling in the mouth and throat or tightening, a runny nose or digestive problems such as cramps, stomach pain, nausea or even vomiting. Severe reactions after eating fish or shellfish have mostly an effect on the respiratory tract. People tend to have difficulties with or a noisy breathing, swelling of the tongue and throat or tightness of the throat and/or wheezing and persistent coughing and dizziness. Eventually someone with a severe reaction can collapse. Young children with fish allergy get pale or tend to flop or hang.

Honey can also cause a allergic reaction. People with a severe allergy to pollen can have a honey allergy. Symptoms are extremely difficulties in breathing or itching or swelling of the mouth, throat and/or skin. The name of the allergic reaction is Anaphylaxis. This reaction is also caused by the sting of a bee. Anaphylaxis can cause a series of symptoms, beginning with a rash, slow heart rate and eventually a shock, known as a anaphylactic shock, blood pressure drops and airways are narrow.

A third and possible allergen that this dish contains is citrus allergy. It is a quite uncommon allergy but it can cause very severe reactions once someone does have this type of allergy. In some cases symptoms show up immediately after contact with citrus fruits, on the other hand it can sometimes take hours to develop symptoms. Reactions mostly take place around the area where the food touched the skin, around and in the mouth. Symptoms can be redness, swelling, blisters, hives, itchy or tingling sensations or a burning feel. In rare cases, citrus allergy can lead to anaphylaxis. Other symptoms are stomach pain, vomiting, coughing, diarrhea and nausea.

Allergy for garlic has mostly the same symptoms as with citrus fruits and honey: hives, nausea, sneezing, swelling of the lips or tongue, anaphylaxis in severe cases or even asthma.

Lastly, wild parsnip can also lead to an allergic reaction, which is called phytophotodermatitis, also known as eczema. Symptoms of this allergy is a rash which develops after plant juices touches the skin in sunlight. This can lead to blistering but even a discoloration of the skin which can last up to several months.
