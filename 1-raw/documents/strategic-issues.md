**Strategic issues**

**How can the restaurant seating area be restructured to make sure that competitors with similar facilities do not out compete us?**

This is a strategic issue because the restaurant seating is a weakness, and we found that this pairs well with the threat that competitors with similar facilities are currently out competing us. The restaurant seating are is a weakness because we found this during our Porters five forces analysis, and this was discussed during the first Q&A that we had with the hotel (porter’s and q&a ref). Next we found that currently competitors with similar facilities are out competing us. This was found in porter’s as well as in the market analysis. We saw that Penta is strugeling to get their ADR at the same level as their competitors and that they are currently ranked fourth out the five competitors when it comes to RevPAR (market analysis ref, porters ref, STR competitors ref).

This issue is important because if this issue is solved a major imbalance surrounding the competitive landscape is solved since the hotel will not have to worry about larger clients not wanting to go to the hotel because they cannot offer the same services as competitors would.

This is an issue for a couple of reasons. First, the average daily rate of Penta is lower than its four main competitors. The competitors are Dorint Hotel Pallas Wiesbaden, Courtyard Wiesbaden Nordenstadt, H+ Wiesbaden Niedernhausen, and Holiday Inn Express Wiesbaden. From Porter's five forces analysis we were able to conclude that out of these four, two have a higher conference room capacity and have more rooms. Since Penta’s market segmentation is mostly focused on corporate, with a 75% exposure to MICE, this is influencing the average daily rate (Bienefeld, 2023). When doing the competitive set analysis, we found that Penta has an average daily rate of €78,17 and the competitive set has an average of €86,31 this is a difference of 9,43%. When looking at the occupancy we find that they differ only 6,1 percentage points, favoring Penta (Gotthardt-Janssen, 2023). This means that the competition can ask more for their rooms and that their occupancy is not impacted as much when they raise the prices and thus hindering Penta’s financial performance. Penta also has two major contracts that restrict its ability to maximize profitability. Marked in the profit and loss statement as GWH, with these contracts the base rate per room is only €38,- instead of the normal BAR or best available rate which is €95,26 on average (Penta Wiesbaden, 2022). Just on these contracts alone, the hotel is drastically decreasing its RevPAR. These contracts also barely breakeven which means that financially speaking they are almost not even worth having. Finally, we have a lack of seating capacity in the restaurant. This is hindering the financial performance because they do not have enough seating area in their restaurant for their larger banquets. In the largest meeting room that they have they can fit 350 people, whilst in their restaurant they can currently only fit 200 (Pentahotel Wiesbaden, 2022; Pentahotels, 2022). This leaves the hotel when their meeting rooms are filled, with 42% of the guests that cannot have lunch or dinner at the restaurant because there are no seats available for them. When looking at competitors you can see that the H+ hotel and the Dorint can accommodate these types of larger groups (H-hotels, 2022; Dorint Hotel, 2022). This is yet again one of the reasons for poor performance.

**How can Penta implement sustainable alternatives to reconstruct the conference rooms? **

The reason this is a strategic issue is because the conference rooms are currently a weakness and to help solve this weakness implementing sustainable alternatives, one of our opportunities, is the way to go. The reason the conference rooms are currently a weakness is because, of the review analysis. Here we found that currently there are six reviews about the fact that the air-conditioning is old and noisy in the living room area and the conference areas (ref review). Furthermore during our visit to Penta our guide told us that the conference rooms had not been fully renovated in over ten years (Q&A Penta Wiesbaden). Next economic sustainability this is an opportunity that we found in our STEEP. \[leg uit wat je vond\]

Solving this strategic issue would be beneficial to the longevity of Penta. In today's world, building a brand that is perceived as sustainable is a requirement; if a brand doesn't adhere, it will be punished. Consumer brand perception now has the power to shape a company's future (Corkery, 2020). So implementing sustainability into the meeting rooms does not only solve the fact that they are becoming out dated it also allows Penta to shape the way their customers need to perceive them.

This can be attributed to three factors. First of all, due to the lack of refurbishments. This can be found in the profit and loss statements that were provided by the hotel (Penta Wiesbaden, 2020). In this statement, we find that there have been no investments in assets in the hotel in 2019 and 2018. Also, during the questions and answering session that we had with the general manager we discussed the capital expenditure plans of Penta for the upcoming years (Bienefeld, 2023). For 2023 they are planning to build air conditioning for the lounge area and invest in furniture, fixtures, and equipment at the same time. There are also talks about the renovations of the façade with the current owners. However, there are no major renovations on the books (Bienefeld, 2023). Next the decline in customers over the last five years. In 2018 Penta still had 74.787 guests. Jumping to 2019 this went up to 85.277 guests (Penta Wiesbaden, 2020). While in 2022 they only had 53.063 guests and in the year before that they had 30.311 guests (Penta Wiesbaden, 2022). Now of course some of these losses in guests can be attributed to COVID and the long lockdowns that Germany had as a whole. However, when looking at the STR data you find that in 2022 most of the hotels in Germany returned to their old occupancies when compared to pre-COVID (Ploppa & Koltze, 2022). This means Penta that the guests' amount should have returned to the 2019 or 2018 levels. But they did not. Finally, there is a lack of sustainability. In today's world, building a brand that is perceived as sustainable is a requirement; if a brand doesn't adhere, it will be punished. Consumer brand perception now has the power to shape a company's future (Corkery, 2020). Currently, the hotel has no plan to invest in sustainability in the near future like some of the other Penta Hotels. Most recently Penta Brussels won the prestigious Green Key award for their leading standard in sustainability (Mice Magazine, 2022). As before mentioned, Penta’s Wiesbaden capital expenditure overview does not include a plan to implement any sustainable changes in the near future. Furthermore, after asking multiple times for a sustainability plan no concrete answer was given. This lack of evidence speaks to the aforementioned statement. The lack of eye for sustainability is making the hotel even more outdated because, right now from the top 12 driving factors influencing what accommodation you choose, 5% of the people say that environmental credentials are one of the most important drivers. Furthermore, the environmental impact that your holiday has is also among the top eight factors in the choice of holiday destinations (Stringer, 2020).

The Balanced Scorecard is a framework that emphasizes the interdependence and connections between the four key perspectives of an organization. The Financial Perspective aims to increase profitability and revenue, and Penta’s target for GOP and GOP margin are €2.370.937,80 and 0,3178, respectively. Penta also aims to increase its total revenue to €7,461,203 and F&B profit to €527,628. Achieving these financial goals is closely linked to the Customer Perspective, where the organization aims to increase customer satisfaction and the number of customers. The target for the number of customers is 96,145.

To achieve these customer goals, the Internal Processes Perspective plays a vital role. Penta wants to increase staff satisfaction and decrease negative comments regarding cleanliness. To do so, they will need to undergo a fragment analysis, this is a way to measure cleanliness. The target for this measure has, sadly, not been provided by the hotel.

The Learning and Growth Perspective is essential in supporting Penta’s goals in all four perspectives. Penta aims to provide cross-training opportunities to employees and they want to have the right amount of people, which will lead to higher efficiency and effectiveness. The target for the full-time equivalent (FTE) is 32,6, and to help the cross training Penta wants their employees to fulfill trainings in the Knowingo app.

Currently the balanced scorecard does give some relevant information that has helped us shape our strategic issues. Out of the six strategic issues that we formulated four are relevant for the balanced scorecard. The living rooms concept, strategic issue one, is quite closely corelated to the objective of making it a neighborhood hotel. The same correlation can be made for strategic issue three and the aforementioned objective. Furthermore strategic issues four and six tend towards the objective of increasing the F&B profit. We formulated issue four knowing that restructuring the restaurant could be what is needed to achieve this ambitious target of half a million euros. It is worth noting that solving these strategic issues would assist in achieving the objectives of the balanced scorecard.
