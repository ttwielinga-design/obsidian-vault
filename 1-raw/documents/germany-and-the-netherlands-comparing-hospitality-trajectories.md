**Chapter: Germany and the Netherlands: Comparing Hospitality Trajectories**

The key differences between the German and Dutch hotel markets must be considered because of BLH's expansion into Germany. As we examine the subtle parallels between these adjacent countries in this chapter, a story of differences and tactical considerations comes to light.

**Economic Scale and Pandemic Impact**

With pre-pandemic peak sales of €15.02 billion in 2019, Germany's hotel business substantially outperformed the Netherlands with €3.1 billion (Statista, 2024a; Statista, 2024b). Both countries saw sharp drops in revenue with the arrival of COVID-19, but estimates indicated that Germany would rebound strongly, with a target of €17.66 billion by 2028, while the Netherlands set a more modest goal of €3.48 billion (Statista, 2024a; Statista, 2024b). Even if Germany made more money, in 2023 the Netherlands proved to be more efficient, earning more per 100,000 visitors (Statista, 2024a; Statista, 2024b).

**Sales Channels Evolution and User Demographics**

Both countries are seeing increases in digital engagement; by 2028, Germany's online sales are predicted to expand sharply, while the Netherlands is setting the standard for digital adoption (Statista, 2024a; Statista, 2024b). Both markets have a younger user base, according to demographic trends, with the Netherlands drawing somewhat more users in the 18–24 age range (Statista, 2024a; Statista, 2024b).

**German Hotel Investment Dynamics (Q1 2022 – Q4 2023)**

Moving on to the investment climate specifically in Germany. Changes in buyer group dominance can be seen as well as varying quantities in the German hotel market's investment climate. According to BNP Paribas Real Estate (2022a, 2022b, 2022c, 2023a), after cautious starts in Q1 2022, the market saw a fall in overall investment volumes by mid-2022, and by Q4 it had further declined. But by the first quarter of 2023, investment in A-locations had completely collapsed, suggesting a shift in strategy toward lower-scale investments and a concentration on smaller agreements despite hesitant signs of recovery (BNP Paribas Real Estate, 2023b, 2023c, 2023d, 2024). This is relevant for BLH when looking at where potential new locations might be seen shortly. This could be due to a market shift toward more conservative investment strategies that prioritize liquidity and manageable projects, which could potentially open up chances in emerging or inexpensive markets that are not generally classified as A-locations.

**Market Overview and Investment Climate**

In 2022, Germany's hotel sector, especially in top locations, saw room rates and RevPAR reaching new highs despite operational challenges (Schappner & Colliers, 2023a, 2023b). Schappner and Colliers (2023a, 2023b) further illustrate the potential investment opportunities that might lie in A-locations. They assert that investment climate highlighted growth in hotel chain rooms, with major cities witnessing aggressive expansion strategies by leading groups (Schappner & Colliers, 2023a, 2023b).

**Supply, Development, and Demand**

In the 2022 Germany Hotel and Chains report Buer (2022) states that the resurgence of high-capacity establishments and nearly 40,000 rooms under construction indicated robust growth. In his demand analysis he reveales there is a growing preference for branded hotels, especially in trade fair cities, where the strategic positioning of chains is evident (Buer, 2022). When linking this back to BLH it is evendent that this growing prefrence for branded hotel is a positive development, because of their collaboration with the major hotel brands.

**Operational Insights and Future Outlook**

Operational challenges have led to increased rates across the German hotel market (Schappner & Colliers, 2023b). Nonetheless, the sector is poised for a gradual recovery, with expansions in the lifestyle and budget segments reflecting confidence in the market's long-term potential despite economic uncertainties (Schappner & Colliers, 2023b).

The conclusion of this chapter reads as follows for BLH: With a pronounced preference for branded hotels, cautious investment methods, and a strong interest in digital engagement, the German market offers BLH significant prospects for economic growth and expansion. They can leverage their understanding of the operational efficiency and digital competency within the Dutch market. Given that these serve as valuable benchmarks (in Germany), they have the potential to shape BLH's strategy in Germany. BLH can develop expansion strategies that are efficient and customized to the specific dynamics of the German hotel industry by utilizing these insights.
