**My Leadership dilemmas from the theory practical week at Refresh.**

The two models I will talk about are:

1.  **The situational leadership model**

2.  **The research by Albert Mehrabian – Silent Messages**

<img src="media/image1.png" style="width:3.17153in;height:3.78333in" alt="Hersey and Blanchard Situational Leadership Model EXPLAINED | B2U" />*Regarding the first model the situation I am going to talk about is regarding Kateryna Trofimova (2063253). Kateryna at the beginning was a good example of a person who had 0 experience with in the kitchen. Throughout the four days we had onsite I saw her develop into a clear RL3 maybe even heading to RL4. Here is how that happened.*

On the first day I could see that she was struggling. I delegated the cold sandwiches to her. She stood at her station, with her arms crossed, not knowing how to start. This to me was a clear indication that she needed some guidance as to how she should approach the dish preparing. Because we only had 1 and a half hours to prepare everything, I directed her on how she should do things. I used a clear S1 (directing) leadership style. I explained to her what to do, I showed her how to do it (the technique), and how to approach the dishes. Then I told her I would come back 15 minutes later to check on her. This worked well and after some last minute helping on my part everything was finished on time.

The second day of kitchen Kateryna helped me with the preparing of the diner dishes. Since we had a really small team and it was for the most part a two-man job we did a lot of it together. On this day I mostly encouraged her and used coaching behavior S2. I saw that Kateryna really got into the swing of things and she was approaching the job with a lot more enthusiasm, she even almost finished everything on her own. She really showed me the RL2 and even some of the RL3 behavior. She sometimes still needed some encouragement and/or instructions but for the most part she was doing things on her own.

On the final day I saw Kateryna showing some different behavior. She was (to my knowing) capable enough now but she, I believe, didn’t really see a challenge anymore. This showed through her slower work pace then normal. That that she bounced in between RL3 and RL4 because when I gave her a compliment and told her you are doing a good job, she really showed me that she was independent and ready to go by herself. But the moment I gave her to much responsibility she slacked a little here and there and didn’t completely finish all her tasks. So, on those aspects I did still need to encourage her to do better and put more passion into it. For me I felt like my leadership style differed between delegating and coaching. Maybe not the best approach because the theory says that I should be supporting and not coaching, but I felt in that particular situation I sometimes had to support her and show her she can in fact do it to encourage her to take the responsibility back.

To conclude I used the readiness levels and the effective leader ship styles in order to coach Kateryna from being a novice to an experienced employee within the kitchen at refresh.

*Regarding the Silent Messages – Albert Mehrabian – I used this in order to coach Caspar van der Vliet (2063594). The situation was as followed. Caspar has worked in the kitchen before and is quite experienced. This is because the place where he worked as a chef has a Michelin star. For me it was quite hard to get through to Caspar because all the feedback I gave him he didn’t expect because, in my opinion, he thought he already knew everything and was better than everyone.*

After seeing this the first two days I talked to him keeping in mind that the words I express may not have an impact on him but, like Mehrabian says, my 93% of my message is how I express my self and not the words I actually use. So, in order to get through to him I tried using more body language and to give a stricter tone of voice.

I told Caspar: “I think you need to show other people your experience instead of doing all your work for 50% and slacking off (…) show us your skills and teach us how to get better at working in the kitchen”. The body language I used here was pretty straightforward. I used my hands openly and my shoulders were laid back signaling that I wasn’t mad or angry. I tried to get him to engage in the conversation with me. Keeping in mind to not be too powerful. Because I didn’t want him to end up being to closed.

Another thing that I kept in mind is not to be too dominant when giving him feedback and when communicating to him. I tried to also show him that he can lead us if we step up and instructs us because I believe he does have more knowledge than most of us combined.

This conversation I believe really worked and I saw that Caspar really stepped up the last day when we were making burgers. He made more than 60 burgers whilst helping the rest of the team as well if they needed it. It to me felt like he did a complete 180 in behavior. He again felt inspired to help his team out and showed to me that he really did know what he was doing in the kitchen.

My preferential style is to be more extroverted and open. I however felt that in this situation I needed to express more power and dominance because I know Caspar is really Red (TTI profile) so I thought that if I came across redder as well that would help me get through to him even more.

To conclude I do believe it worked, being more dominant, I also believe that the message came across better because I kept in mind that I had to be more expressive and using more body language. An expression that I think really summaries this situation is *hard on the content and soft on the relationship*. This is something that I tried to be in this scenario and I believe it worked.
