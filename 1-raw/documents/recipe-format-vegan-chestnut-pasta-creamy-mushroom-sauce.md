<tr>
<td colspan="4" style="text-align: center;">Dish: the Pasta</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">200 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Flour</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">80 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Chest flour</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">140 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Water</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">Preparation method:</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">1</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Mix all the ingredients, salt &amp; some pepper into a smooth dough ball. Knead this for 10 minutes or until the dough feels very soft.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Wrap the pasta dough in Bee's Wrap or in some foil, and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least one hour.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">3</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Take the pasta dough from the refrigerator so it can adjust to room temperature. Slightly flour the kitchen counter and cut the pasta dough in two pieces. Take half of the pasta and roll it out to a thickness of 0.5 cm</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">4</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Use a pasta cutter wheel to cut long strips of dough. Note: I normally create long strips of dough, however you can create any shape of course!</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">5</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Repeat the rolling and cutting until all your dough is shaped into pasta.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">6</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Once the water is cooking, you can add the pasta. The pasta is ready when it floats on top of the water (this will probably take about 2 minutes).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">7</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Drain the pasta, and add it to the mushroom sauce. Heat shortly until the pasta is coated with the sauce.</td>
</tr>

<tr>
<td colspan="4" style="text-align: center;">Dish: Mushroom sauce</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">20 gram (1 shallot)</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Shallot</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Olive oil</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">150 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Chestnut mushrooms</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">0.2 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Bay leaves</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">200 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Mushroom stock</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">100 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">White wine</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">100 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Cream (plant based)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">Preparation method:</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">1</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Clean and cut all mushrooms (400gram) into quarters. Cut the shallot in small cubes and the spring onion into strips.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Heat the olive oil in the frying pan. Once warm, you can add the shallot and the mushrooms. Bake these until brown and cooked.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">3</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Time to separate the mushrooms for the sauce and the decoration! Take half of the baked mushroom/shallot mixture and keep this on the side in a bowl. Just before serving, we'll bake this shortly.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">4</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Add the white wine, madeira, bay leaf and mushroom stock to the frying pan, in which you still have half of the baked mushroom mix. Bring this to a boil and let it cook for at least 15 minutes.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">5</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Add a large amount of water and 1 tablespoon of salt to your cooking pan. Bring this to a boil!</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">6</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Remove the bay leaf from the mushroom sauce, and pour the sauce into a blender. Add the cream and blend until smooth.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">7</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Heat the mushrooms/shallot mixture in frying pan one, and the mushroom sauce in frying pan 2.</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
