**How to Assess a Job Candidate: Look for These Things in Their Resume**

By [YEC](https://www.allbusiness.com/author/yec) \| In: [<u>Hiring & Firing</u>](https://www.allbusiness.com/staffing-hr/hiring-firing/)

<img src="media/image1.jpeg" style="width:3.125in;height:2.45139in" alt="Interview" />

Companies receive a lot of resumes, and deciding which candidates to bring in for an in-person interview can be a hard decision. For many hiring managers, it all starts with what’s on the applicant’s resume. What element makes a resume stand out, allowing that person to move on to the next round of the hiring process?

To find out more, we asked [<u>Young Entrepreneur Council</u>](https://yec.co/) (YEC) members the following question.

**Q. What’s one thing a resume must have in order for you to invite the candidate in for an in-person interview?**

**1. Proof**

<img src="media/image2.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Frederik-Bussler.jpg" />

I always look for proof. If a resume tells me that someone is an expert in AI, I ask myself: “Where’s the evidence?” In order to take a resume seriously, I need concrete evidence for every skill listed, whether it’s a project they built, code they wrote, or even strong references. —[<u>Frederik Bussler</u>](https://www.linkedin.com/in/frederikbussler), [<u>AngelStarter</u>](https://www.linkedin.com/company/angelstarter)

| GOLD SILVER <span class="mark">BRONZE</span> Comment: I see no proof you only put in the info and that is it |
|----|

**2. Effort**

<img src="media/image3.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Blair-Williams.jpg" />

You can tell the difference between a candidate who typed up their resume in five minutes, versus one who spent hours crafting an informative and impressive resume prior to applying. People who put effort into writing their resume instantly win favor in my book. —[<u>Blair Williams</u>](https://twitter.com/supercleanse), [<u>MemberPress</u>](https://memberpress.com/)

| GOLD SILVER <span class="mark">BRONZE</span> Comment: It feels like you only put in the things that came first to your mind and actual experience with more explanation is not in here. |
|----|

**3. Accomplishments**

<img src="media/image4.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Stephanie-Wells.jpg" />

Instead of just listing their skills, I look for candidates who list their accomplishments on their resume. For instance, instead of only listing “social media marketing” as a skill, I like to see an accomplishment related to it, such as “I grew the company’s Facebook group by attracting 2,000 new members in three months.” Anyone can list a skill on their resume, but the results are even better. —[<u>Stephanie Wells</u>](https://twitter.com/thestephwells), [<u>Formidable Forms</u>](https://formidableforms.com/)

| GOLD <span class="mark">SILVER</span> BRONZE Comment: You do show your accomplishments in your education as well as your work experiance |
|----|

**4. Soft Skills**

<img src="media/image5.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Jared-Atchison.jpg" />

If a candidate doesn’t take the time to tell me how they work well with others, communicate, listen, etc., then it’s not someone I’m keen on interviewing. Businesses require soft skills just as much as technical skills, if not more. You can always build onto your technical expertise, but behavioral habits are much more difficult to make and break. —[<u>Jared Atchison</u>](https://twitter.com/jaredatch), [<u>WPForms</u>](https://wpforms.com/)

| GOLD SILVER <span class="mark">BRONZE</span> Comment: I feel that you did not show enough soft skills in your resume you only have your hard skill in there |
|----|

**5. Track Record of Success**

<img src="media/image6.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Serenity-Gibbons.jpg" />

A resume must show qualitative or quantitative results you have accomplished for other employers. This shows a track record of meeting goals and achieving objectives for companies or organizations you’ve worked for.  —[<u>Serenity Gibbons</u>](https://twitter.com/naacp), [<u>NAACP</u>](https://www.naacp.org/)

| GOLD SILVER <span class="mark">BRONZE</span> Comment: You do not have a record of success yet, this could be a great improvement of your CV because then people can see what you have accomplished in your professional life |
|----|

 

**6. Character**

<img src="media/image7.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Syed-Balkhi.jpg" />

Our business is the sum of everyone who works for us. We are all humans with interests, personalities, and families. When I am looking through resumes, I try to pick people who demonstrate that they are charismatic and have character through their writing. These people are usually creative and talented, making them assets for my company. —[<u>Syed Balkhi</u>](https://twitter.com/syedbalkhi), [<u>WPBeginner</u>](http://www.wpbeginner.com/)

| GOLD <span class="mark">SILVER</span> BRONZE Comment: I feel like you do not show your interests, personality, and family in this resume you don’t show your full character. |
|----|

**7. Good Writing**

<img src="media/image8.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Kristin-Kimberly-Marquet.jpg" />

I look for proper grammar, active voice, and strong writing when reviewing and sorting through resumes. If I notice typos, grammatical errors, or other syntax issues, then I will put the resume into the “trash pile”. If a candidate can’t take a minute to proofread their resume, then there’s no reason why I should move them onto the next round. —[<u>Kristin Kimberly Marquet</u>](https://twitter.com/kristinmarquet), [<u>Marquet Media, LLC</u>](http://www.femfounder.co/)

| <span class="mark">GOLD</span> SILVER BRONZE Comment: I see that there are no mistakes, talking about grammar and writing, in your CV which is always good |
|----|

**8. Tailored Content**

<img src="media/image9.jpeg" style="width:1.45833in;height:1.45833in" alt="https://www.allbusiness.com/asset/2019/10/Baruch-Labunski.jpg" />

One thing that’s important to get an interview here is a resume that has some tailored content. If you’re not able to speak to how your experience makes you a fit for the company and position, then it’s not likely going to work. A resume should be a story about the candidate, but that story should be tailored to the job and company. [<u>Baruch Labunski</u>](https://twitter.com/baruch_labunski), [<u>Rank Secure</u>](https://www.ranksecure.ca/)

| GOLD SILVER <span class="mark">BRONZE</span> Comment: It would be great if your were to include some tailored content, this could really spruce up your CV, make your CV more like a story. |
