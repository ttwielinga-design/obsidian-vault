**BODY**

**Theme 1: CSR, RE, and Responsibility in Business**

*Main idea: CSR and Responsibility in Business are gaining prominence.*

- Supporting idea 1: Many firms emphasize corporate responsibility (Tiba et al., 2018, p. 265).

- Supporting idea 2: CEOs recognize the importance of non-financial success (PWC, 2016).

- Supporting idea 3: Governments and NGOs advocate for increased corporate responsibility (Godfrey & Hatch, 2007).

*Main idea: Responsibility in Startups vs. Established Corporations.*

- Supporting idea 1: Startups have environmental and regulatory challenges (Fuller & Tian, 2006; Wang & Bansal, 2012).

- Supporting idea 2: Examples of Uber and Airbnb facing criticism (Hofmann, 2014; Tickle, 2016).

- Supporting idea 3: Investors value responsible behavior in companies (Bocken, 2015; Murguia & Lence, 2015).

*Main idea: Academic Focus on Responsibility.*

- Supporting idea 1: Focus on non-profit social entrepreneurs (Hörisch, 2015; Peredo & McLean, 2006; Santos, 2012).

- Supporting idea 2: Limitations of viewing entrepreneurship as only prioritizing social value (Haugh, 2005).

*Main idea: Expanding Academic Research.*

- Supporting idea 1: Suggestion to explore responsibility in diverse business models (Tiba et al., 2018).

- Supporting idea 2: Benefit of insights from CSR literature for entrepreneurship research (Bacq & Janssen, 2011; Gast, Gundolf, & Cesinger, 2017).

**Theme 2: CSR, Innovation, and Organizational Forms**

*Main idea: Co-evolution of CSR, Innovation, and Organizational Forms.*

- Supporting idea 1: CSR reporting lowers capital cost and enhances transparency (Hu & Zhang, 2023).

- Supporting idea 2: CSR mitigates uncertainty and ethical concerns in innovation (Hu & Zhang, 2023).

- Supporting idea 3: Dynamic nature of organizational forms (Hu & Zhang, 2023).

*Main idea: Impact of CSR on Different Types of Firms.*

- Supporting idea 1: Value of CSR reporting for start-ups and privately owned enterprises (Hu & Zhang, 2023).

- Supporting idea 2: Diminishing influence of CSR reporting as start-ups transition to established firms (Hu & Zhang, 2023).

**Theme 3: Measurement of Corporate Socially RE (CSRE)**

*Main idea: Developing a Measurement Scale for CSRE.*

- Supporting idea 1: Importance of integrating CSR principles into entrepreneurship (Gallardo-Vázquez et al., 2023).

- Supporting idea 2: Development of CSRE-s measurement scale (Gallardo-Vázquez et al., 2023).

*Main idea: Findings and Implications of CSRE Measurement.*

- Supporting idea 1: CSRE involves a combination of entrepreneurship and CSR traits (Gallardo-Vázquez et al., 2023).

- Supporting idea 2: Implications for promoting CSR values in entrepreneurship training and policies (Gallardo-Vázquez et al., 2023).
