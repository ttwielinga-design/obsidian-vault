<td style="text-align: center;">Dogfish filet including skin</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">Preparation method:</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">1</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Separate the skin from the filet. And make sure you cut filets of 150 gram each. (make sure to cut the filets horizontally so you get nice and long ones and not cubes.)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Get all the thyme leaves off of all the thyme stems. Then finely dice the garlic (brunoise). After this make the marinade: add the lemon juice, olive oil, thyme leaves and the garlic (brunoise). Make sure to whisk it until all the ingredients are well mixed.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">3</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Put the filets on a baking tray and pour the marinade on the filets, let it sit for 5 minutes.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">4</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Put the grill on and let the fish grill on direct heat for one minute on each side. After this put it on a tray and in the oven at 160C to finish make sure the core temperature hits 65 degrees.</td>
</tr>

<td colspan="4" style="text-align: center;">Pickled cucumber</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">2 pc</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">cucumbers</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">17 grams</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">salt</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">700 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">White vinegar</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">200 grams</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">sugar</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">10 grams</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Large grains of salt</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">1.75 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Mustard seeds</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">1.75 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Celery seed</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">Preparation method:</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">1</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Cut the ends off of the cucumber and then use a peeler to peel it length wise</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Put the cucumbers in a bowl then sprinkle the cucumbers with the salt and toss the cucumbers with the salt and then let it sit for 3 hours in the refrigerator</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">3</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Now make the brine. Add the vinegar into a saucepan then add the sugar, the salt, mustard seeds. Bring the liquid to a boil and keep on stirring the pan. When the liquid is boiling turn off the heat.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">4</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">After the 3 hours rinse the cucumbers off put them in a jar and add the brine to the jar make sure the cucumbers are completely submerged then add the lid (make sure the substance is room temperature before adding the lid). Then let them pickle in the fridge for 1 – 2 hours.</td>
</tr>

</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">370 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">Parsnip</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">garlic cloves</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">100 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">heavy cream</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">100 ml</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">whole milk</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">25 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">unsalted butter</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">.33 gram</td>
<td style="text-align: center;">salt</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td colspan="3" style="text-align: center;">Preparation method:</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">1</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Wash and peel the parsnip, then thinly slice them into rings after this thinly slice the garlic cloves</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">2</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Bring parsnips, garlic, cream, milk, and butter to a boil in a medium saucepan. When it is boiling reduce the heat, cover the pan, and let it simmer until the parsnip is very soft (10-15 minutes)</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td style="text-align: center;"></td>
<td style="text-align: center;">3</td>
<td colspan="2" style="text-align: center;">Then uncover and cook until liquid is reduced by half, about 5 minutes; season with salt. Purée in a blender until smooth.</td>
</tr>
