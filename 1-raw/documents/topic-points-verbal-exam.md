**Health and safety**

HACCP – Health, Analyses, Critical, Control, and Points – is a prevention plan based on 7 principles:

1)  Make a list of all potential hazards

2)  What measures are needed to control the hazard and establish the CCP’s – Critical Control Points

3)  Indicate the limits per CCP

4)  Establish how they are monitored

5)  What to do when it goes wrong – corrective action

6)  Apply verification

7)  Keep documentation and registration (Horeko)

Personal hygiene:

- Body hygiene

- Short hair and nails

- Wear clean chef clothing

- Chef’s jacket closed

- Don’t wear any for of jewelry or perfume

- Report personal conditions – if bad – before work / class

- Report any wound

- You can’t bring your own food

- Always wash your hands

  - Before you start work

  - After breaks

  - After toilet

  - Performing dirty work

  - Smoking

  - Coughing, sneezing, and blowing the nose

Food hygiene:

- Raw products separate form prepared – CROSS CONTAMINATION

- Clean separate form dirty

- Cover prepared products with plastic

Company hygiene

- Use the right materials

- Disinfect the workbench

- Don’t sit on the workbench

CCP’s for goods

- Correct temperature - \<7C for cooled \>-15 for frozen

- Check packaging

- Check quality

- Check order list

CCP’s for storage

- Raw products separate form prepared – CROSS CONTAMINATION

- Keep meats / fish as cold as possible 0–4 C

- Prepared foods – registered, wrapped, and given a sticker of production date and description

- Prepared foods stored max. of 2 days

CCP’s Prep of food and storage

- Heat products above 75 C - except eggs and red meat

- Serving temp. above 65 C

- Reheating 65 C

Risks during prep.

- Food decay

- Food contamination

- Infections

- Food poisoning, toxic

Disease-causing bacteria such as:

- Salmonella in poultry, fish

- E. Coli intestinal bacterium

- Staphylocoques aureus skin / nose bacteria Clostridium botulinum still water, botulism, canned tins

**Guests – Social hygiene and responsibility**

Managers of the bar in The Netherlands must me:

- 21 years or older

- Not be under legal restraint

- Must have sufficient knowledge

- Responsible behavior

- Must be on the license of the bar

The goal of social responsibility is: to ensure safety for staff and guests. To make sure this is possible a bar can:

1)  Draw up house rules

2)  Enforcing rules

3)  Door policy

4)  Working together with parties

Alcohol and the law:

- Make sure that the sale of alcohol won’t lead to disruption

- Not allowed to serve is someone is heavily under the influence

- Must be 18 years or older

Types of behavior – From guests

- A1 – Making excuses

- A2 – Denying behavior’

- B1 – Criticizing the rule or policy

- B2 – Criticizing the rule enforcement

- C1 – Aggressive behavior

- D – Assault or physical violence
