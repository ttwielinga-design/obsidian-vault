**Questions Interview Florida**

**Stereotypes:**

1.  Did you have any prejudgments when moving to the USA?

    1.  What were they?

**Hofstede’s dimensions:**

1.  What were the cultural differences you were already aware of when moving to Fort Lauderdale?

2.  Were there many different cultures/ethnicities in Fort Lauderdale?

    1.  Which ones were the most prominent?

3.  Do you think the hotel focused on creating a diverse work environment?

    1.  How did you experience this?

    2.  When looking at the main departments, could you sketch us a brief overview of how the teams looked regarding cultures/ethnicities?

4.  How did you experience masculinity at the workplace (e.g., gender roles \[men should do this, women should do that\])?

    1.  And how was this with in private life

    2.  What was the difference, if any, to your own frame of reference (the Netherlands)?

5.  What do you think about the distance of power within the Pelican Grand?

    1.  Were the managers easy to reach if you had a complaint or suggestion?

6.  What did you think of the Long- versus Short-term orientation?

    - *Long-term: good and evil are relative, knows how to adapt, traditions can be changed*

    - *Short-term: good and evil are absolute, someone who is always the same, traditions are there to be kept*

<!-- -->

6.  Generally speaking, do you think the USA is individualistic than the Netherlands?

    1.  And why do you think that is?

**Low- versus High-context:**

1.  Did you ever give feedback to your employees at your work or did you ever receive feedback?

    1.  What is the difference when giving/receiving feedback when comparing it to the Netherlands?

2.  How did the management structure look like at the Pelican Grand hotel?

3.  When making decisions who made them and why?

4.  How does having a disagreement with a coworker play out?

    1.  Is it confrontational? \[head on\]

**Onion model – Schein:**

1.  What were in you opinion the values that the Hotel believed in and that it desired?

2.  Did you see any symbols/artifacts to support these values?

3.  Was there a difference between the things they said that they believed in and what they actually believed in?

    1.  If so, what were the main differences?

    2.  If not, why do you think it is that these align so well at the Pelican Grand?

4.  We read about a reward program for sustainability, was that there when you were an inter? If so, how successful was it?

**Marketing:**

1.  Was there a large emphasis on leisure at the Pelican Grand?

2.  What is their main target segment?

3.  Were, to your knowledge, reoccurring guests normal?

    1.  How did they try to keep these guests coming?

**Salaries and wages:**

1.  How would you describe the average salaries in the USA when comparing it with the salaries in the Netherlands?

2.  Can you explain to us how tipping works in the USA?

    1.  How did this apply to your job?

3.  Could you describe your work to private life to us?

    1.  Did you hang out with your colleagues after work?
