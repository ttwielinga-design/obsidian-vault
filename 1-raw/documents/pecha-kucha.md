Expert workshop 1: Summary.

When most organizations strive to increase collaboration, they approach it too narrowly: as a value to cultivate—not a skill to teach. So they create open offices, talk up collaboration as a corporate goal, and try to influence employees through other superficial means that don’t yield progress. Companies that excel at collaboration, in contrast, realize it involves instilling the right mindset: widespread respect for colleagues’ contributions, openness to experimenting with others’ ideas, and sensitivity to how one’s actions may affect teammates and outcomes. What’s more, these firms have established programs to help employees develop those attitudes.

In her research, Gino, an HBS professor, has identified six types of training techniques used by such programs. They teach people to (1) listen, not talk; (2) practice empathy; (3) be comfortable giving and receiving feedback; (4) lead and follow; (5) speak with clarity and avoid abstractions; and (6) have win-win interactions.

Drawing from her observations of Pixar, Webasto, and other companies, the author shares specific tools and exercises that show employees how to work well together, learn from one another, and connect more fully.

Expert workshop 2:

Summary of the M10 Expert Workshop "Getting what you want: Negotiate your way to success" by Astrid Janssens:

This expert workshop focused on developing negotiation skills for success. The workshop consisted of the following sections:

1\. Introduction: The workshop began with an introduction to negotiation, highlighting the importance of being comfortable with negotiation, assessing the benefits and costs, and determining whether it is a deal or a dispute.

2\. The Negotiation Analysis: Participants were guided through analyzing their overall goals, identifying important issues, determining their Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement (BATNA), reservation price, most likely price, and stretch goal. The Zone of Potential Agreement (ZOPA) was calculated based on this analysis.

3\. Strategies & Tactics: Strategies discussed included getting to know the other side personally, developing power through knowledge and a strong BATNA. Tactics such as questioning the other side's BATNA, using framing, avoiding overconfidence, and taking a big-picture perspective were explored.

4\. The Importance of Body Language: Participants learned to identify baseline body language, monitor important signals such as engagement and stress, look for gesture clusters, and consider the context in order to effectively interpret body language cues during negotiations.

5\. Negotiation Game: A negotiation game or simulation was played to apply the skills and concepts learned during the workshop.

The workshop emphasized ethical considerations throughout, encouraging participants to consider their actions from a moral standpoint and to treat others as they would like to be treated.

Overall, the workshop aimed to equip participants with the necessary skills and knowledge to negotiate effectively and achieve successful outcomes.
