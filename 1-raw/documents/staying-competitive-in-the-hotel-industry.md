The Penta Hotel Wiesbaden is located in the historic spa town of Wiesbaden, 2 kilometers from the Wiesbaden Hauptbahnhof (main train station) and 20 kilometers from Frankfurt Airport. The hotel has 209 rooms over six floors. It attracts both business and leisure travelers as it provides convenient access to many of the city’s main attractions and amenities. The hotel is part of the Penta Hotel group which consists of 20 hotels. Up until 2019, Penta Hotel Wiesbaden showed an upward trend in revenues. Since the coronavirus pandemic in 2020, the hotel landscape has changed drastically. This has also impacted the Penta Hotel in Wiesbaden. What are the issues that Penta Hotel Wiesbaden is currently facing because of this change in landscape? First, the hotel is becoming outdated and therefore less attractive than other hotels in the neighborhood. Also the hotel has shown poor financial performance since 2020.

The hotel is becoming outdated. This can be attributed to three factors. First of all, due to the lack of refurbishments. This can be found in the profit and loss statements that were provided by the hotel (Penta Wiesbaden, 2020). In this statement, we find that there have been no investments in assets in the hotel in 2019 and 2018. Also, during the questions and answering session that we had with the general manager we discussed the capital expenditure plans of Penta for the upcoming years (Bienefeld, 2023). For 2023 they are planning to build air conditioning for the lounge area and invest in furniture, fixtures, and equipment at the same time. There are also talks about the renovations of the façade with the current owners. However, there are no major renovations on the books (Bienefeld, 2023). Next the decline in customers over the last five years. In 2018 Penta still had 74.787 guests. Jumping to 2019 this went up to 85.277 guests (Penta Wiesbaden, 2020). While in 2022 they only had 53.063 guests and in the year before that they had 30.311 guests (Penta Wiesbaden, 2022). Now of course some of these losses in guests can be attributed to COVID and the long lockdowns that Germany had as a whole. However, when looking at the STR data you find that in 2022 most of the hotels in Germany returned to their old occupancies when compared to pre-COVID (Ploppa & Koltze, 2022). This means Penta that the guests' amount should have returned to the 2019 or 2018 levels. But they did not. Finally, there is a lack of sustainability. In today's world, building a brand that is perceived as sustainable is a requirement; if a brand doesn't adhere, it will be punished. Consumer brand perception now has the power to shape a company's future (Corkery, 2020). Currently, the hotel has no plan to invest in sustainability in the near future like some of the other Penta Hotels. Most recently Penta Brussels won the prestigious Green Key award for their leading standard in sustainability (Mice Magazine, 2022). As before mentioned, Penta’s Wiesbaden capital expenditure overview does not include a plan to implement any sustainable changes in the near future. Furthermore, after asking multiple times for a sustainability plan no concrete answer was given. This lack of evidence speaks to the aforementioned statement. The lack of eye for sustainability is making the hotel even more outdated because, right now from the top 12 driving factors influencing what accommodation you choose, 5% of the people say that environmental credentials are one of the most important drivers. Furthermore, the environmental impact that your holiday has is also among the top eight factors in the choice of holiday destinations (Stringer, 2020).

Penta Wiesbaden has not performed well financially since 2020. This can be broken down into three main points. First, the average daily rate of Penta is lower than its four main competitors. The competitors are Dorint Hotel Pallas Wiesbaden, Courtyard Wiesbaden Nordenstadt, H+ Wiesbaden Niedernhausen, and Holiday Inn Express Wiesbaden. From Porter's five forces analysis we were able to conclude that out of these four, two have a higher conference room capacity and have more rooms. Since Penta’s market segmentation is mostly focused on corporate, with a 75% exposure to MICE, this is influencing the average daily rate (Bienefeld, 2023). When doing the competitive set analysis, we found that Penta has an average daily rate of €78,17 and the competitive set has an average of €86,31 this is a difference of 9,43%. When looking at the occupancy we find that they differ only 6,1 percentage points, favoring Penta (Gotthardt-Janssen, 2023). This means that the competition can ask more for their rooms and that their occupancy is not impacted as much when they raise the prices and thus hindering Penta’s financial performance. Penta also has two major contracts that restrict its ability to maximize profitability. Marked in the profit and loss statement as GWH, with these contracts the base rate per room is only €38,- instead of the normal BAR or best available rate which is €95,26 on average (Penta Wiesbaden, 2022). Just on these contracts alone, the hotel is drastically decreasing its RevPAR. These contracts also barely breakeven which means that financially speaking they are almost not even worth having. Finally, we have a lack of seating capacity in the restaurant. This is hindering the financial performance because they do not have enough seating area in their restaurant for their larger banquets. In the largest meeting room that they have they can fit 350 people, whilst in their restaurant they can currently only fit 200 (Pentahotel Wiesbaden, 2022; Pentahotels, 2022). This leaves the hotel when their meeting rooms are filled, with 42% of the guests that cannot have lunch or dinner at the restaurant because there are no seats available for them. When looking at competitors you can see that the H+ hotel and the Dorint can accommodate these types of larger groups (H-hotels, 2022; Dorint Hotel, 2022). This is yet again one of the reasons for poor performance.
