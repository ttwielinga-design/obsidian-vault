**Serving a pregnant woman alcohol, what?**

<img src="media/image1.png" style="width:2.81667in;height:3.61667in" />I have been living in Fort Lauderdale for around two and a half months now. The overall experience has been amazing, I am learning a lot at work getting all the opportunities I ask for, and I have an incredible team. During my time working at the W, I have experienced a few to me mind-blowing truths. The most mind-blowing truth I will discuss in this blog.

3 weeks ago, I had my TIPS training this is training required by law to make sure that you are knowledgeable and responsible enough to serve alcohol to guests. During this training, we discussed things like how to pour, serving sizes, and when to cut somebody off. As part of this training, we also had some practice situation videos. In these videos, we discussed what we would do in certain situations.

The first situation was quite simple. A guy was sitting at the bar, and he had had six whiskeys but didn’t show any signs of being drunk. He indicated to one of the servers that he wanted another drink, and after that drink, he would drive himself home. The question was: what would you do? We, as a group, decided to give him the whiskey and let him know that we would call a taxi to pick him up.

A few more of these relatively “normal” circumstances went by, and then a situation that blew my mind. This case was as follows: A highly pregnant woman walks into the bar and sits down with some friends. She orders an incredibly heavy alcoholic drink; I can’t quite remember what exactly but anyway. The question of course is would you serve her this drink?

Since I was the only non-American in this class the lecturer asked me: “Thomas what would you do?” I blurted out the obvious answer, to me, which was I would of course refuse to serve her. This the lecturer explained to me is incorrect and could get me into some very serious trouble and even a lawsuit! Because it is discriminating to refuse to serve a pregnant woman alcohol. I honestly can’t believe that this is the law here.

What I learned from this situation is that every country has its own laws and regulations and how strange they may seem to you they are normal to most people who live there.
