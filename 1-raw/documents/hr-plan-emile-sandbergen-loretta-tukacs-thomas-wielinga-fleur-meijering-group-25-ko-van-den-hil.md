**Introduction**

In the following Human Resource plan, our team, consisting of Thomas Wielinga, Emile Sandbergen, Fleur Meijering and Loretta Tukacs, will outline our vision for Hotel Ultima. For this plan we have taken into account the wishes and visions of the owner in order to bring his ideas to life to the best of our capabilities. 

This document will contain the following aspects of human resources:

- Organizational culture

- Organizational structure

- Qualitative staffing

- Quantitative staffing

- Performance appraisal interview outline

- Training and development of employees

- Internal communication

This human resource plan is intended as a guide to develop the hotel as desired by the owner whilst taking into account the financial aspects of human resource as well. We aim to provide a helicopter view of the hotel as a whole making sure all parts are connected and come together to perfect the Ultima experience for both the guests and the employees. Each of these sections of the human resource plan will be based on in-depth research, models and theories as well as from lectures from industry professionals.

**Organizational culture**

Whilst describing the company culture of Hotel Ultima we made sure to take into account all the core values namely, sassy, fueled and extravagant, as the owner has mentioned as well as the description of the atmosphere in the work environment the owner would like to create. Hotel Ultima's mission statement is to be loud and proud. To serve the guests and the community in the Ultimate way. Nothing is too extravagant; everything is ultimately possible. Their vision is to have Ultima challenge and disrupt, but deeply rooted in the community and attached to their guests. 

In the following explanation of organizational culture, we will make use of Quinn's competing values framework model to best demonstrate the culture we would like to implement. Within the competing values framework we believe that hotel Ultima would be best suited for an adhocracy culture with some elements from the clan nature as well. Adhocracy is described as “encouraging risk-taking, and focused on innovation. In this less structured culture, all employees are encouraged to participate regardless of their position because you never know where the next big idea will come from!”. This culture would allow Ultima to become a unique and innovative work environment for all of its employees, they could all contribute their ideas and would have the power to bring their ideas to life and into practice.  

The owner, George Papadopoulos has also expressed his desire to create a culture in which employees have a strong sense of community, social responsibility and care for associates. This can be reflected through the clan framework. Creating the clan framework puts emphasis on collaboration and creates a high sense of community within an organization.  

In order to achieve this type of culture different kind of employees can be hired. It is important to keep some of the staff young or trainees who may have innovative ideas and be creative. Younger employees may lack experience but will bring the youthful atmosphere of the hotel to life. Combining younger employees with more experienced mentors or department heads will allow you to still keep to the standard operating procedure and have an individual within their department to whom they can look up to for advice.  

Another aspect to keep in mind is focusing on diversity within the hiring process. Making sure to hire not only locals but international staff as well. Keeping a diverse work environment will make sure that people from different backgrounds or nationalities can bring their personal touch to the hotel. Focusing on not only surface level diversity but also deep level diversity will create a melting pot of personalities, values and will create an environment in which guests from all over the world can feel at home. Whilst talking about diversity it is important to mention that working together with different types of people may not be smooth from the start however, through intercultural trainings and team building activities one can bridge the gap between people. 

It is also important to provide training for the employees on feedback culture through which they can discuss any issues or problems they may face within the organization. Creating an atmosphere where feedback is not only accepted but appreciated will increase trust between employees and may even better their working relationship. Using the self-determination theory, we can deduct that people are generally intrinsically motivated. This hotel has a strong potential for building intrinsic motivation as it allows their employees to be themselves and take pride in the work, they perform every day. Taking into account the hotel's attitude towards its employees it is clear to see that they want empowered and autonomous employees who have the freedom to make decisions within the bounds of the standard operating procedures.

**Organizational structure**

Looking at the organizational structure we have decided to base it off the common line structure, as seen below on the organogram we created specifically for hotel Ultima. However, one main difference is that we decided against hiring assistant managers of any kind. This decision is just as much practical as it is fiscally responsible, as from the wage of one assistant manager we can hire on average 2 extra employees. Yet, we would like to use the money we “saved” to focus on the training of the department heads so in case the General Manager is sick one of the department heads can step up for the time being. This structure also allows for less of the “middleman” as information gets passed straight from the general manager to the department heads. Clear communication between departments and silos in the structure allow for smooth and rapid execution of tasks. We chose this structure to create a more family-like feel as there is less focus on hierarchy and the general manager will be more available and easier to reach for employees as well as department heads. This structure ties into the above-mentioned company culture as it highlights the Clan section from the competing values framework. We want the employees to see their superiors more like mentors and less like bosses. This would allow for easier bottom-up communication as well as top down.

**Qualitative staffing**

Personal skills for the staff:

- **Be willing to do whatever it takes**, go the extra mile for the guest and never say no to a guests’ wish

- **Team orientated associates** who are unique individuals and can operate in a team

- **Have a sense of responsibility** (staff is empowered to take decisions and enjoy a fair amount of freedom)

- **Have interpersonal skills**, to have good communication with the guest and staff

- **Are result oriented**, to be able to think fast and adapt quickly

- **Are unique**, and are open-minded to new trends and cultures

These skills are all the aspects that we look for in an employee. The employees are both hospitality oriented, as well as open minded to new trends. These employees function as individuals, when necessary, but are team players most of the time. Next to these personal skills, Ultima asks for some previous experiences and basic skills:

- English speaker, B2

- Communicative skills

- 6 months of work in the hospitality industry (basic employee)

- 2 years of work in the hospitality industry (supervisor)

- Able to handle stressful situations 

**Front office employee**

The front office employee is responsible for checking guests in and out of their rooms. They are often the guest’s first encounter with an employee when entering a hotel, so it’s important the employee leaves a good first impression. Next to checking in, the front office employee is able to answer general questions and give advice on things to do in the area.

The employee should have some experience in hospitality, preferably in the front or back-office department. Furthermore, the employee should have a high-school degree and should speak English and Dutch at B2 level. The employee should also have good interpersonal skills, should be able to multitask, and should be detail-oriented. 

Personality traits:

- Hospitable

- Attention to detail

- Multitasker

- Discretion

- Team player

- Local knowledge

**F&B Manager**

The F&B manager is responsible for making sure both the restaurant and bar perform optimally, on quality of the products as well as the guest’s experience. The manager handles customer complaints, exemplifies the core values of the hotel, and makes sure all health & safety regulations are met. They should be in direct contact with the kitchen, and together they should be able to create a menu and pair it with drinks. 

The F&B manager should have a culinary education and 2 years of management experience or have a management education with 4 years of hospitality experience. Also, they should handle both problems and conflicts with the guest and within the team. 

Personality traits:

- Attentiveness 

- Punctuality 

- Organized 

- Local knowledge

- Innovative thinker

- Youthful 

- Sense of urgency

> **Quantitative staffing**

For the Quantitative staffing, payroll and related expenses (manning guide) we have tried to keep in mind the following ratio’s: Average payroll per employee, Payroll percentage and payroll percentage per department. We did not look at FTE / Room since we cannot compare our hotel with the Statista data as we have 4 F&B outlets and that is not the average for a 5-star hotel. Below we will present the ratios with some explanation, for the full payroll please see the appendix.

For our gross operating revenue we ended up at € 20.987.511,25. Our ADR is €226,-. With this information we were able to calculate that our REVPAR =€185,55. TREVPAR is €287,50 and our GOPPAR = € 139,83.

**Average payroll per employee**

For us this is at € 38.416 ,11, this is almost € 200 lower than the low benchmark for our ratio’s. This is likely due to the fact that we have a lot of trainees in our hotel. We believe in bringing in a lot of new energy and inspiration, as we believe that young people possess such traits. We also show that we are invested in the trainees since we pay them 50% more than normal[^1].

**Payroll %**

Our “Total Payroll incl. employers’ benefits / Total Revenue” is 29% which fits perfectly between the Statista averages which are between 27% and 32%.

**Payroll% per department**

- Rooms: 14%, for Statista is between 14% and 17%.

- Food and Beverage: 37%, for Statista is between 40% and 45%.

- M.O.D.: 76%, for Statista this is between 33% and 50%.

- A.&G.: 3%, for Statista this is between 3% and 4%.

- S.&M.: 2%, for Statista this is between 3% and 4%.

- Property operations: 2%, for Statista this is between 2% and 3%.

For F&B it is clear that we are on the high side, this is due to the fact that we have more F&B outlets than average for a 5-star hotel. The Tube aspires to be a Michelin star restaurant hence, we want to make sure that there is enough staff to go around.

The M.O.D. average is rather high for us since we don’t have that much revenue other than the spa and that is not that lucrative because it needs a lot of man-hours to be fully operational. We also have one employee working in the gym who are only needed form Wednesday until Sunday from between 8 and 4 because we expect those are the hours the gym is busiest on the other days/hours the spa employees can help out. For the rest we don’t have any other M.O.D. departments other than the spa.

For our S.&M. we also have a lower than usual ratio since we make a lot of revenue per year because of our F&B outlets. That is why our S.&M. payroll is lower.

To conclude we feel that the 157 is a realistic amount of FTE’s necessary to be fully operational with this hotel. Most of our ratios are similar to the Statista average. We feel we can confidently give this payroll / manning guide to hotel Ultima, and with using this they will be successful.

**Elective theme one: Performance appraisal**

The Ultima is a vibrant hotel with a young attitude, meaning that management acts on futuristic HR trends. With this futuristic take on HR management, The Ultima strives to make the employee journey more easy-going. Performance appraisal interviews are seen as stressful because they have a reputation for being negative. 

To make the employee journey less stressful, HR management has decided to set up a performance review structure of its own consisting of frequency, preparation, interview, and a check-up.

- Frequency: usually performance appraisals are done once or twice a year, but management wants performance reviews in the hotel to have a more positive assumption by scheduling the interviews more regularly. Management wants to do a performance review every quarter, where the last of the year is a more thorough interview, and the other three are quicker. 

<!-- -->

- Preparation: the management of The Ultima asks both the employee and manager to prepare for the interview. The manager does a 360-degree evaluation, which consists of feedback from the employee from their clients, co-workers, and supervisors. After, the manager looks at the previously set goals and if these have been met. 

- For the employee, they should prepare by looking at what they think they could improve upon, and what they think they strived for since the last interview. They should also look at if they achieved their previously set goals. The shift before the interview, the manager sends the agenda of the points to the employee to minimize surprises. There is a clear date and time set for the interview beforehand.

<!-- -->

- Interview: The manager discusses the agenda and puts the interviewee at ease. Next, the manager indicates the goal, time, and structure of the interview. The manager gives the interviewee first an opportunity to ask any questions about management. After, the evaluation of the employee starts. Here, the manager needs to discuss all points that were on the agenda for legal reasons. 

- It is important for the manager to start with the positive feedback in order to kick off the interview on a positive note and engage the employee further into the conversation (Van der Molen & Gramsbergen-Hoogland 2019) It’s important to discuss both negative and positive points. After the points are discussed, goals are set for the upcoming time, and concrete agreements are made. Then, the manager summarizes, repeats the goals and agreements, and shares their opinion on how the interview went. It’s also important that the interviewee gives their opinion on the interview.

<!-- -->

- Check-up: After a short while, management wants to do small check-ups with the employees, just to see if the set agreements have been met and if the employee is improving. 

- Desired outcome: With the transparency between management and employee, management hopes to create a more open-minded communication for feedback. Like mentioned in Gomez: “Performance discussions can be difficult for managers, and a worker may disagree that there is a performance issue and become emotional”. Because of this issue, we believe that a more open and more frequent performance appraisal will resolve the issue of performance discussions.

**Employee journey: Training and development **

A topic we believe is crucial to take into consideration, is training and development as it is an extremely important HR policy regarding retention. In this chapter we are going to give in depth information on how the Ultima hotel behavior and organizational structure should influence the overall retention and which policies should be utilized.

 

It has become significantly harder to find the right individual for a particular job which sometimes can lead to recruiting employees that aren’t able to perform a certain role/task. When employees are aware and trained for the expectations of a hotel their capabilities will significantly increase leading to content employees who are more productive, inspired, motivated and less likely to quit. Not only will this have a positive effect on the employees but also on the hotel. Therefore, training is an essential part of the development of your employees.

To attract, train and retain the right employees to increase their capability in an effective manner, there are some steps that we recommend that should be taken. These are:

- Reward the high performing employees, this is a powerful motivator, which can bring out the greatest performance within a team. When employees know that there will be some sort of reward, it is more likely that they will put more effort into their work and achieve better results. This will create a dynamic and healthy competitive atmosphere between the team that will further engage and thrill them. This is an example of extrinsic motivation.

- Individual training, we think this should only be provided, when there is a significant difference between the employee’s performance, for instance when a housekeeper is struggling and not on a similar level compared to their team, then we should give them some personal attention, which will allow them to grow.

- Group training is usually more superficial than individual training, but if this is done correctly and consistently then the team collaboration will improve and enhance the group performance. Having your employees go through experiences together will boost their ability to work together well, as the hotel Ultima strategy is to have “**associates that are able to function in a team environment”.**

- We recommend hiring specialists in leadership courses and give lectures to the department heads. Being a good leader is a crucial skill to manage while being a superior. These courses will boost their interpersonal skills, teach them how to use effective communication skills to influence and persuade your staff and allow leaders to have the ability to be more innovative and think out of the box, which as we know are keys to success. This will all result in managers that “**go the extra mile”** are good in making decisions, have more employee engagement and retain people.

- Personal attention: The supervisors should have individual meetings once in while with their employees, not only to talk about their performance but also about their personal circumstances or anything they would like to share. This will enhance the relationship and trust, which will create a healthy workplace and connection between the supervisors and the staff. The supervisors could arrange this by scheduling a meeting at least once a month, this can happen during work hours but if more convenient they can also take place offline when employees are not present on specific dates. 

By following these recommended steps, the Ultima hotel will develop into a successful company. Employees will have a sense of fulfillment and therefore be satisfied, which makes them more likely to retain, increase their productivity, efficiencies and increase the company's fortune.

**Internal communication**

The purpose of internal communication is to provide a successful flow of information between the company’s departments and the employees. When the communication is used in an effective manner, all parties will be satisfied and have a feeling of fulfilment.  

A term frequently used is “Communication is key” which we truly agree with. Concerning this case, the Hotel Ultima is a company that allows a lot of freedom for the employees and **“empowers their staff to take decisions”,** will create a team that is motivated and work productively together to reach their missions, goals, and values in an efficient way. 

 

In this chapter we are going to give advice on how we think the internal communication should happen in the hotel Ultima, as this is shaped through the structure and culture of the establishment.

**Employee engagement survey** 

Engagement surveys are crucial, as they give the employees the opportunity to have a direct voice and establish a two-way communication between the employer and the employee and form a better connection, which will improve the employee's safety and satisfaction. This should be done annually at the hotel, so the connection that the staff have towards the hotel can be measured as well as, giving employees the opportunity to report problems and share their emotions. 

By getting a good insight on the employee’s thoughts, the company can get an understanding where improvements should be made and get a better understanding on what your employees needs are.

The employee engagement survey should make the employees feel that they are being heard and that their opinions matter, by doing this the employers show that they care and value their feelings and that they appreciate them.

**Shifts**

The hotel has different shifts both day and night in most services, therefore it is crucial that the change of shifts goes smoothly. To do so, there should be a brief discussion at the end of each shift, to inform the employees of the new service correctly. This information can be for example, things that aren’t working properly, preparations that need to be finished or done and what the hotel's occupancy is by looking at the amount of check-ins. In that manner the employees know beforehand what is expected of them and what needs to be done and avoids unnecessary confusion.

**Performance meetings **

The aim of the performance meetings is to build an open communication between the manager and their employees. Therefore, the managers and supervisors in the hotel Ultima must have one-on-one conversations with their employees bimonthly to discuss their strategic priorities for a period and if the previous goals have been reached. This gives the employees the opportunity to get personal and discuss their concerns and opinions in a comfortable and collaborative setting. We believe that by doing so it will help supervisors and the managers to keep track of their employees’ performance and whether they might need some extra support or if they might be able to handle a higher position at the hotel. This can also be very useful to formulate a clear picture of all your staff members and what their weaknesses, strengths and personality traits are.

Employees that feel trusted and heard at work, tend to be more confident and perform at a higher level. When there is a good relationship between the manager and employee it usually results in more openness and forthcoming about problems that they might be facing and impacting their performance. 

**Training and courses**

All new employees require an onboarding process before starting their job at the hotel.  We think that it would be very beneficial if all new employees received a course about communication as the hotel service style is unique, **“formal, modern, and cheeky, yet polite”** this should be taught to them. This course could also approach topics such as how the communication and information is shared between the staff at the hotel and where they can find this information, as the employees always need to be aware of the relevant and new information.  Another topic we want to discuss in this course is discrimination and racism and that the hotel has a zero-tolerance policy for this. 

**Conclusion**

- As stated in the introduction we created this personalized plan to fit the wishes and visions of the owner. We hope that we have achieved this goal and wrote a plan that brings his vision to life in all aspects. To summarize our ideas in a clear way please take a look at the following information:

- Organizational culture consists of adhocracy and a touch of clan, as based on the competing values framework. 

- Organizational structure was created to fit the culture; no assistant managers are to be hired to create an easier flow of information both top down and bottom up. 

- Qualitative staffing consists of personal skills, educational requirements and skills, and job descriptions.

- Staffing wise our research has led us to believe that 157 FTEs will provide everyone with a comfortable work environment whilst maintaining a profitable organization.

- Conducting the performance appraisal interview provides feedback to both the employee and management. This moment of reflection sets goals and gives a possibility to grow for the employee.

- Internal communication purpose is to provide a successful flow of information between the company’s departments and the employees. Therefore, we gave some advice on topics such as shifts, performance meetings, training and courses and employee engagement surveys. By following these suggestions, we expect that all parties will be satisfied and have a feeling of fulfillment which will lead to more productivity and increase the profitability of the hotel.

- Training development is crucial to have in a company as this will attract, train and retain the right employees, which will increase their capability and the employee’s satisfaction. Steps that should be taken are rewarding the high performing employees, individual and group training, personal attention and offering leadership courses to the managers.
