# Chapter 1. Introduction

In the fast-paced hospitality sector, Black Label Hospitality (BLH), a Third-Party White Label Operator (TPWLO), is focused on luxury and lifestyle-oriented services. With multiple multi-unit development agreements secured with the leading hotel chains - Marriott, Accor, Hilton, Wyndham, Hyatt, and IHG, BLH is now setting its sights on expansion (Black label hospitality, 2023). But first, what is TPWLO? In short, it is a hotel management firm without a trademark (Wagner, 2024). In other words, a person or hotel management company that is hired to run and oversee a hotel (Pecunica, 2020). As a TPWLO, BLH acts as a crucial intermediary between property developers/owners and hotel brands, facilitating the enhancement of its partners' brand value and operational efficiency. The company aims to shift its focus from an exclusive concentration on the Netherlands to broadening its presence in Germany. BLH prioritizes projects with financial viability, aiming for a first stable year GOP of 35%, and aligning with their standards and strategic objectives, such as securing long-term leases of 20 years or more (Black label hospitality, 2023).

Currently, little is known about the dynamics of the TPWLO market in Germany. There is a scarcity of information, this poses a significant challenge. This is because the German TPWLO market is notably opaque, with secondary research on this topic proving difficult, if not impossible, to find. This observation was made concrete by two industry professionals namely, Hurkmans (2024) and Schöningh (2024). They both emphasized the importance of interviews to get a better understanding of the inner workings of the market. The research gap becomes evident through these first interviews in combination with the lack of secondary research. Thus, this paper seeks to bridge this gap by exploring **how BLH can optimize its strategy for entering the German market to strengthen its white-label positioning**. This management question is crucial not only for BLH's successful market penetration but also for contributing to the broader practical understanding of the TPWLO market due to its closed nature. By focusing on primary data collection in the form of interviews, this research aims to provide valuable insights for BLH as they investigate this opportunity.

This paper is divided into seven chapters and contains research on the German TPWLO market. Chapter two begins with a thorough literature review, which incorporates secondary sources and expert insights and leads to research questions. The process is described in Chapter Three, with a focus on gathering fresh material via interviews. Chapter Four presents the results of the qualitative research, and Chapter Five integrates these findings to meet the research questions. Chapter six finishes this article with a thoughtful analysis and practical recommendations for future research projects and managerial applications. Chapter seven finishes by discussing the study's limitations and providing an honest appraisal of areas that should be improved in future research.

### 2.2.1 Vehicles 

Within the strategy diamond framework, "Vehicles" represent the critical actions required to achieve your strategic objectives. They function as the roadmap, guiding you from your desired arenas (target markets) and differentiators (competitive advantages) to strategic success (Coyne & Whittington, 2015).

Franchise agreements are a strategic choice for hotel operators seeking success in a competitive industry, offering a powerful combination of brand recognition, operational support, and financial advantages (Carlton Fields, 2022). Compared to other models like management contracts, franchises provide more control over day-to-day operations while still benefiting from an established brand (Mahroos, 2021). Operators retain decision-making authority but comply with brand standards (Mahroos, 2021).

Franchisors typically provide expertise, support, training, and access to knowledge bases, marketing resources, and best practices, enhancing operational efficiency and brand alignment (Mahroos, 2021). Franchising also facilitates market expansion. Brands leverage local franchisees with established networks to penetrate new markets (Carpentier & Collins, 2022).

In essence, a franchise agreement is a license agreement between the hotel owner (franchisee) and the brand owner (franchisor) (Carlton Fields, 2022). It outlines the rights and obligations for operating under the brand in exchange for fees (Carlton Fields, 2022). While offering flexibility, these agreements are personal and cannot be transferred by the current owner (Carlton Fields, 2022).

Branded hotel companies strategically use key money during franchise applications to secure agreements and attract owners/developers (Shindler, 2023; Kornreich, 2017). This financial incentive benefits both parties involved in hotel transactions. For hotel brands, key money secures the right property in a competitive location, strengthening their market presence (Ploppa, 2022). Property owners benefit from an upfront payment that reduces financial risk and provides security (Shindler, 2023).

The structure of key money agreements varies between hotel companies (Shindler, 2023). The most common structure is a self-amortizing loan, where the key money is forgiven if the agreement is fulfilled. Early termination requires repayment of the remaining balance (Shindler, 2023). Some companies use annual reduction, gradually reducing the outstanding amount throughout the agreement to discourage early termination (Shindler, 2023). Alternatively, repayment upon termination requires the unamortized amount to be repaid if the agreement ends prematurely (Shindler, 2023). There are also custom structures, where some companies use the term "consideration" within the agreement (Shindler, 2023).

The direction of payment depends on the company type (Shindler, 2023; Hendricks, 2024; Hodgson et al., 2022). Typically, the franchisor pays the franchisee. In rare cases, an operator might pay the owner, particularly in high-demand locations (Hodgson et al., 2022). Asset-light operators, like BLH, typically receive key money from brands, while asset-heavy operators generally do not (Hendricks, 2024; Hodgson et al., 2022). In highly sought-after locations, property owners might demand key money due to scarcity and high demand (Hodgson et al., 2022). Overall, key money plays a significant role in shaping hotel agreements and attracting owners to specific brands (Shindler, 2023; Kornreich, 2017).

In the hospitality industry, the Step-in clause is a critical safeguard for property owners. It ensures that they receive support in the event of a management failure by the service provider. For BLH, operating as a third-party white-label operator, this clause is particularly crucial. It allows the hotel brand to take over management duties if Black Label fails to meet its obligations, ensuring continuous support for the property owner and uninterrupted hotel operations (Sreenivasan & Ang, 2022).

The Step-in clause protects the property owner’s interests by facilitating a smooth transition of management responsibilities to the hotel brand in case of BLH ’s underperformance (Goodwin, 2019).

Considering all secondary conditions above the question remains what the differences are offered by the brands to TPWLO’s. Through numerous interviews with brands an answer to this question could be found.

### 2.2.3 Staging

This facet addresses the timing and sequence of strategic actions that will be taken (Morris, 2019). This involves planning the steps and pacing needed to execute the strategy (Morris, 2019). The goal of staging is to create a structured roadmap to implement the strategy over time by identifying and resolving the gaps within this facet.

BLH will experience strong supplier power in Germany, particularly in desirable areas where real estate is in high demand and limited supply (Bauer, 2022). There may not be as many prime locations available for newly constructed or converted hotels, which could raise expenses and affect profitability (Bauer, 2022). To reduce this danger, BLH would have to work out advantageous conditions with property owners and guarantee access to a wide range of suppliers (Porter, 1979; MTCT \[Mind Tools Content Team\], 2024).

German customers are renowned for having high standards for both value and quality of service (Statista, 2022; Brougham, 2023). The market's strong purchasing power must be taken into consideration in BLH's expansion plan, which calls for an emphasis on competitive prices and customer satisfaction (Statista, 2024a). Stressing BLH's proven track record of excellent guest experiences and operational efficiency may aid in drawing in new business and keeping existing clients (Porter, 1979; Kleinekoort et al., 2023; MTCT, 2024).

Alternative lodging options like vacation rentals, boutique hotels, and low-cost lodging options pose a threat of substitution to the German hotel industry (BNP PARIBAS REAL ESTATE, 2023c, 2023d). To counter this danger, BLH's strategy could emphasize its distinctive products and services, such as customized hospitality experiences, and use technology to improve guest services. This will provide it with a competitive advantage (Porter, 1979; MTCT, 2024).

Several obstacles can prevent someone from entering the German hotel industry, as high initial costs, legal restrictions, and the demand for local market expertise (Hotel Speak, 2020; Ploppa & Koltze, 2021). However, BLH's asset-light business strategy, which is predicated on long-term lease agreements, puts it in a better position to handle these difficulties. # Chapter 3. Methodology

The methodological approach for this study was determined by the relatively unexplored territory of the TPWLO market, which is characterized by its closed nature and scarcity of literature (Hendricks, 2024).

## 3.1 Explain Your Methodological Approach

A qualitative research strategy based on semi-structured interviews with industry specialists proved to be the most successful method for gaining deep insights into this specific field (McCombes, 2023). This method is chosen because direct interactions with TPWLO specialists will provide the most relevant and detailed understanding of market dynamics, entry plans, and competitive positioning in the German environment.

Given the specialist nature of this study, guaranteeing the reliability and validity of the research findings was critical in establishing the credibility of the conclusions reached. For this purpose, numerous tactics were used:

1.  **Development of a Semi-Structured Interview Guide (Appendix):** Designed to promote unity throughout interviews while allowing for the investigation of emerging themes (McCombes, 2023). This guide was guided by a thorough analysis of current literature and is intended to delve into important topics related to BLH’s market positioning and strategic ambitions.

2.  **Pilot-testing:** Before full-scale implementation, the interview questions have been pilot-tested with a group of industry experts, such as two employees from different two different competitor companies, experts from chains and other industry experts (McCombes, 2023). This phase was critical for refining the questions to improve clarity and ensuring they revealed full insights for each important stakeholder, such as developers, hotel chains, private equity, competitors, and industry experts.

3.  **Expert Selection and Training:** To increase the validity of the findings, experts were chosen for their extensive expertise and experience in the TPWLO sector (McCombes, 2023). Furthermore, as interviewers, we reviewed the questions together to ensure consistency in interviewing abilities and to reduce potential misinterpretations.

4.  **Minimizing Bias**: Recognizing the potential for various forms of bias to influence the data, measures were taken to mitigate their impact (McCombes, 2023). This included the strategic development of the interview guide, careful selection, and training of interviewers, and consistently maintaining a reflective perspective throughout the research process to critically evaluate the influence of the researchers' assumptions and biases (Appendix).

In conducting this study, the ethical considerations were important, especially given the qualitative approach.

- **Confidentiality and Anonymity**: To uphold the ethical standards of research, strict measures were implemented to ensure the confidentiality and anonymity of participants' responses, like keeping the participants anonymous when disclosing information to BLH (McCombes, 2023). This was crucial to encourage open and honest discourse, allowing participants to share insights without fear of repercussion or exposure.

- **Informed Consent**: Participants were fully informed about the study's objectives, the nature of their involvement, and how their data would be used. Informed consent was obtained from all participants before their participation, ensuring they were willingly and knowingly contributing to the research.

These ethical considerations were integral to the methodological approach, ensuring that the study not only adhered to the highest standards of research ethics but also contributed valuable and credible insights into the TPWLO market.

## 3.2 Describe Your Data Collection Methods

This section describes the complete approach used to acquire data. Participants were chosen based on their direct connection with or knowledge of the TPWLO market, which included industry professionals, hotel operators, and consultants with experience in Germany. ## 3.3 Data Processing and Analysis

Following data collection, thematic analysis was used for most of the analysis. This method is especially useful for detecting, evaluating, and reporting patterns (themes) in data. The methodology was separated into six parts to ensure a thorough and fine understanding of the TPWLO market findings.

Initially, the study team reviewed the interview recordings and went on to transcribe them. This process entailed repeatedly listening to the recordings and writing transcripts. After this we reread them to make ourselves more familiar with the data, acquiring an early idea of the participant's views.

The next step was to systematically code the data. A form of inductive coding was used in these steps, namely thematic coding. This analysis is the process of segmenting and categorizing data to find parts that are relevant to different themes. This was accomplished by highlighting and annotating text segments within the transcripts that reflected noteworthy or intriguing topics relevant to our study's research questions.

After coding, the team organized the codes into potential themes. This entailed grouping similar codes into thematic groups that captured the key notions arising from the data. Each forthcoming theme was assessed to the coded sections and the complete data set to ensure it represented a meaningful pattern.

The following phase entailed assessing the selected themes to ensure that they followed a consistent pattern, were unique from one another, and were relevant to the study topics. This process occasionally prompted the team to go back over the data and re-code or modify the themes to properly reflect the results.

After the themes had been refined, they were defined and given names. This procedure required writing analyses for each topic, describing what it revealed about the data and why it was relevant to the research goals. The themes were named briefly to capture their essence.

The final stage was to include the thematic analysis in the research report. This involved selecting vivid and representative quotes from the transcripts to highlight each concept. The report narratively presented how the themes emerged from the codes and how they linked to the study objectives, providing a comprehensive, thorough, and complicated description of the data.

The purposive sample technique, which included people with direct relevance and knowledge, increased the validity of the insights gained (Caulfield, 2023). Measures such as interviewer training and reflexive practice are intended to reduce biases, increasing the dependability of the data collected.

Through semi-structured interviews, this qualitative approach goes into the complexity of the German TPWLO market, adding new knowledge (Caulfield, 2023). The methodological design of this study positions it to produce accurate and reliable data that provide important insights into the TPWLO market.
