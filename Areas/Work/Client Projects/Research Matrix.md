# Research area matrix

|  | Research Area | Research Focus | Potential Solution | Research Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  | Short-term vs. Long-term Focus | Examine how the current ad-hoc, short-term focus detracts from long-term strategic planning. Investigate common "fires" that can be systematized to reduce disruptions. | Design a framework to balance short-term tasks with long-term strategic goals by categorizing tasks as urgent, important, or non-essential, and developing protocols for managing each. | Conduct interviews to identify recurring short-term challenges and explore potential task categorization and prioritization methods. |
|  | Overlapping Roles and Responsibilities | Investigate whether unclear roles and overlapping responsibilities lead to inefficiencies and if formalizing roles could streamline workflows and improve accountability. | Propose clear role definitions for each stakeholder, with detailed breakdowns of responsibilities and authority to reduce overlap and confusion. | Interview stakeholders (Rowin, Jeroen, Wesley, Madelon, Ajar) to understand where lack of clarity in roles affects their work and how it could be improved. |
|  | Ideal World Vision for Stakeholders | Explore what the ideal process would look like for each stakeholder in terms of responsibilities, timelines, and accountability. | Develop a structure where tasks are iteratively reviewed, signed off, and handed over seamlessly, with a maximum number of iterations before moving forward. | Ask stakeholders for their vision of accountability, feedback loops, and decision-making, and how they see the process working in an "ideal" scenario. |
|  | Accountability and Sign-offs | Explore when and how different stakeholders (Development, Operations, MD) should sign off on work to avoid delays and conflict, focusing on authority and accountability across levels. | Create a clear accountability structure, defining sign-off responsibilities, with a specific focus on how much authority department heads (Joost, Amber) have before escalating to the Managing Director. | Investigate the existing approval workflow, and propose more efficient sign-off mechanisms to minimize bottlenecks in the approval process. |
|  | Authority and Culture of Accountability | Investigate the cultural and structural reasons for past accountability failures, especially considering the Managing Director’s role, and whether authority concentration leads to inefficiencies. | Propose a decision-making framework with decentralized authority, reducing the MD’s involvement in daily approvals, while fostering a culture of accountability at all levels. | Explore how a culture shift toward accountability can be encouraged, perhaps by involving team leaders more in decision-making and implementing clear deadlines. |
